# 06 — Channel Playbook
## Arista Networks (ANET)

---

## 1. Channel Strategy Overview

Arista's go-to-market is fundamentally a direct sales + technical partner model. The brand does not rely on mass-market distribution or reseller volume — instead it deploys highly technical Systems Engineers (SEs) alongside a curated set of value-added resellers (VARs) and system integrators who can support complex enterprise and cloud deployments. Channel marketing is engineering-credibility-first, not campaign-driven. (estimated)

## 6. Battle Cards — Channel-Specific

### Battle Card 1 — Winning Through the VAR Channel vs. Cisco

**Scenario:** VAR account manager is also a Cisco Gold Partner and defaults to Cisco for data center refresh.

**Arista's Counter:**
- Arista's margin structure for VARs is competitive with Cisco's enterprise switching margins on equivalent deal size (estimated)
- Arista SEs are available to co-sell without requiring the VAR to have certified Arista engineers on staff initially
- ACE certification is faster to complete than CCNA/CCNP curriculum — lower enablement cost for the partner's team
- CloudVision managed services create recurring revenue opportunity for the VAR that Cisco DNA Center does not replicate cleanly

**Key Ask to VAR AM:** "Register one Arista deal in the next quarter. We'll put an SE on it. If we don't win, you've lost nothing. If we do, you've opened a new line of revenue."

---

### Battle Card 2 — Winning the Federal Channel vs. Cisco + Juniper

**Scenario:** Federal SI evaluating network refresh for a civilian agency; Cisco and Juniper are incumbents on approved product lists.

**Arista's Counter:**
- EOS achieves DISA STIG compliance; Arista TAA-compliant hardware available for federal procurement (official)
- CloudVision provides the audit trail and change management documentation required by federal compliance frameworks (FISMA, NIST 800-53) (estimated)
- Single NOS across all Arista hardware reduces the attack surface and simplifies STIG implementation compared to managing multiple Cisco operating systems
- Arista's federal partner program supports GSA Schedule and SEWP contracting vehicles (estimated)

**Key Ask to Federal SI:** "Let us run a STIG compliance gap analysis on the incumbent environment. We'll show you exactly where EOS reduces your compliance burden — before any procurement decision is made."

---

### Battle Card 3 — Winning Through Cloud Marketplace vs. Traditional Procurement

**Scenario:** Cloud-native enterprise or startup evaluating CloudVision-as-a-Service through AWS or Azure Marketplace; procurement team prefers cloud billing over traditional CapEx PO.

**Arista's Counter:**
- CloudVision available via AWS Marketplace — consumption billed to existing AWS account; no new vendor procurement process required (estimated)
- Attractive for organizations with committed cloud spend (AWS EDP / Azure MACC) where marketplace purchases draw down existing commitments
- Faster time-to-value: CloudVision SaaS trial can be activated in hours vs. weeks for on-premises deployment
- Removes the "hardware-first" barrier — customers can evaluate CloudVision's operational intelligence before committing to Arista hardware refresh

**Key Ask to Cloud Procurement Team:** "You already have AWS committed spend. Activate CloudVision on Marketplace this week — we'll validate your existing network visibility gaps at no additional procurement friction."

## 7. Steal Sheets — Channel-Specific

### Steal Sheet 1 — Steal Cisco VAR Mindshare

**Target:** VARs with existing Cisco Gold/Premier status who have not registered an Arista deal in 12+ months.

**The Play:**
1. Identify the VAR's top 5 data center accounts where Cisco hardware is approaching EOL in the next 18 months.
2. Offer a joint account planning session — Arista SE + VAR AM — to build a replacement proposal.
3. Position Arista's faster ACE certification path as a way for the VAR to differentiate from other Cisco-only resellers.
4. Provide co-branded competitive TCO analysis (Arista vs. Cisco Nexus) that the VAR can use directly with the end customer.

**Expected Outcome:** One registered deal per VAR; SE co-sell support converts at higher rate than cold VAR-only outreach. (estimated)

---

### Steal Sheet 2 — Steal from Juniper's EMEA Partner Base

**Target:** Juniper VARs in EMEA (UK, Germany, Netherlands, Nordics) serving financial services and telco customers.

**The Pain:** Juniper's EMEA partner program has experienced disruption following HPE's acquisition of Juniper (announced 2023, closed 2024). Partner uncertainty around program continuity, margin structure, and roadmap is elevated. (estimated)

**The Play:**
1. Proactively recruit Juniper EMEA partners with strong financial services practice.
2. Offer accelerated onboarding: Arista pays for ACE certification lab time for partner engineers during transition.
3. Lead with Arista's independence — "Arista is not being acquired. Our roadmap is not being integrated into a broader portfolio. Your customers get a focused networking company." (estimated)
4. Provide customer-facing "Why Arista now" deck co-branded with the partner.

**Expected Outcome:** Partner recruitment of 5–10 Juniper-displaced EMEA VARs per region per year; each brings 3–8 named accounts. (estimated)

---

### Steal Sheet 3 — Steal from Legacy Direct Sales with CloudVision SaaS Land-and-Expand

**Target:** Mid-market enterprise (500–5,000 employees) that has never engaged with Arista because direct SE coverage is insufficient at their deal size.

**The Pain:** Arista's direct sales model historically under-serves mid-market accounts that don't justify a dedicated SE. These accounts default to Cisco or Juniper by default, not preference.

**The Play:**
1. Route mid-market accounts to CloudVision SaaS via AWS/Azure Marketplace — low-friction entry point with no hardware requirement.
2. Partner with a CloudVision-certified VAR to provide managed services wrapper (monitoring, alerting, change management) that makes the offer turnkey for lean IT teams.
3. Once CloudVision is deployed on existing (non-Arista) hardware, use telemetry data to identify network health gaps and position Arista switching as the upgrade path.
4. Land on CloudVision SaaS → expand to Arista hardware refresh → grow to full CloudVision-managed campus + data center.

**Expected Outcome:** 18–24 month land-and-expand motion from CloudVision SaaS customer to full Arista hardware customer; higher conversion rate than cold hardware-first outreach. (estimated)
