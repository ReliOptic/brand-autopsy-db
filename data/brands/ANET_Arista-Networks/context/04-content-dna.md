# 04 — Content DNA
## Arista Networks (ANET)

---

## 1. Content Philosophy

Arista's content operates on a single governing principle: **proof over prose**. Every content asset is expected to earn credibility through data, specifications, and reproducible results — not through storytelling or emotional appeal. The brand publishes at a depth that peers find credible and competitors find difficult to match. (estimated)

## 3. Content Formats by Funnel Stage

### Top of Funnel (Awareness)
- **Conference presentations** — NANOG, ONS, IEEE, Interop; technical depth signals credibility to the right audience (estimated)
- **LinkedIn thought leadership posts** — authored by Arista engineers and executives; focus on protocol debates, industry trends, and AI networking (estimated)
- **Press releases** — product launches, financial results, customer wins (official)
- **Analyst briefings** — Gartner, IDC, Dell'Oro; shapes Magic Quadrant positioning (estimated)

### Middle of Funnel (Consideration)
- **White papers** — published at arista.com; topics include BGP EVPN design, AI fabric architecture, zero-trust segmentation (official)
- **Solution briefs** — 2–4 page technical summaries for specific use cases (official)
- **Webinars** — product deep-dives hosted by Arista SEs and product managers (estimated)
- **Reference architectures** — validated design guides for spine-leaf, campus, and AI fabric topologies (official)
- **CloudVision demos** — interactive or recorded; positioned as the operational intelligence differentiator (estimated)

### Bottom of Funnel (Decision)
- **Customer case studies** — named where possible; quantified outcomes (latency reduction, OPEX savings, incident MTTR) (official)
- **TCO comparison tools** — Arista vs. incumbent (Cisco) over 3-year ownership horizon (estimated)
- **Proof-of-concept support** — dedicated SE and free CloudVision trial (estimated)
- **EOS datasheets** — hardware platform specs, forwarding rates, latency figures (official)

## 4. Editorial Voice Rules

| Rule | Explanation |
|---|---|
| Lead with the metric | When a number exists, use it. "Sub-500ns latency" beats "ultra-low latency." |
| Cite the mechanism | Don't claim a benefit without explaining the architectural reason it's true. |
| No vendor bashing | Arista names competitors only in formal competitive materials; public content references "legacy architectures" or "proprietary solutions." |
| Engineer-to-engineer register | Write as a senior engineer explaining to a peer, not a marketer explaining to a buyer. |
| Active voice, short sentences | Technical content can be dense; sentence structure must not add to cognitive load. |
| Acronyms defined on first use | EVPN, VXLAN, MLAG, ISSU — all defined at first mention, then used freely. |
| Version-specific accuracy | EOS feature claims must reference the specific EOS version where the feature shipped. |

## 6. Content Anti-Patterns (what Arista avoids)

| Anti-Pattern | Why It's Avoided |
|---|---|
| Infographic-first product launches | The audience reads datasheets, not one-pagers with icons |
| CEO thought leadership on LinkedIn that is not technical | Jayshree Ullal posts are respected because they mix business insight with technical substance |
| Gated white papers with no preview | Arista tends toward low-friction access for technical content to build engineer goodwill |
| Broadcast advertising | No evidence of TV, outdoor, or display campaigns; audience is too narrow and too cynical (estimated) |
| Customer testimonial videos without specifics | "Arista transformed our network" with no data is discarded by technical buyers |

## 9. Tone Spectrum Examples

**Too weak (avoid):**
> "Arista provides networking solutions that help organizations manage their infrastructure more effectively."

**On-brand (use):**
> "Arista EOS 4.31.0 introduces EVPN multihoming with ESI-LAG across 7050X4 and 7280R3 platforms, enabling active-active redundancy without spanning tree for data center fabrics requiring sub-second failover."

**Too promotional (avoid):**
> "Arista is revolutionizing the way enterprises think about networking with our industry-leading CloudVision platform!"

**On-brand (use):**
> "CloudVision's streaming telemetry ingests device state every second — not every 5 minutes — so when a BGP session flaps at 2 a.m., your on-call engineer has the exact sequence of events before they dial into the bridge."
