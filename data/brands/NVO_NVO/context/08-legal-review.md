## Major Litigation and Regulatory Proceedings

### Pricing and Access Investigations

**U.S. Senate Finance Committee — Insulin Pricing Investigation (2021)**
The U.S. Senate Finance Committee published a report examining insulin pricing practices by Novo Nordisk, Eli Lilly, and Sanofi. The report raised questions about the relationship between list price increases, rebate practices, and patient out-of-pocket costs. (T3_SECONDARY_RELIABLE, Senate Finance Committee report publicly available) Novo Nordisk responded by implementing the NovoCare patient assistance program and capping certain insulin prices. The investigation did not result in enforcement action against Novo Nordisk as of the date of this analysis. (T3_SECONDARY_RELIABLE)

**FTC and State Attorney General Interest in Pharmaceutical Pricing**
Pharmaceutical pricing practices broadly, including GLP-1 products, have attracted attention from the Federal Trade Commission and state attorneys general. No specific enforcement action against Novo Nordisk in GLP-1 pricing has been confirmed as of this analysis. (T3_SECONDARY_RELIABLE, public statements and reports; data not available for confirmed enforcement actions)

### Patent Litigation

Novo Nordisk has been involved in patent litigation typical of large pharmaceutical companies, including:

- **ANDA (Abbreviated New Drug Application) patent challenges**: Generic manufacturers routinely file Paragraph IV certifications challenging pharmaceutical patents as part of the HATCH-Waxman process. Novo Nordisk's response to such challenges is not fully disclosed in public sources for all products. (T4_INFERRED from standard pharmaceutical litigation patterns)
- **Biosimilar insulin competition**: Patent disputes related to insulin biosimilars are an ongoing area of litigation in the pharmaceutical industry. Novo Nordisk's insulin products face biosimilar competition which may involve litigation. (T3_SECONDARY_RELIABLE)

### Regulatory Enforcement

**FDA Warning Letters**: No FDA Warning Letter addressed to Novo Nordisk for GLP-1 promotional violations has been identified in publicly available FDA enforcement records as of this analysis. (data not available — analysis based on publicly accessible FDA database search)

## Regulatory Landscape

### FDA (United States)

Novo Nordisk's US pharmaceutical operations are regulated by the FDA under:
- **21 CFR Part 202**: Prescription drug advertising regulations
- **21 CFR Part 314**: New drug applications (NDAs) for small molecule products (oral semaglutide)
- **21 CFR Part 601**: Biologics license applications (BLAs) for injectable biologics
- **FDCA Section 505**: Misbranding and off-label promotion prohibitions

All promotional materials for Novo Nordisk prescription products are subject to FDA Office of Prescription Drug Promotion (OPDP) review authority. (regulatory requirement)

### EMA (European Union)

Novo Nordisk's EU operations are regulated by the EMA and national competent authorities under:
- **Directive 2001/83/EC**: Medicinal products for human use
- **Regulation (EC) No 726/2004**: Centralized marketing authorization procedures

### Medicare Drug Price Negotiation (IRA, 2022)

The Inflation Reduction Act of 2022 established a framework for Medicare direct drug price negotiation beginning in 2026. Semaglutide products may be subject to this negotiation framework depending on qualifying criteria and CMS selection. Novo Nordisk identifies this as a material risk factor. (T1_OFFICIAL, Annual Report 2023 risk factors; T3_SECONDARY_RELIABLE, CMS implementation guidance)

### Litigation Posture Assessment

**Risk level for this project: MEDIUM**

Novo Nordisk is a publicly traded pharmaceutical company with a documented pattern of active IP defense and engagement with regulatory and congressional investigations. The company has legal resources commensurate with its scale (approximately $33B USD in revenue). However, the company has not demonstrated a pattern of suing brand analysts, journalists, or independent researchers for accurate, sourced commentary on its public business. The primary legal exposure for this project comes from:

1. Inaccurate clinical claims (stating a product does something not supported by the label)
2. Characterizing pricing practices as fraudulent or deceptive without regulatory citation

### DO NOT WRITE — High legal exposure for this project

| Prohibited Content | Risk |
|-------------------|------|
| Characterizing pricing practices as "fraudulent," "deceptive," or "exploitative" without direct regulatory citation | Defamation risk; pejorative assertion without regulatory basis |
| Asserting that Novo Nordisk caused patient harm through pricing with attribution of motive or intent | Intent attribution without evidentiary basis |
| Stating that a product is unsafe without citing a specific, identified regulatory finding | False statement of fact risk |
| Claiming off-label promotion occurred without citing a specific FDA warning letter or enforcement action | Defamation risk; unverifiable factual claim |
| Characterizing patent litigation strategy as designed to harm patients | Intent attribution; unsourced characterization |
| Reproducing full text of copyrighted clinical trial publications | Copyright infringement risk |
| Publishing any non-public information about the company's internal strategies | Potential trade secret or confidentiality issues if source is non-public |
