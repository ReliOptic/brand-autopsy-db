## Primary Personas

### Persona 1: The MS Specialist Neurologist

**Role**: Neurologist with MS subspecialty focus, academic medical center or MS specialty practice
**Patient volume**: 200–500 active MS patients (estimated)

**Pain**:
- Managing a heterogeneous MS patient population across relapsing-remitting, secondary progressive, and primary progressive subtypes with distinct treatment algorithms
- Navigating a crowded MS therapy landscape with oral, injectable, and infusion options from multiple manufacturers — including biosimilar pressure on older agents
- Staying current with evolving MS treatment guidelines and real-world evidence studies while managing clinical workload

**Gain**:
- Established clinical evidence base for Biogen's MS portfolio across long-term outcome data sets
- Biogen Medical Science Liaisons providing peer-level scientific exchange on MS disease biology and clinical data
- Patient support programs (Biogen's Avonex and Tysabri REMS programs) that assist with prior authorization, infusion center coordination, and patient adherence

**Buying trigger**: New MS diagnosis requiring initial therapy selection, disease breakthrough on current therapy requiring switch, biosimilar launch requiring formulary evaluation, new clinical guideline publication

**Channel**: Direct field medical/sales team, MS Society conferences (ECTRIMS, ACTRIMS, AAN), medical literature, peer-to-peer MSL engagement (observed, Biogen.com medical professionals section)

---

### Persona 2: The Behavioral Neurologist / Memory Specialist

**Role**: Behavioral neurologist or geriatric psychiatrist specializing in Alzheimer's disease, academic memory center
**Setting**: Academic medical center with PET imaging capability and infusion infrastructure (estimated)

**Pain**:
- Identifying appropriate Leqembi candidates requires amyloid PET or CSF biomarker confirmation — an infrastructure and cost barrier that limits prescribing to well-resourced academic centers currently
- Managing ARIA (amyloid-related imaging abnormalities) — the primary safety risk with anti-amyloid therapies — requires MRI monitoring protocols and patient/caregiver education
- Navigating Medicare coverage requirements for Leqembi under the Coverage with Evidence Development (CED) framework (as of Leqembi's launch period)

**Gain**:
- The first approved Alzheimer's therapy with demonstrated slowing of clinical decline — enabling a meaningful therapeutic conversation with patients and families previously told "there is nothing we can do"
- Biogen/Eisai's Leqembi prescriber support infrastructure (ARIA management protocols, patient support services, reimbursement assistance)
- Access to the Clarity AD trial data and Biogen's ongoing Alzheimer's biomarker research through MSL relationships

**Buying trigger**: Appropriate early Alzheimer's patient identified with confirmed amyloid pathology, patient/family requesting Leqembi, institutional protocol development for anti-amyloid therapy initiation

**Channel**: Direct MSL engagement, AAT (Alzheimer's Association International Conference), medical literature, Biogen/Eisai co-promotion (T4_ESTIMATED)

---

### Persona 3: The SMA Pediatric Neurologist

**Role**: Pediatric neurologist specializing in neuromuscular disease, children's hospital
**Setting**: NMD specialty clinic or pediatric academic medical center (estimated)

**Pain**:
- SMA treatment decisions are time-critical — early intervention before motor neuron loss optimizes outcomes; delays in diagnosis and treatment initiation are significant
- Navigating three approved SMA treatments (Spinraza, Zolgensma, Risdiplam) with different mechanisms, administration routes, and price points requires individualized benefit-risk discussions with families
- Insurance prior authorization for Spinraza (list price approximately $750,000 for first year) is a significant administrative burden (T3_SECONDARY_RELIABLE, published SMA drug pricing data)

**Gain**:
- Spinraza's extensive long-term safety and efficacy data set across pre- and post-symptomatic SMA patients provides clinical confidence for treatment decisions
- Biogen's patient support services for Spinraza navigation, prior authorization assistance, and intrathecal injection training
- Long-term follow-up data from ENDEAR, CHERISH, and SHINE trials supporting ongoing treatment decisions (official, Biogen clinical data publications)

**Buying trigger**: New SMA diagnosis (newborn screening trigger), transition from other SMA therapy, patient/family requesting long-term data review

**Channel**: Child Neurology Society, SMA patient advocacy organizations, direct Biogen neuromuscular field team (observed, Biogen.com)

## Anti-Persona

The primary care physician managing patients with mild memory complaints who has no access to amyloid PET imaging, no infusion center, and no neurological subspecialty training. While Alzheimer's disease is extremely common, Leqembi's current treatment paradigm — requiring biomarker confirmation, MRI monitoring, and IV infusion — places it outside the primary care prescribing context in the near term. ## Steal Sheet — 3 Transferable Principles

**1. Segment your prescriber audience by infrastructure capability, not just specialty.**
Leqembi's accessible prescriber universe is initially limited by amyloid PET availability, MRI monitoring capability, and infusion infrastructure — not just neurological subspecialty. Biogen's initial commercial focus on academic medical centers and memory centers reflects infrastructure-based segmentation. Transferable: when a product's adoption requires infrastructure your customer may not have, segment first by infrastructure readiness and invest in expanding that infrastructure as a commercial strategy.

**2. Invest in patient advocacy relationships years before commercial launch.**
Biogen's relationships with MS Society, NMSS, Alzheimer's Association, and SMA patient advocacy organizations were built over years, not at product launch. These organizations influence patient education, treatment guidelines, and payer policy. Transferable: in disease areas with active patient advocacy, begin building genuine programmatic relationships during Phase 2 or Phase 3, not at NDA submission.

**3. Use the caregiving burden as an audience touchpoint alongside the patient.**
For Alzheimer's disease, the caregiver is often the medical decision-maker and the most emotionally motivated advocate for treatment. Biogen's Leqembi communications address both the patient and the caregiver — acknowledging the full household impact of Alzheimer's disease. Transferable: in any therapeutic area where a family member or caregiver makes or strongly influences treatment decisions, design audience mapping and messaging for the caregiver alongside the patient.
