# 02 — Audience Map
## Brand: Biogen (BIIB)
## Sector: Health Care — Biotechnology

---

## 1. Audience Architecture Overview

Biogen operates across a multi-stakeholder ecosystem. The primary purchase decision in neurological therapeutics involves at minimum three parties: the prescribing specialist, the patient/caregiver, and the payer. Each layer has distinct motivations, vocabularies, and trust thresholds.

| Audience Tier | Label | Role in Decision |
|---|---|---|
| Tier 1 | Neurology Specialists (HCPs) | Prescribe; gate clinical access |
| Tier 2 | Patients & Caregivers | Drive adherence; influence prescriber via advocacy |
| Tier 3 | Payers & PBMs | Control formulary access; negotiate rebates |
| Tier 4 | Investors & Analysts | Capital allocation; pipeline valuation |
| Tier 5 | Regulators (FDA, EMA) | Approval; label language; post-market surveillance |
| Tier 6 | Patient Advocacy Organizations | Trust amplifiers; access advocates |

**Professional Profile:**
James has spent 20 years on the payer side. He has seen every pharmaceutical manufacturer's HEOR deck. He distrusts list price and focuses on net price, real-world outcomes, and comparative effectiveness. He is deeply skeptical of accelerated approvals without confirmatory trial data (the Aduhelm controversy informs his lens on Biogen specifically). He is also responsible for the plan's biosimilar adoption strategy.

**Goals:**
- Manage specialty pharmacy trend below 15% annual growth
- Ensure step therapy and prior authorization protocols protect the plan
- Negotiate best-in-class rebates for preferred formulary placement
- Demonstrate PMPM impact to the CFO

**Pain Points:**
- Biogen's Aduhelm launch (high price, contested evidence) created institutional skepticism
- Lack of outcomes-based contracting flexibility in neurological biologics
- ARIA monitoring costs add real-world cost burden not captured in clinical trial HEOR
- MS high-efficacy therapies (natalizumab, alemtuzumab) carry monitoring burdens that drive medical spend

**Message Resonance:**
- Cost-per-relapse avoided; cost-per-disability-progression prevented
- Real-world evidence of reduced hospitalizations and ER visits
- Outcomes-based contract structures (performance guarantees)
- Long-term actuarial modeling showing disease modification vs. symptomatic management

**Channels:**
- AMCP (Academy of Managed Care Pharmacy) Annual Meeting
- ICER reports and value framework
- Direct account management / payer relations team
- Health economics and outcomes research (HEOR) publications
- Advisory board engagements

## 5. Audience Tension Map

| Tension | Audience A | Audience B | Nature of Conflict |
|---|---|---|---|
| Access vs. Evidence | Patients (want fastest access) | Payers (want confirmatory data) | Accelerated approvals create split-decision formulary reality |
| HCP autonomy vs. utilization management | Neurologists (prescribe freely) | Payers (prior auth restrictions) | Step therapy forces inefficacy-first prescribing |
| Scientific complexity vs. patient comprehension | Biogen (data-first language) | Patients (need accessible narratives) | Brand voice optimized for HCPs alienates patients |
| Price vs. value | Investors (maximize revenue) | Payers/patients (minimize cost) | Specialty drug pricing creates access inequality and reputational risk |

## 7. Steal Sheets

### Steal Sheet 1: Steal the Specialist-First Strategy
**What Biogen does:** They build the entire commercial infrastructure around the 800-1,200 MS specialists who manage 80% of high-efficacy patients. KOL strategy, MSL investment, and conference presence are all precision-targeted.
**How to steal:** Map the 20% of prescribers who control 80% of prescriptions in your disease area. Over-invest in that cohort. One deeply informed MSL relationship is worth 100 rep calls.

### Steal Sheet 2: Steal the Patient Support Program Moat
**What Biogen does:** BiogenONE wraps the therapy in services — co-pay support, nursing support, adherence programs — that make switching costly not just clinically but logistically.
**How to steal:** Build services around your therapy that create switching costs beyond clinical efficacy. Every touchpoint that Biogen owns in the patient journey is a competitor touchpoint blocked.

### Steal Sheet 3: Steal the Disease Community Ownership Model
**What Biogen does:** They fund the National MS Society, patient registries, diagnosis tools, and awareness campaigns. When a patient is diagnosed with MS, Biogen-funded infrastructure is often the first thing they encounter.
**How to steal:** Fund diagnosis infrastructure in your disease area before your drug launches. Disease registries, screening tools, and advocacy grants are not just altruistic — they are commercial moats built ahead of launch.
