## Hook Patterns

**Hook 1: The Disease Burden Reality**
Open with a documented scale of unmet medical need.
> "More than 6 million Americans are living with Alzheimer's disease today. For decades, there was no therapy proven to slow its progression. That changed." (T4_ESTIMATED, based on observed Biogen Alzheimer's communications; Alzheimer's Association prevalence data)
This hook establishes the scale of need and positions Biogen's work as consequential, not commercial.

**Hook 2: The Patient Voice Opening**
Begin with an unscripted patient or caregiver statement that captures the reality of living with a neurological disease.
> "When my father was diagnosed with MS, we were told he would be in a wheelchair within five years. That was twenty years ago." (T4_ESTIMATED, representative of observed Biogen patient narrative content format)
Patient voices carry emotional authority that clinical data cannot replicate — and they do not require hedging language.

**Hook 3: The Scientific Milestone Frame**
Lead with the historical significance of a clinical result.
> "For the first time in the history of Alzheimer's disease research, a therapy has been shown in a Phase 3 trial to statistically significantly slow clinical decline. This is what 45 years of neuroscience looks like." (T4_ESTIMATED, based on observed Biogen Leqembi milestone communications)

**Hook 4: The "Why We Stay" Narrative**
Address the years of failure directly, then pivot to persistence.
> "We have been working on Alzheimer's disease since before most people recognized it as a public health crisis. We did not stop when others left the field. Here is why." (T4_ESTIMATED, based on observed Biogen long-form content on Alzheimer's commitment)
This hook preempts skepticism about Biogen's motivations by acknowledging the difficulty and cost of sustained commitment.

## Tone and Mood Guide

| Situation | Tone | Mood | Example Approach |
|-----------|------|------|-----------------|
| **Clinical data communication (HCP)** | Precise, methodologically rigorous, citation-complete | Authoritative, peer-level | Endpoint definitions, confidence intervals, p-values, safety data presented with complete context — no selective emphasis |
| **Patient and caregiver content** | Warm, honest, hope-grounded | Empathetic, realistic | Acknowledge disease severity; present treatment options as tools, not promises; center the patient's experience |
| **Corporate thought leadership** | Measured, mission-driven, evidence-anchored | Purposeful, serious | Biogen's Alzheimer's commitment story; why neuroscience specialization matters; access and equity framing |
| **Crisis / controversy response** | Factual, accountable, forward-looking | Serious, grounded | Acknowledge concern; present facts; describe remediation or evolution — do not minimize |

## Content Prohibitions

1. **Do not make efficacy claims that exceed FDA-approved labeling.** Biogen's promotional content for Leqembi, Tysabri, Spinraza, and other products must strictly adhere to approved indications and label language. Off-label promotion is a regulatory violation with substantial legal exposure. (T4_ESTIMATED — FDA promotional compliance requirement)
2. **Do not minimize the ARIA risk associated with Leqembi.** ARIA (amyloid-related imaging abnormalities) is a significant safety consideration that requires MRI monitoring and patient education; content that downplays this risk violates REMS program obligations and medical ethics. (T4_ESTIMATED — FDA REMS requirement)
3. **Do not use Aduhelm case-study content in a way that conflates it with Leqembi's evidence base.** Aduhelm's accelerated approval and subsequent Medicare coverage denial created a distinct regulatory and clinical context from Leqembi's traditional approval; conflating the two undermines Leqembi's distinct clinical credibility. (T4_ESTIMATED)
4. **Do not publish patient testimonials that imply cure or guarantee clinical outcomes.** Patient stories must be framed as individual experiences, not predictive of general outcomes; appropriate disclaimers are required. (T4_ESTIMATED — FDA testimonial guidance)
5. **Do not make comparative efficacy claims between Leqembi and donanemab** without citing peer-reviewed head-to-head data if it exists, or clearly labeling the comparison as cross-trial (not head-to-head). (T4_ESTIMATED — FDA comparative claim standards)
6. **Do not use fear-based language around neurological disease progression** to motivate treatment decisions. Patient safety content must inform, not alarm; fear appeals in pharmaceutical promotion raise ethical and regulatory concerns. (T4_ESTIMATED)

## Steal Sheet — 3 Transferable Principles

**1. Separate disease education content from product promotion.**
Biogen maintains disease state websites (for MS, Alzheimer's, SMA) that educate patients about their condition, symptoms, and care options — without being primarily promotional. This content builds trust with patients and caregivers before they encounter the product brand. Transferable: in any healthcare or high-consideration market, invest in disease education content that serves the patient's informational need first; product introduction follows naturally from trust established through non-promotional education.

**2. Use MSL scientific exchange as the premium channel for KOL engagement.**
Biogen's Medical Science Liaison network provides peer-level scientific exchange with academic neurologists who would not engage with a traditional sales representative but will engage with a scientifically credentialed peer. The MSL is not a sales channel — it is a scientific channel that generates clinical insight and credibility without promotional intent. Transferable: in any market with technically expert buyers who are resistant to commercial engagement, invest in credentialed subject matter experts who can engage peer-to-peer on technical content.

**3. Acknowledge the history of failure before claiming breakthrough.**
Biogen's most credible Alzheimer's content explicitly acknowledges the decades of failed trials and industry skepticism before presenting Leqembi's data. This acknowledgment makes the breakthrough claim more believable, not less — it demonstrates that Biogen understands the evidence standards of the field it operates in. Transferable: in any market where your audience has been burned by previous overclaiming, acknowledging past failures before presenting current evidence is a trust-building mechanism.
