# 03 — Competitive Landscape
## Brand: Biogen (BIIB)
## Sector: Health Care — Biotechnology

---

## 1. Competitive Arena Overview

Biogen competes across three distinct disease battlegrounds, each with its own competitive dynamics, pricing pressures, and market maturity:

| Arena | Biogen Position | Market Stage |
|---|---|---|
| Multiple Sclerosis (MS) | Established incumbent; aging portfolio | Mature; generic/biosimilar erosion beginning |
| Alzheimer's Disease | Co-commercializing Leqembi with Eisai | Early launch; market creation phase |
| Rare Neurological Diseases | Niche leader (Spinraza in SMA) | Niche; gene therapy disruption underway |
| Neuropsychiatry | Emerging pipeline (postpartum depression) | Early; Zurzuvae (zuranolone) newly launched |

## 7. Steal Sheets

### Steal Sheet 1: Steal the KOL Architecture
**What Biogen does:** Biogen has built a 40-year network of neurology key opinion leaders (KOLs) who trust Biogen science, co-author papers, and speak at conferences funded by Biogen medical education grants. This network is effectively a co-ownership of the disease area's intellectual agenda.
**How to steal:** Map the 50 most published and most cited physicians in your disease category. Design a medical education grant strategy that brings them inside the scientific conversation before your drug launches. Advisory boards are the entry point; co-authoring real-world evidence papers is the moat.

### Steal Sheet 2: Steal the Real-World Evidence Machine
**What Biogen does:** Biogen funds patient registries (MSBase, NARCOMS) and sponsors real-world evidence studies that generate data showing Biogen therapies perform in the real world as they performed in trials. This creates a perpetual evidence engine that competitors cannot easily replicate.
**How to steal:** At launch, commit to a real-world evidence program with specific publication timelines. Partner with academic medical centers on registry enrollment. The goal is to produce 5-year, 10-year real-world data that your competitors — who launched after you — cannot yet match.

### Steal Sheet 3: Steal the Progressive Disease Positioning
**What Biogen does poorly (and what to steal by doing it better):** Biogen has historically been weak in progressive MS — a significant unmet need where 15% of MS patients start and where 85% of relapsing patients eventually arrive. BTK inhibitors (Sanofi, Roche) are now targeting this gap.
**How to steal:** Identify the underserved, high-unmet-need adjacent population in your disease category that your competitor is neglecting. Position your pipeline there first. Owning "progression" in a disease area is both a clinical and a market access unlock that competitors cannot block without their own pipeline.
