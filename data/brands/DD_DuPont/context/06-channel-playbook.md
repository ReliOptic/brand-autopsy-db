## 2. Channel Tone Variations

DuPont's core brand voice is precise, science-grounded, and credibility-forward. It adjusts
by channel while maintaining the underlying authority register.

| Channel | Formality Level | Key Adjustments |
|---|---|---|
| **dupont.com** | Baseline (7/10) | Full technical vocabulary; evidence and specification grounded; direct B2B application framing |
| **LinkedIn** | Professional-plus (7/10) | Thought leadership framing; slightly warmer executive voice permissible; industry insight emphasis |
| **Twitter / X** | Accessible (4/10) | Shorter sentences; factual claims only; links to full technical content; no hyperbole |
| **YouTube** | Educational (5/10) | Narrative pacing; show-don't-tell visual storytelling; technical substance delivered accessibly |
| **IR / SEC filings** | Maximum formal (9/10) | Zero marketing language; all claims traceable to financials; safe harbor language for forward-looking statements |
| **Trade Media** | Technical (8/10) | Full specification vocabulary; peer-level technical authority; citation of test methods and standards |
| **Email** | Personalized (6/10) | Direct address; solution-oriented CTA; clear value proposition per segment |

## 4. Channel-Specific Prohibitions

### dupont.com
- **No unsubstantiated superlative claims.** All product performance assertions must
  reference test data, standards, or specifications. "Best in class" without a defined
  metric and measurement source is not consistent with DuPont's evidence-based brand
  voice (T4_INFERRED).
- **No consumer lifestyle imagery without material application context.** Product pages
  require industrial or technical application photography, not aspirational lifestyle
  staging (observed on dupont.com).
- **No forward-looking product roadmap disclosure outside IR-approved channels.** Product
  development timelines that could constitute material non-public information must not
  appear in marketing content (T4_INFERRED; SEC Regulation FD implications).

### LinkedIn
- **No consumer-casual tone or meme-format content.** DuPont's LinkedIn audience is
  professional and technical. Humor-forward or meme-based content is inconsistent with
  the engineering brand authority expected by this audience (T4_INFERRED).
- **No unattributed competitive characterizations.** If a post references competitive
  performance, it must cite a specific, publicly available benchmark or test result
  (T4_INFERRED; legal risk management).

### Twitter / X
- **No engagement with bad-faith criticism or adversarial accounts.** Brand account
  maintains professional distance from trolling and politically motivated commentary
  (T4_INFERRED).
- **No sharing of unverified third-party claims about DuPont products.** Only direct
  links to primary technical sources or official communications (T4_INFERRED).

### IR / SEC Channels
- **No marketing language in financial communications.** All earnings releases and
  SEC filings use factual, auditor-consistent language with no promotional framing
  (T1_OFFICIAL, SEC requirements).
- **No forward-looking statements without safe harbor disclaimers.** All guidance and
  projections in IR materials carry required forward-looking statement language
  (T1_OFFICIAL, SEC requirements).
- **No selective disclosure.** Material information must be disseminated broadly per
  Regulation FD; not selectively to analysts or media before public release (T1_OFFICIAL).

### Trade Media
- **No claims that cannot be supported by published test data.** Technical articles
  must reference ASTM, ISO, or equivalent standards for product property claims
  (T4_INFERRED; brand credibility requirement).

## 5. Crisis Response Protocol

### Scenario 1 — Product Safety or Regulatory Action (Highest Priority)

DuPont operates in specialty chemicals, a sector with material exposure to product safety
and environmental regulation. A product safety event or regulatory enforcement action
requires the highest speed and precision of response.

**Response sequence:**
1. **T+0 to T+12h:** Internal legal, communications, and executive team assessment.
   Confirm facts from regulatory agency and internal testing records.
2. **T+12 to T+24h:** Legal review of all public statement language.
   No factual assertions beyond confirmed information.
3. **T+24 to T+48h:** Official statement on dupont.com/newsroom.
   SEC 8-K filing if the event meets materiality threshold (T1_OFFICIAL, SEC requirements).
4. **T+48h onward:** Direct customer and stakeholder notification via email and account
   management channels. Remediation program communicated factually.
5. **Ongoing:** Status updates on dupont.com until regulatory resolution.

**Tone guidance:** Factual, calm, attributing actions to confirmed data.
Avoid: Minimizing language, pre-emptive exoneration, speculative cause attribution.

### Scenario 2 — Environmental or PFAS-Related Legal Proceedings

Historical PFAS-related litigation has been a material item in DuPont's disclosed legal
proceedings (as referenced in prior SEC filings; T1_OFFICIAL). New related developments
require careful, legally-reviewed communications.

**Response sequence:**
1. Legal review of any public statement by outside counsel.
2. SEC 8-K or 10-Q disclosure as required for material legal developments.
3. Official statement on dupont.com citing the legal proceeding, without characterizing
   outcomes or admitting liability beyond what has been judicially established.
4. IR communications to institutional investors via appropriate channels.
5. No speculative commentary on litigation outcomes in any public channel.

**Tone guidance:** Reference official court and regulatory records only.
Avoid: Predicting outcomes, minimizing disclosed proceedings, attributing intent to
regulatory agencies or opposing parties.

### Scenario 3 — Executive Leadership Change or Corporate Restructuring

Material leadership or structural changes require coordinated disclosure across channels.

**Response sequence:**
1. SEC 8-K filing for material executive departures or appointments (T1_OFFICIAL).
2. Official press release on dupont.com/newsroom.
3. Internal communications to employees prior to external announcement if operationally
   feasible.
4. LinkedIn post from incoming/continuing executive with strategic continuity framing.
5. IR communications addressing implications for disclosed strategy.

**Tone guidance:** Confident, forward-looking, transition-framed.
Avoid: Retrospective criticism of departing leadership, speculative commentary on
strategic rationale not yet publicly disclosed.

## 6. Steal Sheet — 5 Transferable Principles

**1. Separate brand color from action color for B2B clarity.**
DuPont's red-for-brand, blue-for-action system creates instant visual disambiguation
for technical audiences who scan pages for relevant interactive elements. Transferable:
use your brand's primary color for identification, and a distinct secondary color
exclusively for clickable/actionable elements.

**2. Lead with application context, follow with specifications.**
DuPont's product pages open with the material in its real-world application, then move
to spec tables. This sequence validates relevance before demanding attention for technical
data. Transferable: prove the application value before the specification data will be read.

**3. Technical credibility is built by publishing test data, not marketing claims.**
Trade publications, white papers, and specification sheets with ASTM/ISO references build
more trust with B2B buyers than superlative marketing language. Transferable: invest in
published technical content even when it requires more production rigor.

**4. IR precision as a brand asset.**
DuPont's SEC filings and earnings communications reflect the same evidence-based standards
as its technical product claims. Financial credibility and product credibility reinforce
each other. Transferable: treat financial communications as brand-building, not just
compliance.

**5. Channel formality matching reduces audience friction.**
DuPont's strict formality gradient — technical on the website, professional on LinkedIn,
factual-and-brief on Twitter/X — ensures content is calibrated for each audience's
context and expectations. Transferable: formally define formality levels per channel
before writing any content.
