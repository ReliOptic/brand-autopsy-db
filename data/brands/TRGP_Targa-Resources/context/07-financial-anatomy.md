> **Note**: FY2022 revenue is not separately tagged in the available EDGAR XBRL dataset for this filing period. The FY2021 net loss reflects significant midstream sector commodity headwinds and Targa's capital restructuring. The revenue near-tripling from FY2021 to FY2024 reflects both commodity price recovery and substantial infrastructure volume growth. (T4_INFERRED from disclosed XBRL figures)

### 2.2 Segment Revenue

Targa Resources operates predominantly in two reportable segments: **Gathering & Processing (G&P)** — natural gas gathering, compression, treating, and processing — and **Logistics & Transportation (L&T)** — NGL fractionation, transportation, terminaling, and storage. Per-segment revenue XBRL tags are not separately available in this dataset; full segment disclosures appear in the 10-K Segment Information notes. (data not disclosed in EDGAR XBRL at segment level)

> **Source**: SEC 10-K — Note on Segment Information; [TRGP EDGAR](https://www.sec.gov/cgi-bin/browse-edgar?action=getcompany&CIK=0001389170&type=10-K&dateb=&owner=include&count=10)

### 2.3 Geographic Revenue

Targa Resources operates exclusively within the United States, with core infrastructure assets concentrated in the Permian Basin (Texas/New Mexico), Mid-Continent (Oklahoma/Kansas), and Gulf Coast regions. No international revenue is disclosed. (SEC 10-K FY2024)

## 7. Financial Risk Matrix

### Based on SEC 10-K Risk Factors (Item 1A)

| Risk Category | Key Content | Brand Strategy Impact |
|--------------|-------------|----------------------|
| Commodity Price & Volume Risk | Midstream revenues influenced by natural gas and NGL throughput volumes tied to upstream producer activity; price declines reduce producer drilling incentives | Brand stability as infrastructure provider partially dependent on commodity cycle; brand narrative of fee-based revenue requires continued volume throughput |
| Counterparty / Credit Risk | Revenue concentration among upstream producers; producer financial distress could reduce contracted volumes | B2B brand relationships with producers are mission-critical; any producer default creates revenue risk and potential reputational exposure |
| Regulatory & Environmental Risk | Operations subject to federal/state pipeline safety regulations, EPA standards, FERC oversight, and evolving carbon regulation | Environmental incidents carry significant reputational and legal exposure; regulatory enforcement actions could materially impair brand standing |
| Leverage / Financing Risk | Long-term debt of $12.95B (FY2024) requires ongoing capital market access for refinancing; interest rate increases raise cost of capital | Financial flexibility constrained; brand's growth narrative depends on investor confidence and debt market access |
| Operational Safety Risk | Pipeline and processing facility failures, explosions, or spills could cause significant property damage, injury, or environmental harm | Operational incidents in the energy sector attract media and regulatory scrutiny; safety record is a critical brand trust factor in B2B midstream relationships |

> **Original filing**: [TRGP 10-K Risk Factors](https://www.sec.gov/cgi-bin/browse-edgar?action=getcompany&CIK=0001389170&type=10-K&dateb=&owner=include&count=10)

## 8. Brand-Finance Nexus

### 8.1 What Financial Data Reveals About Brand Strategy

| Financial Signal | Brand Strategy Interpretation |
|-----------------|------------------------------|
| Revenue growth from $6.87B (FY2021) to $19.82B (FY2024) | Scale is a primary brand differentiator in midstream; this growth positions Targa as one of the largest independent midstream operators, supporting B2B credibility (T4_INFERRED) |
| CapEx surge to $2.39B in FY2025 | Signals long-term infrastructure commitment; in B2B energy markets, demonstrated capital investment functions as a brand promise of capacity reliability to producer customers |
| Debt/equity ratio ~4.7x (estimated, FY2024) | High leverage is common in infrastructure but limits financial resilience; institutional investor brand perception depends on disciplined debt management narrative |
| Return to profitability FY2023–24 after FY2021 net loss of -$1.55B | Recovery narrative is a brand asset in investor relations; management credibility rebuilt through operational execution is a durable competitive signal |
| SG&A declining as % of revenue (3.7% FY2021 → 1.6% FY2024) | Operating leverage signals scalable platform brand positioning; each new gathering system added improves system-wide economics |

### 8.2 Brand-Finance Value Cycle

```
Permian Basin Volume Growth
        |
        v

        |
        v
```
