## Layout Principles

1. **Hierarchy of data over imagery**: In corporate communications (annual reports, investor presentations, press releases), data tables and charts precede photography. The numbers establish credibility before the visual emotional argument is made. This is the inverse of most consumer brand layouts. (observed on exxonmobil.com investor content)

2. **White space as institutional authority**: ExxonMobil's corporate digital properties use generous white space — not as minimalism for aesthetic reasons, but as a signal of deliberate, unhurried institutional communication. Dense layouts read as reactive; spacious layouts read as confident. (observed on exxonmobil.com)

3. **Mobil 1 dark-theme performance aesthetic**: Consumer-facing Mobil 1 content, particularly Racing content, uses a dark (#1A1A1A or near-black) background with high-contrast red and white type. This aesthetic connotes motorsport, night racing, and technical precision — the emotional opposite of the corporate brand's light institutional feel. (observed on Mobil 1 Racing content pages)

4. **The flywheel logo as anchor**: The ExxonMobil corporate brand consistently uses the red "EM" monogram and the Mobil flying horse (Pegasus) as anchor marks on all materials. No content lacks brand identification. This is particularly important in B2B contexts where procurement documents and technical spec sheets reach audiences who may not encounter marketing content. (observed on all ExxonMobil official publications)

5. **Photography subjects: infrastructure over people**: ExxonMobil's corporate photography prioritizes industrial infrastructure — platforms, refineries, pipelines — over people. This signals scale and capability. Consumer Mobil 1 content inverts this, featuring vehicles and racing scenes with human drivers. (observed on exxonmobil.com and mobil.com)

## Icon Style

- **Corporate icons**: Flat, single-color (navy or red), geometric forms representing energy concepts: flame, droplet, molecule, turbine blade. No gradients. (observed on exxonmobil.com)
- **Mobil 1 icons**: The flying horse (Pegasus) is the primary icon — dynamic, speed-implying, in red against white or black backgrounds. Performance and racing icons in secondary usage. (observed on mobil.com and Mobil 1 packaging)
- **Data visualization icons**: Simple chart and graph icons; arrow-up/down for production trend indicators in investor materials. (observed on ExxonMobil investor presentations)

## Design Prohibitions

1. **No unbranded ExxonMobil content**: All ExxonMobil-associated design materials must carry the appropriate logo mark. Unbranded content creates attribution confusion and weakens brand recall in B2B procurement contexts. (T4_INFERRED from brand standards)
2. **No gradient backgrounds in corporate context**: The institutional corporate palette uses flat fills. Gradient treatments belong to the Mobil 1 consumer sub-brand's energy/motion aesthetic, not corporate investor communications. (T4_INFERRED from observed design patterns)
3. **No consumer lifestyle photography in corporate investor materials**: Photography of families, recreational activities, or aspirational lifestyle imagery is inconsistent with the institutional authority positioning of ExxonMobil's corporate brand. (T4_INFERRED)
4. **No unauthorized use of the Pegasus mark**: The Mobil flying horse is a registered trademark with significant brand equity. The Pegasus must not be used in derivative, modified, or parody forms in brand materials. (T4_INFERRED from trademark protection standards)
5. **No competitor logo or product imagery in comparative contexts**: Visual comparisons featuring competitor product packaging create advertising claim liability. Competitive differentiation is expressed through specification tables, OEM approval badges, and performance test result citations, not visual product juxtaposition. (T4_INFERRED from advertising standards)
6. **No dark-theme design for ESG/sustainability content**: Sustainability content is designed to signal transparency and openness — a light, airy aesthetic signals accessibility and good faith. Dark-theme treatment of sustainability content could unintentionally connote concealment. (T4_INFERRED)
