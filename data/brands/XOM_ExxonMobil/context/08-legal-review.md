## Major Litigation Summary

The following proceedings are disclosed in ExxonMobil's SEC 10-K filings or are matters of public record. This summary is for brand risk analysis only and does not characterize any party's legal position.

### Climate-Related Litigation

ExxonMobil is named as a defendant in multiple legal proceedings brought by state and municipal governments alleging that the company's communications regarding climate change caused or contributed to harm. As of the FY2023 10-K filing, ExxonMobil discloses the existence of these proceedings in its Risk Factors and Litigation sections. (SEC 10-K FY2023, Note on Commitments and Contingencies)

ExxonMobil has publicly disputed the legal and factual basis of these claims and has, in some instances, initiated counterclaims. (T3_SECONDARY_RELIABLE, multiple legal journalism sources including Reuters and Bloomberg) The outcomes of these proceedings are uncertain; no liability has been adjudicated as of the date of this analysis. (T4_INFERRED for current status characterization)

### FTC / Antitrust — Pioneer Acquisition Review

The Federal Trade Commission reviewed the $59.5B acquisition of Pioneer Natural Resources. The FTC took no action to block the merger, which closed in October 2023. However, the FTC separately took action regarding the merger regarding specific Pioneer executive matters unrelated to the core acquisition. (T3_SECONDARY_RELIABLE, FTC press release; SEC 8-K filings related to Pioneer acquisition)

### Environmental Enforcement Actions

ExxonMobil, as an operator of refineries, chemical plants, and pipelines in multiple US states, has been subject to various EPA and state environmental enforcement actions over its operational history. These are disclosed in ExxonMobil's annual 10-K filings under Litigation. (SEC 10-K FY2023) Specific ongoing proceedings are described in the filing's Commitments and Contingencies note.

## Writing Risk Guide

### SAFE — Use These Constructions

- "According to ExxonMobil's FY2023 10-K filing, revenues were $334.7B." (T1_OFFICIAL citation)
- "ExxonMobil's official website states that the company's purpose is [exact quote]." (T1_OFFICIAL)
- "ExxonMobil has disclosed in its SEC filings that it faces climate-related litigation from multiple state and municipal governments." (T1_OFFICIAL, factual statement of disclosed risk)
- "Multiple state and municipal governments have filed or are pursuing legal proceedings against ExxonMobil related to climate change. ExxonMobil has publicly disputed these claims." (T3_SECONDARY_RELIABLE with attribution)
- "As of ExxonMobil's FY2023 10-K, the company's production was approximately 3.7 million BOE/day." (T1_OFFICIAL)
- "This analysis interprets ExxonMobil's brand positioning as..." (T4_INFERRED, explicit labeling)

### HEDGE — Use These Only With Qualification

- Any characterization of ExxonMobil's internal decision-making must be explicitly labeled T4_INFERRED
- Any statements about historical communications regarding climate change must be carefully hedged and sourced to specific documents
- Any financial projections must be labeled (estimated) and sourced to either company guidance or analyst consensus
- Any characterization of the merits of pending litigation must be explicitly disclaimed as analysis, not legal opinion

### DO NOT WRITE

- Any assertion that ExxonMobil acted with deliberate awareness of harm to the public regarding climate change — this constitutes intent attribution without judicial finding and creates defamation/libel exposure for this project.
- Any assertion that ExxonMobil's communications were dishonest or constituted misrepresentation — no court has adjudicated this; asserting it as fact is legally untenable.
- "ExxonMobil is guilty of [any specific legal violation]." — Legal guilt requires judicial determination.
- "ExxonMobil's executives decided to [specific internal decision without official disclosure]." — Asserting internal decisions without a disclosed source is fabrication.
- Any specific carbon emissions figures or environmental performance numbers unless directly sourced to ExxonMobil's published Sustainability Report or SEC disclosures.
- "ExxonMobil is the most polluting company in [category]." — Ranking superlatives about pollution require verified, comparable third-party data; even then, extremely high sensitivity.
