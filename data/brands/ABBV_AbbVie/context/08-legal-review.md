### 2.2 Patent Portfolio — Humira (adalimumab)

Humira's patent portfolio became the subject of significant public and regulatory attention.
Based on publicly available reports, Humira accumulated a portfolio of over 130 patents over
its commercial life (T3_SECONDARY_RELIABLE, I-MAK "Humira's Patent Fortress" report, 2021;
this is a reported figure from a policy advocacy organization and is not independently verified
by this project). AbbVie's patent strategy for Humira has been discussed in legislative
hearings and policy contexts; this project reports those discussions factually without
characterizing the legal activities as improper or unlawful.

**Patent Orange Book listings**: Drugs with FDA approval have their associated patents listed
in the FDA Orange Book (T1_OFFICIAL, FDA Orange Book database). Biosimilar applicants may
challenge these patents through the BPCIA (Biologics Price Competition and Innovation Act)
litigation pathway. AbbVie settled litigation with all major Humira biosimilar applicants
with agreed-upon U.S. entry dates beginning January 2023 (T3_SECONDARY_RELIABLE, multiple
news sources including Reuters, Bloomberg; T1_OFFICIAL, AbbVie litigation disclosures in 10-K).

### 2.3 Patent Portfolio — Skyrizi, Rinvoq, and Aesthetics

## 3. Major Litigation (Public Record)

> All descriptions below are factual summaries of publicly disclosed legal proceedings.
> This document does not characterize any party's conduct beyond what is stated in official
> filings, court orders, or regulatory agency press releases.

### 3.1 Humira Patent Litigation — BPCIA Proceedings

**Nature**: Multiple biosimilar manufacturers filed abbreviated biologics license applications
(aBLAs) for adalimumab biosimilars, triggering patent litigation under the BPCIA pathway.

**AbbVie's approach**: AbbVie engaged in patent settlement negotiations with all major biosimilar
applicants. Settlement agreements established U.S. biosimilar entry dates beginning January 2023.
Amgen's Amjevita was the first to enter; multiple other biosimilars followed throughout 2023.
(T3_SECONDARY_RELIABLE, Reuters, Bloomberg, FiercePharma; T1_OFFICIAL, AbbVie 10-K litigation
disclosures)

**Current status**: U.S. Humira biosimilar competition is active as of FY2023. Settlement terms
are not fully public; the agreed entry dates are a matter of record from the biosimilars' launch
dates. (T1_OFFICIAL, FDA biosimilar approval records)

**Implication for this project**: Do not characterize AbbVie's settlement agreements as evidence
of improper conduct. Patent settlements in the pharmaceutical industry are standard commercial
practice. Describe the biosimilar entry timeline factually, sourced from disclosed entry dates.

### 3.2 Antitrust Litigation — Humira Patent Strategy

**Nature**: Multiple state attorneys general and private plaintiffs have filed antitrust actions
alleging that AbbVie's patent strategy for Humira was anticompetitive, delaying biosimilar
competition beyond what the patent merits warranted. These proceedings are ongoing as of the
most recent publicly available information. (T3_SECONDARY_RELIABLE, Reuters, Bloomberg;
T1_OFFICIAL, AbbVie litigation disclosures in SEC 10-K FY2023)

**AbbVie's position**: AbbVie has denied the allegations in these proceedings and defended
its patent portfolio as legally obtained and properly asserted (T3_SECONDARY_RELIABLE,
press reports of AbbVie's legal filings).

**Status**: Proceedings ongoing; no final judgment issued as of this document's reference date.
Verify current status in AbbVie's most recent SEC 10-K or 8-K filings.

**Implication for this project**: Report these proceedings factually. Do not assert that
AbbVie's patent strategy was anticompetitive or unlawful — no court has made that final
determination in these proceedings. Describe the allegations as allegations, noting
the plaintiff parties and the nature of the claims.

### 3.3 Allergan Legacy Litigation — Opioid-Related Claims

**Nature**: Following the Allergan acquisition (May 2020), AbbVie assumed certain legacy
litigation obligations. Allergan's predecessor entity Actavis was involved in opioid-related
marketing litigation in multiple states and federal proceedings prior to the acquisition.
Allergan reached settlements related to these matters before the AbbVie acquisition closed.
(T1_OFFICIAL, AbbVie 10-K FY2023, Note on Commitments and Contingencies)

**Current status**: AbbVie continues to disclose ongoing opioid-related litigation obligations
in its SEC filings. Verify current status and settlement amounts in the most recent 10-K or 8-K.

**Implication for this project**: Clearly date-stamp all references to opioid-related litigation
as pre-acquisition Allergan conduct. Do not conflate legacy Allergan opioid marketing practices
with AbbVie's independent pharmaceutical practices post-acquisition. AbbVie did not manufacture
or market the opioid products at issue; these are inherited legal obligations from the acquisition.

### 3.4 Government Pricing and Rebate Investigations

**Nature**: AbbVie, like other major pharmaceutical companies, has faced government inquiries
and investigations related to drug pricing practices, rebate structures, and interactions with
pharmacy benefit managers. AbbVie discloses government investigations and subpoenas in its
SEC filings as they become material. (T1_OFFICIAL, AbbVie 10-K FY2023, Risk Factors and
Legal Proceedings — verify specific disclosures in current filing)

**Implication for this project**: Report disclosed government inquiries factually, citing SEC
filing disclosure language. Do not interpret the existence of an investigation as evidence of
wrongdoing — investigations are disclosed as risk factors, not admissions.

### 3.5 Inflation Reduction Act — Drug Price Negotiation

**Nature**: The Inflation Reduction Act of 2022 (IRA) established a Medicare Drug Price
Negotiation Program under which CMS selects high-expenditure Medicare Part D drugs for
negotiated pricing beginning in 2026. AbbVie products may be selected for negotiation in
future rounds as the program expands. (T1_OFFICIAL, Inflation Reduction Act, 42 U.S.C. §
1320f; CMS implementation guidance)

**Impact on AbbVie**: AbbVie has acknowledged IRA drug price negotiation as a material risk
factor in its SEC 10-K filings (T1_OFFICIAL). The financial impact will depend on which
products are selected, the negotiated prices, and the timeline of implementation.

**Implication for this project**: Report IRA negotiation as a disclosed risk factor, not as
a certainty or as evidence of pricing impropriety. The IRA represents a policy decision by
the U.S. government applicable to the entire pharmaceutical industry.

### 5.1 AbbVie's Litigation Posture Assessment

**Assessment**: MODERATE-HIGH (T4_INFERRED based on public record)

Basis for this assessment:
- AbbVie is an active participant in pharmaceutical patent litigation (BPCIA proceedings for
  Humira), demonstrating willingness to assert IP rights aggressively when commercially justified
  (T1_OFFICIAL, public court records).
- The company faces antitrust litigation from multiple state attorneys general and private
  plaintiffs regarding Humira patent practices — a contested legal area that AbbVie is
  actively defending (T3_SECONDARY_RELIABLE, press reports; T1_OFFICIAL, 10-K disclosures).
- AbbVie's pharmaceutical industry peers have historically pursued cease-and-desist actions
  against misuse of branded drug names and misleading comparative claims (T4_INFERRED from
  pharmaceutical industry practice).
- However: AbbVie has not, based on publicly available records, pursued defamation actions
  against brand analysts, financial researchers, or journalistic institutions for properly
  sourced, factually accurate analysis of its business. The primary litigation exposure for
  this project is NOT defamation — it is trademark misuse and factual inaccuracy.

**Implication for this project**: Primary risks are (a) trademark misuse — unauthorized use
of ABBVIE, HUMIRA, SKYRIZI, RINVOQ, BOTOX as branding elements in our outputs; (b) false
factual assertions that could constitute defamation; (c) characterizing pending litigation
outcomes before courts have ruled; (d) confusing Allergan pre-acquisition conduct with
post-acquisition AbbVie conduct.

#### SAFE TO WRITE (with proper sourcing)

- Dividend history and capital allocation based on official announcements
- ESG commitments and patient assistance program facts from official AbbVie ESG reports
- FDA boxed warning for JAK inhibitor class (Rinvoq), citing the FDA safety communication

#### REQUIRES CAREFUL HEDGING

| Topic | Required Hedging Approach |
|-------|--------------------------|
| Humira patent strategy | "AbbVie accumulated a large patent portfolio for Humira, as reported by public analysis organizations including I-MAK; antitrust proceedings regarding this strategy are ongoing." NEVER attribute motive or assert anticompetitive purpose without a final court ruling. |
| Drug pricing | Always distinguish list price from net price after rebates; cite the complexity of pharmaceutical pricing; never assert that list price equals patient cost |
| Allergan opioid legacy | "Prior to the AbbVie acquisition, Allergan's predecessor entity reached settlements related to opioid marketing practices." NEVER conflate with AbbVie's post-acquisition conduct |
| Antitrust proceedings | "Multiple state attorneys general have filed antitrust actions alleging [specific allegation]. AbbVie has denied the allegations. The proceedings are ongoing." NEVER assert the allegations are proven |
| Pipeline projections | "AbbVie's management has indicated..." or "Based on AbbVie's disclosed pipeline timeline..." — NEVER assert clinical or commercial success as certain |
| JAK inhibitor safety | Cite FDA-mandated boxed warning language precisely; do not editorialize about safety profile beyond the regulatory language |
| IRA negotiation impact | "AbbVie has disclosed IRA drug price negotiation as a material risk factor." NEVER assert specific financial impact as certain |

- "AbbVie deceived prescribers about Rinvoq's safety." — Pejorative assertion; cite specific FDA enforcement actions if any exist.
- Any unhedged comparative clinical superiority claim (e.g., "Skyrizi is more effective than Stelara") without citing specific head-to-head trial data.

- Conflating Allergan pre-acquisition opioid conduct with AbbVie's post-acquisition pharmaceutical practices.
- Asserting that any ongoing legal proceeding has a predetermined outcome.
- Reproducing AbbVie's promotional materials or patient testimonials verbatim without transformative purpose.
- Using AbbVie's registered trademarks (HUMIRA, BOTOX, etc.) in ways that could imply endorsement of this project.
