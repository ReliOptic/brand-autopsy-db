## Battle Card 1: AbbVie Skyrizi vs. J&J Tremfya (IL-23 Inhibitors, Psoriasis/PsA/Crohn's)

**AbbVie's position**: Skyrizi (risankizumab) demonstrated skin clearance rates (PASI 90/100) in Phase III trials including ULTIMMA-1 and ULTIMMA-2. Approved for plaque psoriasis, psoriatic arthritis, Crohn's disease, and ulcerative colitis. (T1_OFFICIAL, FDA approval records)

**J&J's position**: Tremfya (guselkumab) approved for plaque psoriasis and psoriatic arthritis. (T1_OFFICIAL, FDA approval records)

**Observable differentiation**: Skyrizi has a broader approved indication set including Crohn's disease and ulcerative colitis as of 2022-2023, while Tremfya's IBD indications were in development as of this analysis. (T1_OFFICIAL, FDA labels; note: indication landscape evolves — verify current labels)

**Prescriber consideration**: Dosing frequency and delivery mechanism vary; HCPs assess individual patient preference. AbbVie's existing Humira relationship provides organizational familiarity. (T4_INFERRED from sales force training literature)

---

## Battle Card 2: AbbVie Rinvoq vs. Pfizer Xeljanz (JAK Inhibitors)

**AbbVie's position**: Rinvoq (upadacitinib) is a selective JAK1 inhibitor with approvals in RA, PsA, AS, atopic dermatitis, Crohn's disease, and ulcerative colitis. (T1_OFFICIAL, FDA approval records)

**Pfizer's position**: Xeljanz (tofacitinib) was the first approved oral JAK inhibitor but carries a broader JAK inhibition profile (JAK1/JAK3). FDA issued a boxed warning update in 2021 related to cardiovascular and malignancy risks based on the ORAL Surveillance study. (T1_OFFICIAL, FDA safety communication 2021)

**Observable differentiation**: Rinvoq's selectivity profile and post-ORAL Surveillance positioning have been highlighted in AbbVie's medical communications, though Rinvoq also carries the class-wide JAK inhibitor boxed warnings. (T1_OFFICIAL, Rinvoq FDA label; T4_INFERRED for commercial positioning analysis)

## Battle Card 3: AbbVie Imbruvica vs. AstraZeneca/Acerta Calquence (BTK Inhibitors, Oncology)

**AbbVie's position**: Imbruvica (ibrutinib), co-developed with Janssen (J&J) and marketed jointly, was the first approved BTK inhibitor for CLL/SLL. Revenue has faced pressure from next-generation BTK inhibitors. (official, SEC 10-K FY2023 — Imbruvica revenues declined year-over-year)

**AstraZeneca's position**: Calquence (acalabrutinib) and Beigene's Brukinsa (zanubrutinib) are next-generation BTK inhibitors with selectivity profiles that have attracted prescribers in head-to-head comparisons. (T3_SECONDARY_RELIABLE, medical literature)

**Observable differentiation**: AbbVie has acknowledged Imbruvica revenue pressure in public filings, noting competitive dynamics as a contributing factor. The company's oncology platform diversification toward Venclexta (venetoclax) represents a strategic response. (official, SEC 10-K FY2023)

## Threats

1. **Biosimilar erosion of Humira revenues**: Multiple Humira biosimilars launched in the U.S. from January 2023. AbbVie disclosed U.S. Humira net revenues declined approximately 35% in FY2023 vs. FY2022. (official, SEC 10-K FY2023)
2. **IRA drug price negotiation**: The Inflation Reduction Act enables Medicare to negotiate prices for high-expenditure drugs. AbbVie products are potential future negotiation targets. (T1_OFFICIAL, IRA legislation; T4_INFERRED for brand financial risk)
3. **JAK inhibitor safety scrutiny**: Class-wide FDA boxed warning updates may affect Rinvoq prescribing in certain patient populations. (T1_OFFICIAL, FDA safety communications 2021)
4. **Pipeline execution risk**: Late-stage trial failures can reduce projected future revenues. (T4_INFERRED, standard pharmaceutical industry risk)

---

## Opportunities

1. **Skyrizi and Rinvoq expansion**: Both products have active regulatory submissions for additional indications, expanding the addressable patient population. (official, AbbVie pipeline disclosures)
2. **Aesthetics growth**: Botox Cosmetic and Juvederm represent exposure to the growing medical aesthetics market, with recurring-revenue dynamics from repeat treatments. (T4_INFERRED from market growth data)
3. **Neuroscience pipeline**: Emraclidine (schizophrenia) and other CNS assets in Phase II/III represent pipeline diversification beyond immunology and oncology. (official, AbbVie pipeline page)
4. **International immunology expansion**: Skyrizi and Rinvoq penetration in ex-U.S. markets remains below U.S. penetration levels, suggesting growth runway. (T4_INFERRED from geographic revenue mix in SEC 10-K FY2023)

## Steal Sheet — 3 Transferable Principles

**1. Sequence the portfolio transition before the cliff arrives.**
AbbVie began building Skyrizi and Rinvoq momentum years before Humira biosimilar entry, ensuring the replacement pipeline was already generating billions in revenue when erosion began. Transferable: any company with a dominant product facing disruption should begin portfolio diversification while the core product still generates maximum cash flow, not after revenue has already declined.

**2. Use indication expansion as a competitive moat strategy.**
Every additional FDA indication for Skyrizi or Rinvoq expands the patient pool, adds formulary negotiating leverage, and increases the complexity gap between AbbVie's portfolio and a single-indication competitor. Transferable for platform software: adding verticals or use cases to a core product creates compounding switching costs and broadens the addressable market without proportional incremental investment.

**3. Acknowledge competitive headwinds explicitly in investor communications.**
AbbVie's transparent disclosure of Imbruvica competitive pressure and Humira biosimilar impact — rather than minimizing or obscuring these trends — maintains credibility with institutional investors. Transferable: if your core product faces documented competitive threats, acknowledging them publicly while presenting a credible response plan preserves more trust than defensive messaging that audiences can see through.
