## 2. Business Model Overview

AbbVie operates as an integrated biopharmaceutical company with revenue derived from:

1. **Commercial pharmaceutical products**: Prescription biologics, small molecules, and aesthetic
   products sold in the U.S. and internationally. The majority of revenues are generated through
   relationships with pharmaceutical distributors and specialty pharmacies, rather than direct
   sales to end patients (T1_OFFICIAL, SEC 10-K FY2023).

2. **Collaboration revenues**: AbbVie's collaboration with Janssen Biotech (J&J subsidiary) for
   Imbruvica (ibrutinib) generates collaboration revenues based on profit sharing arrangements
   (T1_OFFICIAL, SEC 10-K FY2023).

3. **No generics business**: AbbVie does not manufacture generic pharmaceuticals; its portfolio
   is entirely branded and proprietary (T4_INFERRED from product portfolio disclosure).

**Revenue recognition**: Net revenues reflect gross product sales less chargebacks, rebates,
discounts, and returns. For specialty biologics, the difference between gross and net revenues
is significant — rebates to PBMs and government programs represent a material reduction from
list prices (T4_INFERRED from pharmaceutical industry revenue recognition norms; T1_OFFICIAL,
AbbVie revenue recognition policy disclosed in 10-K).

## 3. Five-Year Revenue Trend

| Fiscal Year | Net Revenues | YoY Change | Key Driver |
|-------------|-------------|------------|------------|
| FY2019 | $33.3B | — | Humira dominance; Imbruvica growth; pre-Allergan |
| FY2020 | $45.8B | +37.5% | Allergan acquisition closed May 2020; partial-year consolidation (T1_OFFICIAL, SEC 10-K FY2020) |
| FY2021 | $56.2B | +22.7% | Full-year Allergan revenue consolidation; Skyrizi and Rinvoq ramping (T1_OFFICIAL, SEC 10-K FY2021) |

**Narrative interpretation** (T4_INFERRED): The revenue trajectory reflects the complete arc
of a major pharmaceutical product lifecycle event. The Allergan acquisition in FY2020 added
approximately $16–18B in annualized revenue, diversifying away from Humira dependency. The
FY2023 decline represents the first year of U.S. Humira biosimilar competition — an event that
AbbVie disclosed, prepared for, and committed to managing through Skyrizi/Rinvoq scaling.

## 13. Brand Valuation Context

AbbVie's financial value is substantially embedded in product-level brand franchises and
regulatory exclusivities rather than the corporate brand. Key intangible assets include:

**Skyrizi brand equity** (emerging — T4_INFERRED): Skyrizi's rapid revenue growth (51% in
FY2023 per AbbVie's estimates) reflects prescriber adoption driven by clinical data profile
and AbbVie's commercial execution. As the product expands across psoriasis, PsA, Crohn's,
and UC indications, brand equity with dermatologists and gastroenterologists is compounding.

**Rinvoq brand equity** (emerging — T4_INFERRED): Similar trajectory to Skyrizi; oral
administration in atopic dermatitis (a condition with large DTC-reachable patient population)
provides a different commercial dynamic from injectable biologics.

**Botox brand equity** (mature, very high — T3_SECONDARY_RELIABLE, brand valuation commentary):
Botox, acquired via Allergan, represents one of the most recognized pharmaceutical brand names
globally in both therapeutic and cosmetic contexts. The brand's equity is durable: it has crossed
into common noun usage in consumer culture while remaining a premium medical brand in clinical
contexts. This dual-positioning is rare and valuable.

**Humira brand equity** (declining commercially but historically exceptional — T4_INFERRED):
Humira was the world's best-selling drug by annual revenue for over a decade. Its brand equity
with rheumatologists is being actively transitioned to Skyrizi and Rinvoq through AbbVie's
commercial transition.

**Corporate brand equity**: AbbVie's employer brand is rated favorably in pharmaceutical talent
attraction contexts based on pipeline opportunity and culture (T3_SECONDARY_RELIABLE, Glassdoor
and employer brand surveys). The corporate brand's value is partially a function of Humira's
historical dominance — AbbVie is associated with pharmaceutical success even as that success
transitions to new products.

## 15. Brand-Finance Nexus

AbbVie's brand creates measurable financial value through three observable mechanisms:

**1. Prescriber loyalty as revenue protection** (T4_INFERRED): HCP familiarity with AbbVie's
clinical support infrastructure, MSL access, and patient support programs creates switching costs
that slow competitive erosion. When a prescriber has established a relationship with AbbVie's
commercial organization — field reps, MSLs, access support teams — the threshold to switch to a
competitor product is higher than the clinical data alone would justify. This loyalty premium
is not quantifiable from public data but is reflected in AbbVie's market share retention metrics.

**2. Patient assistance brand premium — adherence as revenue** (T4_INFERRED from adherence
literature): myAbbVie Assist and co-pay programs directly improve patient adherence. Patients
who discontinue therapy generate no ongoing revenue; patients who adhere generate recurring
revenue for the duration of treatment. Every dollar invested in patient adherence programs that
extends the average treatment duration above the counterfactual is revenue positive — the
brand investment has a directly calculable financial return in the specialty biologic context.

**3. Allergan brand acquisition as financial asset** (T4_INFERRED from acquisition rationale):
The $63B Allergan acquisition was substantially a brand acquisition. Botox Cosmetic's 50+ years
of brand equity — consumer recognition, physician familiarity, premium pricing authority — and
Juvederm's portfolio represent intangible assets with durable revenue generation that face
different competitive dynamics than small-molecule drugs. Brand value that cannot be replicated
by a biosimilar entrant is a premium financial asset in the pharmaceutical context.
