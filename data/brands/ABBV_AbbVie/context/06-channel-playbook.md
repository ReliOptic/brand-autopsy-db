## 1. Channel Architecture Overview

AbbVie operates one of the most complex multi-channel architectures in pharmaceutical marketing,
reflecting the need to simultaneously reach and influence five distinct stakeholder groups:
healthcare professionals (HCPs), patients and caregivers, institutional payers, investors, and
prospective employees. Each group requires different content, different regulatory frameworks,
and different channel selection — yet all must reinforce a coherent brand identity centered on
scientific authority and patient-centered care (T4_INFERRED from observed communications).

The channel architecture is bifurcated between regulated promotional channels (subject to FDA
promotional oversight for prescription drug advertising) and non-promotional channels (corporate
communications, disease awareness, science education, ESG reporting). This bifurcation is not an
organizational accident — it is the structural solution to operating across both the prescription
drug commercial world and the broader corporate communications landscape simultaneously.

## 5. Platform-Specific Execution Standards

### abbvie.com — Corporate Hub

**Function**: Primary source of record for all corporate information. Every other channel routes
back to abbvie.com for the authoritative version of any claim.

**Content cadence**: News releases published same-day as events; pipeline page updated quarterly
or at material milestones; ESG content updated annually with interim updates on major achievements
(observed on abbvie.com/newsroom).

**SEO strategy**: Disease state landing pages (unbranded) capture patient search traffic;
product pages serve patients already aware of the brand; pipeline pages serve investor and HCP
research intent (T4_INFERRED from observed URL structure and page content).

**Accessibility**: AbbVie's corporate site applies WCAG 2.1 AA standards as observed through
structure (alt text on images, keyboard navigation, skip-to-content links). Pharmaceutical
companies face heightened accessibility expectations given patient audiences with disabilities
(T4_INFERRED from ADA and WCAG compliance requirements).

### LinkedIn — Science and Corporate Channel

**Content categories observed on AbbVie LinkedIn** (observed on AbbVie LinkedIn company page):
1. Pipeline and research milestones — data readouts, approvals, partnerships
2. Employee and culture content — research team spotlights, workplace awards
3. ESG content — patient assistance statistics, environmental milestones, diversity metrics
4. Executive thought leadership — CEO and CMO perspectives on healthcare trends
5. Disease awareness — unbranded educational content about AbbVie therapeutic areas

**Frequency**: 3–5 posts per week observed across corporate and subsidiary pages (estimated).

**Format mix**: Static images and graphics (highest engagement for data milestones),
short video (employee stories, lab tours), document carousels (pipeline summaries), text
posts for commentary on industry developments (T4_INFERRED from observed LinkedIn patterns).

**Prohibited on LinkedIn**: Direct product promotion to audiences where HCP credentials cannot
be verified; material non-public financial information; promotional claims without fair balance.

### Medical Congresses — Highest-Credibility HCP Channel

AbbVie's observed presence at major medical congresses includes (T3_SECONDARY_RELIABLE, medical
conference programs; T4_INFERRED from standard pharmaceutical industry practice):

**ACR Convergence (American College of Rheumatology)**
- Sponsored symposia presenting Skyrizi and Rinvoq data in rheumatology indications
- Large exhibition booth with product information stations and MSL availability
- Poster presentations of investigator-initiated research and company-sponsored studies
- Satellite events for KOL roundtables

**EULAR (European Alliance of Associations for Rheumatology)**
- Similar structure to ACR; European regulatory context; EU-approved indications featured
- Local regulatory compliance for each EU country's promotional requirements

**ACR, DDW, ASCO, ASH** — indication-specific congress presence for gastroenterology,
oncology, and hematology product portfolios

**Congress compliance**: All congress promotional activities must comply with FDA promotional
regulations, PhRMA Code provisions on pharmaceutical company interactions with HCPs, and
local country regulations for ex-U.S. congresses (T1_OFFICIAL, PhRMA Code 2022 edition).

### DTC Television and Streaming

AbbVie's direct-to-consumer advertising follows the established pharmaceutical DTC format
required by FDA:

**Required elements** (T1_OFFICIAL, FDA requirements for prescription drug advertising):
- Product name and indication statement in the ad
- Most important risks in audio and/or visual format
- Direction to talk to a healthcare provider
- Reference to full prescribing information (product website or 1-800 number)

**Observed creative approach** (T4_INFERRED from AbbVie DTC advertising patterns):
- Opening with patient lifestyle vignette (30–45 seconds of 60-second spot)
- AbbVie brand and product close (5–10 seconds)
- Fair balance audio running concurrent with lifestyle visuals (15–25 seconds)
- End card: product name, indication, website URL, "Ask your doctor" CTA

**Streaming / digital video**: AbbVie has extended DTC advertising to streaming platforms,
targeting adults in disease demographics based on age, geography, and content category
context (T4_INFERRED from observed digital advertising industry practice).

## 6. Channel Prohibitions

1. **No branded product promotion to channels where audience HCP status cannot be verified.**
   Prescription drug promotion is restricted by FDA to appropriate healthcare professionals.
   Social media channels without HCP verification gates must carry only disease awareness
   (unbranded) or corporate content, not branded promotional materials. (T1_OFFICIAL, FDA
   guidance on social media promotion of prescription drugs)

2. **No simultaneous selective disclosure of material non-public information.** SEC
   Regulation FD requires that when AbbVie discloses material non-public information to
   select individuals (analysts, specific investors), it must simultaneously make that
   information public. Earnings guidance, pipeline updates, and financial projections shared
   in private analyst briefings must be simultaneously made publicly available. (T1_OFFICIAL,
   SEC Regulation FD)

3. **No off-label product information sharing by sales representatives.** The sales force
   channel is restricted to FDA-approved indications, dosing, and safety information in
   the approved label. Scientific exchange regarding non-approved uses must route through
   MSLs in a non-promotional, physician-initiated context. (T1_OFFICIAL, FDA promotional
   regulations 21 CFR Part 202)

4. **No patient testimonials in promotional materials without appropriate disclaimers.**
   Individual patient experiences presented in promotional contexts must include disclosures
   that individual results may vary, and must be consistent with the approved label's safety
   profile. Testimonials that emphasize benefits without contextualizing risks violate fair
   balance requirements. (T4_INFERRED from FDA guidance on patient testimonials)

5. **No social media engagement that provides specific medical advice to individual patients.**
   AbbVie's social media channels may not respond to individual patient questions about
   dosing, side effects, or treatment decisions in ways that constitute medical advice.
   Inquiries must be routed to the patient's healthcare provider. (T4_INFERRED from FDA
   and FTC guidance on pharmaceutical social media conduct)

6. **No premature public disclosure of trial results.** Phase III trial results not yet
   presented at a peer-reviewed congress or submitted for publication must not be promoted
   through commercial channels. Pre-publication commercial amplification of trial data
   creates FDA regulatory and investor relations legal risk. (T4_INFERRED from FDA
   and SEC requirements)

7. **No channel mixing that contaminates patient data with commercial objectives.**
   Patient assistance program data (myAbbVie Assist enrollment, patient health information)
   must not be used for commercial targeting without appropriate patient consent and HIPAA
   compliance. The patient support infrastructure is operationally separate from the
   commercial marketing organization for regulatory and ethical reasons. (T4_INFERRED from
   HIPAA requirements and pharmaceutical industry compliance standards)

## 7. Crisis Communication Protocol

### Scenario 1 — Drug Safety Signal Identified Post-Approval

**Trigger**: Spontaneous adverse event reports, safety surveillance data, or regulator inquiry
identifies a potential new safety signal for an approved product.

**Protocol**:
1. Convene Pharmacovigilance Safety Review Committee within 24–48 hours of signal identification.
2. If FDA-reportable threshold met: MedWatch report filed within required timeframe (15 days for
   serious unexpected events; T1_OFFICIAL, FDA 21 CFR 314.81).
3. Legal and regulatory counsel engaged for materiality assessment under SEC disclosure rules.
4. If material: 8-K filing with SEC; press release with clinical language.
5. Field force: promotion of affected product paused pending regulatory guidance update.
6. External communications: factual clinical language; lead with patient safety commitment;
   acknowledge investigation; do not speculate on causation before investigation complete.
7. All external communications cleared by medical, regulatory, and legal before release.
(T4_INFERRED from pharmaceutical crisis communication best practice and FDA regulatory requirements)

### Scenario 2 — Drug Pricing Congressional Scrutiny or Media Attention

**Trigger**: Congressional hearing, investigative journalism, or advocacy group attention on
AbbVie drug pricing (particularly Humira historical pricing or Skyrizi/Rinvoq launch prices).

**Protocol**:
1. Prepare factual response document: R&D investment per approved drug (from 10-K), patient
   assistance program statistics, net price vs. list price context, rebate disclosure framework.
2. Messaging framework: Lead with patient access; acknowledge pricing complexity; cite published
   data on R&D cost recovery; avoid per-unit cost-of-goods comparisons.
3. Do not characterize legislators, journalists, or advocacy organizations.
4. CEO or designated Head of Communications is sole spokesperson; no multiple conflicting voices.
5. Investor relations briefing: SEC Regulation FD compliant response to analyst questions.
6. Social media: factual statements only; no adversarial tone toward critics.
(T4_INFERRED from observed pharmaceutical company responses to pricing scrutiny)

### Scenario 3 — Clinical Trial Failure (Phase III)

**Trigger**: A Phase III trial for an AbbVie compound fails to meet primary endpoints.

**Protocol**:
1. SEC 8-K filing within required disclosure window if material to financial projections.
2. Press release: factual statement of trial outcome; patient safety summary; context of
   program within broader pipeline.
3. Investor/analyst call: scientific context for the failure; portfolio impact assessment;
   pipeline alternatives with remaining value.
4. HCP communications: straightforward scientific update; do not promote the compound further
   in the failed indication.
5. Do not: minimize the failure; speculate on alternative data interpretations; pivot immediately
   to other products in the same communication without allowing the data to be stated clearly.
(T4_INFERRED from SEC disclosure requirements and investor relations best practice)

### Scenario 4 — Product Recall or Manufacturing Issue

**Trigger**: FDA recall notification, manufacturing deviation, or product quality incident.

**Protocol**:
1. Coordinate with FDA on recall classification and scope.
2. Field force: immediate notification; inventory retrieval process activated.
3. HCP communications: factual notification with next steps; prescribing alternatives if available.
4. Patient communications: clear, simple, actionable instructions; safety hotline activated.
5. Press release: factual; recall classification; consumer action required; where to get more info.
6. SEC 8-K if financial impact is material.
(T4_INFERRED from FDA recall procedures and pharmaceutical industry best practice)

## 8. Steal Sheet — 3 Transferable Principles

**1. Build the regulatory constraint into the channel architecture, not against it.**
AbbVie's channel structure (MSLs for scientific exchange, sales force for promotion, patient
websites with full fair balance, unbranded disease awareness on social media) is designed so that
each channel operates within its regulatory boundary rather than treating compliance as an external
constraint. The separation of promotional and non-promotional channels is not bureaucratic friction —
it is the structural solution that allows AbbVie to communicate across all audiences without
regulatory exposure. Transferable for regulated businesses: design distinct channel roles for
distinct compliance environments so that no single channel is asked to carry more compliance risk
than its design allows.

**2. The FDA approval press release is a multi-stakeholder editorial product.**
When AbbVie publishes an FDA approval press release, it simultaneously satisfies the SEC 8-K
material event disclosure requirement, provides the media with the news hook, gives HCPs the
clinical summary they need, and tells patients how to access the product. Each section is written
for a primary reader who may not read beyond their relevant paragraph. The paragraph order is not
arbitrary — it follows the audience priority hierarchy for that specific approval. Transferable for
any B2B company with multiple stakeholder groups: structure major announcements as multi-track
editorial products where each stakeholder finds their information without requiring the full document.

**3. Convert patient adherence infrastructure into commercial evidence.**
Patient assistance programs that improve adherence generate real-world outcomes data. That data,
properly consented and published, becomes health economics evidence that supports formulary
positioning — which reduces access barriers — which improves adherence. The loop is self-reinforcing.
AbbVie's myAbbVie Assist and nurse ambassador programs are not charitably motivated without
commercial logic — they are the mechanism through which adherence generates evidence that supports
the commercial access infrastructure. Transferable for any subscription or recurring-revenue
business: customer success programs that generate measurable retention outcomes create commercial
evidence for enterprise sales and renewal cycles.
