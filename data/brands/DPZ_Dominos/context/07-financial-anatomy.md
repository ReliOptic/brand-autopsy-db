## 2. Revenue Anatomy

### 2.1 The Asset-Light Revenue Model

Domino's financial structure is unusual in the QSR category. Because approximately 99% of stores are franchised (T1_OFFICIAL, SEC 10-K FY2023), Domino's reported revenue does NOT represent total system-wide customer sales. Instead, DPZ's reported revenue consists of:

1. **U.S. franchise royalties and fees** — a percentage of franchisee sales (royalty rate approximately 5.5% of net sales, T1_OFFICIAL, SEC 10-K FY2023)
2. **Supply chain revenues** — sales of food, ingredients, and equipment to U.S. franchisees through Domino's commissary system
3. **U.S. company-owned store revenues** — a small portion (~1% of stores)
4. **International franchise royalties and fees** — royalties from master franchise agreements
5. **International supply chain revenues** — supply chain sales to international markets where Domino's operates supply chain centers

This structure means **global retail sales (system-wide sales) is the most meaningful top-line metric**, while reported revenue substantially underrepresents the economic scale of the system.

This divergence reflects FX headwinds on international royalty translation and supply chain revenue timing rather than underlying demand deterioration. FY2020 reported revenue growth (+13.8%) reflects COVID-19 tailwinds: lockdown conditions increased at-home food delivery demand significantly, benefiting Domino's delivery-centric model. (T3_SECONDARY_RELIABLE, earnings call and press commentary)

This supply chain control is both a revenue source and a product consistency mechanism. (T4_INFERRED from revenue composition analysis)

### 2.4 Global Retail Sales vs. Reported Revenue Context

| Metric | FY2023 | Significance |
|--------|--------|-------------|
| Global Retail Sales (system-wide) | ~$18.3B | (T1_OFFICIAL, SEC 10-K FY2023) — represents total customer payments at all stores globally |

The remaining ~75.5% flows to franchisees (covering their food costs, labor, rent, and profit). This is the fundamental economics of an asset-light franchise model.

## 3. Same-Store Sales Performance

Same-store sales (comps) growth is the primary operational metric for QSR businesses — it measures revenue growth from existing stores, excluding the effect of store openings and closings.

### 3.1 U.S. Same-Store Sales Growth

| Fiscal Year | U.S. Comp Growth | Source |
|-------------|-----------------|--------|
| FY2019 | +3.0% | (SEC 10-K FY2019, T1_OFFICIAL) |
| FY2020 | +11.5% | (SEC 10-K FY2020, T1_OFFICIAL — COVID-19 delivery tailwind) |
| FY2021 | +3.5% | (SEC 10-K FY2021, T1_OFFICIAL) |
| FY2022 | −0.8% | (SEC 10-K FY2022, T1_OFFICIAL — post-COVID normalization + driver staffing pressures) |
| FY2023 | +3.6% (approximately) | (SEC 10-K FY2023, T1_OFFICIAL — recovery; exact figure per filing) |

The FY2022 negative comp was notable — it was driven by delivery driver staffing shortages (industry-wide labor market tightness post-COVID), which temporarily constrained Domino's delivery capacity. (T3_SECONDARY_RELIABLE, earnings call commentary and press reporting) The company acknowledged this driver availability challenge explicitly in investor communications. Domino's carries substantial long-term debt, primarily from past leveraged recapitalization transactions that funded aggressive share buybacks.

| Metric | FY2023 | Source |

## 8. Financial Risk Matrix

| # | Risk Category | Disclosed Risk Summary | Severity (estimated) |
|---|---------------|------------------------|---------------------|
| 1 | **Leverage / Interest Rate** | ~$5.1–5.2B in long-term debt (estimated) creates significant interest expense. Rising interest rates increase refinancing cost. The leverage was accumulated in conjunction with share repurchase activity, as disclosed in SEC filings; it remains a structural financial risk. | High |
| 2 | **Franchisee Health** | DPZ's revenue depends on franchisee store performance. If franchisees face profitability pressure (from rising food costs, minimum wage increases, driver labor costs), they may reduce store investment or close stores, reducing royalty flows. | High |
| 3 | **Same-Store Sales Stagnation** | FY2022 negative comp (−0.8%) demonstrated that delivery driver availability and labor market conditions can directly impact comp growth. Any sustained comp deceleration directly reduces royalty revenue. | High |
| 4 | **Third-Party Platform Competition** | DoorDash, Uber Eats, and Grubhub compete for the "what to order" decision moment. Habitual platform users may not default to Domino's. The Uber Eats partnership (2023) is a tactical response to this structural risk. | Medium-High |
| 5 | **International FX** | Approximately 13,900 international stores generate royalties in local currencies. USD strengthening reduces the USD value of international royalties as reported. FX was a meaningful headwind in FY2022. | Medium |
| 6 | **Food and Commodity Costs** | Supply chain revenue margins are sensitive to commodity prices (wheat for dough, cheese, chicken). Cost increases in supply chain reduce supply chain segment margins and/or require franchisee price increases. | Medium |
| 7 | **Technology Infrastructure** | Domino's proprietary digital ordering stack is critical infrastructure. System outages, cybersecurity incidents, or competitive digital displacement represent technology-layer risks. | Medium |
| 8 | **Regulatory / Labor** | See Layer 8 for detailed treatment of franchisee labor classification, driver contractor debate, and regulatory exposure. | Medium-High |

## 9. Brand-Finance Nexus

### 9.1 Five Brand-Finance Signals

**Signal 1 — Digital Ordering as Revenue Multiplier**
Approximately 75%+ of U.S. orders are digital (estimated). Digital orders enable personalized upsell, loyalty point accumulation (driving repeat purchase), and push notification re-engagement. Each percentage point of digital adoption is a revenue efficiency improvement. (T4_INFERRED from ordering economics)

**Signal 2 — Rewards Program as Frequency Driver**
The 2023 Domino's Rewards revamp was explicitly designed to drive higher order frequency by making free pizza accessible more quickly. Higher frequency directly increases royalty revenue to DPZ, Inc. without incremental marketing cost for the marginal order. (T4_INFERRED; official, company communications on Rewards redesign rationale)

**Signal 3 — Global Retail Sales Growth Rate**
Global retail sales grew from ~$14.3B (FY2019) to ~$18.3B (FY2023), a ~28% increase over four fiscal years (T1_OFFICIAL). This compound growth in the underlying system — while reported revenue grew more modestly due to FX — demonstrates the brand's global demand expansion. (T4_INFERRED from multi-year figure comparison)

**Signal 4 — Fortress Strategy as Same-Store Sales Defense**
The fortress strategy's store density reduces competitive penetration by third parties. Higher density means Domino's can serve delivery occasions that competitors cannot economically reach. This structural advantage protects comp sales over time. (T4_INFERRED)

**Signal 5 — Negative Equity as Brand Confidence Signal**
The deliberate leveraged recapitalization structure — borrowing against franchise royalty streams to buy back stock — reflects management's confidence in the predictability and durability of the Domino's brand cash flows. Lenders agreed to provide ~$5B in debt against a franchise system with ~20,500 stores and ~$18.3B in annual global retail sales. The financing itself validates the brand's economic moat. (T4_INFERRED)
