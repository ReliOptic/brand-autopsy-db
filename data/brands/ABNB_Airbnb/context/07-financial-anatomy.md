## Revenue Model

Airbnb generates revenue from service fees charged to guests and hosts:
- **Guest service fee**: Typically 14.2% of booking subtotal (estimated range; actual varies by booking value and location) (T4_INFERRED from disclosed fee ranges and academic analysis)
- **Host service fee**: Typically 3% of booking subtotal for standard hosts; 14-16% for hosts using simplified pricing (official, airbnb.com/help/fees)
- **Experiences**: Commission on Experiences bookings (official, airbnb.com/experiences)

Revenue is recognized at checkout (when the guest's stay begins). (official, SEC 10-K FY2023 — Revenue Recognition policy)

## Brand Valuation Context

Airbnb's brand value resides in:
- **Supply-side trust**: 7.7M active listings representing hosts' willingness to stake their homes and income on the Airbnb platform
- **"Belong Anywhere" equity**: Brand recognition synonymous with short-term rental globally; "airbnb" used as a verb in multiple languages (T4_INFERRED from cultural observation)
- **Two-sided network effects**: Each new host attracts more guests; each new guest market attracts more hosts — the brand is the network

## Brand-Finance Nexus

1. **Take-rate leverage**: Airbnb's 83% gross margin reflects the asset-light marketplace model — no owned real estate, no payroll for hosts. Brand trust is the asset that allows Airbnb to charge service fees without owning the underlying inventory.
2. **ADR as a brand metric**: Average Daily Rate of ~$174 in FY2023 reflects the premium that Airbnb's unique inventory commands relative to comparable-date hotel rates in many markets. Brand positioning toward uniqueness supports pricing power at the host listing level.
3. **Geographic expansion as brand building**: Airbnb enters new markets by building host supply first — the brand promise to guests is only as strong as the inventory behind it. Host acquisition investment is brand investment.
