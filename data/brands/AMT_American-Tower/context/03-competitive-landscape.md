Concentrated in U.S. and Latin America.

| Metric | American Tower (AMT) | SBA Communications (SBAC) | Source |
|--------|---------------------|--------------------------|--------|
| Tax structure | REIT | C-Corporation | (T1_OFFICIAL, respective 10-K filings) |
| Geographic scope | ~40 countries | U.S., Latin America, Africa, Canada, Philippines | (T1_OFFICIAL, SBAC 10-K) |
| Approximate site count | ~225,000 (estimated) | ~39,000 (estimated) | (estimated, respective IR materials) |

## Differentiation Summary

American Tower's structural competitive advantages, based on observable public information, rest on four dimensions:

1. **Global scale and geographic diversification**: Approximately 225,000 sites across approximately 40 countries provides AMT with the broadest international footprint among publicly traded tower companies (estimated, based on observable portfolio comparisons).
2. **Long-term contracted revenue base**: MLAs with embedded escalators provide multi-year revenue visibility that is structurally more stable than spot-market contracts. Disclosed in AMT's 10-K as a core business characteristic. (T1_OFFICIAL, AMT 10-K FY2024)
3. **Multitenant co-location economics**: Each additional tenant on an existing tower structure generates incrementally high-margin revenue, as fixed tower costs are largely sunk. This operating leverage is a defining financial characteristic of the tower model. (T1_OFFICIAL, AMT 10-K FY2024, Business Description)
4. **CoreSite data center adjacency**: AMT's ownership of CoreSite creates an infrastructure portfolio spanning wireless towers and carrier-neutral data centers — positioning AMT for hybrid wireless-wireline infrastructure demand. (T1_OFFICIAL, AMT 10-K FY2024, CoreSite segment)

## Steal Sheet — 3 Transferable Principles

**1. Own a geographic dimension your competitors have structurally chosen to forfeit.**
Crown Castle is U.S.-only by deliberate strategic choice. SBA is Americas-focused. Cellnex is Europe-focused. AMT occupies the only position that is simultaneously large in the U.S. and large internationally. Transferable principle: in any market with regional competitors, geographic breadth is a differentiation axis that pure domestic players cannot quickly replicate without enormous capital commitment.

**2. Use structural differences (REIT vs. C-Corp) as a positioning tool with different investor segments.**
AMT's REIT structure commits it to high distributions — a feature, not a bug, for income-oriented investors. SBA's C-Corp structure allows capital retention — a feature for growth-oriented investors. Transferable insight: corporate structure choices carry brand implications for investor audience segments. Communicate the structural rationale explicitly and position it as alignment with a specific investor profile.

**3. Co-location economics are a permanent competitive moat against carrier self-build.**
A carrier building its own tower network bears 100% of the fixed cost. A carrier leasing space on an AMT tower where two or three other carriers also co-locate shares that fixed cost. This is not a marketing claim — it is a structural economic argument that can be quantified. Transferable principle: if your business model has a structural cost advantage over the customer's self-build alternative, quantify it explicitly. The cost comparison is the sales argument.
