# 02 — Audience Map
## Ball Corporation (BALL)

---

## 1. Audience Architecture Overview

Ball operates in a B2B2C model. The primary buyer audience is institutional (CPG companies, beverage brands, aerospace contractors); the secondary audience is the end consumer who chooses aluminum at the shelf. Both layers require distinct messaging but a unified brand logic.

| Tier | Audience Type | Influence on Revenue |
|---|---|---|
| **Tier 1** | CPG / Beverage Brand Procurement & Operations | Direct (contract-driven; 60–70% of revenue) |
| **Tier 2** | CPG Sustainability & ESG Officers | Indirect (shapes spec decisions, supplier scorecards) |
| **Tier 3** | End Consumers (beverage buyers) | Downstream (shapes CPG preference for aluminum) |
| **Tier 4** | Aerospace / Government Contractors | Direct (Ball Aerospace division, now Voyager Space) |
| **Tier 5** | Investors & ESG Analysts | Capital markets (BALL stock, ESG ratings) |

## 4. Battle Cards

### Battle Card 1 — Winning the Sustainability Officer
| Field | Content |
|---|---|
| **Objective** | Become the default supplier on ESG scorecards |
| **Key Move** | Offer Ball's Lifecycle Assessment (LCA) co-authorship — shared data, shared credit |
| **Differentiator** | Ball is the only major can maker with a fully published Scope 3 supply chain emissions inventory |
| **Objection Handler** | "Cost per unit is higher" → "EPR penalties and greenwashing fines will cost more; we're the cheap option when you price in regulatory risk" |

### Battle Card 2 — Winning the Procurement Director
| Field | Content |
|---|---|
| **Objective** | Lock in multi-year supply agreements with SLA guarantees |
| **Key Move** | Lead with plant proximity map and on-time delivery data (target: 98.5%+ OTD) |
| **Differentiator** | Global footprint with regional manufacturing = no single point of failure |
| **Objection Handler** | "Your MOQ is too high" → Introduce Ball's flexible run partnerships or co-packing network referrals |

### Battle Card 3 — Winning the Consumer (Through Brand Partners)
| Field | Content |
|---|---|
| **Objective** | Make "Ball can" a positive attribute in brand partner marketing |
| **Key Move** | Provide co-branded toolkit: "Packaged in Ball Aluminum" badge + QR code linking to recycling story |
| **Differentiator** | 60-day can-to-shelf-again story is uniquely compelling and verifiable |
| **Objection Handler** | "Consumers don't care who made the can" → Show research: 68% of Gen Z check packaging sustainability before purchase |

## 5. Steal Sheets

### Steal Sheet 1 — "The Ball Audience Brief for CPG Partners"
- **What it does:** Gives Ball's CPG brand partners a one-page snapshot of end-consumer aluminum preferences
- **Key insight:** Canned format is +14% YoY growth in premium non-alcoholic beverages (Nielsen 2024)
- **Use case:** Sales deck slide for regional beverage brand pitches

### Steal Sheet 2 — "Procurement Decision Matrix: Aluminum vs. Alternatives"
- **What it does:** Side-by-side TCO comparison across aluminum, PET, glass for a standard 12 oz. SKU
- **Key insight:** Aluminum achieves cost parity with PET at ~120M units/year when EPR compliance costs are included
- **Use case:** CFO and procurement committee presentation leave-behind

### Steal Sheet 3 — "Consumer Recycling Behavior Snapshot"
- **What it does:** Summarizes consumer recycling intent and action rates by format and geography
- **Key insight:** 74% of US consumers say they are "more likely" to purchase a beverage in a recyclable container; aluminum leads all formats in perceived recyclability
- **Use case:** Co-marketing asset for Ball's brand partners to use in retail/DTC campaigns
