## Channel Prohibitions

1. Do not discuss specific customer contract terms on any public channel
2. Do not publish aluminum commodity price predictions or hedging positions in public content
3. Do not reference plant production capacities in ways that could signal supply position to competitors
4. Do not make environmental claims that have not been independently verified (risk of greenwashing allegations)
5. Do not publish content about ongoing labor negotiations or workforce matters before employee communication

## Crisis Protocol

### Scenario 1: Plant Closure / Workforce Reduction
- **Immediate**: Employee communication before public announcement
- **Community**: Local government and media notification; community impact statement
- **Tone**: Respectful of affected employees; specific about support resources; rationale explained clearly
- **Social**: Acknowledge publicly; redirect to official statement; do not engage with hostile social commentary

### Scenario 2: Product Quality / Food Safety Issue
- **Regulatory**: FDA/USDA notification if applicable; beverage brand customer notification immediately
- **Public**: Statement through ball.com newsroom; specific about what is and is not affected
- **Tone**: Safety-first; transparent about remediation steps; no minimization

### Scenario 3: Sustainability Claim Challenge (Greenwashing Allegation)
- **Response**: Publish supporting data immediately; cite third-party verification
- **Tone**: Data-grounded, not defensive; "Here is the underlying data that supports this claim"
- **Prevention**: All sustainability claims pre-reviewed against FTC Green Guides (T4_INFERRED from compliance best practices)

## Steal Sheet — 3 Transferable Principles

**1. In B2B industrial sales, the trade show floor is your brand's highest-ROI channel.**
Ball's most important sales interactions happen at Pack Expo and BevTech, not on digital channels. The physical product demonstration — the weight of an aluminum cup, the precision of a sleek can — creates a sensory brand experience that no digital content replicates. Transferable: for physical product companies, identify the one or two trade events that drive the most meaningful customer conversations and treat them as your primary marketing investment.

**2. Publish your sustainability data before someone else publishes it for you.**
NGOs, regulatory bodies, and competitors' marketing teams will characterize your environmental performance using whatever data is available. Ball's proactive publication of lifecycle assessments, recycling rates, and emissions data means Ball controls the framing. Transferable: in any category with environmental or social performance scrutiny, publish your own verified data first.

**3. Consumer sub-brands can access channels the corporate brand cannot.**
Ball Corporation cannot easily run Instagram campaigns as an industrial B2B company. Ball Aluminum Cup can — it is a consumer product in a consumer context. The consumer sub-brand generates brand awareness and retail relationships that the corporate brand accesses through a different channel entirely. Transferable: consider whether a consumer-facing sub-brand could create market access and awareness that benefits your B2B corporate brand indirectly.
