## Threats

1. **Volume volatility**: Hard seltzer category growth (which drove 2020–2021 can demand spike) has moderated; overcapacity built for the spike now requires rationalization. Ball announced plant closures in 2022–2023. (T3_SECONDARY_RELIABLE, official press releases)
2. **Aluminum cost inflation**: Ball passes aluminum commodity costs through in pricing, but conversion margin can be compressed during rapid cost spikes. (SEC 10-K risk factors, T1_OFFICIAL)
3. **Glass resurgence**: Premium beverage brands occasionally shift to glass for sustainability/premium positioning; limited but ongoing competitive threat. (T4_INFERRED)
4. **Regulatory pressure on cans**: Some European markets are exploring extended producer responsibility schemes that could affect can economics. (T3_SECONDARY_RELIABLE)

## Opportunities

1. **Aluminum Cup expansion**: The Ball Aluminum Cup (reusable/recyclable alternative to plastic cups) targets the foodservice and events market — a new category. (official, ballaluminumcup.com)
2. **Hard-to-recycle plastic substitution**: EU single-use plastics directive drives brand owner substitution to aluminum. (T3_SECONDARY_RELIABLE, EU regulation)
3. **Emerging market growth**: Ball's presence in Brazil and other high-growth beverage markets positions it for volume growth as per-capita can consumption rises. (T4_INFERRED from geographic segment disclosures)

## Steal Sheet — 3 Transferable Principles

**1. Build new product categories within your material expertise.**
The Ball Aluminum Cup extends Ball's aluminum manufacturing expertise into a new end market (foodservice cups) without requiring new material capabilities. The brand is the material, not the product format. Transferable: identify the core material, process, or technical capability your business has mastered and ask which adjacent product categories could be served with the same expertise.

**2. In oligopoly markets, capacity discipline is competitive strategy.**
Ball's plant closure announcements in 2022–2023 signal discipline about not sustaining overcapacity that would pressure pricing for the entire industry. In concentrated industrial markets, capacity decisions by the market leader shape economics for all participants. Transferable: in industries with high fixed costs and limited competitors, the decision to rationalize capacity can be as strategically important as the decision to expand it.

**3. Your material's environmental story is your marketing budget alternative.**
Ball does not spend heavily on consumer-facing advertising because aluminum's recycling story — "infinitely recyclable," 50%+ recycling rate — is told by its beverage brand customers in their own marketing. Ball invests in generating and publishing the underlying data. Transferable: identify the third-party validation or publicly verifiable data about your product that your customers will amplify in their own communications, and invest in creating that data.
