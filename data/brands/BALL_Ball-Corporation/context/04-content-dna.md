# 04 — Content DNA
## Ball Corporation (BALL)

---

## 1. Content Philosophy

Ball's content operates on a single organizing principle: **aluminum is not a commodity — it is a circular asset.** Every piece of content, from a technical datasheet to an investor earnings script, must either reinforce this belief or provide the evidence that makes it credible.

Ball does not produce content about itself. Ball produces content about what aluminum makes possible — for brands, for consumers, for the planet.

---

## 2. Narrative Architecture

### Level 1 — Category Narrative (Owned by Ball, shared with industry)
> "Aluminum is the most recycled beverage container material in the world. The circular economy for beverages already exists. It runs on aluminum."

Ball uses this layer to elevate the entire aluminum category, which benefits Ball as the category leader.

### Level 2 — Company Narrative (Ball-specific)
> "Ball builds the infrastructure of that circular economy — the cans, the partnerships, the data, and the innovation — at a scale no one else can match."

### Level 3 — Product / Solution Narrative (Campaign-level)
> "Every Ball can is engineered to come back. The can you're holding may have been a can 60 days ago. It will be one again in 60 more."

## 4. Tone Variants by Context

| Context | Tone Adjustment | Example Opening |
|---|---|---|
| **Investor Relations** | Precise, data-forward, no metaphor | "Net revenue increased 4.2% year-over-year, driven by volume growth in North American beverage cans..." |
| **CPG Sales Materials** | Advisory, partnership-framing | "As your packaging partner, Ball can help you meet your 2030 Scope 3 targets without compromising fill-line efficiency." |
| **Sustainability Reports** | Earnest, accountable, specific | "In 2024, Ball reduced its Scope 1 and 2 greenhouse gas emissions by 12% against a 2019 baseline." |
| **Consumer-Facing / Social** | Warm, direct, action-oriented | "Your can had a past life. In 60 days, it'll have a next one. That's the loop." |
| **Trade Press / B2B** | Technical, peer-to-peer | "Our new DWI (Draw and Wall Iron) tooling reduces material gauge by 3% while maintaining burst pressure to ASTM D2660." |

---

## 5. Content Don'ts

| Don't | Why |
|---|---|
| Lead with Ball's history (founded 1880, Mason jars) | Positions Ball as legacy, not forward-looking |
| Use vague sustainability language ("eco-friendly," "green") | Legally exposed and brand-diluting |
| Publish unverified recycling statistics | Third-party audit is a Ball brand promise |
| Produce content that competes with customers' brand stories | Ball amplifies brands, not overshadows them |
| Use stock imagery of generic cans | Every Ball content asset uses Ball-specific or co-branded imagery |

---

## 6. Content Archetypes in Active Use

### Archetype A — "The Proof"
**Purpose:** Establish credibility with skeptics.
**Structure:** Claim → Verified Data → Third-party Source → Implication
**Example use:** Sustainability report chapters, CDP response, media fact sheets

### Archetype B — "The Loop Story"
**Purpose:** Make circular economy tangible for non-technical audiences.
**Structure:** Can leaves shelf → consumer uses it → can is recycled → back on shelf in 60 days
**Example use:** On-can QR code destination, festival/event activations, social video scripts

### Archetype C — "The Co-Development Story"
**Purpose:** Show Ball as an innovation partner, not a vendor.
**Structure:** Customer challenge → Ball embedded team → engineering solution → market result
**Example use:** Case studies, trade conference presentations, sales deck case examples

### Archetype D — "The Regulatory Signal"
**Purpose:** Alert procurement and legal teams to incoming policy shifts that favor aluminum.
**Structure:** Regulation summary → timeline → implication for packaging choices → Ball's readiness
**Example use:** Policy briefs, LinkedIn thought leadership, analyst briefing materials

## 8. Battle Cards

### Battle Card 1 — When Accused of Greenwashing
| Field | Content |
|---|---|
| **Scenario** | Journalist, NGO, or social media challenge: "Ball's sustainability claims are marketing." |
| **Content Response** | Publish the full LCA data, third-party audit certificates, and CDP score. Don't summarize — show the source documents. |
| **Key Message** | "Our sustainability claims are not aspirational. They are audited, published, and available for verification." |
| **Avoid** | Defensive language, counter-accusations, platform-specific snark |

### Battle Card 2 — When Customers Ask for "Simpler" Messaging
| Field | Content |
|---|---|
| **Scenario** | CPG marketing team says Ball's content is "too technical" for their consumer campaign. |
| **Content Response** | Offer the "Loop Story" archetype — visual, emotional, simple. Provide the 60-day narrative in a co-branded format. |
| **Key Message** | "We have two modes: proof for your procurement team, story for your consumers. Both are true." |
| **Avoid** | Dumbing down the science in ways that sacrifice accuracy |

### Battle Card 3 — When Competing for Thought Leadership Space
| Field | Content |
|---|---|
| **Scenario** | Crown Holdings or Amcor publishes a sustainability report that gets media coverage Ball expected to own. |
| **Content Response** | Publish comparison data (without naming competitors directly); issue a "state of the circular economy" report that Ball clearly leads. |
| **Key Message** | "Category leadership means you publish the benchmark everyone else is measured against." |
| **Avoid** | Reactive publishing schedule; competitor name-drops in official content |

## 9. Steal Sheets

### Steal Sheet 1 — "Ball Content Toolkit for Brand Partners"
- **What:** Co-branded content assets (video scripts, social copy, QR destination pages) for CPG brands using Ball cans
- **Core asset:** "Packaged in Ball Aluminum" badge + 60-day loop story in 3 formats (15s video, carousel, static)
- **Audience:** CPG marketing and social media teams
- **Outcome:** CPG brand amplifies Ball's sustainability story while enhancing their own

### Steal Sheet 2 — "The Sustainability Proof Pack"
- **What:** Pre-assembled evidence bundle for procurement and ESG use
- **Includes:** Ball Sustainability Report summary, verified recycled content cert, CDP score card, LCA summary
- **Audience:** Ball sales team to deploy in RFP responses
- **Outcome:** Eliminates the "we can't verify your claims" objection before it is raised

### Steal Sheet 3 — "60-Day Loop: Consumer Education Campaign Blueprint"
- **What:** Turn-key campaign brief for co-marketing with retail partners
- **Channels:** POS display, shelf-talker copy, Instagram carousel, TikTok stitch-ready video
- **Message:** Aluminum's circular journey in simple, visual terms
- **Audience:** Retail buyers and brand managers at natural/specialty grocery channels
