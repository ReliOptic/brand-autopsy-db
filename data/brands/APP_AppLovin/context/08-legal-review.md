## Major Matters and Disputes

### Matter 1: Short-Seller Research Reports (2024)

**Status**: Active/ongoing reputational matter; not a formal legal proceeding as of publicly available information.

**Facts**: In early 2024, Muddy Waters Research and Fuzzy Panda Research published reports alleging that AppLovin's advertising attribution system overstated advertiser ROAS by attributing organic installs to paid campaigns. The reports also made other allegations regarding AppLovin's business practices. AppLovin disputed the characterizations in these reports and published a detailed technical response. (T3_SECONDARY_RELIABLE, Muddy Waters and Fuzzy Panda published reports 2024; AppLovin response published via SEC 8-K filings 2024)

**Note on this project's treatment**: This analysis describes the existence of the dispute and the parties' public positions. It does not independently verify the accuracy of either the short-seller reports or AppLovin's response. Both sides' published positions are cited with their respective source tiers.

**Brand impact**: Measurement credibility is fundamental to AppLovin's advertiser value proposition. Any sustained perception of attribution methodology issues among advertisers could affect UA budget allocation decisions. (T4_INFERRED)

### Matter 2: Privacy Regulatory Environment

**Context**: AppLovin's publisher network collects device-level and behavioral data to power its ad targeting. This data collection is subject to:
- Apple's App Tracking Transparency (ATT) framework (requires opt-in consent for IDFA tracking)
- GDPR (EU) and national data protection laws in European markets
- CCPA/CPRA (California Consumer Privacy Act and its amendment)
- Potential US federal privacy legislation

**Current status**: AppLovin has disclosed in its SEC filings that it is subject to privacy regulation and has taken steps to comply with applicable requirements, including SDK updates to support ATT consent flows. (SEC 10-K FY2023) No formal regulatory enforcement action against AppLovin has been identified in publicly available information as of this analysis.

**Brand impact**: Privacy regulation is an ongoing operational and brand risk. AppLovin's ability to maintain ad targeting performance under increasingly restrictive privacy frameworks is directly relevant to its core product value proposition. (T4_INFERRED)

---

### Matter 3: MoPub Acquisition Integration and Legacy Litigation

**Context**: AppLovin acquired Twitter's MoPub mediation platform in January 2022 for $1.05B. The acquisition brought existing publisher relationships and potential legacy legal obligations into AppLovin's corporate structure. (SEC 8-K, January 2022; SEC 10-K FY2022)

AppLovin's SEC filings note that ordinary course litigation matters may arise from business operations, including matters related to acquired businesses. Specific litigation from the MoPub acquisition, if any, is not prominently disclosed as material in the FY2023 10-K. (SEC 10-K FY2023 legal proceedings section)

## Regulatory Landscape

**FTC and Digital Advertising**: The FTC has been active in scrutinizing digital advertising practices, data collection, and ad technology competition. AppLovin's business model — collecting behavioral data across its publisher network to power targeted advertising — is within the scope of ongoing FTC attention to ad tech. No specific FTC action against AppLovin has been identified in publicly available information. (T4_INFERRED from regulatory environment; T3_SECONDARY_RELIABLE for general FTC ad tech scrutiny)

**DOJ / Competition**: The US Department of Justice's antitrust action against Google's ad tech stack (filed January 2023) involves the broader programmatic advertising ecosystem. AppLovin is not a party to this action but operates in the market affected by its outcome. Changes in the programmatic landscape resulting from this action could affect AppLovin's competitive position. (T3_SECONDARY_RELIABLE, DOJ complaint January 2023)

**EU Digital Markets Act**: The DMA designates "gatekeepers" subject to interoperability and data sharing requirements. AppLovin is unlikely to be designated a gatekeeper at current scale, but the DMA shapes the competitive environment in which it operates, particularly regarding Apple and Google's platform policies that affect AppLovin's publisher network. (T4_INFERRED from DMA regulatory framework)

## Writing Risk Guide

### SAFE — Language cleared for use in this analysis:

- "As disclosed in AppLovin's SEC 10-K FY2023, ..."
- "Short-seller reports published in 2024 alleged [specific claim]; AppLovin disputed these allegations in its published response (SEC 8-K filings, 2024)."
- "AppLovin's Software Platform segment generated approximately $1.9B in revenue in FY2023, representing 68% year-over-year growth, per the company's SEC 10-K FY2023."
- "This analysis interprets AppLovin's strategy as suggesting ..."
- "Based on publicly available information, AppLovin's AXON system ..."

### HEDGE — Language requiring qualification:

- Any claim about AXON's attribution accuracy → describe AppLovin's disclosed methodology and the existence of the dispute; do not independently adjudicate the accuracy of either side's claims.
- Any claim about AppLovin's data collection practices → describe what is disclosed in SEC filings and privacy policies; do not characterize practices beyond disclosed information.
- Financial projections → always mark as (estimated) with basis stated.

### DO NOT WRITE — Language prohibited for this analysis:

- Any assertion that AppLovin's attribution system was designed to misrepresent results — this is not established by public record and constitutes an unwarranted characterization.
- Any assertion characterizing AppLovin's data collection as violating specific laws — no regulatory finding has established this as of publicly available information.
- Any assertion that specific short-seller claims are proven facts — the short-seller reports are allegations; AppLovin disputes them; neither has been adjudicated.
- Any framing that characterizes AppLovin's business model as inherently dishonest — this would be a pejorative assertion not supported by regulatory findings.
