## 7. Financial Risk Matrix

### Based on SEC 10-K FY2025 Risk Factors (Item 1A)

| Risk Category | Key Content | Brand Strategy Impact |
|--------------|-------------|----------------------|
| Macroeconomic Conditions | 10-K cites inflation, interest rates, tariffs, and trade restrictions as material risks | Commodity cost inflation compresses margins on branded products; pricing power becomes a brand equity test |
| Tariff & Trade Policy | 10-K notes "recent U.S. tariffs imposed or threatened on a variety of countries" could increase key input costs | Import-dependent ingredients at cost risk; affects competitive pricing of branded items vs. private label |
| Consumer Demand Volatility | Risk factors note customers may "delay or reduce purchases" during downturns; distributor inventory reduction risk | Hormel's premium branded positioning must sustain value perception during consumer trade-down cycles |
| Commodity Price Exposure | Hedging instruments may become ineffective; volatile commodity prices affect earnings | Pork, turkey, nuts, and other commodity inputs are subject to price swings; margin compression risk if costs cannot be passed through |
| Supply Chain Disruption | Fuel, transportation, and supply chain shortages cited as ongoing operational risks | Disruptions could impair product availability, damaging brand reliability with retail partners |
| Capital Market Volatility | Pension plan and supplemental retirement plan assets subject to market fluctuations | Elevated pension obligations may constrain financial flexibility for brand investment (T4_INFERRED) |

> **Source**: SEC 10-K FY2025 — Part I, Item 1A, Risk Factors

## 8. Brand-Finance Nexus

### 8.1 What Financial Data Reveals About Brand Strategy

| Financial Signal | Brand Strategy Interpretation |
|-----------------|------------------------------|
| FY2025 net income decline of 20.6% | Margin pressure suggests commodity cost pass-through is incomplete; brand pricing power has limits in the current consumer environment |
| SG&A rising to $942M in FY2025 | Sustained brand marketing investment even as profitability contracts; indicates management's commitment to brand equity maintenance |
| 55+ consecutive years of dividend growth | Financial discipline signals brand stability and long-term orientation; reinforces Hormel's identity as a reliable consumer staples company |
| R&D at ~$33M (estimated <0.5% of revenue) | Brand innovation relies on brand extensions and acquisitions rather than fundamental R&D; differentiation is brand-led |
| Post-Planters debt reduction trend | Capital allocation prioritizes deleveraging over buybacks; financial discipline supports long-term brand investment capacity |
| Total assets stable at ~$13.4B | Manufacturing scale across diversified portfolio is a competitive advantage in retail shelf negotiations |

### 8.2 Brand-Finance Feedback Loop

