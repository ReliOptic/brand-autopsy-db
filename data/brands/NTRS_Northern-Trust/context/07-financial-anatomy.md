## 2. Revenue Anatomy

### 2.1 Five-Year Revenue Trend

Northern Trust reports net revenue characteristic of a financial services firm — encompassing
fee-based revenues from trust, custody, and investment management, plus net interest income.
The XBRL-reported revenue figures below are as filed.

| Fiscal Year | Total Revenue | YoY Change | Net Income | Net Margin | Source |
|-------------|---------------|------------|------------|------------|--------|
| FY2021 | $3.852B | — | $1.492B | 38.7% | (SEC 10-K FY2021) |
| FY2022 | $3.995B | +3.7% | $1.209B | 30.3% | (SEC 10-K FY2022) |
| FY2023 | $4.361B | +9.2% | $1.545B | 35.4% | (SEC 10-K FY2023) |
| FY2024 | $4.433B | +1.6% | $1.336B | 30.1% | (SEC 10-K FY2024) |
| FY2025 | $4.362B | -1.6% | $1.107B | 25.4% | (SEC 10-K FY2025) |

> **5-Year Revenue CAGR (FY2021–FY2025)**: approximately +3.2% (estimated, derived from XBRL
> figures above). Revenue has remained in a relatively narrow band between $3.9B and $4.4B,
> reflecting Northern Trust's position as a mature, fee-dependent financial institution whose
> revenues are sensitive to market levels and interest rate movements. Net margin compression
> from 38.7% (FY2021) to 25.4% (FY2025) indicates (T4_INFERRED) rising expense pressure or
> changing revenue mix over this period.

### 2.2 Revenue Composition Note

Northern Trust's revenues are primarily fee-based (trust, investment management, custody,
securities lending fees) combined with net interest income. The company does not report a
traditional product/service revenue segmentation comparable to industrial companies. Segment-level
and geographic revenue details are disclosed in Northern Trust's 10-K notes. Readers are directed
to the EDGAR filing linked above for precise segment breakdowns. (T1_OFFICIAL)

Report on Form 10-K (T1_OFFICIAL):

| # | Risk Category | Description | Brand-Finance Impact |
|---|---------------|-------------|----------------------|
| 1 | **Market Risk — Fee Revenue** | Northern Trust is "dependent on fee-based business for a majority of revenues, which may be affected adversely by market volatility, a downturn in economic conditions, underperformance and/or negative trends in investment preferences." (SEC 10-K FY2025, Item 1A) | Revenue concentration in fee-based income makes top-line results directly correlated to equity and fixed income market levels. A bear market scenario directly compresses revenues. |
| 2 | **Interest Rate Risk** | "Changes in interest rates can affect our earnings negatively." (SEC 10-K FY2025, Item 1A) | Net interest income is sensitive to Federal Reserve monetary policy. Rapid rate changes can create asset-liability mismatches. |
| 3 | **Macroeconomic / Geopolitical Risk** | "Macroeconomic conditions and uncertainty in the global economy, including the financial stability of various regions or countries across the globe, including the risk of defaults on sovereign debt and related stresses on financial markets, could have a significant adverse effect on our earnings." | 6 | **Operational Risk** | "We are subject to many types of operational risks that could affect our earnings negatively." ## 8. Brand-Finance Nexus

### 8.1 How Financial Structure Shapes Brand Positioning

Northern Trust's financial anatomy directly informs and constrains its brand strategy:

**Fee-Revenue Model Supports Premium Positioning**: Revenue is predominantly fee-based rather
than spread-based, which aligns with Northern Trust's premium wealth management and institutional
custody brand. Fee income depends on assets under management and custody (AUM/AUC) levels, which
are in turn a function of client trust and retention. The brand's emphasis on institutional
credibility and long-term relationships is structurally necessary to protect this revenue model.
(T4_INFERRED from revenue structure and brand positioning)

**Stable Dividend Program Signals Financial Conservatism**: Consistent dividend payments
($529.7M–$750.2M annually over FY2021–FY2025) communicate financial stability to both
shareholders and institutional clients who value counterparty reliability. For a firm managing
ultra-high-net-worth and institutional assets, demonstrable financial conservatism is itself a
brand asset. (T4_INFERRED)

**CapEx Moderation and Technology Infrastructure**: Declining CapEx as a percentage of revenue
(from 4.1% in FY2021 to 2.7% in FY2025) may indicate (T4_INFERRED) a shift toward cloud-based
and third-party technology infrastructure. For clients evaluating custody and technology platforms,
this could signal either efficient capital deployment or reduced proprietary system investment.

**Net Margin Compression Warrants Monitoring**: The decline in net margin from 35.4% (FY2023)
to 25.4% (FY2025) represents approximately 10 percentage points of compression in two years.
If this trend continues, it may constrain Northern Trust's capacity to invest in the premium
service delivery and technology infrastructure that underpins its brand differentiation. (T4_INFERRED)

**Balance Sheet Scale Communicates Institutional Credibility**: Total assets of $150.8B (FY2025),
even after declining from the FY2023 peak of $183.9B, communicate institutional scale relevant
to large sovereign wealth funds, endowments, and family offices evaluating custody and investment
management counterparties. (T4_INFERRED)
