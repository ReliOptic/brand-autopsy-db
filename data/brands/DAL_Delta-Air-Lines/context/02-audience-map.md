## Persona Gallery

### Persona 1: The Corporate Business Traveler

**Role**: Senior consultant, management consulting firm, Atlanta-based, 80+ flight segments per year
**Age**: 38

**Pain Points**:
- Every delayed flight has a downstream business consequence: missed client meetings, reputational risk with clients
- Expense policy requires booking cheapest available fare, but personal preference is Delta for reliability
- Connection anxiety at ATL: knows that a 45-minute domestic connection is viable but not comfortable

**Gain Goals**:
- Achieve Diamond Medallion status to secure upgrades to first class on domestic routes
- Consistent seat assignment in Comfort+ or first class, reserved weeks in advance
- 24/7 Medallion phone line access to rebook instantly when disruptions occur

**Trigger Events**:
- Missed a client presentation due to a United flight delay; switched loyalty to Delta
- Employer introduced a Delta corporate agreement with discounted fares and bonus miles

**Delta Solution Fit**: Diamond Medallion status with complimentary upgrades; Delta Reserve AmEx card for SkyMiles earning acceleration; dedicated Medallion phone service for irregular operations recovery. (official, delta.com/skymiles/medallion-program)

---

### Persona 2: The Premium Leisure Traveler

**Role**: Marketing executive, Seattle-based, couple traveling 3–4 times per year for leisure, occasional business travel
**Age**: 52

**Pain Points**:
- Increasingly unwilling to fly in economy on transatlantic routes (8+ hours)
- Has flown Delta One to Europe twice and finds returning to premium economy painful
- Confused by SkyMiles award availability and award chart changes

**Gain Goals**:
- Consistent access to Delta One award availability using SkyMiles from the Delta AmEx Platinum card
- Premium Select or Delta One on transatlantic routes for annual Europe trip
- Lounge access (Delta Sky Club or partner) on international itineraries

**Trigger Events**:
- Delta One promotion to Milan for a milestone anniversary trip was the first premium cabin experience
- Partner airline reciprocal upgrade privileges on Air France/KLM via SkyTeam alliance

**Delta Solution Fit**: Delta AmEx Reserve card for companion certificates and Sky Club access; SkyMiles award availability on Delta One transatlantic; SkyTeam alliance network for European destinations. (official, delta.com/skymiles; delta.com/partners)

---

### Persona 3: The Corporate Travel Manager

**Role**: Director of Travel, Fortune 500 healthcare company, 2,000+ employee travelers
**Age**: 47

**Pain Points**:
- Duty of care obligation: needs real-time location data for travelers during irregular operations
- CFO pressure to reduce total travel spend while maintaining productivity
- Employees resent booking restrictions that force them onto connecting itineraries when nonstop alternatives exist

**Gain Goals**:
- Delta corporate account with negotiated fares, SkyMiles corporate earning, and rebate structure
- Delta Edge negotiated fare program to reduce blended cost-per-mile
- Reporting dashboard for spend visibility and traveler location

**Trigger Events**:
- Company travel policy review following a major storm event that stranded 40 employees
- New CFO demanding consolidated carrier strategy to maximize volume discounts

**Delta Solution Fit**: Delta Edge corporate account program with negotiated discounts and SkyMiles earning for company travel; Delta Meeting & Events for group bookings; duty of care integration via corporate travel management system partners. (official, delta.com/corporate)

## Anti-Persona — Who Delta Is NOT Targeting as Primary

| Profile | Reason |
|---------|--------|

## Steal Sheet — 3 Transferable Principles

**1. Design your loyalty program to create switching costs, not just rewards.**
Delta Medallion status creates switching costs that go beyond points accumulation: upgrade priority, dedicated phone lines, checked bag fee waivers, and boarding priority all degrade immediately when a customer switches carriers. The cost of switching is not just losing points — it is losing operational advantages. Transferable: design loyalty programs with operational privileges that are not portable, not just reward balances that are.

**2. The corporate account is a multiplier, not just a revenue line.**
A single corporate account agreement can deliver hundreds or thousands of individual travelers who would not have chosen Delta on their own. Corporate travel managers are influencers at scale. Transferable: identify the organizational buyers who influence the individual purchasing decisions of large groups, and build a dedicated sales motion for them.

**3. Hub dominance creates self-reinforcing network effects.**
Delta's dominance at Atlanta Hartsfield-Jackson (the world's busiest airport) means that passengers traveling through Atlanta often have no non-stop alternative to Delta. Hub dominance is a brand asset that marketing cannot replicate. Transferable: in any network business, identifying and dominating a critical node creates structural advantages that pricing and advertising cannot counter.
