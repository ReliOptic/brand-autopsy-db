## Hook Patterns

**Hook 1: The Reliability Proof Point**
Opens with a specific, verifiable operational achievement.
> "For the third consecutive year, Delta ranked first among North American carriers in on-time performance. Here is how we do it."
Why it works: Cites a measurable outcome before making any brand claim. The proof comes before the promise.

**Hook 2: The Premium Reveal**
Opens with a sensory detail of the Delta One experience.
> "Lie-flat. Noise-canceling. Champagne before takeoff. Delta One to Paris — here is what to expect."
Why it works: Premium cabin content converts aspirational awareness into redemption and upgrade behavior among SkyMiles members.

**Hook 3: The Loyalty Math**
Opens with a SkyMiles earning or redemption calculation that creates urgency.
> "Fly to Tokyo in Delta One for 80,000 SkyMiles round trip. Here is how Diamond Medallion members are booking it."
Why it works: Converts abstract miles balances into concrete aspirational travel plans. Creates direct booking urgency.

**Hook 4: The Employee Story**
Opens with an individual Delta employee's story.
> "Flight Attendant Maria has been with Delta for 27 years. This is what 'The Delta Way' means to her."
Why it works: Humanizes operational reliability. Demonstrates that the brand promise is delivered by real people, not systems.

**Hook 5: The Destination as Reason**
Opens with a destination that Delta serves exclusively or best.
> "Nonstop to Reykjavik, Iceland. Delta is the only US carrier flying direct from JFK. Here is why now is the moment to go."
Why it works: Route exclusivity converted into travel urgency. Destinations create aspiration that routes fulfill.

## Tone and Mood Guide

| Situation | Tone | Example |
|-----------|------|---------|
| **Irregular operations / delay** | Empathetic, factual, action-oriented — acknowledge the inconvenience, state the facts, provide a clear next step | "We know this isn't the experience you expected. Your new flight departs at [time] from Gate [X]. Here is what we are doing to get you there." |
| **Premium product launch** | Aspirational, sensory, confident — lead with experience detail, not specification | "The Delta One Suite. A full door. A flat bed. The Atlantic, below you." |
| **Loyalty promotion** | Warm, specific, time-bound — personal acknowledgment of loyalty status and clear benefit | "As a Gold Medallion member, you have earned a bonus miles offer. Here is what you can redeem right now." |
| **Corporate sustainability** | Measured, specific, evidence-based — avoids greenwashing language; cites specific investments | "Delta has invested [amount] in Sustainable Aviation Fuel commitments with [partners]. Here is our current progress toward our 2035 targets." |

## Content Prohibitions

1. **No fare advertising without complete fare disclosure.** US DOT regulations require that advertised fares include all mandatory taxes and fees. Delta's advertising must comply with 14 CFR Part 399. (T1_OFFICIAL, US DOT regulations)
2. **No comparative superiority claims without substantiation.** "The best airline in America" requires a cited source. Claims like "#1 in on-time performance" must cite the specific source (e.g., Cirium data) and measurement period. (T4_INFERRED from legal compliance requirements)
3. **No content that implies guaranteed upgrade availability.** Upgrades are subject to availability and Medallion status priority. Content that implies guaranteed upgrade access creates false expectation and complaint exposure. (T4_INFERRED)
4. **No health claim content without medical review.** Content about Delta's inflight wellness features (air filtration, sleep aids in Delta One) must be reviewed for accuracy and not overstate health outcomes. (T4_INFERRED)
5. **No social media content referencing specific customers without consent.** Individual customer recognition (e.g., celebrating a passenger's milestone flight) requires explicit consent. (T4_INFERRED from privacy policy obligations)

## Steal Sheet — 3 Transferable Principles

**1. Employee stories are reliability proof.**
When Delta publishes a story about a flight attendant with 27 years of service, it is not just feel-good content — it is proof that the reliability promise is delivered by stable, experienced, motivated people. Long-tenured employee stories convert into brand credibility in a way that operational statistics alone cannot. Transferable: in any service business, front-line employee tenure and satisfaction are brand assets. Publish them.

**2. Convert loyalty program moments into content triggers.**
Every SkyMiles milestone — first Medallion status achievement, one millionth mile, anniversary of first flight — is a content and communication trigger. Delta captures these moments and uses them to generate shareable content with the customer's consent. Transferable: design your loyalty or engagement program to identify milestone moments and build automated, personalized content generation around them.

**3. Destination content is search-engine-friendly product content.**
"Nonstop to Reykjavik from JFK" is both a travel inspiration article and an SEO-optimized product page for Delta's route. Every destination content piece that Delta publishes drives organic search traffic for relevant flight queries. Transferable: for any product with a geographic or location dimension, content about places is simultaneously content about your product.
