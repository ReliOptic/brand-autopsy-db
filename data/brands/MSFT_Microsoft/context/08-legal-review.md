## Major Litigation Summary

### Antitrust History — US DOJ (1998–2001)

The United States Department of Justice and 20 state attorneys general filed an antitrust action against Microsoft in 1998, alleging anticompetitive conduct in the browser market. A district court found Microsoft liable in 2000; this finding was partially affirmed on appeal in 2001. A consent decree was entered in 2001 and a final judgment in 2002. (T1_OFFICIAL, public court records; SEC 10-K historical disclosures) This matter concluded over two decades ago. It is referenced here as historical context for understanding Microsoft's regulatory relationship. No assertion is made about current conduct.

### EU Antitrust — Teams Bundling Investigation (2023)

The European Commission opened a formal antitrust investigation in 2023 into whether Microsoft's bundling of Teams with Microsoft 365 harmed competition in the communications market. (T3_SECONDARY_RELIABLE, European Commission press release, July 2023) Microsoft subsequently announced it would separate Teams from Microsoft 365 in European markets. (official, Microsoft press release, August 2023) The investigation remained open as of the FY2023 10-K disclosure period. (SEC 10-K FY2023, Risk Factors)

### Activision Blizzard Acquisition Regulatory Review

The $68.7B acquisition of Activision Blizzard faced regulatory review from the UK's Competition and Markets Authority (CMA), the EU Commission, and the US FTC:
- **EU Commission**: cleared the acquisition with conditions in May 2023 (T3_SECONDARY_RELIABLE, EU Commission press release)
- **UK CMA**: initially blocked, then approved with structural remedies in October 2023 (T3_SECONDARY_RELIABLE, CMA press release)
- **US FTC**: sought to block via preliminary injunction; US federal court denied the injunction in July 2023. The acquisition closed October 2023. (T3_SECONDARY_RELIABLE, public court records; official, Microsoft press release)

### Ongoing Litigation

Microsoft is subject to various other legal proceedings as disclosed in its 10-K filings. (SEC 10-K FY2023, Commitments and Contingencies note) These include intellectual property disputes, employment matters, and contract disputes standard for a company of Microsoft's size and global operations.

### SAFE — Use These Constructions

- "According to Microsoft's FY2023 10-K, the Intelligent Cloud segment generated $87.9B in revenue." (T1_OFFICIAL)
- "Microsoft's stated mission is 'to empower every person and every organization on the planet to achieve more.'" (T1_OFFICIAL, with quotation marks)
- "The European Commission opened a formal antitrust investigation in 2023 into Microsoft's bundling of Teams with Microsoft 365. Microsoft has publicly stated it is cooperating with the investigation." (T3_SECONDARY_RELIABLE with attribution)
- "This analysis interprets Microsoft's brand archetype as primarily 'Sage' based on observed post-2014 communications." (T4_INFERRED, explicitly labeled)
- "Microsoft's CSS data shows Segoe UI as the primary typeface across microsoft.com." (observed on website)
- "Azure's market share is estimated at 22–23% of global cloud infrastructure by Synergy Research Group." (T3_SECONDARY_RELIABLE, named source)

### HEDGE — Use These Only With Qualification

- Any characterization of Microsoft's internal decision-making processes: must be labeled T4_INFERRED
- Any forward-looking statements about Azure growth rates or AI revenue: must be labeled (estimated) with basis
- Any characterization of the Activision acquisition's impact on the gaming market: use hedged language and label analysis as T4_INFERRED
- Any discussion of AI model quality comparisons: use verifiable benchmark data or label as T4_INFERRED

### DO NOT WRITE

- Any assertion that Microsoft engaged in anticompetitive conduct in current markets — the historical DOJ consent decree was concluded; asserting ongoing illegal conduct would require judicial determination this project does not possess
- Any characterization of Microsoft's AI or privacy practices as harmful to users without specific regulatory finding to cite
- Any attribution of internal decision-making to specific Microsoft executives without official disclosure source
- Any claim that Microsoft's products are less secure than competitors without independently verifiable security research citation
- Any description of Microsoft's data practices that goes beyond what Microsoft's own published privacy documentation states
