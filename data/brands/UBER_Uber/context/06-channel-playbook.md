## Cross-Channel Synergy (min 3 routes)

**Route 1 — The Uber One Subscription Funnel**
In-app promotional banner → push notification with personalized savings figure ("You could have saved $X this month with Uber One") → email with projected savings projection → Instagram retargeting → subscription conversion in app.
The funnel personalizes the savings argument using the individual user's actual ride and Eats history, making the conversion argument specific rather than generic. (T4_INFERRED, based on observed Uber One marketing patterns)

**Route 2 — Safety Report to Earned Media to Consumer Trust**
Annual Safety Report publication (owned media) → Twitter/X thread on key safety statistics → YouTube explainer on new safety feature improvements → LinkedIn post targeting HR and corporate travel managers → press pickup in transportation and technology media → ongoing in-app onboarding content highlighting safety features to new users.
A legal risk management obligation is converted into proactive brand trust content distributed across channels for months. (T4_INFERRED, based on observed Uber Safety Report publication and media pattern)

**Route 3 — Driver Recruitment Multi-Channel**
Local market driver supply gap identified → OOH advertising in target driver neighborhoods → Instagram and Facebook geo-targeted ads → in-app driver referral prompts to existing drivers → driver earnings story published on YouTube and LinkedIn → Uber newsroom feature on earnings flexibility → local press coverage.
Every driver earnings story also functions as consumer trust content — demonstrating that drivers choose Uber for meaningful reasons. (T4_INFERRED)

## Channel Prohibitions

1. **No real-time surge pricing promotion on brand channels** — Surge pricing is algorithmically triggered and not brand-controlled. Advertising during surge events or promoting surge as a brand feature generates consumer resentment. (T4_INFERRED)
2. **No unverified safety incident commentary before investigation completion** — Any social media response to a safety incident before facts are confirmed creates legal exposure and potentially contradicts a later official position. (T4_INFERRED; legal risk context)
3. **No political statements on gig worker classification from brand channels** — Worker classification debates are material to ongoing litigation and should be handled through government affairs channels, not brand social media. (T4_INFERRED)
4. **No direct competitor ridicule or naming in brand content** — Category-leader register does not include punching down at Lyft or DoorDash by name in consumer brand content. (T4_INFERRED)

## Crisis Protocol (min 3 scenarios)

**Scenario 1 — Serious Safety Incident Involving a Driver or Rider**
*Trigger*: Media reports of a serious safety incident (assault, serious accident) involving the Uber platform.
*Response Protocol*:
- Immediate: Express concern for those involved. No characterization of facts before investigation is complete.
- Within 24 hours: Confirm Uber's cooperation with law enforcement. Reference specific available safety features (share-your-trip, emergency SOS button).
- Post-investigation: Publish findings and any safety improvement commitments via Uber newsroom.
- Brand Voice: Serious, direct, compassionate. Never defensive in the initial response window.
- Prohibited: Statistical minimization in the immediate response ("most rides are safe") — reads as dismissive of specific harm.
(T4_INFERRED; SEC 10-K FY2023 safety risk disclosures)

**Scenario 2 — Widespread App Outage**
*Trigger*: Riders and drivers unable to request or accept trips due to app or server failure.
*Response Protocol*:
- Immediate: Acknowledge on Twitter/X within 30 minutes. Provide a status page link.
- Ongoing: Update every 30 minutes with factual status until resolution.
- Post-resolution: Rider goodwill gesture (promo code) via push notification and email.
- Brand Voice: Transparent, accountable, practical.
- Prohibited: Silence for more than 30 minutes while social media fills with complaints.
(T4_INFERRED, based on standard technology platform crisis protocol)

**Scenario 3 — Driver Pay or Classification Controversy**
*Trigger*: Public driver protests over pay rates; major media coverage of gig worker classification legislation adverse to Uber.
*Response Protocol*:
- Brand channel: Publish driver earnings data and any earnings guarantee programs with full methodology disclosure.
- Government affairs: Engage regulatory proceedings separately from brand communications.
- Brand Voice: Factual about earnings programs; acknowledge driver feedback shapes product decisions; avoid language that dismisses driver concerns.
- Prohibited: Brand content that directly comments on pending legislation; characterizing drivers as satisfied without specific supporting evidence.
(T4_INFERRED; SEC 10-K FY2023 labor classification risk disclosures)

## Steal Sheet

**1. The push notification is the most valuable owned channel.**
Uber's push notification channel reaches users at peak intent — when they are considering travel or food. A well-timed, personalized push converts at multiples of any paid channel. The discipline required is restraint: over-notifying trains users to ignore or disable push permanently. Notification frequency must be governed by user behavior triggers, not a marketing calendar.

**2. Safety content is underspent as a brand channel.**
Uber's Annual Safety Report is a significant transparency investment. But most consumers do not read PDFs. The safety features documented in those reports — share-your-trip, the emergency button, two-way ratings — should be demonstrated in short product-demo social content repeatedly and year-round, not released once in an annual document. Familiarity with safety features is itself a safety outcome.
