## Major Litigation and Regulatory History

**1. Driver Classification — United States (California AB5 and Proposition 22)**
California's AB5 (2019) extended employee classification protections to gig workers. Proposition 22 (November 2020), supported by Uber, Lyft, and DoorDash, passed by ballot measure and established that app-based ride-hailing and delivery drivers remain independent contractors in California under specific conditions. The California Supreme Court upheld Proposition 22's constitutionality in July 2024. (T3_SECONDARY_RELIABLE, public court records and press reporting; SEC 10-K FY2023)

**Note**: This project does not characterize Uber's driver compensation practices as adequate or inadequate. The proceedings reflect genuinely contested legal questions about platform-economy worker classification.

**2. UK Supreme Court Ruling — Aslam v Uber (2021)**
The UK Supreme Court ruled in Aslam v Uber [2021] UKSC 5 that a group of Uber drivers were "workers" — an intermediate UK employment classification — entitled to minimum wage and holiday pay during logged-on time. Uber has since provided UK drivers with worker classification, minimum wage, and holiday pay as required by UK law. (T3_SECONDARY_RELIABLE, public court records; SEC 10-K FY2023)

**3. EU Platform Work Directive (2024)**
The EU's Platform Work Directive, adopted in 2024, creates a legal presumption of employment for platform workers in EU member states. Implementation timelines vary by member state. Uber has disclosed this as a material risk factor in SEC filings. (SEC 10-K FY2023; T3_SECONDARY_RELIABLE, EU official communications)

**4. FTC Data Privacy Settlement (2018)**
The FTC reached a settlement with Uber in 2018 related to data privacy practices, including the handling of a 2016 data breach notification. Uber agreed to a 20-year consent decree requiring enhanced privacy and security practices. (T3_SECONDARY_RELIABLE, FTC public records; SEC 10-K FY2023)

**5. Greyball Tool (2017)**
In 2017, reports emerged that Uber had used a software tool ("Greyball") to identify and withhold ride service from individuals Uber believed were conducting regulatory enforcement operations in certain jurisdictions. Uber stated it has retired this use of the software. The DOJ conducted an investigation; no criminal charges were filed against the company. (T3_SECONDARY_RELIABLE, public reporting and regulatory records)

**Note**: This project does not characterize the Greyball matter as constituting proven intentional wrongdoing. The reported facts are documented from public sources only.

## PROJECT RISK ASSESSMENT

### Litigation Posture — How Carefully Must We Write About Uber?

**Risk Level**: HIGH

Uber is among the most litigated companies in the platform economy. Key sensitivity areas:

1. **Driver classification**: The most sensitive topic. Any characterization of driver compensation adequacy, working conditions, or classification requires reliance on established legal facts and explicit hedging. Do not compare driver earnings to living wage standards without citing specific study methodology.
2. **Historical conduct**: Uber's history includes high-profile incidents (2016 data breach, Greyball, early culture concerns under the Kalanick era). These are reportable from documented public sources with careful framing that avoids editorializing about the intent of individuals.
3. **Safety claims**: Uber's published Annual Safety Reports are self-reported data. Statistics from those reports must be attributed as such — not presented as independently verified facts.

## Writing Risk Guide

### SAFE — Write freely with source citation

- Revenue, Gross Bookings, MAPC, and trip count data from SEC 10-K with explicit citation
- Description of Uber's product portfolio with official attribution
- Description of legal proceedings as disclosed in SEC 10-K (with explicit citation)
- The Aslam v Uber UK Supreme Court ruling and its stated outcome (T3_SECONDARY_RELIABLE with citation)
- Description of Proposition 22 and its stated result (T3_SECONDARY_RELIABLE with citation)
- Uber's stated safety features (share-your-trip, emergency button, background checks) with official attribution
- Uber's stated EV transition commitments with official citation

### HEDGE — Write with explicit hedging language

- Driver earnings adequacy: "Uber has published average hourly earnings figures for drivers in select markets. Independent analyses of net driver earnings after vehicle costs reach varying conclusions. This analysis does not make a determination on the adequacy of driver compensation."
- Safety record: "Uber publishes an Annual Safety Report with incident statistics. These figures are self-reported by Uber and have not been independently audited by this project."
- Greyball and historical conduct: "According to public reporting and SEC disclosures, Uber acknowledged [specific fact]. The company has stated [remediation action taken]. No criminal charges were filed."
- Autonomous vehicle timeline: "Analysts have noted the potential long-term impact of autonomous vehicles on Uber's driver supply model. This analysis does not predict the commercial timeline of autonomous vehicle deployment."

### DO NOT WRITE

- Any characterization of Uber's driver classification as confirmed illegal or as exploitation, without citing a specific court ruling using those precise terms
- Any characterization of the Greyball tool as constituting criminal conduct (no criminal charges were filed by DOJ)
- Any assertion about specific named individuals' personal conduct at Uber without citing active legal proceedings or official statements from those proceedings
- Any claim that Uber's published safety statistics are fabricated or materially incomplete without a regulatory finding to that effect
- Any comparison of Uber driver earnings to a specific living wage standard without citing the study and its full methodology

## ESG Overview

**Environmental**: Uber has committed to becoming a zero-emission platform by 2040 in the US, Canada, and Europe, meaning all rides on the Uber platform will be completed in electric vehicles. Uber Green (electric and hybrid vehicle option) is available in select markets. Driver EV transition incentives are offered in certain markets. (official, Uber ESG Report; uber.com/sustainability)

**Social**: Uber's published safety commitments include the Annual US Safety Report, global background check programs, and the Uber Safety Toolkit. The Uber Pro loyalty program offers driver benefits at higher rating and acceptance rate thresholds. Earnings guarantee pilots have been announced in select markets. (official, Uber Safety Report; uber.com/drive)

**Governance**: Standard US public company governance. Board includes independent directors. Dara Khosrowshahi is CEO. Executive compensation and board composition disclosed in annual proxy filings. (T1_OFFICIAL, SEC DEF 14A)
