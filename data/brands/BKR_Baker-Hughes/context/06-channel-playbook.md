## Cross-Channel Synergy Routes

**Route 1: Technical Paper → Website → Conference**
An SPE technical paper documenting a field performance outcome is published with BKR as co-author → abstracted as a case study on bakerhughes.com → used as a demonstration anchor at ADIPEC or OTC → referenced in sales conversations for the subsequent 12-24 months. This route converts a single field performance event into a multi-year sales asset. (T4_ESTIMATED)

**Route 2: LinkedIn Executive Post → Website Long-Form → Media Pickup**
CEO or CTO publishes a LinkedIn commentary on an energy transition topic (LNG demand, CCS technology, methane regulation) → links to a full white paper on bakerhughes.com → energy trade media (Hart's E&P, Upstream, Rigzone) picks up the perspective → amplifies to a broader industry audience not reachable through direct BKR channels. (T4_ESTIMATED)

**Route 3: Earnings Call → IR Website → LinkedIn**
Quarterly earnings call introduces a strategic narrative (IET growth, energy transition revenue milestone, new market entry) → full transcript published on IR website → key metrics and quotes excerpted for LinkedIn posts targeting investor and analyst audiences → reinforces the strategic story between quarterly reporting cycles. (T4_ESTIMATED)

## Channel Prohibitions

1. **Do not use social channels for premature contract announcements** — Customer confidentiality requirements at NOC and major IOC accounts require explicit customer approval before public announcement of contract wins. (T4_ESTIMATED, standard industry practice)
2. **Do not publish speculative technology claims** — Performance claims for products not yet in commercial deployment must be clearly labeled as development-stage or laboratory-validated, not commercial-ready. (T4_ESTIMATED)
3. **Do not use Instagram or TikTok as primary B2B channels** — The core buyer demographic does not use these platforms in a professional discovery context; resources allocated here have low ROI for BKR's customer base. (T4_ESTIMATED)
4. **Do not allow inconsistent segment language across channels** — OFSE and IET must be consistently named and defined across all channels; inconsistent segment labeling confuses analysts and investors. (T4_ESTIMATED)
5. **Do not respond to competitor comparisons on social media** — Public social engagement with competitive claims creates unnecessary reputational risk; competitive positioning belongs in direct sales conversations and RFP responses. (T4_ESTIMATED)

## Crisis Communication Protocol

### Scenario 1: Major Operational Incident (Well Blowout, Pipeline Failure involving BKR Equipment)
**Response protocol**:
- Immediate: Activate legal and communications review before any public statement (estimated, standard protocol)
- Within 24 hours: Issue a factual statement confirming awareness of the incident, BKR's cooperation with investigation, and commitment to safety — without admitting liability or characterizing causes
- Channel: Formal press release via bakerhughes.com newsroom and wire services; CEO or authorized spokesperson only
- Prohibited: Social media commentary, speculation on causes, any statement that could be construed as accepting fault before investigation completion
- Tone: Grave, factual, human — acknowledge the seriousness without characterizing events not yet established by investigation

### Scenario 2: Regulatory Investigation or Legal Action
**Response protocol**:
- Disclose material legal proceedings per SEC 10-K Risk Factor and Note to Financial Statements requirements (SEC reporting obligation)
- Public communications limited to legally reviewed statements aligned with SEC disclosures
- Do not use social media to defend the company against regulatory allegations; direct media inquiries to official spokesperson
- Monitor and do not engage with speculative media coverage that mischaracterizes disclosed proceedings

### Scenario 3: Energy Transition Criticism (ESG / Climate Advocacy Attack)
**Response protocol**:
- Respond with factual data from BKR's sustainability report and IET segment disclosures — emissions reduction metrics, CCS installations, methane detection technology
- Do not engage in political debate about energy policy or climate science; redirect to technology capability and operational data
- Use CEO LinkedIn platform for measured, data-supported response to substantive criticism
- Acknowledge the energy transition challenge; frame BKR's role as part of the solution rather than defensively justifying oil and gas business

## Steal Sheet — 3 Transferable Principles

**1. Make the conference the closing mechanism, not the awareness mechanism.**
Baker Hughes generates awareness through digital content (LinkedIn, white papers, case studies) but closes major contracts through face-to-face technical engagement at ADIPEC, SPE, Gastech, and OTC. Digital channels are engineered to drive conference meeting scheduling, not to directly convert. Transferable: in high-value B2B sales, design digital content to generate qualified inbound interest for in-person events rather than attempting to close digitally.

**2. Let the IR channel carry the strategic narrative.**
Baker Hughes's most explicit and detailed articulation of its strategic direction — IET growth, energy transition investment, segment revenue targets — appears in earnings call transcripts and investor presentations, not in marketing materials. These documents are publicly available and read by sophisticated audiences. Transferable: treat IR communications as a strategic brand asset, not just a financial compliance obligation; the narrative you build in investor communications shapes how analysts, media, and sophisticated customers understand your strategy.

**3. Build channel-specific technical credibility before commercial messaging.**
Petroleum engineers who encounter BKR through SPE papers or conference technical sessions have been primed with engineering credibility before they enter a commercial conversation. The sequence — technical credibility first, commercial engagement second — is embedded in the channel architecture. Transferable: map the channel sequence your buyers actually follow and ensure credibility-building content precedes commercial content at every step.
