## Major Litigation and Regulatory Proceedings

Baker Hughes discloses material legal proceedings in its SEC 10-K annual reports under the Legal Proceedings section. As of the most recent available public disclosures:

- **Environmental proceedings**: BKR discloses ongoing remediation obligations related to historical manufacturing sites and operational activities, consistent with disclosures by other large industrial companies. The 10-K states these are not expected to have a material adverse effect on financial condition in the aggregate. (SEC 10-K FY2023, Legal Proceedings and Note to Financial Statements)
- **Commercial disputes**: Standard oilfield services contract disputes arise in the ordinary course of business. BKR's 10-K acknowledges certain pending commercial claims without characterizing their outcome. (SEC 10-K FY2023)
- **FCPA and anti-corruption**: The oilfield services sector has historically faced Foreign Corrupt Practices Act (FCPA) scrutiny given the prevalence of NOC customers in jurisdictions with elevated corruption risk. BKR's predecessor companies have had FCPA-related proceedings in the past; current status is disclosed in active SEC filings. Researchers should consult the most recent 10-K Legal Proceedings section for current disclosures. (SEC 10-K, Legal Proceedings — consult current filing)

> This analysis does not characterize any disclosed legal proceeding as indicative of wrongdoing. All characterizations of legal matters are based on the company's own public disclosures.

## Writing Risk Guide

### SAFE — Write Freely
- Any claim directly sourced from BKR's SEC filings (10-K, 10-Q, 8-K, proxy)
- Revenue, segment, and geographic data as disclosed in official financial statements
- Product and service descriptions as presented on bakerhughes.com
- Publicly announced contract wins, technology milestones, and partnership announcements
- Environmental and sustainability data from BKR's official sustainability report
- Descriptions of the oilfield services and industrial technology markets BKR operates in

### HEDGE — Write with Attribution and Qualification
- Market share estimates: Label explicitly as "(estimated)" and cite the basis (analyst report, company disclosure, etc.)
- IET segment growth trajectory and energy transition positioning: Frame as stated company strategy ("Baker Hughes has stated its intention to..."), not as confirmed outcomes
- Competitive comparisons: Use metric-based language only ("BKR's IET revenue of approximately $X exceeded [competitor]'s comparable segment by...") — never "BKR is better than..."
- Historical FCPA or regulatory proceedings: Describe only what is in official public records; do not editorialize about causes or characterize outcomes not formally established
- Energy transition market size projections: Attribute to named third-party research sources; do not present as BKR's own forecasts

### DO NOT WRITE
- Any assertion that BKR or its personnel acted with improper intent in any disclosed legal or regulatory matter
- Characterizations of BKR's environmental record using pejorative language not found in regulatory agency formal findings
- Statements about BKR's customer relationships that could be construed as revealing confidential commercial information
- Comparative claims about BKR's technology that are not directly supported by publicly available technical data
- Any financial projections presented as BKR's guidance unless directly sourced from official BKR investor communications
