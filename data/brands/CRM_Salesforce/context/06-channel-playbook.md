## Cross-Channel Synergy (min 3 routes)

**Route 1 — The Annual Research Report Launch**
State of Sales / Service / Marketing publication flow:
Press embargo and analyst briefings with Salesforce Research team → publication on blog plus gated landing page → LinkedIn article and social data-clip posts → Twitter thread of top statistics → podcast episode discussing key findings → Trailhead module connecting report findings to platform capability → PR pickup and industry citations → SEO traffic from referenced statistics for 12+ months.
A single research investment generates six or more months of content, earned media, and lead generation. (T4_INFERRED, based on observed Salesforce Research distribution patterns)

**Route 2 — Dreamforce to Year-Round Community**
Dreamforce (September) → session recordings uploaded to YouTube and Salesforce+ → key product announcements become blog posts → Trailhead modules added for newly announced features → Trailblazer community discussion threads → Salesforce+ on-demand library → admin podcast episode recapping highlights → LinkedIn feature content running through Q4.
The conference is not a one-week event — it seeds approximately three months of downstream content. (T4_INFERRED, based on observed post-Dreamforce content patterns)

**Route 3 — Customer Success Story Amplification**
Customer agrees to case study → video testimonial produced → blog post published → LinkedIn post with customer executive quote → Gartner Peer Insights review requested → customer invited to speak at Dreamforce or a regional event → Trailblazer community success profile created.
A single customer success story, properly activated, yields seven or more content assets across channels over 6–12 months. (T4_INFERRED, based on observed B2B content amplification patterns)

## Channel Prohibitions

1. **No unvetted customer quotes without written approval** — Customer quotes in brand content require explicit approval from the customer's communications team. Using unapproved attributions creates legal and relationship risk. (T4_INFERRED, standard B2B marketing practice)
2. **No securities-relevant information via social channels without proper disclosure** — Salesforce executives with access to material non-public information must comply with SEC Regulation FD. Social posts by executives are subject to the same disclosure requirements as press releases. (T4_INFERRED; SEC Regulation FD)
3. **No political position statements on brand channels without executive governance** — Salesforce has historically engaged on social issues via Marc Benioff personally. Brand channel political content requires careful governance to manage stakeholder relationships across global markets. (T4_INFERRED)
4. **No competitive fear-based messaging in brand content** — Tactics relying on fear, uncertainty, or doubt against named competitors belong in sales enablement contexts, not brand-level social or content marketing. (T4_INFERRED)

## Crisis Protocol (min 3 scenarios)

**Scenario 1 — Platform Outage (Salesforce.com Downtime)**
*Trigger*: Customer-reported inability to access Salesforce, confirmed via trust.salesforce.com.
*Response Protocol*:
- Immediate: Update trust.salesforce.com with factual incident status (Salesforce maintains a public system status page — official, trust.salesforce.com).
- Within 1 hour: Twitter/X acknowledgment from @Salesforce with link to status page.
- Post-resolution: Root cause analysis published on trust.salesforce.com.
- Brand Voice: Technical, transparent, specific. "Trust is our #1 value" must be demonstrated in the response, not merely cited.
- Prohibited: Deflecting to customer configuration issues before investigation is complete; delayed initial acknowledgment.
(T4_INFERRED; Salesforce trust.salesforce.com is an official public commitment)

**Scenario 2 — Data Security or Privacy Incident**
*Trigger*: Enterprise customer data accessed without authorization via the Salesforce platform or a third-party AppExchange application.
*Response Protocol*:
- Immediate: Engage legal, security, and communications teams simultaneously. No public statement before scope is confirmed.
- Notification timeline: Comply with GDPR Article 33 (72-hour notification to supervisory authority) and applicable US state breach notification laws.
- Brand Voice: Direct, responsible, and specific about scope and remediation. Lead with "what we did to protect you" and "what you should do now."
- Prohibited: Characterizing the incident as minor before investigation completes; providing only partial scope in initial communications.
(T4_INFERRED; GDPR Article 33; SEC 10-K FY2024 cybersecurity risk disclosures)

**Scenario 3 — CEO Public Statement Controversy**
*Trigger*: Marc Benioff makes a public statement on a social or political issue that generates significant negative response from customers, investors, or employees.
*Response Protocol*:
- Brand channel: Maintain normal editorial calendar; do not amplify the controversial statement from brand accounts.
- If material to investors: Investor relations team activates per standard IR protocol.
- Brand Voice: Maintain clear separation between Benioff's personal public voice (which has historically been values-forward and sometimes controversial) and Salesforce's official brand channel voice.
- Note: Benioff's public persona as a vocal CEO is an established and accepted element of Salesforce's brand character. The crisis response should reinforce the personal/corporate channel distinction rather than disavow the association entirely.
(T4_INFERRED, based on observed history of Benioff public statements and Salesforce communications management)

## Steal Sheet

**1. Own a channel no competitor can easily replicate.**
Trailhead is a learning channel that Salesforce owns and controls. Competitors can match product features; they cannot quickly replicate a learning community with millions of certified users. In B2B, owning a community platform is more defensible than owning a media budget.

**2. The conference as content production facility.**
Dreamforce generates more content in one week than most B2B companies produce in a year. Session recordings become YouTube videos, LinkedIn posts, podcast episodes, and blog content for months afterward. Salesforce+ reflects this model taken to its logical conclusion: the conference becomes a streaming platform with a permanent on-demand library. (official, Salesforce+)
