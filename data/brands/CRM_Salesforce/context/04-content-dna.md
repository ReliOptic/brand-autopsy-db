## Hook Patterns (min 4)

**Hook 1 — The Trailblazer Origin Story**
Structure: A specific person, in a specific situation, who used Salesforce or Trailhead to transform their career or company.
Example format: A career-changer who discovered Trailhead and moved from a non-technical role into a Salesforce admin position. (T4_INFERRED, based on observed Trailblazer story format on salesforce.com)
Why it works: The career transformation narrative converts product education into personal aspiration. It makes the platform feel accessible and life-changing simultaneously.

**Hook 2 — The Industry Data Lead**
Structure: Open with a striking statistic from Salesforce Research, then connect to platform or community.
Example format: A headline statistic about quota attainment rates from the State of Sales report, followed by the behaviors observed among top performers. (T4_INFERRED, based on State of Sales content patterns)
Why it works: Salesforce Research data is cited by journalists and analysts, making the hook simultaneously earned media content and brand authority positioning.

**Hook 3 — The AI Capability Before/After**
Structure: Show a workflow — a manual task performed by a human, then the same task completed by an Agentforce agent.
Example format: A customer service ticket resolution time comparison — manual process vs. AI-assisted. (T4_INFERRED, based on Agentforce launch content patterns)
Why it works: Tangible time savings in a specific workflow is the most effective enterprise software proof point. It bypasses abstract AI claims and goes directly to outcome.

**Hook 4 — The Dreamforce Countdown or Release Announcement**
Structure: Build anticipation for major announcements around Dreamforce (September) or quarterly platform releases (Spring, Summer, Winter).
Example format: "Spring '25 is here. What's new for Sales teams?" with feature summary. (official, Salesforce release communication format)
Why it works: Dreamforce is among the largest technology conferences globally. Pre-event and post-event content leverages a massive earned attention peak that competitors cannot match without a comparable event.

**Hook 5 — The CEO Thought Leadership**
Structure: Marc Benioff commentary on enterprise technology, AI ethics, or broader societal issues generates news cycle coverage.
Example format: Benioff commentary on AI regulation, stakeholder capitalism, or climate at Davos or in public interviews. (T3_SECONDARY_RELIABLE, observed media coverage of Benioff public statements)
Why it works: A CEO with an established public voice on technology ethics is a brand asset that cannot be purchased. It generates earned media and positions Salesforce as a company with a point of view.

## Tone & Mood Guide (4 Situations)

| Situation | Tone | Example |
|-----------|------|---------|
| **Trailblazer success story** | Warm, proud, specific — celebrate the individual's achievement | "Maria didn't just implement Salesforce. She rebuilt how her entire company thinks about customers." |

| **Trust or security messaging** | Calm, transparent, direct — grounded in Salesforce's #1 value | "We take data security seriously. Here is exactly what happened, what we have done, and what you should know." |

## Content Prohibitions (min 5)

1. **No direct attacks on specific competitors by name in brand content** — Salesforce's category leadership position means the brand does not punch down. Battle cards and comparisons exist for sales enablement, not public brand content. (T4_INFERRED from observed brand content patterns)
2. **No content implying enterprise implementations are simple or instant** — Salesforce implementations can be complex multi-month projects. Overpromising simplicity creates a trust gap with the admin and CIO personas who know the operational reality. (T4_INFERRED)
3. **No unqualified AI performance claims** — AI capability claims must be grounded in specific, demonstrable use cases with appropriate accuracy caveats. Broad claims about AI accuracy or reliability without qualification risk regulatory and credibility exposure. (T4_INFERRED, legal and brand risk)
4. **No forward-looking financial statements in brand content** — Revenue guidance and financial projections are governed by SEC Regulation FD. Brand content must not contain language that could be construed as financial guidance outside of official SEC filings and earnings calls. (T4_INFERRED; SEC regulatory requirement)
5. **No culture claims without measurable backing** — Salesforce's Ohana, equality, and sustainability claims must be grounded in disclosed programs and measurable data. Culture content not backed by actual programs (Pledge 1%, pay equity audits, representation data) risks credibility loss with sophisticated enterprise audiences. (T4_INFERRED; SEC 10-K ESG disclosures)
6. **No oversimplification of customer data handling** — Given GDPR, CCPA, and AI governance concerns, Salesforce must be precise about what data the platform holds, who owns it, and how AI models interact with it. (T4_INFERRED, legal risk)

## Steal Sheet

**1. The learning platform IS content marketing.**
Trailhead is simultaneously Salesforce's community, its customer retention tool, and its content marketing engine. Every module teaches Salesforce while generating brand loyalty and certification lock-in. The most powerful content strategy is one where the content itself is the product — useful independent of a sale.

**2. Make your research the citation everyone uses.**
The State of Sales report is cited in competitor content, journalism, and academic research. When a brand's data becomes the industry standard reference, every citation is a brand impression — earned, not paid. Owning the research agenda in your category is a long-term competitive moat.
