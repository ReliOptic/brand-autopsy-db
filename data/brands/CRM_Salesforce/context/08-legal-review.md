## Major Litigation and Regulatory History

**1. Securities Litigation Related to Acquisitions**
Following the acquisition of Slack Technologies in 2021 ($27.7B), Salesforce faced investor scrutiny around integration progress and Slack's revenue contribution relative to acquisition-period representations. Various securities-related claims have been made by shareholders in connection with forward-looking statements about acquired asset performance. Salesforce has addressed these claims in its SEC 10-K disclosures. (SEC 10-K FY2024; T1_OFFICIAL for disclosed litigation; T3_SECONDARY_RELIABLE for financial press coverage)

**Note**: This project does not characterize Salesforce's acquisition-period statements as having been inaccurate or misleading. SEC disclosures are the authoritative source for the current litigation status.

**2. Employment and Workplace Culture Claims**
Salesforce has faced employment-related claims, including assertions related to gender pay equity. Salesforce has published annual Equal Pay Assessments and has stated publicly that it addresses pay gaps identified through these audits. (official, Salesforce Equal Pay assessment publications; SEC 10-K FY2024) No specific judicial liability determination is cited in this document.

**3. Platform and AppExchange Competitive Conduct Questions**
As Salesforce's AppExchange has grown and Salesforce itself offers competing applications on the same marketplace, questions have been raised in industry discussions about whether platform operators who compete with their own app partners face scrutiny regarding marketplace practices. To date, no major regulatory proceeding targeting Salesforce's AppExchange practices has concluded with a formal adverse determination. (T3_SECONDARY_RELIABLE, industry press; SEC 10-K FY2024 risk factors)

**4. Data Privacy and GDPR Compliance**
As a processor of enterprise customer data across the EU and globally, Salesforce is subject to GDPR, CCPA, and expanding global data protection regulation. Salesforce maintains a Data Processing Addendum (DPA), publishes its privacy commitments publicly, and has faced data processing inquiries in EU contexts. (official, salesforce.com/privacy; T3_SECONDARY_RELIABLE)

## PROJECT RISK ASSESSMENT

### Litigation Posture — How Carefully Must We Write About Salesforce?

**Risk Level**: MODERATE

Salesforce is a large, well-resourced public company with active legal monitoring of its public representation. Key sensitivity areas:

1. **AI capability claims**: Agentforce and Einstein AI claims are a high-sensitivity area given the regulatory and market scrutiny of enterprise AI. Analysis must accurately reflect what Salesforce has officially stated versus what can be analytically inferred or projected.
2. **Acquisition valuations and integration**: Discussion of the Slack ($27.7B), Tableau ($15.7B), and MuleSoft ($6.5B) acquisitions must rely on public financial records. Characterizing these acquisitions as having succeeded or underperformed commercially requires grounding in official financial disclosures.
3. **Workplace culture**: Employment-related matters should be reported as disclosed in SEC filings without editorial characterization of individuals or internal culture.

## Writing Risk Guide

### SAFE — Write freely with source citation

- Revenue, RPO, and financial metrics directly from SEC 10-K with explicit citation
- Cloud product portfolio description with official attribution
- Trailhead, Dreamforce, Ohana, and AppExchange descriptions with official attribution
- Description of litigation as reported in SEC 10-K and financial press with source cited
- Salesforce's stated values (Trust, Customer Success, Innovation, Equality, Sustainability) with official citation
- The 1-1-1 model and Pledge 1% program description with official citation
- Agentforce description based on official launch materials and official salesforce.com content

### HEDGE — Write with explicit hedging language

- Assessments of whether Salesforce's acquisitions have delivered expected value: "Salesforce's Slack acquisition has received mixed analyst assessments regarding revenue contribution relative to acquisition price" not "Salesforce overpaid for Slack"
- Competitive position claims in AI: "Salesforce has positioned Agentforce as its enterprise AI agent platform, with early customer deployments announced in late 2024" not "Salesforce leads the enterprise AI market"
- Culture and equality commitments: "Salesforce has published annual equal pay assessments and stated commitments to closing identified pay gaps" not "Salesforce has achieved full pay equity"
- Marc Benioff personal statements: attribute to Benioff personally, not to Salesforce as a corporate policy, unless the statement appears in official corporate communications

### DO NOT WRITE

- Any assertion that Salesforce's financial disclosures were inaccurate or misleading, without a concluded regulatory finding
- Any characterization that Salesforce's workplace practices constitute illegal discrimination — employment litigation in this area remains active and contested
- Any AI performance claims that go beyond Salesforce's officially published specifications and documented customer case studies
- Any assertion that AppExchange practices constitute anticompetitive conduct as an established factual matter
- Any claim about future financial performance, acquisition ROI, or stock price direction

## ESG Overview

**Environmental**: Salesforce has committed to net-zero greenhouse gas emissions across its value chain by 2040, with an interim target of 50% absolute emissions reduction by 2030. Data center efficiency and renewable energy procurement programs are ongoing. (official, Salesforce Sustainability reporting)

**Social**: The 1-1-1 model (Pledge 1%): 1% equity, 1% product, 1% employee time donated. As of Salesforce's most recent sustainability reporting, the company has donated over $600 million in grants, approximately 9.5 million hours of community service, and product access to more than 67,000 nonprofits and educational institutions. (official, Salesforce.org and Salesforce Sustainability Report)

**Governance**: Salesforce has a Racial Equality and Justice Task Force, publishes annual workforce representation data, and conducts annual pay equity audits. Marc Benioff holds both CEO and Chairman roles, which has been a noted governance discussion point in proxy advisory contexts. (official, Salesforce proxy filings; SEC DEF 14A)
