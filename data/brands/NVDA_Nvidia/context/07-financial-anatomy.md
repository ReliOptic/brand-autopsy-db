## 2. Revenue Anatomy

### 2.1 Revenue Trajectory — Five-Year Arc

The following figures are sourced from Nvidia's SEC 10-K filings. (T1_OFFICIAL)

| Fiscal Year | Total Revenue | YoY Growth | Primary Growth Driver |
|-------------|--------------|------------|----------------------|
| **FY2021** | $16.7 billion | +53% | Gaming (COVID PC upgrade cycle), Data Center growth beginning |
| **FY2022** | $26.9 billion | +61% | Gaming peak; Data Center ($10.6B); Mellanox integration |
| **FY2023** | $26.97 billion | +0.2% | Gaming normalization post-COVID; Data Center $15.0B |
| **FY2024** | $60.92 billion | +122% | AI training GPU demand (H100); Data Center $47.5B |
| **FY2025** | $130.5 billion | +114% | H100/H200 + Blackwell ramp; Data Center $115.2B |

(All figures: T1_OFFICIAL, SEC 10-K filings for respective fiscal years)

**Interpretation**: The revenue trajectory from FY2023 ($27.0B) to FY2025 ($130.5B) — a ~383% increase in two fiscal years — is among the fastest top-line growth episodes ever recorded by a company of Nvidia's scale. This growth is directly attributable to enterprise AI training demand for H100 GPUs, as disclosed in segment reporting. (T4_INFERRED from segment data; T3_SECONDARY_RELIABLE, multiple analyst commentaries)

## 4. Investment Structure

| Metric | FY2025 | FY2024 | FY2023 |
|--------|--------|--------|--------|

**Interpretation**: R&D as a percentage of revenue has declined as revenue has grown exponentially, but absolute R&D dollars have increased significantly. (T4_INFERRED from comparative analysis) The Blackwell architecture (B100/B200/GB200) development costs are embedded in this R&D spend. (T4_INFERRED)

### 4.2 Selling, General & Administrative (SG&A)

Per Nvidia's FY2025 10-K (T1_OFFICIAL):

| Metric | FY2025 | FY2024 |
|--------|--------|--------|
| **SG&A Expense** | ~$2.6 billion | ~$2.2 billion |
| **SG&A as % of Revenue** | ~2.0% | ~3.6% |

**Interpretation**: Nvidia's SG&A as a percentage of revenue is exceptionally low for a company of its scale, reflecting the demand-driven nature of the current AI GPU market — customers are seeking Nvidia, reducing traditional sales and marketing cost requirements. (T4_INFERRED)

### 4.3 Capital Expenditures

Per Nvidia's FY2025 10-K cash flow statement (T1_OFFICIAL):

| Metric | FY2025 | FY2024 |
|--------|--------|--------|
| **CapEx** | ~$1.3 billion | ~$1.1 billion |
| **CapEx as % of Revenue** | ~1.0% | ~1.8% |

**Interpretation**: Nvidia's CapEx intensity is extremely low because it is a fabless semiconductor company — manufacturing is outsourced to TSMC (Taiwan Semiconductor Manufacturing Company). Nvidia does not own fabs. This fabless model provides capital efficiency at the cost of manufacturing dependency concentration risk (explicitly disclosed in the 10-K). (T1_OFFICIAL, Risk Factors)

## 9. Brand-Finance Nexus — How Brand Equity Drives Financial Performance

The relationship between Nvidia's brand strength and financial performance is observable through four specific mechanisms:

**Mechanism 1 — Pricing Power via CUDA Ecosystem**
Nvidia's H100 GPU has been reported by multiple analysts at $25,000–$40,000 per card (spot market) during FY2023–FY2024 supply constraints, compared to manufacturing cost estimates that are significantly lower. (T3_SECONDARY_RELIABLE, analyst pricing estimates) This pricing premium is sustained by CUDA ecosystem switching costs, not by hardware superiority alone. (T4_INFERRED)

**Mechanism 2 — Demand Signal: Jensen Huang's GTC Announcements**
Product announcements at GTC have observable short-term impacts on order pipelines, as discussed in subsequent earnings calls. Hyperscaler capital expenditure announcements (Microsoft, Google, Amazon) have referenced GPU supply in investor calls, creating a demand visibility signal. (T2_PRIMARY_RELIABLE, hyperscaler earnings call transcripts)

**Mechanism 3 — SG&A Efficiency via Brand Pull**
Nvidia's SG&A at ~2.0% of revenue (FY2025) is exceptionally low because the brand's reputation, CUDA ecosystem, and GTC conference generate inbound enterprise demand without requiring heavy sales infrastructure. (T4_INFERRED from SG&A-to-revenue ratio analysis)

**Mechanism 4 — Stock Multiple Premium**
Nvidia's price-to-earnings multiple has consistently exceeded semiconductor sector averages, reflecting investor valuation of the CUDA ecosystem moat as a durable competitive advantage. (T4_INFERRED — market pricing observation, not investment advice)
