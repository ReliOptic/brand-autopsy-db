## Channel Prohibitions

1. **Do not break character on Parks social channels**: @DisneyParks social content never acknowledges operational problems, negative reviews, or cast member disputes in the consumer voice. Issues are handled via separate corporate communications channels. (T4_INFERRED from observed @DisneyParks account behavior)
2. **No pricing discussion in aspirational content contexts**: Parks pricing is disclosed on booking pages but never featured in brand marketing content, which would disrupt the "magic" frame. (T4_INFERRED)
3. **ESPN voice does not bleed into Disney family brand channels**: Sports authority tone is operationally separated from family brand warmth. (T4_INFERRED)
4. **No franchise spoilers in brand channels before embargo lift**: Disney enforces strict embargo protocols for franchise content; official channels do not confirm or deny spoilers. (observed from Disney communications practice)

## Crisis Protocol

### Scenario 1 — Park Operational Incident (Guest Safety Event)

**Immediate response (0-4 hours)**: Corporate communications issues factual statement acknowledging the incident, confirming guest safety priority, and stating that an investigation is underway. Consumer-facing social channels go quiet on non-essential content. (T4_INFERRED from observed Disney crisis communications practice)

**Short-term (24-72 hours)**: Official statement updated with factual findings as available. Media inquiries routed to corporate communications, not social. Disney Parks account may post content acknowledging guests and expressing care without disclosing legally sensitive details.

**Recovery**: Return to regular content programming once the incident is resolved and communications team has cleared. No acknowledgment in brand marketing content of the incident; operational communications separated.

### Scenario 2 — Franchise Content Controversy (Audience Backlash)

**Response posture**: Disney does not typically respond to fan criticism of creative decisions via official social channels. Franchises are managed through creative leadership statements if response is warranted (e.g., Lucasfilm president addressing Star Wars fan concerns). Consumer social channels continue regular programming. (T4_INFERRED from observed Disney franchise management)

### Scenario 3 — Corporate Governance / Political Issue

**Response posture**: Disney's corporate communications respond to governance matters through official press statements and SEC disclosures as required. Consumer-facing channels are insulated from political messaging except in cases where Disney has made a formal public stance decision at the executive level. (T4_INFERRED from observed Disney corporate communications)

## Steal Sheet — 3 Transferable Principles

**1. Build the synergy map before the channel plan.**
Disney's channel strategy derives its power from the flywheel connecting theatrical, streaming, parks, and merchandise — not from optimizing any single channel in isolation. Transferable: before assigning channel budgets, map the revenue flywheel: how does each channel generate demand for others? Channels that feed the flywheel get investment priority over channels that operate in isolation.

**2. Separate the brand voice from the crisis voice.**
Disney maintains operationally separate tones for consumer brand content and corporate crisis communications. The "magical" brand voice is never deployed in a crisis context, and the crisis communications register never appears in consumer channels. Transferable: build two separate communication templates — one for brand promotion, one for operational incidents — and explicitly separate the teams and channels responsible for each.

**3. Let UGC do the reach work; do the curation work.**
Disney's parks generate millions of pieces of organic social content annually at zero incremental cost. Disney's role is curation and amplification, not generation. Transferable: design your product or service experience to generate one specific, predictable social moment. Then build a curation and amplification process, not a content creation budget.
