## Competitive Context Note

Ameren Corporation operates as a regulated electric and natural gas utility with state-granted service territory exclusivity in Missouri and Illinois. Within its service territories, Ameren does not face direct competition for customer acquisition. The competitive dynamics that are strategically relevant to Ameren occur in three distinct arenas:

1. **Capital markets competition**: Ameren competes with peer regulated utilities for institutional investor capital; valuation differentials affect cost of capital for infrastructure investment.
2. **Regulatory narrative competition**: In rate cases and policy proceedings, Ameren's track record and narrative compete against stakeholder advocates who may argue for different investment priorities or rate structures.
3. **Talent competition**: Ameren competes with peer utilities, energy companies, and infrastructure firms for engineering, technical, and trades talent.

The four primary peers analyzed below are selected based on: comparable regulated utility business model, overlapping investor base, similar geographic/regulatory context, and public data availability. (T4_INFERRED, based on Ameren's own peer group disclosures in proxy filings — DEF 14A — and analyst coverage)

### Competitive Positioning (T4_INFERRED)

Ameren is significantly larger than Evergy by revenue, customer count, and capital deployment. Both serve Missouri, creating a geographic overlap that is relevant in state legislative and regulatory proceedings: both companies participate in Missouri energy policy debates. Evergy's western Missouri territory (Kansas City area) does not overlap with Ameren's eastern Missouri territory (St. Louis area), so there is no direct service territory competition.

From an investor standpoint, Ameren's higher EPS growth guidance and larger capital program indicate (T4_INFERRED) a higher growth profile within the regulated utility peer group, though investment returns depend on regulatory outcomes.

## Competitor 3 — CenterPoint Energy, Inc. (CNP)

**Ticker**: CNP | **Sector**: Utilities — Multi-Utilities | **Service Territory**: Indiana, Ohio, Texas, Louisiana, Oklahoma, Arkansas, Minnesota

### Business Profile

CenterPoint Energy provides electric and natural gas utility services across eight states, with approximately 7 million metered customers. (T1_OFFICIAL, CenterPoint 10-K FY2023) CenterPoint's electric service territory is primarily Houston, Texas (ERCOT market) and Indiana.

### Competitive Positioning (T4_INFERRED)

CenterPoint is larger than Ameren by customer count but faces materially different operational risk from Gulf Coast weather exposure. CenterPoint's Houston Electric segment (ERCOT) was subject to significant regulatory and public scrutiny following Hurricane Beryl in 2024, which caused extended outages in the Houston area. (T3_SECONDARY_RELIABLE, Reuters, Houston Chronicle) Ameren's Midwest service territory carries lower hurricane/tropical storm risk, though it faces significant ice storm and severe thunderstorm exposure, particularly in Missouri and Illinois.

Both companies target similar EPS growth guidance ranges (6–8%), making them close comparables in institutional investor portfolio decisions. Ameren's simpler, two-state regulatory structure may present (T4_INFERRED) lower regulatory complexity than CenterPoint's eight-state footprint.

## Strategic Brand Positioning in Competitive Context

Based on the comparative analysis above (T4_INFERRED):

**Ameren's strongest differentiators in the institutional investor audience** are: consistent EPS growth guidance in the upper range of the regulated peer group (6–8%), a clear two-state regulatory footprint without major nuclear construction risk or offshore wind exposure, and a $21.8B capital plan that provides visibility into rate base growth. These characteristics distinguish Ameren from Eversource (which encountered offshore wind losses) and Southern Company (which absorbed Vogtle cost overruns).

**Ameren's primary competitive vulnerability** in capital market positioning is its smaller absolute scale relative to Southern Company and Eversource, which limits index weight and may reduce liquidity in certain institutional mandates. In talent markets, Ameren competes against all four peers and broader energy sector employers for STEM and trades talent in Midwest labor markets.

**In the regulatory narrative context**, Ameren's track record with MoPSC and ICC is a direct competitive asset versus national and regional comparisons, as constructive regulatory relationships directly enable the capital investment program that drives earnings growth. (T4_INFERRED)
