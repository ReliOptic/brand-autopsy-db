## Hook Patterns

### Hook 1 — Outage/Storm Response
Real-time communication during weather events. "Crews are working to restore power to X customers."

### Hook 2 — Savings Opportunity
"Save up to $X on your energy bill with these rebates." Direct financial benefit hook.

### Hook 3 — Clean Energy Milestone
"Ameren Missouri's new 300 MW solar facility is now online." Progress toward net-zero.

### Hook 4 — Community Investment
"Ameren invested $X in local community programs this year."

## Content Prohibitions

1. **No price comparison with other utilities.** Rates are regulated; comparison implies competition that does not exist. (T4_INFERRED)
2. **No blame language for outages.** "We are working to restore" — never "the storm caused" (customers do not care about cause during an outage). (T4_INFERRED)
3. **No aspirational clean energy language without specific commitments.** "Net-zero by 2045" is specific; "we care about the planet" is not. (T4_INFERRED)
4. **No political positioning on energy policy.** Utilities serve all customers regardless of political orientation. (T4_INFERRED)
5. **No dismissal of rate increase concerns.** Acknowledge cost impacts to customers when communicating rate changes. (T4_INFERRED)

## Steal Sheet — 3 Transferable Principles

**1. Real-time outage communication is the most important content a utility produces.** During outages, the outage map IS the brand. Transferable: identify the crisis moment when your customers need information most and make that communication excellent.

**2. Make savings tangible.** "$50 rebate on a smart thermostat" is actionable content. "Save energy" is not. Transferable: quantify the benefit in your marketing copy.

**3. Sustainability reporting is brand content.** Ameren's sustainability report communicates environmental commitment while serving regulatory and investor audiences. Triple-duty content.
