# 06. Channel Playbook — Ameren Corporation (AEE)

> **Disclaimer**: This document is brand strategy analysis based on publicly accessible sources. Source notation: `(official)` = Ameren-published; `(observed)` = direct observation; `(estimated)` = project inference.

---

## 1. Channel Strategy Matrix

| Channel | Role | Audience | Core Content |
|---|---|---|---|
| **ameren.com** | Service hub; bill payment; outage reporting; program info | All customers | Account management, outage map, rebate programs, rates |
| **Mobile App** | Bill payment; outage reporting; usage monitoring | Residential customers | Bill pay, usage alerts, outage notifications |
| **Email/Direct Mail** | Rate change notifications; program promotion; regulatory notices | All customers | Rate notices, efficiency programs, regulatory communications |
| **Social Media (Facebook, Twitter)** | Outage updates; community engagement; safety tips | Residential customers, community | Storm updates, safety content, community events |
| **Investor Relations (ameren.com/investors)** | Financial communications | Investors, analysts | Earnings, presentations, SEC filings |
| **Regulatory Filings** | Rate cases; compliance | Regulators, intervenors | Rate case testimony, compliance reports |
| **Community Events** | Local presence; brand building | Service territory residents | Sponsorships, safety demonstrations, career fairs |

## 2. Cross-Channel Synergy

### Route A — Storm Response
```
[Weather Event Detected]
        │
        ├──► [ameren.com Outage Map — Real-time Updates]
        ├──► [Mobile App Push Notifications to Affected Customers]
        ├──► [Social Media — Crew Deployment Updates]
        └──► [IVR / Call Center — Outage Reporting]
                │
                ▼
        [Restoration Complete → Post-Storm Communication]
```

### Route B — Rate Case Communication
```
[Regulatory Filing Submitted]
        │
        ├──► [ameren.com — Rate Case Information Page]
        ├──► [Direct Mail to Customers — Rate Change Notice]
        ├──► [Public Hearing Notices — Regulatory Requirement]
        └──► [Investor Relations — Rate Case Summary]
```

---

## 3. Channel Prohibitions

- **No promotional language in regulatory communications.** Rate notices are factual, not marketing. (T4_INFERRED)
- **No social media engagement on rate criticism.** Acknowledge concern; direct to official channels. (T4_INFERRED)
- **No delayed outage communication.** Real-time updates during storms are non-negotiable. (T4_INFERRED)

---

## 4. Crisis Protocol

### Scenario 1 — Major Grid Outage (Grade 1)
**Response**: Real-time outage map → social media updates every 30–60 minutes → executive communication if outage exceeds 24 hours → post-event restoration timeline publication.

### Scenario 2 — Rate Increase Backlash (Grade 3)
**Response**: Publish rate case explainer on ameren.com → direct customer communications explaining investment rationale → regulatory commission public hearing participation.

### Scenario 3 — Environmental Incident (Grade 1)
**Response**: Regulatory notification → community notification → remediation plan → ongoing progress communication.

---

## 5. Steal Sheet

**1. "Your outage map is your most important product."** During storms, the outage map receives orders of magnitude more traffic than normal. Invest in this tool proportional to its crisis importance.

**2. "Regulatory communication IS customer communication."** Rate case filings, public hearing notices, and compliance reports reach customers and shape brand perception.

**3. "Community events build the trust that survives rate increases."** When Ameren sponsors a local charity run, it builds goodwill that provides a buffer when rates increase.
