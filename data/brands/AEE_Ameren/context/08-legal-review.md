### 2.2 Patent Portfolio

Ameren's patent portfolio, as a regulated utility, is expected to be limited compared to technology or manufacturing companies. Utility companies may hold patents related to grid technology, metering systems, or operational processes. Ameren does not prominently disclose patent holdings in its 10-K. (T1_OFFICIAL, Ameren 10-K — no material patent portfolio disclosed)

Because Ameren's core assets are regulated infrastructure (power lines, substations, gas pipelines) rather than proprietary technology, IP litigation exposure from Ameren's side is significantly lower than for technology sector companies. (T4_INFERRED)

### 2.3 Franchise Territory Rights

The most significant IP-equivalent asset for a regulated utility is not a trademark or patent but rather the **exclusive franchise territory** granted by state regulatory commissions. Ameren's Missouri and Illinois operating subsidiaries hold certificates of convenience and necessity (CCNs) or equivalent regulatory authorizations granting exclusive rights to serve defined geographic territories. (T1_OFFICIAL, Ameren 10-K FY2024 — regulatory framework description)

These franchise rights are not intellectual property in the USPTO sense but are legally protected exclusivity grants with ongoing regulatory obligation. They represent Ameren's most economically significant proprietary asset.

## 3. Regulatory Proceedings (Active and Material)

> All descriptions below are factual summaries of publicly disclosed regulatory and legal proceedings.
> This document does not characterize any party's conduct beyond what is stated in official
> filings, commission orders, or agency press releases.

### 3.1 Missouri Rate Cases (MoPSC)

**Forum**: Missouri Public Service Commission (MoPSC)
**Nature**: Ameren Missouri files general rate cases with MoPSC to update electric and gas rates to reflect the cost of service, capital investment, and authorized return on equity. Rate cases occur periodically, typically every two to three years based on infrastructure investment pace. (T1_OFFICIAL, Ameren 10-K FY2024)
**Financial significance**: Rate case outcomes directly determine Ameren Missouri's allowed revenues and return on equity — the primary earnings driver. (T1_OFFICIAL)
**Key considerations for this project**: MoPSC rate case dockets are public records accessible at efis.psc.mo.gov. Filings include detailed testimony about costs, capital investment, and service quality that constitute T1_OFFICIAL sources for financial and operational claims about Ameren Missouri.
**Source**: MoPSC Case Management System — public docket access (T1_OFFICIAL)

### 3.2 Illinois Rate Cases and PBR Proceedings (ICC)

**Forum**: Illinois Commerce Commission (ICC)
**Nature**: Ameren Illinois files rate cases and participates in annual Performance-Based Ratemaking (PBR) revenue update proceedings. Illinois energy legislation (FEJA and CEJA) established the PBR framework that links Ameren Illinois's electric distribution revenues to reliability and customer service performance metrics. (T1_OFFICIAL, Ameren 10-K FY2024)
**Key legislation**: Future Energy Jobs Act (FEJA, 2016); Climate and Equitable Jobs Act (CEJA, 2021) — both are public statutes defining Ameren Illinois's operating regulatory environment.
**Financial significance**: PBR outcomes affect Ameren Illinois Electric Distribution's allowed annual revenue; natural gas distribution revenues are set through separate ICC rate cases. (T1_OFFICIAL)
**Source**: ICC Docket Search System — public docket access (T1_OFFICIAL)

### 3.3 FERC Transmission Rate Proceedings

**Forum**: Federal Energy Regulatory Commission (FERC)
**Entity**: Ameren Transmission Company of Illinois (ATXI) and Ameren Missouri (transmission assets)
**Nature**: Ameren's transmission subsidiaries file annual formula rate updates with FERC for transmission revenue requirements. Formula rates are subject to annual review by transmission customers and FERC. (T1_OFFICIAL, Ameren 10-K FY2024)
**MISO participation**: Ameren participates in MISO's transmission planning process (MTEP); MISO-approved projects receive cost recovery through the regional tariff. (T1_OFFICIAL, Ameren 10-K)
**Source**: FERC eLibrary — public filing access (T1_OFFICIAL)

### 3.4 Environmental Regulatory Proceedings

**Forum**: U.S. Environmental Protection Agency (EPA); Missouri Department of Natural Resources (MDNR); Illinois Environmental Protection Agency (IEPA)
**Nature**: Ameren operates coal-fired generation units in Missouri subject to EPA air quality regulations, including Clean Air Act standards. (T1_OFFICIAL, Ameren 10-K Risk Factors) Environmental compliance obligations and coal plant retirement timelines are subject to regulatory proceedings.
**Key disclosures** (T1_OFFICIAL, Ameren 10-K FY2024):
- Ameren's coal generation fleet in Missouri is subject to EPA effluent limitation guidelines and coal combustion residuals (CCR) regulations
- Coal combustion residual disposal and site remediation obligations are disclosed as environmental liabilities in Ameren's financial statements
- Ash pond remediation and CCR compliance represent disclosed environmental cost obligations
**Source**: EPA ECHO enforcement database (echo.epa.gov); Ameren 10-K environmental liability disclosures (T1_OFFICIAL)

### 3.5 General Legal Proceedings

Ameren's FY2024 10-K discloses that the company is involved in various legal proceedings in the ordinary course of business. (T1_OFFICIAL, Ameren 10-K FY2024, Part I, Item 3) These include:
- Environmental claims and remediation obligations at former industrial sites
- Customer and employee-related claims in the ordinary course
- Regulatory proceedings before MoPSC, ICC, FERC, and EPA

Ameren has not disclosed any single legal proceeding in its FY2024 10-K that rises to a level requiring specific quantified disclosure under SEC rules, beyond the environmental and regulatory matters noted above. (T1_OFFICIAL, Ameren 10-K FY2024, Part I, Item 3 — verify against filed 10-K for current status)

### 5.4 Writing Risk Guide — Ameren Corporation

> This is the most operationally critical section for day-to-day content creation.
> Follow these guidelines for every sentence written about Ameren in this project.

#### SAFE TO WRITE (with proper sourcing)

- Revenue, net income, EPS, rate base figures from Ameren's SEC filings, cited as "(SEC 10-K FY20XX)"
- Segment revenue breakdowns cited to the 10-K
- Descriptions of Ameren's regulated utility services based on ameren.com or official tariff filings
- Summaries of rate case proceedings citing specific commission orders, docket numbers, and official records
- Descriptions of Ameren's trademarks citing USPTO records
- Credit rating statements citing public agency publications (with caveat that ratings are subject to change)
- Historical dividend and EPS figures from IR materials and SEC filings
- Descriptions of Ameren's publicly stated capital plan, EPS guidance, and clean energy timeline
- Comparison of Ameren's financial metrics to peer utilities using cited, public data
- Summaries of environmental compliance obligations citing EPA records and Ameren 10-K disclosures
- Quotations from Ameren's SEC filings, earnings call transcripts, or press releases with attribution

#### REQUIRES CAREFUL HEDGING

| Topic | Required Hedging Approach |
|-------|--------------------------|
| Rate case outcomes (pending) | "The proceeding is pending before MoPSC / ICC" — NEVER predict outcomes |
| Capital plan projections | "Ameren has publicly guided to..." and "subject to regulatory approval" — NEVER state as guaranteed |
| Environmental compliance status | "As disclosed in Ameren's 10-K..." and "subject to ongoing regulatory processes" |
| Regulatory relationship quality | "Publicly available rate case records indicate..." — NEVER characterize without specific evidence |
| EPS growth trajectory | Use Ameren's stated guidance range with attribution; tag forward projections as estimates |
| Clean energy transition timeline | "Ameren's publicly stated goal is..." and "subject to regulatory approval and IRP processes" |
| Coal plant environmental liability | Cite specific 10-K environmental liability disclosures; do not characterize severity beyond disclosed figures |
| Customer rate affordability | Use specific rate data from tariff filings; avoid characterizing affordability without metric-based comparison |
| Competitor utility comparisons | Metric-based only (revenue, EPS, customer count); no qualitative superiority claims |
| Regulatory commission decisions | Describe filed positions and outcomes; do not attribute motives to commissioners or parties |

#### DO NOT WRITE (High Legal / Project Integrity Risk)

- Any assertion that Ameren engaged in fraud, regulatory deception, or criminal conduct without direct citation from a final court judgment or regulatory order using those specific terms
- Characterizing Ameren's rate case positions as attempts to overcharge customers — describe rate requests factually and note the regulatory review process
- Claiming knowledge of Ameren's internal regulatory strategy, settlement negotiations, or executive intent not disclosed in public filings
- Reproducing substantial portions of Ameren's copyrighted investor presentations, rate case exhibits, or marketing materials verbatim
- Asserting that any pending regulatory proceeding or environmental matter has a predetermined outcome
- Making claims about Ameren environmental compliance violations not documented in final EPA or state agency enforcement orders
- Generalizing about customer satisfaction or dissatisfaction without survey data or regulatory performance metric citation
- Asserting that Ameren's coal plant retirement timeline is accelerating or decelerating beyond what is disclosed in IRP filings or 10-K disclosures
- Repeating unverified social media claims or anonymous forum posts about Ameren outages, billing, or environmental incidents as factual statements
- Using Ameren's logo, trademarks, or photography without transformative editorial context

#### Recommended Phrasings Table (Ameren-Specific)

| Topic | AVOID | USE INSTEAD |
|-------|-------|-------------|
| Rate increases | "Ameren raised rates on customers" | "Ameren Missouri filed a rate case with MoPSC seeking a rate increase; the commission's order addressed the request. (T1_OFFICIAL, MoPSC public order)" |
| Environmental compliance | "Ameren's coal plants are polluting Missouri" | "Ameren's Missouri coal generation facilities are subject to EPA Clean Air Act standards and coal combustion residuals regulations, as disclosed in Ameren's 10-K risk factors. (T1_OFFICIAL)" |
