# 02. Audience Map — Ameren Corporation (AEE)

> **Disclaimer**: This analysis is based on publicly available information including SEC filings and official company communications. It does not constitute investment, legal, or strategic advice. Estimates are explicitly marked `(estimated)`.

---

## Ideal Customer Profile (ICP)

| Dimension | Profile |
|-----------|---------|
| **Customer base** | 2.4 million electric customers and 900,000 natural gas customers in Missouri and Illinois (SEC 10-K FY2024) |
| **Segments** | Residential (~60% of customers, ~35% of revenue), Commercial (~30% of customers, ~30% of revenue), Industrial (~5% of customers, ~25% of revenue), Other (estimated) |
| **Geographic** | Central and eastern Missouri (Ameren Missouri); central and southern Illinois (Ameren Illinois) |
| **Relationship** | Non-voluntary — customers in Ameren's service territory have limited or no choice of electricity provider for delivery service |

---

## Core Personas

### Persona 1 — Maria, Residential Customer
- **Location**: Suburban St. Louis, Missouri
- **Relationship**: Ameren Missouri provides electricity and natural gas
- **Core pain**: Monthly energy bill management; power outages during storms; understanding rate changes
- **Ameren interaction**: Bill payment, outage reporting, energy efficiency rebate programs
- **Churn risk**: Near zero — regulated monopoly in service territory. Dissatisfaction manifests as regulatory complaints, not switching. (T4_INFERRED)

### Persona 2 — Tom, Small Business Owner
- **Business**: Restaurant in Springfield, Illinois
- **Relationship**: Ameren Illinois provides electricity
- **Core pain**: Energy costs as a significant operating expense; needs reliability for food safety (refrigeration)
- **Ameren interaction**: Commercial rate programs, demand response programs, energy efficiency audits
- **Churn risk**: Near zero for delivery; may choose alternative energy supplier in Illinois retail choice market. (T4_INFERRED)

### Persona 3 — Regional Economic Development Agency
- **Role**: Attracting businesses to Ameren's service territory
- **Relationship**: Ameren as partner in economic development — reliable, affordable energy attracts employers
- **Core need**: Competitive energy rates, grid reliability data, renewable energy availability for corporate sustainability commitments
- **Ameren interaction**: Economic development team partnerships, site selection support (official, ameren.com/economic-development)

## Purchase Journey — AARRR Framework

| Stage | Mechanism |
|-------|----------|
| **Acquisition** | Not applicable in traditional sense — customers are acquired by geography (move into service territory) |
| **Activation** | Service connection request → meter installation → first bill |
| **Retention** | Mandatory within service territory; retention efforts focus on satisfaction, not churn prevention |
| **Referral** | Not applicable — utility is not a referral business |
| **Revenue** | Regulated tariff rates × consumption; rate base investment drives regulated returns (SEC 10-K FY2024) |

## Anti-Persona

|------|---------------|
| **Off-grid homesteader** | Generates own power; no utility relationship needed |
| **Customer outside service territory** | Ameren serves only Missouri and Illinois territories |
| **Large industrial with on-site generation** | May generate own power and use Ameren only for backup |

## Steal Sheet — 3 Transferable Principles

**1. When your customers can't leave, invest in satisfaction anyway.** Ameren's monopoly position does not eliminate the need for customer satisfaction — dissatisfied customers file regulatory complaints, attend public hearings, and vote on utility commissioners. Transferable: even with captive customers, service quality affects your operating environment.

**2. Economic development as B2B marketing.** Ameren's economic development team helps attract businesses to its service territory, which increases the customer base and load. Transferable: if your growth depends on a geographic market, invest in making that market attractive to new entrants.

**3. Energy efficiency programs are counter-intuitive brand investments.** Ameren helps customers use LESS of its product (energy efficiency rebates), which builds trust and regulatory goodwill while managing peak demand costs. Transferable: helping customers use your product more efficiently can be a brand and operational advantage.
