## Ideal Customer Profile (ICP)

ELC's multi-brand portfolio serves multiple distinct customer segments simultaneously. The parent company ICP is best described at the portfolio level:

**Primary ICP**: Women ages 25–55 globally, middle-to-upper income, who allocate meaningful discretionary budget to prestige beauty and define beauty as a form of self-care and self-expression. The Asia-Pacific market (particularly China, South Korea, Japan) represents a critical and growing segment. (T4_INFERRED from ELC geographic revenue disclosures showing Asia-Pacific as major revenue driver; SEC 10-K FY2024)

**Secondary ICP**: Professional makeup artists and beauty industry professionals — particularly relevant for MAC and Bobbi Brown brands. This professional audience serves as a credibility bridge to mainstream consumers. (official, MAC brand history)

**Tertiary ICP**: Male consumers — a growing segment for fragrance (Tom Ford Beauty, Jo Malone) and targeted skincare. ELC brands have expanded gender-inclusive positioning. (T4_INFERRED from observed product positioning)

## Persona 1 — The Prestige Skincare Investor

**Name**: Amanda, 42
**Role**: Marketing executive; high discretionary beauty spend
**Location**: New York, London, or Shanghai
**Annual beauty spend**: $2,000–$5,000 (estimated)

**Pain Points**:
- Information overload from mass-market beauty claims that cannot be distinguished from clinically supported ones
- Anti-aging efficacy concerns that require dermatologically credible formulations
- Sustainability concerns about ingredient sourcing and packaging

**Gain Drivers**:
- Clinical-adjacent language and dermatologist association (Clinique, Estée Lauder Advanced Night Repair)
- Prestige packaging and in-store experience as part of the product value
- Brand heritage as quality signal — "this brand has been trusted for decades"

**Brand Relationship**: Loyal to specific SKUs (Advanced Night Repair serum, La Mer Moisturizing Cream) rather than the parent company. Repurchases are driven by efficacy experience, not advertising. (T4_INFERRED)

## Persona 2 — The Global Luxury Fragrance Consumer

**Name**: Li Wei, 31
**Role**: Finance professional; Shanghai
**Annual luxury goods spend**: $8,000–$15,000 (estimated)

**Pain Points**:
- Counterfeit luxury products in certain distribution channels
- Limited discovery mechanism for niche fragrance brands
- Desire for personalization and uniqueness in fragrance selection

**Gain Drivers**:
- Jo Malone London's layering philosophy — customizable, personal scent identity
- Tom Ford Beauty's aspirational luxury positioning aligned with overall luxury lifestyle
- Gift-giving utility — fragrance as status-appropriate luxury gift

**Brand Relationship**: Discovery often occurs through department store or travel retail exposure. China is a key market for ELC's prestige brands, with travel retail (duty-free, airports) a significant acquisition channel. (T4_INFERRED from ELC disclosed channel strategy; Asia-Pacific revenue concentration in 10-K)

## Persona 3 — The MAC Artistry Community Member

**Name**: Jordan, 26
**Role**: Makeup artist; beauty content creator
**Location**: Los Angeles, Milan, or Lagos

## Anti-Persona

**The Mass Beauty Consumer**: A consumer who primarily shops at drugstores (CVS, Walgreens), whose maximum beauty spend per product is $20, and who does not experience a meaningful utility difference between prestige and mass formulations. ELC brands do not address this segment; they require the consumer to perceive — and pay for — prestige distinction. (T4_INFERRED)

## Steal Sheet — 3 Transferable Principles

**1. Sampling as the highest-ROI acquisition investment in prestige beauty.**
Department store beauty counters are, at their core, sampling machines. The "free gift with purchase" and complimentary sample mechanic converts browsers into buyers more efficiently than digital advertising at the prestige price point. Transferable: for any high-consideration, high-priced product, design a structured sampling program rather than relying on advertising to convert cold audiences.

**2. Travel retail as a global brand introduction channel.**
ELC's Asia-Pacific revenue concentration is partly driven by travel retail — duty-free shops in airports where affluent travelers encounter prestige beauty brands in a purchase-motivated context. Transferable: identify the one channel where your target customer is already in "purchase mode" and has elevated price tolerance, and prioritize that channel over high-friction digital acquisition.

**3. Professional community adoption as the most credible consumer signal.**
MAC's professional artist community is its most powerful marketing asset — more credible than advertising because it represents peer endorsement from acknowledged experts. Transferable: before spending on consumer advertising, identify and win the professional or expert community that your target consumer looks to for guidance.
