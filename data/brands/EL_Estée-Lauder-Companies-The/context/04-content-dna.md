## Hook Patterns

**Hook 1 — The Before/After Skin Story**
"[X weeks] of [product] — here's what I noticed."
Mechanism: Time-based efficacy narrative with visual documentation. Creates anticipation in the setup and credibility in the outcome. Advanced Night Repair and La Mer generate extensive user-generated before/after content. (observed, social beauty content patterns)

**Hook 2 — The Fragrance Memory**
"The scent that takes me back to [place / moment]."
Mechanism: Fragrance's neurological link to memory makes this hook uniquely effective for Jo Malone and Tom Ford Beauty. The product becomes a sensory trigger for an emotional memory. (T4_INFERRED from observed Jo Malone brand content)

**Hook 3 — The Shade Match Reveal**
"We found [persona's] perfect shade — shade [X] in [foundation]."
Mechanism: Personalization demonstration that makes the brand's shade range depth feel individually relevant rather than abstractly inclusive. MAC's 150+ foundation shades generate endless personalized content. (observed, MAC social content)

**Hook 4 — The Expert Endorsement**
"[Dermatologist / makeup artist / beauty editor] explains why [product]."
Mechanism: Expert voice borrows professional credibility to support efficacy claims. Clinique's dermatologist-tested positioning and MAC's professional artist heritage make this hook credible. (observed, brand content and partnership patterns)

## Tone Guide

| Situation | Tone | Example |
|-----------|------|---------|
| **Skincare science content** | Expert, warm, credible | Clinical language made accessible; no jargon without explanation; reassuring about efficacy. |
| **Makeup artistry content** | Inspirational, celebratory, inclusive | Celebrates creativity and self-expression; no prescriptive beauty standards. |
| **Luxury fragrance content** | Sensory, evocative, poetic | Appeals to imagination and emotion; avoids product specification; creates atmosphere. |
| **Sustainability content** | Honest, progress-oriented, specific | Cites actual metrics; acknowledges work remaining; avoids greenwashing hyperbole. |

## Content Prohibitions

1. **No homogenized beauty standards**: ELC brand content avoids imagery that implies a single beauty ideal. MAC's founding philosophy of inclusion is a brand-wide standard. (official, MAC brand values)
2. **No unsubstantiated clinical claims**: Efficacy claims for skincare products must be substantiated per FTC guidelines and international equivalents. "Clinically proven" requires substantiation. (T4_INFERRED from regulatory compliance requirements; see FTC guidelines on advertising substantiation)
3. **No price comparison to mass-market products**: Prestige positioning requires avoiding category confusion with mass-market equivalents. (T4_INFERRED)
4. **No greenwashing language**: ELC's sustainability commitments are disclosed with specificity; vague "eco-friendly" or "natural" language without substantiation is prohibited. (T4_INFERRED from observed ELC sustainability communications and regulatory context)
5. **No appropriation without partnership**: Use of cultural aesthetics or community references in beauty content requires authentic community involvement and partnership, not imitation. (T4_INFERRED from observed industry standards and MAC's community engagement model)

## Steal Sheet — 3 Transferable Principles

**1. Efficacy narrative creates repurchase; aspiration creates acquisition.**
ELC's content architecture uses aspiration to attract new customers (fragrance lifestyle content, luxury imagery) and efficacy narrative to convert and retain them (before/after skin stories, dermatologist endorsements). These are different content jobs for different funnel stages. Transferable: map your content by funnel stage — acquisition content needs a different mechanism than retention content, and the same piece rarely serves both jobs optimally.

**2. Inclusion is a product strategy, not just a marketing message.**
MAC's 150+ foundation shades is not a communications decision — it is a product development decision that then generates authentic inclusion communications. Transferable: audit whether your diversity and inclusion brand messaging is backed by actual product or service design decisions, or whether the messaging is running ahead of the product reality.

**3. The sample mechanic closes the prestige price hesitation gap.**
ELC brands consistently use complimentary sampling (with purchase, at counter, by mail) to eliminate the risk barrier of prestige pricing. The sample converts consideration into trial; trial converts to purchase at a measurable rate. Transferable: calculate the cost per trial via sampling versus cost per conversion via advertising for your highest-priced product, and compare conversion rates.
