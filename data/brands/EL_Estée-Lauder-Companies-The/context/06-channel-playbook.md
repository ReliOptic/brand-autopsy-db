## Channel Prohibitions

1. **No price discounting in brand-direct channels without strategic justification**: Prestige brands protect price integrity; unapproved discounting on brand.com undermines the prestige signal. (T4_INFERRED)
2. **No unauthorized third-party resellers**: Gray market and unauthorized reseller channels undermine quality control and brand experience. ELC's distribution agreements govern authorized retail. (T4_INFERRED)
3. **No substantiated clinical claims in channels without regulatory review**: Health claims in beauty advertising are subject to FTC and international advertising standards; channel-specific review is required. (T4_INFERRED from regulatory compliance requirements)
4. **China-specific content must comply with local advertising and ingredient regulations**: China's cosmetics regulations (NMPA) govern ingredient listing, advertising claims, and label requirements. ELC has dedicated regulatory affairs resources for the China market. (T4_INFERRED from disclosed regulatory complexity in China market; T1_OFFICIAL 10-K risk factors)

## Crisis Protocol

### Scenario 1 — Product Safety Complaint / Adverse Reaction

**Response**: Product safety complaints are routed to regulatory and product safety teams. Consumer communications acknowledge concern, provide contact for formal product complaint submission, and do not admit or deny causation without investigation. Social media comments alleging adverse reactions are handled individually with private channel follow-up. (T4_INFERRED from beauty industry crisis management practice)

### Scenario 2 — China Market Revenue Miss / Trade Disruption

**Response**: Investor-facing communications (earnings call, 8-K if material) provide factual explanation of market conditions. Consumer-facing channels in China continue standard programming unless market conditions require adjustment. This scenario has been active in ELC's recent reporting periods; management commentary on earnings calls is the authoritative channel for investor audiences. (T2_PRIMARY_RELIABLE, ELC earnings calls FY2023–FY2024)

### Scenario 3 — Influencer / KOL Controversy

**Response**: When a brand partner creates controversy, ELC brands evaluate continued partnership against brand value alignment. Official response through brand social channels acknowledges the partnership review without pre-judging outcomes. Brand dissociation, if chosen, is communicated through a brief factual statement. (T4_INFERRED from observed beauty industry practice)

## Steal Sheet — 3 Transferable Principles

**1. The sampling cascade creates channel-independent trial.**
ELC's sampling mechanic works across every channel — department store GWP, online order insert, press seeding, Sephora Rewards Bazaar — because it solves the same problem in every context: eliminating the price-risk barrier for a new prestige product. Transferable: design your trial mechanism to be channel-agnostic so it can be deployed wherever the target customer already is.

**2. KOL strategy in China is a different marketing discipline, not just influencer marketing.**
China's KOL ecosystem (Weibo, Xiaohongshu, Douyin, WeChat) operates by different rules than Western influencer marketing — higher production expectations, different trust dynamics, platform-specific livestream selling mechanics. ELC has invested in China-specific team capabilities. Transferable: do not apply Western social media playbooks to China market entry; treat it as a separate capability requiring separate investment.

**3. The travel retail channel is a brand laboratory for gift positioning.**
Travel retail forces brands to package products for gift-purchase decisions — the buyer is often purchasing for someone else, in a time-constrained context, at a value above everyday spend. ELC's travel retail gift sets reveal which product combinations and price points work as gifts. Transferable: design one channel specifically for gift purchase occasions and use what you learn there to improve year-round packaging and bundling.
