## 1. Channel Architecture Overview

Zebra Technologies operates a predominantly indirect sales model, with the majority of revenue flowing through a network of channel partners, distributors, and resellers rather than direct enterprise sales. Zebra's FY2023 10-K states: "We primarily sell our products, solutions, and services through a global network of distributors and resellers, as well as through direct sales." (SEC 10-K FY2023)

### Revenue Channel Mix (estimated)

| Channel | Estimated Revenue Share | Basis |
|---------|------------------------|-------|
| **Distributors & Resellers** | ~70-75% | Zebra's 10-K identifies its primary revenue channel as indirect, through distributors. Two distributors (ScanSource and Ingram Micro/TD SYNNEX) have historically been significant customers. (SEC 10-K FY2023; T4_INFERRED for percentage estimate) |
| **Direct Enterprise Sales** | ~15-20% | Large enterprise accounts and government contracts sold through Zebra's direct sales organization. (estimated; T4_INFERRED) |
| **OEM / Embedded** | ~5-10% | Zebra's print engines and scan engines embedded in third-party systems (e.g., kiosk manufacturers, ATM vendors). (estimated; T4_INFERRED) |

### Key Distribution Partners

Zebra's FY2023 10-K discloses significant customer concentration: "Sales to our two largest customers, both distributors, accounted for approximately 16% and 15% of Net sales in 2023." (SEC 10-K FY2023)

| Partner Type | Examples | Role |
|-------------|----------|------|
| **Broadline IT Distributors** | ScanSource, TD SYNNEX (formerly Tech Data/SYNNEX), Ingram Micro | Inventory, order fulfillment, credit, logistics to reseller channel. These distributors carry Zebra's full product catalog and serve as the primary fulfillment layer. (SEC 10-K FY2023; official, partner program information on zebra.com) |
| **Value-Added Resellers (VARs)** | Hundreds of VARs globally, including specialized industry VARs (healthcare IT, warehouse technology, retail technology) | Solution selling, implementation, integration services, ongoing support. | **ISVs (Independent Software Vendors)** | WMS vendors, EMR vendors, retail POS vendors | Software partners whose applications run on Zebra devices. Zebra's developer program (developer.zebra.com) provides SDKs and tools for ISV integration. (official, developer.zebra.com) |

## Steal Sheet — 3 Transferable Principles

**1. Build the partner program as a revenue multiplier, not a cost center.**
Zebra's indirect channel model means that 70-75% of revenue (estimated) is generated by partners who carry inventory, manage customer relationships, and provide implementation services — all at the partner's cost. Zebra's investment is in enablement (training, tools, MDF), not in hiring a proportional direct sales force. Transferable structure: for B2B products with complex deployment requirements, invest in partner enablement infrastructure (certification programs, co-marketing, technical support) that makes partners more productive selling your product than competing alternatives.

**2. Use trade shows as the top of the enterprise sales funnel.**
In B2B AIDC, the trade show booth is where the buyer first handles the device. Digital marketing generates awareness; the trade show generates conviction. Zebra's Tier 1 event presence (NRF, HIMSS, ProMat) is designed for hands-on demonstration, not brand awareness. Transferable structure: for physical products sold to enterprise buyers, invest in trade show presence that enables direct product interaction. Design the booth for demonstration, not decoration.

**3. Make the service contract the retention mechanism, not the device.**
Zebra OneCare service contracts (1-5 year terms) create a recurring revenue relationship and a customer retention hook that extends beyond the device purchase cycle. A customer with an active OneCare contract is significantly less likely to evaluate competitors at refresh time. Transferable structure: design a post-sale service program that provides enough value (faster support, advance replacement, extended security updates) to justify annual renewal. The service contract is the customer retention strategy; the device is the customer acquisition strategy.
