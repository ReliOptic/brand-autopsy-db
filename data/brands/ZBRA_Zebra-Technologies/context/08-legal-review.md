### 2.2 Patent Portfolio

Zebra maintains a substantial patent portfolio in automatic identification and data capture (AIDC) technology. Key patent domains include:
- Barcode scanning algorithms and optical decoding (core legacy technology)
- RFID reader and tag technology
- Mobile computer hardware design (inherited from Motorola Enterprise acquisition, 2014)
- Thermal printing technology and print head design
- Machine vision and industrial imaging (acquired via Matrox, 2022)
- Autonomous mobile robot navigation (acquired via Fetch Robotics, 2021)

Zebra's 10-K states the company holds a significant number of patents and patent applications across multiple jurisdictions. The Motorola Enterprise acquisition substantially expanded the patent portfolio in mobile computing. (SEC 10-K FY2023; T4_INFERRED)

### 2.3 Trade Secrets

Zebra's proprietary firmware, scanning algorithms, and enterprise software platforms (Workcloud, Reflexis) represent significant trade secret value. The company's 10-K identifies intellectual property protection as a key competitive factor. (SEC 10-K FY2023)

## 3. Major Litigation

### 3.1 Patent Litigation Environment

Zebra operates in a technology space where patent litigation is common. The company's 10-K discloses that it is party to various patent claims and proceedings in the ordinary course of business. Key dynamics:

- The barcode scanning and mobile computing space involves overlapping patent portfolios among Zebra, Honeywell, Datalogic, and other participants. (T4_INFERRED from industry structure)
- The Motorola Enterprise acquisition brought inherited patent litigation exposure and patent defense capabilities. (T4_INFERRED)
- Zebra's 10-K Item 3 (Legal Proceedings) discloses specific proceedings when they meet materiality thresholds. (SEC 10-K FY2023)

### 3.2 Honeywell Competitive Dynamics

Honeywell's Productivity Solutions and Safety segment is Zebra's most direct competitor in barcode scanning and mobile computing. Competition between the two companies includes patent assertion and defense activities characteristic of overlapping technology portfolios in enterprise mobility. (T4_INFERRED from publicly observable competitive dynamics)

### 3.3 Regulatory Compliance

Zebra's products are subject to various regulatory requirements:
- **FCC certification**: Required for all radio-frequency devices including mobile computers, RFID readers, and wireless scanners. (T1_OFFICIAL)
- **CE marking**: Required for European market access. (T1_OFFICIAL)
- **Healthcare device regulation**: Devices used in clinical settings may be subject to FDA oversight depending on classification and intended use. (T4_INFERRED)
- **Export controls**: Technology products subject to EAR and potentially ITAR where applicable. (SEC 10-K FY2023)
- **Environmental compliance**: RoHS/WEEE directives (EU), hazardous materials regulations for batteries in mobile devices. (SEC 10-K FY2023)

### 5.4 Writing Risk Guide — Zebra Technologies

#### SAFE TO WRITE
- Revenue, margins, and financial data from SEC filings with citation
- Product descriptions based on zebra.com and public data sheets
- Patent portfolio descriptions from public USPTO records
- Acquisition history from SEC filings and press releases
- Competitive landscape using disclosed financial metrics

#### REQUIRES CAREFUL HEDGING
| Topic | Required Approach |
|-------|-------------------|
| Market share claims | "Zebra is described as a leader in..." with third-party source; not as bare assertion |
| Honeywell competition | Metric-based comparisons only; no characterization of competitive conduct |
| Customer concentration | Describe disclosed risk factors; do not name specific customers unless disclosed in filings |
| Workforce reductions | Factual description citing source; no characterization of management quality |
| Acquisition valuations | Describe disclosed terms; hedge assessments of whether acquisitions "succeeded" |

#### DO NOT WRITE
- Assertions about undisclosed customer identities or contract terms
- Pejorative characterization of Zebra's competitive tactics
- Product quality or reliability claims without cited evidence
- Predictions about patent litigation outcomes
- Unverified market share figures presented as fact
