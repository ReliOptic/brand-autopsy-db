## 2. IP Portfolio

### 2.1 Trademark Portfolio (T1_OFFICIAL, USPTO Public Records)

Gartner maintains trademark registrations protecting its core research brand names and
frameworks. Key marks as publicly searchable via USPTO TESS:

| Mark | Class(es) | Notes |
|------|-----------|-------|
| GARTNER | IC 35, 42 (research, advisory services) | House mark; broadly protected in technology research and advisory categories |
| MAGIC QUADRANT | IC 35, 42 | Core research framework mark; extensively licensed to vendors for "Leader" badge marketing use |
| HYPE CYCLE | IC 35, 42 | Proprietary research framework; widely recognized in enterprise technology media |
| GARTNER PEER INSIGHTS | IC 35, 42 | Community and reviews platform mark |
| GARTNER IT SYMPOSIUM | IC 41, 35 | Conference brand; global event series |

Gartner actively defends its research framework marks, particularly MAGIC QUADRANT and
HYPE CYCLE, which have achieved significant brand recognition in enterprise technology markets.
Third parties using these marks without license risk infringement proceedings (T4_INFERRED
from Gartner's observed trademark enforcement posture and official licensing program).

### 2.2 Research Content Copyright

Gartner's Magic Quadrant reports, Hype Cycle reports, Market Guides, and other published
research notes are copyrighted works. Key copyright considerations for this project:

- Subscribers may use Gartner research content within the scope of their subscription license
  agreement terms (T1_OFFICIAL, Gartner subscription terms of use)
- Vendors who purchase "Magic Quadrant Leader" badges license specific graphic elements for
  marketing use under a formal Gartner licensing program (official, Gartner vendor licensing)
- Reproducing Gartner research content beyond fair use — including republishing Magic Quadrant
  charts or substantial portions of research reports — without license constitutes copyright
  infringement (T4_INFERRED from copyright law and Gartner's stated terms of use)

### 2.3 Research Methodology as Trade Secret

Gartner's research scoring methodologies — the specific weighting and evaluation criteria
applied in Magic Quadrant assessments beyond what is publicly disclosed — may constitute
proprietary trade secrets (T4_INFERRED). Gartner publishes research methodology notes that
explain the framework without disclosing the full scoring rubric.

## 3. Major Litigation and Legal Proceedings

> All descriptions below are factual summaries of publicly disclosed legal proceedings.
> This document does not characterize any party's conduct beyond what official filings,
> court orders, or regulatory agency press releases state.

### 3.1 Commercial and IP Litigation (General)

Gartner's 10-K, Part I, Item 3 discloses that Gartner is involved from time to time in
various legal proceedings arising in the normal course of business (T1_OFFICIAL, Gartner 10-K).
Investors and analysts should reference the most recent 10-K for current material litigation
disclosures.

### 3.2 Research Independence Legal Risk (Historical Context)

Gartner has periodically faced allegations — in media, academic research, and from vendors —
regarding the independence of its research from its commercial relationships. These allegations
have not, based on publicly available records as of the date of this analysis, resulted in
material regulatory enforcement actions or court judgments against Gartner's research practices
(T3_SECONDARY_RELIABLE, historical press coverage; T1_OFFICIAL — Gartner's 10-K does not
disclose material regulatory proceedings related to research independence).

**For this project**: Any characterization of Gartner's research independence or bias must
be based on publicly documented sources (court records, regulatory proceedings, or formally
published academic research) — not inferred from vendor complaints in social media or
unverified press allegations.

### 3.3 Employee and Labor Matters

Gartner employs thousands of analysts, sales, and support staff globally. Employment-related
claims — including those related to commission disputes, non-compete agreements, and
classification — are disclosed in the 10-K as part of normal business legal proceedings
(T1_OFFICIAL). No specific material employment litigation is publicly identified as of
this analysis date.

### 4.3 Research Market Regulation Risk

Unlike financial services research (equity analyst research, credit ratings), enterprise
technology research advisory is not subject to sector-specific regulatory frameworks in the
United States (T4_INFERRED). This means Gartner's research independence is maintained by
commercial incentive and reputational risk rather than regulatory mandate — a distinction
that is material for understanding Gartner's legal risk profile.

### 4.4 Privacy and Data Regulation

Gartner collects user data through its research portal, Peer Insights platform, event
registration, and direct client engagement. Applicable frameworks include:
- **GDPR** (EU): Applies to EU client and research portal user data
- **CCPA/CPRA** (California): Applies to California residents interacting with Gartner's platforms
- **Various national privacy laws**: Apply in jurisdictions where Gartner operates (T1_OFFICIAL,
  Gartner privacy policy at gartner.com/en/about/policies/privacy)

## 5. Project Risk Assessment — Brand Autopsy DB

> This section assesses the legal risk specifically for the Brand Autopsy DB project when
> writing about Gartner. It is the primary operational purpose of this layer.

### 5.1 Gartner's Litigation Posture

**Assessment**: MODERATE — IP-PROTECTIVE (T4_INFERRED based on public record)

Basis:
- Gartner actively enforces its Magic Quadrant and Hype Cycle trademarks, particularly
  against vendors using these marks without license in marketing materials (T4_INFERRED
  from observed licensing program and trademark registration breadth)
- Gartner's terms of use for its research portal explicitly limit reproduction and distribution
  of research content beyond subscriber use (T1_OFFICIAL, Gartner terms of use)
- Gartner does not, based on publicly available records, pursue aggressive defamation
  litigation against analysts, journalists, or researchers discussing its business practices
  with appropriate sourcing and hedging (T4_INFERRED)

**Implication for this project**: Primary risks are (a) reproducing Gartner research content
or Magic Quadrant charts beyond fair use, (b) using "Magic Quadrant" or "Hype Cycle" marks
as if describing our own research framework, and (c) making unsourced characterizations of
Gartner's research independence or business practices.

### 5.3 Fair Use Analysis (17 U.S.C. § 107)

**Factor 1 — Purpose**: Analytical, educational, transformative. We analyze Gartner's brand
and business model, not reproduce its research product. FAVORS FAIR USE.

**Factor 2 — Nature**: We primarily work with factual data (SEC filings, observable brand
elements) and publicly available research summaries. FAVORS FAIR USE.

**Factor 3 — Amount**: We do not reproduce Magic Quadrant charts, full research note text,
or subscriber-only content. FAVORS FAIR USE (provided discipline maintained).

**Factor 4 — Market Effect**: Our analysis does not substitute for a Gartner research
subscription or replace Gartner's commercial research products. FAVORS FAIR USE.

**Overall**: Defensible under fair use (T4_INFERRED; not legal advice).

### 5.4 Writing Risk Guide — Gartner, Inc.

> Follow these guidelines for every sentence written about Gartner in this project.

#### SAFE TO WRITE (with proper sourcing)

- Revenue, CV growth, and financial metrics from Gartner's SEC filings, cited as "(T1_OFFICIAL, Gartner 10-K FY20XX)"
- Segment revenue breakdown cited to the 10-K segment notes
- Descriptions of Gartner's publicly stated products and services (Magic Quadrant, Hype Cycle, Peer Insights, Symposium) as described on gartner.com
- Summaries of Gartner's publicly stated research methodology as published in methodology notes
- Descriptions of Gartner's trademark portfolio citing USPTO records
- Competitive landscape observations based on public market data and financial journalism
- Descriptions of conference format, event series, and audience based on public event marketing

#### REQUIRES CAREFUL HEDGING

| Topic | Required Hedging Approach |
|-------|--------------------------|
| Research independence | "Gartner's published methodology states..." — NEVER "Gartner's research is/is not biased" as bare assertion |
| Magic Quadrant vendor placement | Reference specific publicly disclosed MQ editions only; never assert current placement without current-date citation |
| Analyst expertise claims | "Gartner describes its analyst team as..." — do not personally characterize individual analyst expertise levels |
| Revenue projections | "Gartner's 10-K discloses CV of..." — never project forward revenue without analyst-cited source |
| Research methodology critique | If citing academic or media critique, tag as T3_SECONDARY_RELIABLE and attribute specifically |
| Vendor relationships | "Gartner's terms of service describe vendor engagement programs as..." — do not characterize commercial relationships without specific sourcing |

#### DO NOT WRITE (High Legal / Project Integrity Risk)

- Reproduce Magic Quadrant charts, Hype Cycle curves, or substantial research text without Gartner license — this is copyright infringement
- Use "Magic Quadrant" or "Hype Cycle" as if describing this project's own research framework — trademark infringement risk
- Assert that Gartner's research is biased, paid-for, or commercially influenced without specific citation from a court judgment or regulatory final order
- Characterize individual Gartner analyst opinions as institutional Gartner positions without proper attribution
- Reproduce Gartner subscriber-only research content that was accessed via a subscription
- Make claims about Gartner's internal scoring methodology beyond what Gartner has publicly disclosed in methodology notes
- Assert vendor-specific Magic Quadrant placement changes without citing a specific, dated published MQ edition

#### Recommended Phrasings (Gartner-Specific)

| Topic | AVOID | USE INSTEAD |
|-------|-------|-------------|
| Magic Quadrant | "Gartner's Magic Quadrant shows [Vendor X] is the best" | "In Gartner's [Month Year] Magic Quadrant for [Category], [Vendor X] was placed in the Leaders quadrant, indicating high scores on Gartner's published execution and vision criteria. (T1_OFFICIAL if citing MQ edition date)" |
| Research independence | "Gartner sells favorable research placements" | "Gartner's published conflict-of-interest policy states [quote directly]. Questions about research independence have appeared in media coverage [cite specific T3 source]. No regulatory findings on this matter are publicly available as of this analysis." |
| Subscription pricing | "Gartner's subscription costs a fortune" | "Gartner's research subscription pricing is calibrated for enterprise budgets; specific pricing is not publicly disclosed. Third-party reports suggest enterprise subscription costs range significantly depending on scope (estimated, T3_SECONDARY_RELIABLE)." |
| Analyst prediction accuracy | "Gartner's predictions are often wrong" | "Gartner's published predictions are periodically reviewed by media and academic sources. Retrospective accuracy assessments have appeared in [cite specific sources, T3_SECONDARY_RELIABLE]. This analysis does not independently assess prediction accuracy." |

## 6. ESG & Compliance

### 6.1 Environmental

Gartner publishes sustainability and ESG-related information in its proxy statement (DEF 14A)
and corporate responsibility reporting (T1_OFFICIAL, Gartner DEF 14A and gartner.com/en/about/
corporate-responsibility). As an asset-light services business, Gartner's direct environmental
footprint is primarily related to office facilities and conference venue energy use (T4_INFERRED
from business model characteristics).

### 6.2 Social / Workforce

Gartner's workforce diversity and inclusion programs, analyst development, and employee
engagement initiatives are described in its proxy statement and corporate responsibility
communications (T1_OFFICIAL, Gartner DEF 14A). Gartner employs analysts across dozens of
countries and manages significant talent retention risk given the value of analyst expertise
to the business model.

### 6.3 Governance

Gartner's governance structure — board composition, committee independence, executive
compensation philosophy — is disclosed in the annual DEF 14A proxy statement (T1_OFFICIAL).
Key metrics:
- Board independence: Disclosed in DEF 14A board composition table
- CEO compensation: Disclosed in DEF 14A Summary Compensation Table
- Shareholder voting results: Disclosed in 8-K filings following annual meeting

**Overall Project Risk Level for Gartner Content: MEDIUM**

Gartner's primary IP enforcement posture is trademark and copyright protective — particularly
around Magic Quadrant and Hype Cycle content. The risk to this project is manageable with strict
discipline: describe Gartner's research frameworks by name and methodology description without
reproducing charts; cite financial data to 10-K; hedge any interpretation of research
independence with appropriate sourcing (T4_INFERRED; consult legal counsel before any
public commercial distribution).
