## Cross-Channel Synergy Routes

**Route 1: B-21 Raider Program Milestone**
1. USAF and NOC issue coordinated press release on test flight milestone or production delivery
2. NOC Newsroom publishes full release and media kit with cleared photography
3. LinkedIn: program significance framing with engineering community context
4. Twitter/X: factual milestone announcement with Newsroom link
5. YouTube: if video asset cleared for release, milestone highlight published same week
6. Instagram: high-resolution B-21 photography with milestone caption

**Route 2: James Webb Space Telescope Imagery Release**
1. NASA/ESA/CSA publishes new JWST deep-field or planetary imagery (public domain)
2. NOC publishes social content noting its integration and testing role in the JWST program
3. Twitter/X: JWST image share with attribution and brief engineering context note
4. Instagram: full-resolution image with engineering backstory caption
5. LinkedIn: deeper engineering narrative about NOC's role in JWST development
6. YouTube: if new imagery warrants it, short-form explainer on NOC's JWST contribution

**Route 3: STEM Talent Pipeline**
1. University partnership announcement or STEM program event
2. Careers page updated with featured program and location
3. LinkedIn: engineering challenge or employee spotlight story
4. Instagram: facility photography or intern story
5. Twitter/X: STEM awareness day participation with branded hashtag

## Channel Prohibitions

1. **No classified program detail on any public channel** — Content about programs at any classification level is limited to officially released information. (operational security)
2. **No real-time operational commentary** — NOC does not comment publicly on active military operations involving its systems. (standard defense communications protocol)
3. **No political endorsement** — Non-partisan posture maintained strictly; no elected official endorsement in any channel. (T4_INFERRED)
4. **No program cost or pricing disclosure** — Unit costs and contract values beyond what is publicly reported by DoD are not discussed on any channel. (T4_INFERRED)
5. **No unauthorized JWST imagery use** — Proper NASA/ESA/CSA attribution required for all JWST image uses; failure to attribute creates legal exposure. (T3_SECONDARY_RELIABLE, NASA image use policy)
6. **No adversary nation media amplification** — NOC channels do not share, quote, or engage with state-sponsored media from adversary nations. (T4_INFERRED)

## Crisis Protocol

**Scenario 1: B-21 Cost Overrun / Congressional Criticism**
- Response owner: VP Communications + IR team
- Timeline: Response within one news cycle of report publication
- Channel sequence: Official statement via Newsroom → IR filing if material → LinkedIn and Twitter/X with Newsroom link
- Tone: Factual, accountability-forward, solution-oriented; reference specific corrective actions
- Prohibited: Dismissive language toward Congressional oversight; disputing factual findings without documented counter-evidence. (T4_INFERRED)

**Scenario 2: Sentinel Nunn-McCurdy Breach or Program Restructure**
- Response owner: Government Affairs + Communications + IR
- Timeline: Immediate statement acknowledging public disclosure; fuller response within 24 hours
- Channel sequence: IR press release → Newsroom → LinkedIn and Twitter/X
- Tone: Transparent acknowledgment of cost situation, commitment to program mission, context on technical complexity driving cost
- Prohibited: Minimizing Congressional or public concern; premature commitments on revised cost estimates before analysis is complete. (T4_INFERRED)

**Scenario 3: Supply Chain or Cybersecurity Incident**
- Response owner: CISO + Communications + DoD Program Security Officers
- Timeline: Government customer and regulatory notification before any public statement per contractual requirements
- Channel sequence: Direct government notification → regulatory disclosure → controlled public statement if required
- Tone: Precise, factual, security-aware
- Prohibited: Public technical vulnerability details; minimizing impact before investigation completion. (T4_INFERRED)

**Scenario 4: JWST Mission Anomaly**
- Response owner: Communications + Space Systems business area
- Timeline: Defer to NASA as the prime public communicator; NOC provides supplemental engineering support context only
- Channel sequence: Reference NASA's official communication; do not independently characterize mission status
- Tone: Supportive of NASA communication; focused on engineering collaboration
- Note: NASA is the mission authority and primary public communicator for JWST events; NOC should amplify NASA's official statements, not compete with them. (T4_INFERRED)

## Steal Sheet — 3 Transferable Channel Principles

**1. Public domain assets can be your most powerful channel content.**
James Webb Space Telescope imagery is NASA/ESA/CSA public domain — yet it is Northrop Grumman's highest-engagement social content because it is visually extraordinary and directly documents NOC's engineering capability. The content is free; the credibility it delivers is priceless. Transferable: identify publicly available assets (government publications, academic papers, public data visualizations) that document your work and build a content strategy around sharing them with appropriate context.

**2. B-21 reveal footage was the most efficient defense brand investment in a decade.**
When the B-21 Raider rolled out publicly in December 2022, Northrop Grumman's social media reach and earned media coverage surpassed what most defense advertising budgets could achieve. The reveal event was engineered as a media moment. Transferable: in B2B markets, identify the one physical or digital reveal moment in your product development cycle that is inherently newsworthy and invest in making it a media-worthy event — the earned coverage compounds the investment.

**3. Separate the defense audience channel from the talent audience channel.**
Northrop Grumman uses LinkedIn, Twitter/X, and conference presence for its defense procurement audience, while Instagram and JWST imagery serve its engineering talent pipeline audience. These audiences require different content registers and different visual languages. Transferable: if your product audience and your talent audience are meaningfully different in demographics and interests, maintain separate content tracks rather than blending them into a single brand voice that serves neither well.
