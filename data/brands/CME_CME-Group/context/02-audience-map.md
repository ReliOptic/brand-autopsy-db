# 02 — Audience Map
## CME Group | Financials — Derivatives Exchange

---

## 1. Audience Architecture Overview

CME Group serves a layered, professional-grade audience spanning buy-side institutions, sell-side intermediaries, commercial end-users, proprietary trading firms, regulators, and a nascent retail-adjacent segment. The audience is not homogeneous — product access, risk appetite, and communication preferences vary sharply by segment. CME's messaging stack must accommodate simultaneous conversations with a central bank treasury desk, a grain elevator operator in Iowa, and a systematic quant fund in Singapore.

**Primary Audience Axis:** Institutional B2B
**Secondary Audience Axis:** Sophisticated retail / professional individual traders
**Regulatory Audience:** CFTC, SEC, Federal Reserve, international equivalents (FCA, ESMA, MAS)

## 7. Personas

### Persona 1 — The Asian Macro Fund PM

**Name:** Haruto Nakamura
**Age:** 48
**Ethnicity / Background:** Japanese
**Location:** Tokyo, Japan
**Occupation:** Portfolio Manager, Japan-domiciled Global Macro Hedge Fund (~$4B AUM)
**Education:** BA Economics, University of Tokyo; MBA, Wharton School

**Profile:**
Haruto runs a discretionary global macro book with significant exposure to US interest rates, equity indices, and FX. He trades CME's SOFR futures and E-mini S&P 500 during Asian hours via CME Globex. He engages directly with CME's Tokyo office for product and regulatory updates and relies heavily on CME's research output to calibrate his macro thesis.

**Goals:**
- Access deep US rate and equity futures markets from Tokyo time zone with confidence in GLOBEX liquidity
- Stay ahead of LIBOR-to-SOFR transition implications for his existing rate book
- Engage with CME's research team for data on positioning and open interest

**Pain Points:**
- Asian-hours liquidity in certain CME products thins materially compared to US session
- CME's Japanese-language content is limited; English-only research creates friction for junior staff
- Regulatory differences between Japan FSA rules and CFTC requirements require dual compliance effort

**CME Products Used:** SOFR Futures, E-mini S&P 500, E-mini NASDAQ-100, JPY/USD Futures
**Brand Relationship:** Engaged institutional client; participates in CME conferences and provides feedback through exchange advisory committees.

### Persona 2 — The Agricultural Cooperative CFO

**Name:** Josephine "Jo" Amara
**Age:** 54
**Ethnicity / Background:** African-American (Louisiana Creole heritage)
**Location:** Memphis, Tennessee, USA
**Occupation:** CFO, Mid-Size Grain Cooperative
**Education:** BS Accounting, Xavier University of Louisiana; CPA

**Profile:**
Jo oversees the financial risk management for a cooperative that aggregates corn, soybeans, and wheat from hundreds of farm members. She uses CBOT futures (via CME Group) to hedge the cooperative's grain price exposure and lock in margins for the growing season. She does not trade directly — she works through a registered commodity broker — but she approves the hedging strategy and interacts with CME's compliance and reporting tools.

**Goals:**
- Protect cooperative member revenues from commodity price volatility
- Understand basis risk between CBOT futures prices and local cash prices
- Ensure hedge accounting treatment is properly documented for cooperative financials

**Pain Points:**
- Margin calls during adverse price moves strain the cooperative's operating liquidity
- CBOT futures specifications require ongoing education for her board of directors
- CME's institutional-facing materials are not calibrated for cooperative financial structures

**CME Products Used:** CBOT Corn Futures, CBOT Soybean Futures, CBOT Wheat Futures
**Brand Relationship:** Indirect, intermediated by broker. Views CME as necessary infrastructure. Would benefit from more cooperative-specific educational content.

### Persona 3 — The European Bank Rates Trader

**Name:** Karim El-Fassi
**Age:** 38
**Ethnicity / Background:** Moroccan-French
**Location:** Paris, France
**Occupation:** Senior Rates Trader, European Universal Bank
**Education:** Grandes Ecoles — Ecole Polytechnique; MSc Financial Engineering

**Profile:**
Karim manages a cross-currency interest rate portfolio spanning EUR and USD instruments. He uses CME Treasury futures and SOFR futures to hedge USD rate exposure alongside Eurex Bund products for EUR rates. He is the CME relationship manager's key contact at his bank and participates in CME's EMEA institutional client advisory sessions.

- Participate in CME-Eurex cross-margining programs where available

- European trading hours create latency disadvantage vs. US-based competitors in CME products
- EMIR and CFTC dual compliance reporting is a persistent operational burden
- CME's margining methodology (SPAN 2) requires recalibration of internal risk models

**CME Products Used:** US Treasury Futures, SOFR Futures, EUR/USD Futures, CME ERIS Swap Futures
**Brand Relationship:** Strategic institutional partner; attends CME's EMEA conferences. Advocates internally for CME products vs. Eurex equivalents based on liquidity depth.
