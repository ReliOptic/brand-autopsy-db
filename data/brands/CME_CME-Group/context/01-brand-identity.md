## 9. Steal Sheets

### Steal Sheet 1 — Price Discovery Narrative

**What CME owns:** CME's benchmark prices for S&P 500 futures, WTI Crude, SOFR, and gold are the reference prices for global financial markets. Bloomberg terminals, financial news, and central bank analyses quote CME settlement prices.

**How to steal it:** Any competitor entering these asset classes must either reference CME prices (validating CME's authority) or build liquidity from scratch (a years-long proposition). Own the narrative that "price" without CME's price discovery is just an opinion.

**Headline:** "Markets don't set the price. CME does — and the world agrees."

---

### Steal Sheet 2 — Clearing Safety Moat

**What CME owns:** CME Clearing is one of the world's largest central counterparty clearing houses (CCPs). Post-2008 Dodd-Frank mandates require most standardized derivatives to clear centrally — CME benefits structurally from this regulatory environment.

**How to steal it:** Bilateral OTC risk and offshore venues without comparable clearing cannot match the counterparty safety CME provides. Emphasize that clearing is not a commodity — CME's default fund, margin methodology (SPAN 2), and regulatory standing are decades of institutional trust.

**Headline:** "The counterparty behind your counterparty. CME Clearing — every trade, every day."

---

### Steal Sheet 3 — Globex Uptime and Liquidity Depth

**What CME owns:** CME Globex operates 23 hours per day, 5 days per week, with documented uptime exceeding 99.9%. The co-location facility in Aurora, Illinois is the operational heartbeat for the majority of global systematic trading firms.

**How to steal it:** Competing venues that experience unplanned outages or thin liquidity during off-hours sessions lose the confidence of algorithmic traders and risk managers who cannot tolerate gaps. CME's infrastructure reliability is a switching cost, not just a feature.

**Headline:** "The market that never sleeps. CME Globex — 23 hours of continuous price formation."
