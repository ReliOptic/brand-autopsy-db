## Channel Prohibitions

1. Do not run acquisition creative on channels where current rates or terms cannot be dynamically updated — regulatory compliance requires current disclosures
2. Do not use user-generated content in paid media without explicit written consent
3. Do not target existing cardmembers with acquisition offers in paid media (suppression lists required)
4. Do not publish benefit values without linking to current terms (benefit amounts change; static claims create compliance exposure)
5. Do not allow affiliate partners to make claims AmEx has not approved in writing

## Crisis Protocol

### Scenario 1: Data Breach / Security Incident
- **Immediate**: Activate cardmember notification protocol per regulatory requirements (GDPR, state breach laws)
- **Communication**: Direct email to affected cardmembers within mandated timeframe; no social media first
- **Tone**: Factual, action-oriented, no minimization — "Here is what happened, here is what we are doing, here is what you should do"
- **Channel sequence**: Regulatory notification → Cardmember email → Press release → Social acknowledgment

### Scenario 2: Viral Complaint / Service Failure
- **Detection**: Social listening on brand mentions + sentiment
- **Response time**: Within 2 hours on Twitter/X; within 4 hours on other channels
- **Tone**: Empathetic acknowledgment; direct to private resolution channel
- **Escalation**: Customer Relations team, not social media manager, resolves substantive complaints

### Scenario 3: Regulatory Action (fee, merchant dispute)
- **Channel**: Investor Relations first (SEC disclosure if material); then corporate communications
- **Social**: Do not comment on active regulatory matters on social media; direct to official statement
- **Employee guidance**: Activate internal comms to prevent employee speculation on public channels

## Steal Sheet — 3 Transferable Principles

**1. Use owned content to generate inbound acquisition at zero marginal cost.**
OPEN Forum generates millions of organic visits from small business owners who never see an AmEx ad. The content earns the relationship before the product is introduced. Transferable: a content hub that answers your target customer's real questions is a permanent acquisition asset that compounds over time, unlike paid media that stops the moment spend stops.

**2. Design channel sequences, not channel tactics.**
AmEx's highest-converting acquisition path is not a single ad — it is a Search → Landing Page → Application → Welcome Email → Onboarding Sequence chain. Each step is designed to hand off to the next. Transferable: map your customer's decision journey and audit whether each channel hands off by design to the next, or whether each channel operates as an island.

**3. Suppress cross-audience messaging with precision.**
Showing acquisition offers to existing cardmembers creates brand confusion and is a wasted media dollar. AmEx's CRM sophistication — built on the closed-loop transaction network — allows precise suppression. Transferable even at smaller scale: maintain clean audience lists and suppress existing customers from acquisition campaigns. The savings fund better retention communication.
