## Steal Sheet — Financial Anatomy

**What works and why:**

1. **Booking-to-revenue ratio as a forward signal** — Accenture reports new bookings ($81B in FY2024) exceeding revenue ($64.9B), a ratio that provides a leading indicator of future revenue. For any B2B brand, tracking the pipeline-to-revenue ratio — even informally — reveals whether growth is decelerating before it shows up in revenue figures (T4_INFERRED).

2. **Acquisition-as-capability strategy** — ~40 acquisitions in a single fiscal year suggests Accenture treats M&A as an R&D substitute: buying proven technology capabilities and talent rather than building from scratch. For smaller firms, this translates to: consider acqui-hires or partnership integrations when organic capability development would take too long to meet market timing (T4_INFERRED).

3. **Asset-light model maximizes return on brand equity** — Accenture generates ~$65B in revenue with minimal fixed assets. The brand is the primary asset — it enables premium pricing, client retention, and talent acquisition simultaneously. This reinforces the argument that brand investment in professional services is not a cost center but a direct revenue multiplier (T4_INFERRED).
