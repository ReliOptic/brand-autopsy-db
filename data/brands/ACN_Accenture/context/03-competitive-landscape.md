## Battle Card 1: Accenture vs. Deloitte (Full-Service Professional Services)

| Dimension | Accenture | Deloitte |
|-----------|-----------|----------|
| **Revenue model** | 100% consulting/technology/operations — no audit or tax | Audit and tax generate ~55% of revenue; consulting and advisory generate ~45% (estimated) |
| **Conflict advantage** | No audit conflicts — Accenture can advise and implement for any company, including audit clients of competitors | Audit independence rules restrict advisory work with audit clients (SOX/PCAOB constraints) |
| **Technology focus** | Deep alliance ecosystem (AWS, Azure, Google, SAP, Salesforce) with dedicated practice groups | Strong Microsoft and SAP practices; less dominant in cloud-native and hyperscaler alliances |
| **Brand perception** | "The transformation company" — associated with large-scale digital change | "The trusted advisor" — associated with risk management, regulatory compliance, and financial integrity |
| **Talent model** | Heavy offshore/nearshore delivery leverage (India, Philippines, Eastern Europe) | More balanced on/offshore mix; higher proportion of local market delivery |

**Accenture's play**: No audit conflicts. Accenture can pursue advisory mandates at companies where Deloitte's audit relationship creates a legal barrier. Additionally, Accenture's pure-play focus means 100% of investment goes to consulting/technology, while Deloitte must allocate across audit, tax, and advisory. (T4_INFERRED)

**Deloitte's play**: The audit relationship is the deepest enterprise relationship in professional services — auditors see everything. When a Deloitte audit client needs advisory work that is not conflict-restricted, Deloitte's existing institutional knowledge and relationship depth create a formidable incumbent advantage. (T4_INFERRED)

## Battle Card 2: Accenture vs. Infosys/TCS (Indian IT Services)

| Dimension | Accenture | Infosys / TCS |
|-----------|-----------|---------------|
| **Blended bill rate** | $150-$350/hr (estimated) | $50-$150/hr (estimated) |
| **Delivery model** | Onshore advisory + offshore delivery; higher onshore ratio for strategic work | Offshore-first with onshore presence for client management |
| **Strategic advisory** | Full-capability strategy practice (Accenture Strategy, formerly separate from implementation) | Growing advisory capabilities but still primarily associated with implementation and managed services |
| **Technology alliances** | Premier/highest tier partner for all major platforms | Strong partnerships but often positioned as implementation partner, not strategic advisor |
| **Brand perception** | Premium transformation partner | Cost-effective, reliable technology delivery |

**Accenture's play**: Position on value delivered, not cost per hour. Accenture wins when the client conversation is about transformation outcomes, not staff augmentation rates. The strategy-to-implementation continuum justifies the premium because the client avoids the "translation loss" of handing a McKinsey strategy deck to an Infosys implementation team. (T4_INFERRED)

**Infosys/TCS play**: Price transparency and engineering depth. In deals where the scope is well-defined (cloud migration, application modernization, testing), the strategy overlay is unnecessary and the cost differential is difficult to justify. Indian IT firms increasingly invest in design and consulting capabilities (Infosys acquired Wongdoody; TCS has built TCS Interactive) to close the perception gap. (T4_INFERRED)

## Battle Card 3: Accenture vs. McKinsey (Strategic Advisory)

| Dimension | Accenture | McKinsey |
|-----------|-----------|----------|
| **Revenue** | $64.1B (SEC 10-K) | ~$16B (estimated, private) |
| **Service model** | Strategy + implementation + operations | Strategy + implementation (QuantumBlack, McKinsey Digital, Leap) |
| **Client entry point** | CIO, CTO, CFO, and increasingly CEO | CEO and board — highest executive entry point in the industry |
| **Brand prestige** | Respected but not exclusive | Highest prestige brand in professional services (estimated, based on industry surveys) |
| **Implementation scale** | 733,000 people; can staff programs of thousands | ~45,000 people; implementation teams are smaller and more selective |
| **Pricing** | $150-$350/hr blended | $500-$1,000+/hr (estimated) |

**Accenture's play**: "We do what McKinsey recommends." Accenture positions itself as the firm that turns strategy into reality. When a CEO receives a McKinsey recommendation, the question becomes "who executes?" Accenture's scale and technology capability make it the natural implementation partner — sometimes even for McKinsey's own recommendations. (T4_INFERRED)

**McKinsey's play**: "We think, they do." McKinsey maintains the perception that it operates at a higher intellectual altitude — shaping corporate strategy, advising on M&A, and influencing board decisions on existential questions. The implicit claim is that Accenture is excellent at execution but does not match McKinsey's strategic depth. (T4_INFERRED)

## Threats

1. **AI-driven disruption of consulting's labor model**: Generative AI has the potential to automate significant portions of Accenture's delivery — code generation, document analysis, testing, data migration. If AI reduces the human labor required for technology implementations by 30-50%, Accenture's revenue model (which is fundamentally labor-hours × bill rate) faces structural compression. (T4_INFERRED)

2. **Hyperscaler professional services expansion**: AWS, Microsoft, and Google are expanding their own professional services teams. Each hyperscaler has an economic incentive to bring implementation in-house rather than paying Accenture margins on top of platform fees. (T4_INFERRED)

3. **Indian IT services moving upmarket**: Infosys, TCS, and Wipro are investing heavily in design, strategy, and advisory capabilities. As the quality gap narrows, the price gap becomes harder to justify. (T4_INFERRED)

4. **Economic cycle sensitivity**: Enterprise discretionary spending on consulting and transformation programs is among the first budget lines cut in a recession. Accenture's revenue growth is correlated with global GDP growth and enterprise IT spending cycles. (SEC 10-K FY2023 Risk Factors)

---

## Opportunities

1. **Generative AI implementation wave**: While AI threatens the labor model, it also creates an enormous near-term demand for AI strategy, implementation, and change management services. Accenture has invested $3B in AI capabilities and formed dedicated AI practices. (official, Accenture press release 2023) The company that helps enterprises adopt AI captures the next decade's largest consulting spend.

2. **Industry cloud platforms**: Cloud adoption is moving from infrastructure migration to industry-specific cloud platforms (healthcare cloud, financial services cloud, manufacturing cloud). Accenture's industry expertise + cloud alliances position it to lead this second wave. (T4_INFERRED)

3. **Cybersecurity services**: Accenture Security is one of the largest managed security services providers globally. As attack surfaces expand and regulatory requirements tighten, security consulting and managed services represent a high-growth, high-margin opportunity. (SEC 10-K FY2023)

4. **Sustainability and ESG advisory**: As companies face mandatory ESG disclosure requirements (SEC climate rules, EU CSRD), demand for sustainability strategy and reporting implementation is growing. Accenture has positioned sustainability as a core service line. (official, accenture.com/sustainability)

## Steal Sheet — 3 Transferable Principles

**1. Occupy the gap between two established competitors.**
Accenture's strategic brilliance is positioning itself between McKinsey (strategy) and Infosys (implementation). By claiming both capabilities, Accenture captures clients who want a single partner for the full journey. Transferable structure: identify two established competitors in your market who each own one half of the value chain. Position yourself as the bridge that eliminates the handoff.

**2. Turn your largest cost into your primary differentiator.**
Accenture's 733,000 employees are its largest expense and its most compelling selling point. "We can staff your 500-person program in weeks" is a scale claim no competitor can match. Transferable insight: instead of trying to minimize your largest cost, ask whether it can be reframed as a capability that smaller competitors cannot replicate.

**3. Treat thought leadership as a pipeline investment, not a marketing expense.**
The Accenture Technology Vision report does not generate direct revenue. But it positions Accenture as the Sage — the firm that understands where technology is headed. CIOs who read the report and find it credible are pre-sold on Accenture's expertise before the first sales meeting. Transferable structure: calculate how much you spend on thought leadership content and measure it against pipeline influence, not content engagement metrics. The ROI is in the sales conversation it enables, not the downloads it generates.
