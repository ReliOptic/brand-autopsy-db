## Part F: Writing Risk Guide — Company-Specific Dos and Don'ts

This section provides concrete guidance for writing about Accenture in this project's content.

### Permitted Language

| Context | Permitted expression | Reason |
|---|---|---|
| Financial facts | "Accenture reported revenue of approximately $64.9B in FY2024 (SEC 10-K)" | Sourced from official SEC filing |
| Service description | "Accenture describes its services as..." followed by official quote | Attribution to official source |
| Competitive position | "Accenture's FY2024 revenue of ~$64.9B (SEC 10-K) is approximately 2.2x TCS's reported ~$29.1B (TCS annual report)" | Metric-based comparison with dual sourcing |
| Legal proceedings | "Accenture disclosed in its FY2024 annual filing that it is party to various legal proceedings in the ordinary course of business (SEC 10-K)" | Verbatim SEC disclosure characterization |
| AI capabilities | "Accenture reported approximately $3B in new generative AI bookings in FY2024 (SEC 10-K)" | Directly sourced metric |
| ESG | "Accenture states a target of net zero operational emissions by 2025 (official, 360° Value Report)" | Attributed commitment, not verified outcome |
| Brand positioning | "Accenture's observed public positioning indicates..." (T4_INFERRED) | Clearly labeled interpretation |

### Prohibited Language

| Prohibited expression | Risk | Permitted alternative |
|---|---|---|
| "Accenture defrauded..." | Defamation; fraud allegation without judicial finding | Reference only formally adjudicated fraud findings with specific citation |
| Attributing conscious misleading without a formal finding | Intent attribution without evidence | "Publicly reported proceedings alleged..." (with specific court citation) |
| "Accenture's AI systems are biased" | Unverifiable assertion without specific study | "Questions have been raised in [cited publication] about AI governance standards in enterprise consulting contexts" |
| Asserting that all observers share a negative view of Accenture's delivery record | Unsourced generalisation prohibited by project policy | Remove entirely; replace with specific cited client complaint or regulatory finding if available |
| "Accenture's workforce is exploited" | Pejorative assertion without regulatory finding | Reference specific regulatory findings if documented; otherwise omit |
| Claiming a named competitor outperforms Accenture without a metric basis | Unhedged comparison prohibited by project policy | "Competitor A reported operating margin of Y% (source) versus Accenture's Z% (SEC 10-K)" |
| "Accenture's strategy is to corner the market" | Hidden motive attribution | "Accenture's observed market actions include..." followed by factual description |
| "Clearly, Accenture's management knew about..." | Intent attribution | Remove; reference only formally documented knowledge |

### Comparative Writing Rules

When comparing Accenture to competitors in this project:
1. Always use specific named metrics from specific named sources
2. State the fiscal year of each metric cited
3. Acknowledge if comparison is across different business models (different margin profiles reflect different delivery mix, not superiority or inferiority)
4. Label market share or competitive standing estimates as (estimated) with basis stated
