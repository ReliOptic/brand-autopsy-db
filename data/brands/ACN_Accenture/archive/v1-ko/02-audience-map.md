# Accenture — Layer 02: Audience Map

## Audience Overview

Accenture's primary decision-making audience is concentrated in C-suite and senior technology leadership roles within large enterprises and government bodies (SEC 10-K FY2024). This is a B2B and B2G (business-to-government) engagement model. Purchasing cycles range from several months to multiple years, and average contract values reach the millions of dollars (estimated). The audience segments into three functional roles: economic buyers (CEO, CFO), technical buyers (CIO, CTO, CDO), and influencers (program directors, middle management, and analyst firms).

## Persona 1: The Transformation-Pressured CEO

**Role:** Chief Executive Officer, large-cap manufacturing or industrial conglomerate
**Tenure:** 2–4 years into current role
**Background:** Engineering or finance background; board-facing accountability for digital transformation delivery (T4_INFERRED)

**Core pain points:**
- Internal IT organization is absorbed by legacy system maintenance and lacks capacity for transformation initiatives (T4_INFERRED)
- Strategy consulting engagements have produced roadmaps that stall at the implementation stage
- Board and shareholders require demonstrable ROI on digital investment within defined timeframes

**Core gains sought:**
- A single accountable partner from strategy through implementation — eliminates multi-vendor coordination overhead
- Outcome-based engagement structures that share risk
- Global reference cases: "organizations comparable to ours have done this" — the board-facing proof point

**Accenture touchpoints (T4_INFERRED):**
Accenture Technology Vision annual report → CEO forum or World Economic Forum session → executive briefing → RFP issuance

**Message key:**
> "Total Enterprise Reinvention — one partner accountable from strategy through delivery, with measurable outcomes." (observed on website framing)

## Persona 2: The Cloud Migration CIO

**Role:** Chief Information Officer, major financial institution
**Tenure:** 1–3 years in current role
**Background:** Infrastructure and architecture background; mandated to migrate core systems to cloud while managing regulatory compliance (T4_INFERRED)

**Core pain points:**
- Legacy core banking systems carry regulatory risk during cloud migration (T4_INFERRED)
- Internal teams have limited cloud-native capability; external hiring is constrained by budget
- Multi-cloud vendor selection requires neutral guidance; incumbent vendors introduce selection bias

**Core gains sought:**
- A partner that designs and implements multi-cloud architecture without platform vendor bias
- Demonstrated regulatory compliance experience within financial services
- Managed services continuity post-migration to avoid a capability cliff

**Accenture touchpoints (T4_INFERRED):**
Alliance channel introduction via AWS or Microsoft → proof-of-concept project → enterprise contract

**Message key:**
> "Cloud at scale. Industry-specific. Risk-managed." (T4_INFERRED from observed communications)

## Persona 3: The AI Strategy CDO

**Role:** Chief Digital Officer, large retail or consumer goods enterprise
**Tenure:** First year in a newly created role
**Background:** Technology startup background; recruited to lead enterprise AI and data strategy (T4_INFERRED)

**Core pain points:**
- Generative AI pilots are proliferating without a path to enterprise-scale deployment
- Data silos degrade model training data quality, limiting AI performance
- The strategy question ("what should we do with AI?") and the implementation question ("how do we build it?") require simultaneous resolution

**Core gains sought:**
- AI strategy roadmap, data platform architecture, and model deployment under a single engagement
- Global retail or consumer sector AI reference cases for internal board presentations
- Internal AI capability building via training programs alongside external implementation

**Accenture touchpoints (T4_INFERRED):**
Accenture AI research publication → CDO network referral → Applied Intelligence team engagement

**Message key:**
> "AI at the core. Data-led, cloud-enabled." (observed on website)

## Steal Sheet — Audience Map

**What works and why:**

1. **Dual content stream for economic vs. technical buyers** — Accenture maintains separate research series for CEO-level audiences (Technology Vision, Life Trends — the "why") and CIO-level audiences (technical implementation guides — the "how"). Any B2B brand can replicate this by packaging the same solution as a one-page executive summary and a separate technical specification document (T4_INFERRED).

2. **Alliance channels as audience acquisition** — Accenture gains exposure to existing AWS, SAP, and Salesforce customers through platform partner programs. This converts incumbent vendor relationships into introductions. The structural lesson: identify which platforms your target clients already use, and establish partner presence within those platforms (T4_INFERRED).

3. **Pain → Frame → Proof sales narrative** — Observed Accenture executive engagement sequences consistently open with industry-specific pain definition, follow with an Accenture-framed solution model, and close with a same-industry reference case. This sequence can be adopted in any proposal structure: reorder from "our solution" to "your industry's problem → our approach → comparable success" (T4_INFERRED).
