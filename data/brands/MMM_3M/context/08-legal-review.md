## Major Litigation — Critical for 3M Analysis

### 1. PFAS Public Water System Litigation — $10.3B Settlement

**Nature**: Class action and multi-district litigation brought by public water utilities and municipal water systems across the United States alleging contamination of drinking water supplies by per- and polyfluoroalkyl substances (PFAS) used in 3M's manufacturing operations and products, including Aqueous Film Forming Foam (AFFF).

**Status**: Settlement agreement announced June 2023; 3M agreed to pay approximately $10.3B over 13 years to resolve claims from public water system operators. (SEC 10-K FY2023; T3_SECONDARY_RELIABLE from Bloomberg, Reuters)

**Key disclosures from SEC filings**:
- Settlement does not constitute an admission of liability by 3M (SEC 10-K FY2023)
- Additional claims from individual plaintiffs and state attorneys general remain outstanding as of the FY2023 10-K filing date
- 3M announced the acceleration of its PFAS phase-out, targeting the end of PFAS manufacturing by end of 2025 (official, 3M press release)

**Writing guidance**: Reference this settlement using disclosed figures and SEC language. Do not characterize 3M's conduct as intentional, fraudulent, or criminally motivated. The settlement was reached without admission of liability. Regulatory and scientific bodies have raised questions about PFAS health effects; those characterizations should be attributed to those bodies, not asserted independently.

---

### 2. Combat Arms Earplug Litigation — ~$6B Settlement

**Nature**: Mass tort litigation by U.S. military veterans alleging that dual-ended Combat Arms earplugs (version 2) manufactured by Aearo Technologies (acquired by 3M in 2008) were defective and provided insufficient hearing protection, resulting in hearing loss and tinnitus.

**Status**: 3M announced a $6.01B settlement in August 2023 to resolve approximately 260,000+ claims. (SEC 10-K FY2023; T3_SECONDARY_RELIABLE from Bloomberg, Reuters, WSJ)

**Key disclosures from SEC filings**:
- Settlement resolves the multi-district litigation (MDL) in the U.S. District Court for the Northern District of Florida
- 3M's attempt to use subsidiary Aearo Technologies' bankruptcy to limit liability was rejected by federal courts prior to the settlement (T3_SECONDARY_RELIABLE, widely reported)
- Settlement does not constitute an admission of liability (SEC 10-K FY2023)

**Writing guidance**: This litigation is one of the largest mass tort settlements in U.S. history as measured by claimant count. It may be described factually using the disclosed settlement figure and claimant count. The hearing protection failure allegations were the basis of the litigation; this fact is publicly established through court records. Do not characterize 3M's original conduct as intentional concealment without citing specific court findings to that effect.

---

### 3. Solventum Spin-off — April 2024

**Nature**: Corporate transaction separating 3M's healthcare business into an independent publicly traded company, Solventum Corporation (ticker: SOLV).

**Status**: Completed April 1, 2024. Solventum commenced trading on the NYSE on April 1, 2024. 3M received approximately $7.7B in cash consideration as part of the transaction. (SEC 8-K April 2024; official, 3M press release)

**Writing guidance**: The spin-off is a matter of public record and may be described factually. The $7.7B cash consideration figure is from official disclosures. The strategic rationale (focus on industrial and consumer segments; allow healthcare to operate independently) is management's stated rationale from official communications and may be described as such with attribution.

---

### 4. Other Litigation Notes

- **State attorney general PFAS claims**: Multiple states have filed or reserved the right to file PFAS-related claims outside the scope of the public water system settlement. These remain unresolved as of the FY2023 10-K filing date. (SEC 10-K FY2023)
- **International PFAS regulatory exposure**: EU and other jurisdictions are advancing PFAS restrictions. 3M has disclosed regulatory risk from international actions in its 10-K risk factors. (SEC 10-K FY2023)
- **Asbestos legacy claims**: 3M faces a small volume of ongoing asbestos-related claims through subsidiaries. These are disclosed as a contingent liability in the 10-K but are described as not material to the overall financial position. (SEC 10-K FY2023)

## Project Risk Assessment

### Litigation Posture of 3M as Analysis Subject

3M is a highly litigious company by necessity — the scale of the PFAS and earplug settlements has made it one of the most monitored corporations in the United States for legal exposure. However, 3M does not have a documented history of pursuing defamation or false light claims against research institutions, journalists, or analytical publications. (T4_INFERRED from public record review)

**Risk level for this project**: Medium. The primary risk arises from:
1. Overstating the severity or culpability of PFAS or earplug conduct beyond what has been legally established
2. Characterizing 3M's actions with intent-attribution language (e.g., asserting that the company had prior awareness of defects and chose to conceal them) without citing specific court findings that establish such facts
3. Extrapolating from settled litigation to unresolved claims in a way that prejudges pending proceedings

## Writing Risk Guide — 3M Specific

### SAFE TO WRITE

- Settlement figures from SEC filings with citation: "3M announced a $10.3B settlement with public water utilities in June 2023. (SEC 10-K FY2023)"
- Factual product descriptions based on official materials: "3M's N95 respirators use electrostatically charged nonwoven filtration media. (official, 3m.com)"
- Attribution of regulatory findings to specific agencies: "The EPA has designated certain PFAS compounds as hazardous substances under CERCLA."
- Hedged analytical interpretations clearly labeled: "This analysis interprets the Solventum spin-off as a strategic focus decision. (T4_INFERRED)"
- Reference to litigation as fact of record without characterizing culpability: "3M reached a $6.01B settlement with claimants in the Combat Arms earplug MDL. (SEC 10-K FY2023)"

### HEDGE CAREFULLY

- Statements about ongoing litigation outcomes: Use "as of [date]" and reference the SEC filing period; litigation status may have changed
- Financial projections: Clearly label as estimates with basis stated; distinguish from official guidance
- Credit rating statements: Note that ratings change; advise readers to consult current agency publications
- Statements about the adequacy or completeness of 3M's PFAS remediation: Frame as description of disclosed commitments, not evaluation of sufficiency
- Competitive capability comparisons: Must be metric-based with sources; avoid subjective qualitative comparisons

### DO NOT WRITE

- Any statement attributing intent, knowledge, or motivation to 3M regarding PFAS health impacts or earplug defects that goes beyond what has been established in public court findings
- Any characterization of 3M's PFAS settlement as an "admission" — the settlement explicitly states it is not an admission of liability (SEC 10-K FY2023)
- Any assertion that 3M is "likely to face" future litigation outcomes, criminal charges, or regulatory penalties that are not currently disclosed in public filings
- Any statement claiming that 3M's products caused specific health outcomes to specific populations without citing peer-reviewed scientific findings attributed to those findings' authors
- Any comparison of 3M to specific competitors using subjective quality language ("3M's products are less safe than Honeywell's") without specific, metric-based evidence
- Any statement implying that 3M's post-spin-off financial position indicates impending insolvency or bankruptcy without explicit public evidence
- Use of the words "fraud," "criminal," "cover-up," "conspiracy," or "scheme" in relation to 3M's conduct unless quoting a court finding or regulatory determination that uses those specific terms, with full attribution

## ESG Assessment

### Environmental

3M's environmental record is materially affected by the PFAS issue, which represents one of the most significant corporate environmental legacy liabilities in U.S. industrial history. (T3_SECONDARY_RELIABLE) The company has publicly committed to:
- Exiting all PFAS manufacturing by end of 2025 (official, 3M press release)
- Science-based GHG emissions reduction targets (official, 3M Sustainability Report 2023)
- Water stewardship and waste reduction programs (official, 3M Sustainability Report 2023)

Environmental claims made in this analysis are based on 3M's published sustainability commitments and disclosed litigation settlements. The project does not independently verify 3M's environmental performance claims.

### Social

3M's social record is notable for:
- Long-standing employee innovation culture (15% rule, documented in official 3M Heritage materials)
- Significant U.S. manufacturing employment in Minnesota and other states
- Ongoing scrutiny of workplace safety practices given the nature of the earplug litigation

### Governance

- Board composition: 3M has maintained an independent board majority with separate Chairman and CEO roles (SEC DEF 14A FY2024)
- Executive compensation tied to adjusted earnings, not GAAP earnings — which has the effect of excluding litigation charges from compensation metrics (SEC DEF 14A, T3_SECONDARY_RELIABLE)
- Dividend Aristocrat status requires sustained board commitment to dividend policy over 60+ consecutive years (official, S&P Dow Jones Indices)
