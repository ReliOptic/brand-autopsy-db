## Corporate Structure

Alphabet Inc. (incorporated in Delaware) is the parent holding company of Google LLC and its subsidiaries. Google LLC is the primary operating entity. Alphabet's share classes:
- **Class A (GOOGL)**: One vote per share — publicly traded
- **Class C (GOOG)**: No voting rights — publicly traded (this analysis)
- **Class B**: Ten votes per share — held by founders and insiders; not publicly traded

The economic interest represented by GOOG Class C shares is identical to GOOGL Class A shares on a per-share basis. The distinction is solely in voting rights. (official, Alphabet Certificate of Incorporation, SEC filings)

## Key Financial Trends Relevant to Brand Strategy

1. **Search revenue concentration**: Google Search & Other remains 56% of total revenue, indicating significant dependence on one product category. The 10-K identifies AI competition as a risk to this revenue stream. (official, SEC 10-K FY2023 Risk Factors)

2. **Cloud growth trajectory**: Google Cloud's 26% revenue growth and first full year of profitability signals increasing strategic importance. The company has stated cloud and AI as priority investment areas. (official, Alphabet earnings calls FY2023)

3. **Cost discipline**: Operating margin improved from 26.5% to 27.4% YoY, following a widely reported workforce reduction of approximately 12,000 employees in January 2023. (T3_SECONDARY_RELIABLE, widely reported; Alphabet confirmed the reduction in an employee communication published on blog.google)

4. **Capex acceleration for AI**: The $32.3B capex in FY2023 reflects infrastructure investment for AI data centers; the company has indicated further capex acceleration in forward-looking statements in earnings calls. (official, Q4 2023 earnings call transcript, ir.abc.xyz)
