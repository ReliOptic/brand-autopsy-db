## 3. Material Legal Proceedings (Per 10-K Item 3 Disclosures)

The following proceedings are disclosed or referenced in Alphabet's 10-K FY2023 (official, SEC 10-K FY2023, Item 3):

### 3.1 DOJ v. Google (Search Distribution) — Case 1:20-cv-03010
**Status as of FY2023 10-K filing**: Trial concluded; ruling issued August 2024 (post-10-K). The court found that Google violated Section 2 of the Sherman Antitrust Act through exclusive default search agreements. (T1_OFFICIAL, US District Court ruling, August 5, 2024, public record) Remedies phase ongoing. This proceeding is directly relevant to Google's Search channel distribution strategy and the billions paid annually for default search placement agreements. **Writing note for this project**: Report the court's findings by citing the court's language directly. Do not independently characterize Google's conduct.

### 3.2 EU Commission — Android (AT.40099)
**Status**: €4.34B fine imposed July 2018. Alphabet appealed; General Court reduced fine to €4.125B in September 2022. Further appeal pending at Court of Justice of the EU (CJEU). (T1_OFFICIAL, EU Commission press releases; CJEU case register)

### 3.3 EU Commission — Google Shopping (AT.40253)
**Status**: €2.42B fine imposed June 2017. General Court upheld the decision in November 2021. Alphabet appealed to CJEU. (T1_OFFICIAL, EU Commission press releases)

### 3.4 EU Commission — AdSense for Search (AT.40411)
**Status**: €1.49B fine imposed March 2019. Alphabet appealed. (T1_OFFICIAL, EU Commission press release)

### 3.5 Epic Games v. Google (Google Play)
**Status**: Jury found in favor of Epic on all counts in December 2023. Remedies proceedings ongoing; potential implications for Google Play app distribution terms. (T1_OFFICIAL, public court records, US District Court N.D. Cal.)

### 3.6 DOJ v. Google (Ad Technology)
**Status**: DOJ filed suit in January 2023 alleging monopolization in digital advertising technology markets. Trial completed October 2024; ruling pending as of this writing. (T1_OFFICIAL, public court records)

### 3.7 Privacy and Data Proceedings
Multiple regulatory proceedings globally related to data privacy practices. Key jurisdictions include the EU (GDPR enforcement by various Data Protection Authorities), France (CNIL fines for cookie consent), and US state attorneys general. (T1_OFFICIAL, regulatory press releases; T3_SECONDARY_RELIABLE for proceedings not individually material per 10-K)

## 5. Writing Risk Guide for Brand Autopsy DB

This section provides company-specific language guidance to protect this project from legal exposure when writing about Alphabet/Google.

### 5.1 High-Risk Topics — Approach with Extreme Care

**Search dominance / monopoly language**
- PERMITTED: "Google holds an estimated 90%+ share of global search queries, per publicly reported market data."
- PERMITTED: "The US District Court found in August 2024 that Google violated Section 2 of the Sherman Antitrust Act in connection with its search distribution agreements." (cite court; quote court language)
- AVOID: Independently asserting "Google is a monopoly" or "Google behaved anti-competitively" without direct court citation.

**Privacy and data practices**
- PERMITTED: "Google's advertising business is based on serving targeted ads using signals derived from user activity, as disclosed in Google's privacy policy." (official, policies.google.com)
- PERMITTED: "French regulators (CNIL) issued fines related to cookie consent practices." (cite regulator)
- AVOID: "Google surveils its users" — characterizes conduct in a way that implies illegality without regulatory citation.

**EU antitrust fines**
- PERMITTED: "The EU Commission imposed a €4.34B fine in the Android case (AT.40099), finding that Google had abused its dominant position." (cite Commission decision)
- AVOID: Editorializing on the merits beyond citing the Commission's and court's public findings.

### 5.2 Safe Language Patterns for This Analysis

| Topic | Safe Phrasing |
|-------|--------------|
| Market position | "Google holds an estimated [X]% share of [market], per [source]." |
| Legal proceedings | "The [court/regulator] found/alleged/ruled that..." |
| Data practices | "As disclosed in Google's privacy policy..." |
| Competitive behavior | "Based on public filings and court records..." |
| Financial performance | "Per Alphabet's 10-K for FY[year], revenue was $[X]B." |
| AI claims | "As stated in Alphabet's [year] Annual Report..." |

### 5.3 Prohibited in This Project's Analysis of Alphabet/Google

- Asserting that Google is a monopoly without citing a court or regulatory finding to that effect
- Characterizing data collection as "surveillance" without regulatory citation
- Stating that Google's algorithms are biased without citing specific regulatory or peer-reviewed findings
- Attributing intent to Google executives beyond what is stated in public documents
- Reproducing specific financial projections or targets not in public filings

## 6. Digital Services Act (DSA) and AI Act Relevance

Alphabet/Google is designated as a Very Large Online Platform (VLOP) and Very Large Online Search Engine (VLOSE) under the EU Digital Services Act (DSA). This designation imposes enhanced obligations for transparency, content moderation, and risk assessments. (T1_OFFICIAL, EU Commission VLOP/VLOSE designation list, April 2023)

The EU AI Act (adopted 2024) may impose obligations on certain Google AI products depending on risk classification. (T3_SECONDARY_RELIABLE, EU AI Act legislative text; official EU publications)
