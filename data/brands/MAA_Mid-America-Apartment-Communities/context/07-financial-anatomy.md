### 2.2 Revenue Structure Note

MAA operates as a Real Estate Investment Trust (REIT). Revenue is predominantly rental income from apartment communities across Sun Belt and Southeast U.S. markets. Segment-level geographic submarket revenue is disclosed in the 10-K Segment Information section. Specific submarket figures require reference to the full FY2025 10-K filing. (SEC 10-K FY2025)

## 7. Financial Risk Matrix

### Based on SEC 10-K Risk Factors (Item 1A, FY2025)

| Risk Category | Key Content | Brand Strategy Impact |
|--------------|-------------|----------------------|
| Macroeconomic & Occupancy Risk | Unfavorable economic conditions may reduce occupancy levels and rental revenues; unemployment, consumer debt, and housing market downturns are cited as primary drivers | Brand messaging around stability and value may need to adapt if occupancy pressure intensifies; pricing power is a core brand asset at risk |
| Competitive Supply Risk | Increase in supply in multifamily and other rental markets can adversely affect rental rates; oversupply of apartments in specific markets explicitly flagged | MAA's Sun Belt concentration creates vulnerability if developers oversupply target markets, eroding the premium positioning of MAA communities |
| Interest Rate & Financing Risk | Ability to acquire or dispose of properties on economically favorable terms is linked to capital market conditions; higher rates increase borrowing costs | Rising debt costs constrain the development pipeline that supports brand expansion and portfolio quality |
| Geographic Concentration Risk | Major properties concentrated in Sun Belt and Southeast markets; regional economic downturns would disproportionately impact the portfolio | Brand identity as a Sun Belt specialist is both a strength and a concentration risk — regional economic shock could undermine the geographic brand story |
| Regulatory & Housing Policy Risk | Government incentives for home ownership and regulatory changes affecting rental markets could reduce demand or constrain rent growth | Rent regulation in any core market could impair revenue-per-unit growth, which underpins the brand's premium apartment positioning |

> **Original filing**: [10-K Risk Factors — MAA FY2025](https://www.sec.gov/cgi-bin/browse-edgar?action=getcompany&CIK=0000912595&type=10-K&dateb=&owner=include&count=10)

## 8. Brand-Finance Nexus

### 8.1 What Financial Data Reveals About Brand Strategy

| Financial Signal | Brand Strategy Interpretation |
|-----------------|------------------------------|
| Revenue CAGR of +5.5% (FY2021–FY2025) | Consistent organic growth indicates the Sun Belt geographic brand strategy is generating real demand, supported by demographic migration tailwinds (T4_INFERRED) |
| Dividends grew 49% from FY2021 to FY2025 | Demonstrates financial capacity to reward investors while growing the portfolio — signals operational quality that supports institutional credibility |
| SG&A held to ~2.8% of revenue consistently | Lean corporate cost structure; brand investment occurs at the property level through amenities, maintenance, and leasing experience rather than centralized marketing spend |
| Near-zero cash balances ($41M on $11.8B assets) | Capital is continuously deployed into properties; brand quality is maintained through reinvestment rather than balance sheet reserve |
| No share buybacks; consistent dividend-only returns | Capital allocation philosophy prioritizes income distribution over price appreciation — brand is positioned toward income-oriented institutional and retail investors |
