## Organic Revenue Growth Analysis

PepsiCo tracks "organic revenue growth" — defined as net revenue growth excluding the impact of foreign currency translation, acquisitions, and divestitures. This metric is the primary measure of underlying business momentum used in management commentary and investor guidance.

| Period | Organic Revenue Growth (Reported) |
|--------|----------------------------------|
| FY2022 | approximately +14.4% | 
| FY2023 | approximately +9.5% |
| FY2024 | approximately +1.3% |

(T1_OFFICIAL: PepsiCo earnings releases and annual reports; figures are approximate based on reported results)

**Context**: The significant deceleration from FY2022–FY2023 high-growth to the approximately +1.3% reported for FY2024 reflects: (1) cycling against elevated pricing-driven growth in prior years, (2) volume declines in certain North American categories as consumers reacted to elevated price levels, and (3) macro headwinds in certain international markets. PepsiCo management described the volume-versus-price balance rebalancing as a deliberate transition toward recovering volume elasticity. (T1_OFFICIAL: FY2024 Q4 earnings call commentary — Ramon Laguarta, CEO; Hugh Johnston, CFO)

## Key Financial Risks Disclosed in FY2024 10-K

The following risks are disclosed by PepsiCo management as material financial risks. This is not an exhaustive list — see the full 10-K filing for complete risk factor disclosures. (T1_OFFICIAL: PepsiCo FY2024 10-K, Risk Factors section)

| Risk Category | Description |
|---------------|-------------|
| Foreign Currency | PepsiCo generates approximately 42% of revenue outside North America. Currency depreciation in emerging markets (Mexico peso, Brazilian real, etc.) can materially reduce reported revenue and earnings. |
| Commodity Cost Volatility | Corn, potatoes, oats, cooking oils, aluminum, PET plastic, and natural gas are major input costs. Price spikes reduce gross margin unless passed through via pricing. |
| Consumer Volume Elasticity | If pricing actions exceed consumer willingness to pay, volume declines. FY2024 saw volume pressure in certain categories after multiple years of significant price increases. |
| Regulatory / Tax Risk | Sugar taxes, plastic packaging regulations, and changes in food labeling requirements in multiple jurisdictions could increase costs or restrict marketing activities. |
| Walmart / Customer Concentration | Walmart represents approximately 13% of net revenue. Disruption to this relationship would materially impact results. |
| Quaker Recall Aftermath | The FY2023 Salmonella recall affecting Quaker oats products created consumer trust damage, retailer relationship strain, and financial costs that extended into FY2024. |
| Health and Wellness Trends | Sustained reduction in consumer demand for sugar-sweetened beverages and ultra-processed snacks could structurally reduce addressable market size for core products. |
| GLP-1 Drug Adoption | Emerging evidence that GLP-1 weight-loss drugs (Ozempic, Wegovy) reduce appetite and caloric intake has raised analyst questions about long-term snack food demand. PepsiCo management has stated they are monitoring this but do not currently see material impact. (T1_OFFICIAL: FY2024 earnings call Q&A) |

## Steal Sheet — 3 Transferable Financial Anatomy Principles

**1. The Frito-Lay profit engine subsidizes the beverage portfolio's competitive battles.**
Frito-Lay North America's estimated 28–30% operating margins are substantially higher than PepsiCo Beverages North America's estimated 9–11% margins. This structural profit imbalance means PepsiCo's snack dominance effectively funds its ability to invest aggressively in beverage competition with Coca-Cola. Transferable principle: in a diversified portfolio company, identify which business unit is the structural profit engine — then protect that engine above all else in capital allocation decisions.

**2. Dividend King status creates a capital allocation constraint that disciplines all other spending.**
Having raised the dividend for 50+ consecutive years creates both a reputational commitment and a financial floor on cash distribution. Every acquisition, share repurchase, or capital expenditure decision must be evaluated within the constraint of maintaining dividend growth. This discipline prevents capital misallocation into high-risk ventures that would jeopardize the dividend track record. Transferable principle: a standing financial commitment to shareholders (recurring dividend, debt ceiling, margin floor) can be a productive discipline mechanism that prevents strategic drift.

**3. Organic revenue growth rate is the most honest measure of brand portfolio health.**
PepsiCo's management consistently uses "organic revenue growth" as the primary performance indicator precisely because it strips out the effects of currency, acquisitions, and divestitures to reveal whether the underlying brands are actually growing. The deceleration from +14.4% (FY2022) to +1.3% (FY2024) organic growth, while partly structural, is the metric that most honestly represents demand-side health. Transferable principle: identify the most currency-neutral, acquisition-stripped measure of your business's underlying growth — and use that, not total revenue, as the honest performance scorecard.
