## Cross-Channel Synergy Routes

1. **In-store discovery → Social sharing → New customer acquisition**: A customer finds an item, photographs it, posts to TikTok or Instagram with brand and price visible. This organic content drives new store visits from followers who want the same experience. TJX's primary new customer acquisition loop runs through this route, largely without paid media investment. (T4_INFERRED from observed social dynamics)

2. **Email → Store visit → Basket expansion**: TJX's email campaigns highlight specific finds or categories to drive store visits. The email's function is to prime the category expectation (e.g., "Le Creuset cookware in HomeGoods this week"); the store converts on that category and captures additional unplanned purchases. (T4_INFERRED from observed email content and retail conversion dynamics)

3. **Pinterest / Instagram inspiration → HomeGoods visit**: HomeGoods' strong Pinterest and Instagram presence drives a distinct customer journey: browse inspiration content → identify a home décor need state → visit HomeGoods to fulfill it. This route is particularly effective for seasonal home updates. (observed, HomeGoods social channels; T4_INFERRED for conversion path)

## Channel Prohibitions

1. **Do not build an Amazon-competitive e-commerce experience**: TJX has explicitly chosen not to invest in a full-scale e-commerce operation comparable to Amazon or Wayfair. The FY2024 10-K acknowledges the company's limited online presence as a strategic choice. Building a deep online catalog would cannibalize the in-store drive that makes the physical model work. (SEC 10-K FY2024)
2. **Do not publish real-time inventory online**: Showing specific item availability at specific stores would eliminate the need for a store visit. The uncertainty is intentional. (T4_INFERRED from operational strategy)
3. **Do not use influencer marketing that conflicts with authenticity signal**: Paid influencer content that is visibly staged undermines the organic authenticity that makes TJX haul content credible. Any influencer partnership must maintain the casual, real-find aesthetic. (T4_INFERRED)
4. **Do not run persistent promotional sales**: TJX's model is always-on value; there are no "sales" at TJX in the traditional retail sense. Running scheduled promotional sales events (e.g., "20% off this weekend") would reintroduce the full-price retail cadence that TJX's model is designed to escape. (T4_INFERRED from observed pricing model)

## Crisis Protocol

### Scenario 1: "Compare at" price accuracy challenge
**Trigger**: Consumer advocate, journalist, or regulatory body questions whether TJX's "compare at" price tags accurately reflect actual retail prices.
**Response approach**: Do not contest the framing on social media. Refer to company's documented methodology for establishing compare-at prices. Engage through formal corporate communications channels, not banner social media. Emphasize the company's compliance with applicable retail pricing disclosure regulations. (T4_INFERRED from best practices; note: this is a recurring industry-wide issue per T3_SECONDARY_RELIABLE sources)

### Scenario 2: Product safety or recall
**Trigger**: A vendor's merchandise sold through TJX stores is subject to a recall or safety notice.
**Response approach**: Follow CPSC recall protocol. TJX has established recall communication procedures given the breadth of its vendor base. Communicate prominently via website, in-store signage, and email to affected customers. Do not minimize. (T4_INFERRED from retail industry practice; TJX participates in CPSC recall processes per public records)

### Scenario 3: Data breach / customer data incident
**Trigger**: Unauthorized access to customer payment or personal data. Note: TJX experienced a major data breach in 2006-2007 affecting approximately 45.7 million customer accounts, as disclosed in SEC filings and widely reported. (T1_OFFICIAL, SEC 8-K filings 2007; T3_SECONDARY_RELIABLE, extensive news coverage)
**Response approach**: Immediate disclosure per regulatory requirements. Direct customer notification. Offer credit monitoring for affected customers. TJX has historical experience with this scenario and established response frameworks. (T1_OFFICIAL, 2007 breach response documentation)

## Steal Sheet — 2 Transferable Principles

**1. Physical presence is a channel, not a cost.**
TJX's most powerful marketing channel is the store itself. The store layout, merchandise density, and price tag design are calibrated to maximize discovery and basket conversion. Physical retail brands should treat every in-store element — aisle width, product height, signage language — as a channel decision with measurable impact on customer behavior.

**2. Limit online to preserve offline.**
TJX's deliberate under-investment in e-commerce is not a weakness — it is a strategic choice to protect the in-store foot traffic that makes the discovery model work. Not every channel opportunity should be pursued. The right question is not "should we have a great e-commerce experience?" but "what does a great e-commerce experience do to our primary value driver?"
