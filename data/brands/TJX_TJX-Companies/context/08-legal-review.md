## Major Litigation & Legal History

### 2006–2007 Data Breach — Settled
TJX disclosed in January 2007 that it had experienced an unauthorized intrusion into its computer systems. The breach was subsequently determined to have compromised approximately 45.7 million customer payment card accounts, making it one of the largest retail data breaches reported at the time. (T1_OFFICIAL, SEC 8-K filing January 2007; T3_SECONDARY_RELIABLE, extensively reported by WSJ, Reuters, and others)

TJX reached settlements with multiple parties including:
- Visa and MasterCard issuing banks — settlement totals were disclosed in SEC filings but exact figures varied by tranche. TJX recorded charges of approximately $197 million related to breach costs as of FY2008. (T1_OFFICIAL, SEC 10-K FY2008)
- State attorneys general — multi-state settlement of $9.75 million. (T3_SECONDARY_RELIABLE, state AG press releases)
- Consumer class action — settlement provided affected customers with vouchers and limited cash. (T3_SECONDARY_RELIABLE, court records coverage)

**Current status**: All major litigation from the 2006-2007 breach is resolved. TJX has invested substantially in cybersecurity infrastructure post-breach. (T1_OFFICIAL, subsequent SEC 10-K filings)

### "Compare At" Pricing Litigation
TJX has faced class action litigation in multiple jurisdictions challenging the accuracy of its "compare at" price tags — the labels that display an alleged original retail price alongside TJX's lower selling price. Plaintiffs in such cases have alleged that the "compare at" prices do not always reflect prices at which items were actually sold at regular retail. (T3_SECONDARY_RELIABLE, court filings and legal journalism)

Note: This is a recurring issue across the off-price retail industry broadly, not unique to TJX. Ross Stores and Burlington have faced similar litigation. The legal and regulatory risk around retail price comparison advertising is an industry-level exposure. (T3_SECONDARY_RELIABLE)

**Project note**: When writing about TJX's pricing model, this analysis avoids asserting that TJX's "compare at" prices are inaccurate or constitute any form of misrepresentation. These are active and contested legal matters. See Writing Risk Guide below.

## Regulatory Landscape

### FTC and State Consumer Protection
The FTC and state attorneys general have jurisdiction over retail advertising price accuracy under Section 5 of the FTC Act and analogous state consumer protection statutes. TJX's "compare at" pricing practice is subject to this regulatory environment. No current FTC enforcement action against TJX is identified in public records as of this analysis. (T4_INFERRED from absence of public FTC action; verified against FTC public enforcement database)

### Data Privacy
Following the 2006-2007 breach, TJX operates under enhanced scrutiny regarding data security. U.S. state data breach notification laws (now enacted in all 50 states) and, for European operations, the General Data Protection Regulation (GDPR) apply to TJX's data handling. TJX discloses data privacy and cybersecurity as ongoing risk factors. (SEC 10-K FY2024 risk factors)

### Employment Law
TJX employs approximately 310,000 associates (FY2024). At this workforce scale, employment litigation (wage and hour claims, discrimination claims) is a statistical regularity for U.S. retailers. TJX discloses ongoing employment-related litigation as routine legal proceedings. (SEC 10-K FY2024)

## Writing Risk Guide — TJX-Specific

### SAFE TO WRITE
- Revenue figures, store counts, segment data from SEC 10-K filings, with citation
- Description of the off-price business model as disclosed by TJX in investor communications
- The 2006-2007 data breach facts as disclosed in SEC 8-K filings and settled court records
- Description of "compare at" pricing litigation as a reported, publicly documented matter
- Observation that TJX operates in a competitive market with Ross Stores, Burlington, Nordstrom Rack
- Any claim directly quoted from or attributable to TJX's own public filings or press releases

### HEDGE / USE CAUTION
- Any characterization of "compare at" pricing accuracy — use: "has been the subject of litigation alleging..." not "TJX misrepresents prices"
- Statements about TJX's cybersecurity posture today — acknowledge investments have been made without asserting adequacy
- Comparative quality assertions between TJX banners and full-price retailers without metric basis
- Consumer sentiment claims without survey data — use "consumers have indicated" with source, not "customers believe"

### DO NOT WRITE
- Any assertion that TJX's "compare at" prices are false, misleading, or constitute any form of fraud or misrepresentation — this is actively litigated and this project is not a court
- Any claim that the 2006-2007 data breach resulted from negligence or intentional conduct — courts and settlements determined liability under specific legal standards not replicated here
- Any assertion about TJX's internal motivations, unreported strategies, or hidden business practices
- Pejorative characterizations of TJX's labor practices without specific, documented regulatory findings
