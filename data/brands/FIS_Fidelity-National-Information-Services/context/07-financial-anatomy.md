## 2. Revenue Anatomy

### 2.1 Five-Year Revenue Trend

| Fiscal Year | Total Revenue | YoY Change | Source |
|-------------|---------------|------------|--------|
| FY2021 | $10,333M | — | (SEC 10-K FY2021) |
| FY2022 | $12,552M | +21.5% | (SEC 10-K FY2022) |
| FY2023 | $9,339M | -25.6% | (SEC 10-K FY2023) |
| FY2024 | $9,720M | +4.1% | (SEC 10-K FY2024) |
| FY2025 | $9,831M | +1.1% | (SEC 10-K FY2025) |

The FY2022 revenue spike to $12,552M and subsequent FY2023 decline to $9,339M reflect the partial-year impact of the divestiture of the Merchant Solutions (Worldpay) business segment. FIS completed the sale of a majority stake in Worldpay to GTCR in January 2023, which significantly reduced consolidated revenue. The FY2024–FY2025 revenue range of $9.7–9.8B represents FIS's continuing operations profile post-divestiture. (T4_INFERRED — consult 10-K for precise divestiture accounting)

## 8. Brand-Finance Nexus

Based on publicly available financial data and FIS's disclosed business model (T4_INFERRED where noted):

**Post-Divestiture Repositioning**: FIS's asset base declined by more than half from FY2022 to FY2025 following the Worldpay divestiture. The company's brand narrative has shifted toward its Banking Solutions and Capital Markets segments as the core business. This strategic narrowing may support a cleaner brand identity focused on financial institutions rather than the broad merchant-and-bank positioning of the pre-divestiture entity. (T4_INFERRED)

**Deleveraging Signal**: Long-term debt reduction from $17.3B (FY2021) to $10.7B (FY2025) at a rate of approximately $1.5B per year indicates prioritization of balance sheet health following the Worldpay transaction. This deleveraging trajectory, if continued, would meaningfully reduce interest expense and improve future earnings quality. (T4_INFERRED)

**Operating Margin Recovery**: Operating margin improved from 4.4% (FY2022, impacted by Worldpay) to 14.7% (FY2025), indicating improving operational efficiency in the continuing business. Gross margin stabilized at ~37% in FY2025. (T4_INFERRED)

**Capital Return Complexity**: The combination of $961M–$1,138M in annual dividends alongside significant share repurchases and net losses creates a complex capital allocation picture that should be interpreted in light of the divestiture context and non-cash impairment charges. (T4_INFERRED)
