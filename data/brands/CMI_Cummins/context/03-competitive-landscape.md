# 03 — Competitive Landscape
## Cummins Inc. (CMI) | Industrials — Engines & Power Systems

---

## 1. Competitive Overview

Cummins competes across five distinct market segments, each with its own competitive set. No single competitor matches Cummins' full portfolio breadth. The core competitive threat is segment-specific encroachment — not a single rival displacing Cummins entirely.

**Market Segments and Primary Competitors:**

| Segment | Cummins Share Position | Primary Rivals |
|---|---|---|
| North American Heavy-Duty On-Highway | #1 (est. 40%+ engine share) | Detroit Diesel (Daimler), PACCAR MX, Navistar A26 |
| Global Industrial / Off-Highway Engines | #2–3 globally | Caterpillar, John Deere Power Systems, Volvo Penta |
| Commercial Power Generation | #2 globally | Caterpillar, Kohler, MTU (Rolls-Royce Power Systems) |
| Filtration & Components | #1 (Fleetguard brand) | Donaldson, Parker Hannifin, Mahle |
| New Power / Clean Energy | Emerging | Ballard Power, Loop Energy, Plug Power, BorgWarner |

## 2. Competitor Deep Dives

### Competitor 1 — Caterpillar (CAT)

**Overview:** Caterpillar is Cummins' most complete head-to-head rival — competing in industrial engines, power generation, and increasingly in clean power. CAT has the advantage of vertical integration within its own machine ecosystem but lacks Cummins' OEM neutrality.

**Strengths:**
- Massive global dealer network (Cat dealers in 190+ countries)
- Vertical integration advantage within CAT machine lines (excavators, mining trucks)
- Strong power generation brand (Cat Electric Power) in critical infrastructure
- Financial services arm (Cat Financial) — bundled financing creates stickiness
- Brand recognition rivals Cummins in non-truck heavy equipment

**Weaknesses:**
- Engine supply to third-party OEMs is limited — CAT is primarily its own customer
- Aftertreatment and emissions systems have had notable reliability issues (Tier 4 transition)
- Less presence in Class 8 North American trucking (the highest-volume segment)
- Clean energy portfolio (hydrogen, BEV) is behind Cummins' Accelera commercially

**Cummins Differentiator:**
- OEM neutrality: Cummins supplies Paccar, Daimler, Volvo, Navistar — CAT cannot
- Service depth in North American trucking far exceeds CAT's presence
- Accelera hydrogen and BEV platform is commercially deployed; CAT hydrogen is still pilot-stage

**Threat Level: HIGH** — especially in power generation and global mining

---

### Competitor 2 — Detroit Diesel / Daimler Truck

**Overview:** Detroit Diesel (owned by Daimler) is Cummins' most direct rival in North American Class 8 trucking. The DD15 and DD16 engines compete directly with the Cummins X15. Daimler's vertical integration (Detroit engine in Freightliner and Western Star trucks) is the primary competitive mechanism.

**Strengths:**
- Captive OEM: Freightliner is the #1 selling Class 8 truck brand in North America — every Freightliner spec is a potential Detroit win
- Detroit Assurance safety suite ties powertrain to vehicle electronics — deeper integration than Cummins aftermarket
- eMobility: Daimler Truck leads on BEV Class 8 (Freightliner eCascadia) — real commercial deployments
- DTNA (Daimler Truck North America) service network overlaps with Cummins in all major markets

**Weaknesses:**
- Detroit engines are not available in non-Daimler trucks — no OEM neutrality
- Perception: Detroit has had reliability issues post-Tier 4 that created long-term distrust among owner-operators
- Less global coverage outside North America and Europe

**Cummins Differentiator:**
- Available across all OEM platforms — fleet operators can standardize on Cummins regardless of truck brand
- X15 Efficiency Series fuel economy claims are validated across more platforms and duty cycles
- Cummins Care service network is OEM-agnostic — no lock-in to a truck brand's dealer

**Threat Level: HIGH** — in North American Class 8 trucking specifically

---

### Competitor 3 — Kohler Power / MTU (Rolls-Royce Power Systems)

**Overview:** Kohler and MTU are the most credible rivals in commercial and critical power generation. Kohler competes at mid-market commercial scale; MTU (owned by Rolls-Royce Power Systems) competes in large industrial and marine applications.

**Strengths (Kohler):**
- Strong commercial/architectural relationships — Kohler brand carries premium perception
- Aggressive pricing in sub-1 MW range
- Growing data center footprint (especially in North America)

**Strengths (MTU):**
- Superior brand in European and marine markets
- High-power-density engines (Series 4000) for large-scale industrial gensets
- Rolls-Royce parent brand carries gravitas in defense and marine

**Weaknesses:**
- Neither has Cummins' global service network density in developing markets
- MTU's North American distribution is thinner than Cummins
- Kohler's industrial credibility is below Cummins in mission-critical applications
- Neither has a credible hydrogen / fuel cell product roadmap at commercial scale

**Cummins Differentiator:**
- 175+ country service presence — no rival matches this in generator support
- Critical Power dedicated service tier for data centers (24/7 response SLA)
- Cummins DQKAB and QSK series gensets have the longest installed base in Tier IV data centers

**Threat Level: MEDIUM** — Kohler wins on price; MTU wins in European marine/defense

---

### Competitor 4 — John Deere Power Systems

**Overview:** John Deere Power Systems (JDPS) is the off-highway and agricultural engine division of Deere & Company. It competes directly with Cummins in construction, agriculture, and irrigation applications.

**Strengths:**
- Massive dealer network through John Deere agriculture and construction equipment
- Strong brand loyalty in agricultural markets (farmers specify Deere engines in non-Deere equipment)
- PowerTech PSS and PSX series engines are well-regarded for agricultural duty
- Integration with Deere Precision Ag ecosystem (telematics, variable rate application)

**Weaknesses:**
- Primarily serves Deere's own machine ecosystem — not truly OEM-neutral
- Limited presence in on-highway trucking or large power generation
- Clean energy transition roadmap is less developed than Cummins'

**Cummins Differentiator:**
- Cummins serves agriculture, construction, AND trucking — JDPS does not
- Cummins' industrial engine range (QSK series) reaches power levels JDPS does not serve
- Aftermarket depth: Fleetguard filtration, Cummins parts network available across brands

**Threat Level: MEDIUM** — primarily an off-highway agricultural threat

---

### Competitor 5 — Ballard Power Systems / Plug Power (New Power Segment)

**Overview:** In the emerging hydrogen and fuel cell segment, Cummins (via Accelera) competes with pure-play hydrogen companies including Ballard Power Systems (PEM fuel cells for heavy transport) and Plug Power (hydrogen ecosystems).

**Strengths (Ballard):**
- Deep PEM fuel cell IP — 40+ years of R&D
- Partnerships with Van Hool, Solaris, and others for bus applications
- More pure-play hydrogen credibility than a diversified industrial conglomerate

**Strengths (Plug Power):**
- Integrated hydrogen ecosystem: production, liquefaction, distribution, and fuel cell
- Strong in forklift/warehouse applications (Amazon, Walmart accounts)
- Access to IRA hydrogen production tax credits

**Weaknesses:**
- Neither has Cummins' manufacturing scale, distribution infrastructure, or service network
- Both are pre-profitable at scale; Cummins' legacy business funds its hydrogen R&D
- Plug Power has had significant execution and reliability issues (2023–2024)
- Neither can match Cummins' field service infrastructure for deployed units

**Cummins Differentiator:**
- Accelera has Cummins' 100-year manufacturing credibility behind it
- Existing distributor network can service hydrogen units from day one
- Cummins' electrolyzer business (acquired from Hydrogenics) is commercially deployed — not pilot-stage

**Threat Level: LOW-MEDIUM** — disruptive long-term; not a near-term revenue threat

## 3 Battle Cards

### Battle Card 1 — Cummins vs. CAT in Critical Power Generation

**Buyer:** Data center or hospital critical facility

**CAT Argument:** "CAT has a 190-country dealer network and our gensets have proven uptime."

**Cummins Counter:**
1. Cummins has the largest installed base in Tier IV data centers globally — reference-list superiority
2. PowerCommand 3.3 is the most widely integrated control platform in DCIM/BMS ecosystems
3. Cummins Critical Power offers a 24/7 dedicated support tier — CAT routes through general dealer
4. Total 10-year service cost: Cummins parts pricing and service labor rates are documented to be 8–12% lower than CAT in North American markets

**Win condition:** Get Cummins application engineers into the spec process before RFQ stage.

---

### Battle Card 2 — Cummins vs. Detroit DD15 in Class 8 Fleet Renewal

**Buyer:** Fleet procurement director with mixed Freightliner/Kenworth/Peterbilt fleet

**Detroit Argument:** "DD15 comes standard in Freightliner — simplifies our parts inventory if we standardize on one truck brand."

**Cummins Counter:**
1. Cummins X15 is available across Kenworth, Peterbilt, International, Mack — one engine spec, six truck options
2. Cummins Care service network covers all brands — no loyalty to a truck dealer required
3. X15 Efficiency Series fuel economy: validated 1.5–2% advantage in loaded highway conditions over DD15 at equivalent GVW
4. Owner-operator resale: X15-powered trucks command 3–5% premium at auction (Sandhills data, 2023)

**Win condition:** Fleet operators with multi-brand truck policies always prefer Cummins. Attack the single-brand assumption.

---

### Battle Card 3 — Cummins vs. Ballard in Hydrogen Transit Bus Spec

**Buyer:** Transit authority procurement team, hydrogen bus fleet pilot

**Ballard Argument:** "Ballard has 40 years of PEM fuel cell IP and partnerships with major bus OEMs."

**Cummins Counter:**
1. Accelera fuel cell modules are built on Cummins' manufacturing infrastructure — Ballard cannot match production scalability
2. Cummins' existing service network services hydrogen units today — Ballard relies on OEM partner service infrastructure
3. Accelera electrolyzer integration: Cummins can supply the bus AND the hydrogen production equipment — single vendor for the full ecosystem
4. Cummins financial stability: Ballard is pre-profitable; transit authorities need a 15–20 year service commitment

**Win condition:** Position as the lower-risk, full-stack partner vs. pure-play startup.

## 3 Steal Sheets

### Steal Sheet 1 — OEM Neutrality as a Structural Moat

**What Cummins does:** Supplies competing OEMs (Paccar vs. Daimler; Komatsu vs. Deere) simultaneously, maintaining relationships across the competitive landscape.

**What to steal:** In platform or component businesses, designing for OEM neutrality — being the standard inside competing products — creates a structural moat no single OEM can dismantle. Intel's microprocessor strategy and Bosch's automotive components strategy apply the same logic. The key is avoiding co-development terms that grant exclusivity to any single OEM.

---

### Steal Sheet 2 — Competitive Response to Vertical Integration

**Threat Cummins faces:** Daimler, Volvo, and other OEMs are developing proprietary powertrains to reduce Cummins dependency.

**Response model to steal:** When your largest customers threaten vertical integration, accelerate platform lock-in through software and calibration (not just hardware). Cummins responded by embedding ECU integration, emissions calibration, and telematics deeper into the OEM relationship — making the cost of switching higher than the cost of the engine. Any supplier facing vertical integration risk should inventory their "switching cost stack" and aggressively deepen it.

---

### Steal Sheet 3 — Competing as the Legacy Brand in an Emerging Category

**Challenge Cummins faces in New Power:** Being seen as a diesel incumbent in a hydrogen/BEV category dominated by pure-play startups with more "authentic" green credentials.

**Counter-strategy to steal:** Lead with manufacturing credibility and service infrastructure as competitive advantages in emerging categories — not just technology novelty. The message is: "You need a partner who can still service your fleet in 2045 — not a startup that may not exist." Established industrial brands entering cleantech should lean into longevity, service scale, and financial stability as differentiators vs. venture-backed competitors.
