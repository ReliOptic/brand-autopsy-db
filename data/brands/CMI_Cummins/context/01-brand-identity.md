## 2. Brand Positioning Statement

Cummins is the global standard for reliability in power systems — the engine behind freight, mining, construction, agriculture, data centers, and military operations. Where failure is not an option, Cummins is the first and final answer.

For fleet operators, plant engineers, and infrastructure procurement teams operating under extreme uptime requirements, Cummins delivers industrial-grade power solutions backed by a 100+ year engineering legacy, a global service footprint, and an accelerating portfolio in clean and alternative power — making it the only partner that scales from today's diesel demands to tomorrow's hydrogen economy.

## 3. Brand Architecture

```
CUMMINS INC. (Master Brand)
├── Engine Business
│   ├── Heavy-Duty Diesel (X-Series, L9, B6.7)
│   ├── Mid-Range & Light Commercial
│   └── Industrial Engines (mining, marine, agriculture)
├── Distribution Business
│   └── Global dealer + distributor network (600+ locations)
├── Components Business
│   ├── Filtration (Fleetguard)
│   ├── Aftertreatment (DPF, SCR, DEF systems)
│   └── Turbochargers (Atmus, spun off 2023)
├── Power Systems
│   ├── Generator Sets (commercial, standby, prime)
│   └── Power Electronics
└── New Power (Accelera by Cummins — spun off 2024)
    ├── Battery-Electric Systems
    ├── Fuel Cells (hydrogen PEM)
    └── Electrolyzer Technology
```

**Brand Architecture Type:** Monolithic / Endorsed  
Cummins leads with its master brand across nearly all sub-brands, with Accelera being the most prominent transition to a standalone identity.

## 7. Brand Personality Archetypes

**Primary: The Ruler**
Cummins operates as the definitive authority — the standard by which all others are measured. In industrial procurement, specifying "Cummins-powered" is a quality signal, not just a vendor choice.

**Secondary: The Expert / Sage**
Depth of technical knowledge is the trust currency. Cummins does not simplify; it elevates its audience's engineering literacy.

**Tertiary: The Caretaker**
Visible in community investment (Columbus, IN), workforce development, and supply chain stewardship — institutional responsibility is deeply woven into brand DNA.

## 3 Battle Cards

### Battle Card 1 — vs. Caterpillar (CAT Power)

| Dimension | Cummins | Caterpillar |
|---|---|---|
| Engine Supply | Independent supplier to OEMs | Vertically integrated (CAT machines = CAT engines) |
| OEM Relationships | Paccar, Daimler, Volvo, Navistar | Primarily internal |
| Service Network | 600+ distributor locations, 175+ countries | Cat Dealer Network (strong but tied to Cat equipment) |
| Clean Power Portfolio | Accelera (hydrogen, BEV, fuel cells) | Cat hydrogen pilot programs |
| Win Argument | "We power every brand — our engine is proven across more platforms and environments than any competitor" | — |
| Loss Risk | Customers who buy exclusively Cat machines may default to Cat power units |

**Talking Points:**
- Cummins engines are in 70%+ of North American heavy trucks — OEM-neutral dominance
- Cummins' hydrogen roadmap (PEM fuel cell, electrolyzer) is further along commercially than CAT
- No conflict of interest: Cummins doesn't compete with the OEMs it supplies

## 3 Steal Sheets

### Steal Sheet 1 — Reliability Narrative

**What Cummins owns:** The brand is synonymous with engines that don't fail. "Cummins-powered" is a shorthand for procurement confidence.

**What to steal (for adjacent industrial brands):**
- Lead with uptime metrics, not feature lists
- Build trust through case study specificity: hours of operation, failure rates, environments survived
- Create a "proven in field" narrative backed by real fleet data — not lab specs alone
- Use the language of risk mitigation, not just performance: "Reduces unplanned downtime by X%"

---

### Steal Sheet 2 — Energy Transition Bridge Strategy

**What Cummins does well:** It doesn't abandon its core (diesel/natural gas) while building its future (hydrogen/BEV). It tells a "pathway" story, not a "pivot" story.

**What to steal:**
- Frame today's product as the financially responsible bridge, not the enemy of tomorrow
- Introduce clean variants under the same master brand umbrella before spinning them off
- Use "net-zero pathway" language that ties emissions goals to product milestones — not vague pledges
- Quantify: g CO2/kWh, NOx reduction %, lifecycle emissions vs. baseline

---

### Steal Sheet 3 — OEM Partnership Model as Brand Moat

**What Cummins does well:** Being the preferred supplier to the world's largest truck OEMs (Paccar, Daimler, Navistar) makes Cummins the invisible but indispensable brand inside every vehicle.

**What to steal:**
- Design for co-branding inside someone else's product — the "Intel Inside" model for industrial components
- Build technical integration depth that makes switching suppliers costly (ECU integration, calibration, warranty alignment)
- Pursue certification and regulatory compliance as a marketing asset, not just legal overhead
- Publish OEM integration guides and technical specs publicly — openness as a trust signal
