# 02 — Audience Map
## Cummins Inc. (CMI) | Industrials — Engines & Power Systems

---

## 1. Audience Architecture Overview

Cummins operates in deeply B2B markets. Its audience is not consumers — it is procurement engineers, fleet managers, OEM powertrain teams, infrastructure operators, and sustainability officers. Decision cycles are 12–36 months, committee-based, and driven by TCO, regulatory compliance, and reliability data.

**Audience Tier Model:**

| Tier | Audience Type | Relationship |
|---|---|---|
| Tier 1 | OEM Powertrain Teams | Co-development / spec-in partners |
| Tier 2 | Fleet Operators & Procurement | Direct purchase / TCO buyers |
| Tier 3 | Distributors & Dealers | Channel partners, service delivery |
| Tier 4 | End Operators / Technicians | Daily users, uptime stakeholders |
| Tier 5 | Regulators & Policy Bodies | Compliance gatekeepers |
| Tier 6 | Investors & ESG Analysts | Financial narrative audience |

## 3 Battle Cards (Audience Positioning)

### Battle Card 1 — Winning the Fleet Procurement Director (vs. CAT engine spec-in)

**Situation:** Marcus is evaluating PACCAR MX-13 (CAT-powered) vs. Cummins X15 for next 200-truck order.

**Cummins Argument:**
- X15 Efficiency: 1.5–2% fuel economy advantage at loaded highway conditions (proven in fleet trials)
- Cummins Care network: 600+ service locations vs. CAT's dealer network concentrated in construction-adjacent markets
- PACCAR relationship: Cummins is the preferred powertrain for Kenworth T680 — OEM-aligned choice

**Landmines to avoid:** Don't attack CAT directly. Pivot to TCO data. Let the numbers do the competitive work.

---

### Battle Card 2 — Winning the Data Center Power Spec (vs. Kohler / MTU)

**Situation:** Priya is spec-ing generator sets for a new 20MW hyperscale edge facility. Kohler and MTU are shortlisted.

**Cummins Argument:**
- Largest installed base in Tier IV data centers globally — risk committee favors proven platforms
- PowerCommand 3.3 control system: native integration with BMS and DCIM platforms
- Cummins 24/7 Critical Power support — dedicated data center service tier separate from general commercial
- Parts availability: Cummins maintains critical spare parts at regional depots within 100 miles of major data center corridors

**Landmines to avoid:** Don't over-promise on step-load performance without site-specific data. Get the application engineering team in front of Priya before final spec.

---

### Battle Card 3 — Retaining the Mining Fleet Supervisor (vs. independent rebuild shops)

**Situation:** Darnell is evaluating whether to send QSK60 engines to a non-Cummins rebuild shop at $40K savings per unit vs. Cummins Certified Rebuild at full price.

**Cummins Argument:**
- Cummins Certified Rebuild: factory-spec clearances, OEM parts, and restored warranty — non-certified rebuilds void the extended coverage
- INSITE diagnostic access: post-rebuild, non-certified units lose access to Cummins software updates and fault code libraries
- Lifecycle cost: one premature failure on a non-certified rebuild erases the $40K savings — plus production downtime at $15K–$50K/day

**Landmines to avoid:** Don't make this about loyalty. Make it about risk math. Darnell respects numbers, not brand arguments.

## 3 Steal Sheets (Audience Insights)

### Steal Sheet 1 — The Invisible Decision-Maker Problem

**Observation:** In industrial B2B, the spec-in decision (choosing Cummins at the engineering level) happens 12–18 months before the procurement decision. Brands that only market to procurement lose the spec.

**Steal:** Map your audience into "specifiers" and "approvers" — create different content tracks for each. Win the engineer before the buyer even knows a decision is being made. Application notes, white papers, and technical webinars own the specification phase. Price and warranty win the procurement phase.

---

### Steal Sheet 2 — Field Credibility as a Trust Moat

**Observation:** Darnell Washington trusts Cummins because his father trusted Cummins. Brand continuity across 30-year careers in industrial trades is a compounding asset — no campaign can build it; only operational excellence sustains it.

**Steal:** Invest in service technician relationships as a marketing channel. Technician training programs, certified rebuild networks, and field technical reps are brand ambassadors with more credibility than any ad campaign. Industrial B2B loyalty is person-to-person before it is company-to-company.

---

### Steal Sheet 3 — Sustainability Messaging Must Be Metric-Anchored

**Observation:** Priya Mehta-Sundaram is under pressure to show ESG progress but cannot use vague corporate pledges in engineering specs or board reports. She needs numbers: g CO2/kWh, NOx ppm, lifecycle emissions vs. baseline.

**Steal:** Every sustainability claim in industrial B2B must be paired with a quantifiable metric and a verifiable methodology. "We're committed to net zero" loses. "Our QSK60 LNG variant reduces CO2e by 23% vs. diesel baseline at equivalent load factor — methodology per ISO 14040" wins. Audit your sustainability language for metric density.
