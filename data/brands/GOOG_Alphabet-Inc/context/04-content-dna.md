## Hook Patterns

### Hook 1 — Search Query as Story
Opens with a real search query that reveals a human need or emotion.
- **Example**: Year in Search 2024 — a montage of the year's most-searched queries telling the story of the year. (official, YouTube)
- **Mechanism**: Search queries are intimate — they reveal what people really care about. Using them as storytelling hooks creates emotional connection.

### Hook 2 — AI Demo
Opens with an AI capability demonstration — Gemini answering a complex question, generating content, or assisting with a task.
- **Example**: Google I/O 2024 — Gemini multimodal demo showing AI understanding images, text, and code simultaneously. (official, Google I/O)

### Hook 3 — Scale Statistic
Opens with a number that communicates Google's reach.
- **Example**: "8.5 billion searches per day." (estimated, T3_SECONDARY_RELIABLE)

### Hook 4 — Google Doodle
Interactive or animated homepage logo celebrating a cultural moment, historical figure, or event.
- **Example**: Celebrating the anniversary of a scientific discovery with an interactive game on google.com. (official)

## Tone & Mood Guide

### Product Announcement (Google I/O)
**Mood**: Enthusiastic, technically impressive, optimistic. "The future is here and it's helpful."

### Year in Search
**Mood**: Reflective, emotional, humanistic. No product promotion — pure emotional brand equity.

### Google Cloud Marketing
**Mood**: Professional, data-forward, enterprise-appropriate. Shifts from consumer casual to enterprise formal.

### Privacy & Safety
**Mood**: Reassuring, transparent, accountable. "You're in control."

## Content Prohibitions

1. **No acknowledgment of surveillance-model framing.** Google frames data use as "personalization" and "helpfulness." (T4_INFERRED)
2. **No consumer product pricing in marketing.** Most Google consumer products are free; pricing discussion is enterprise-only. (T4_INFERRED)
3. **No negative competitive naming in consumer content.** (T4_INFERRED)
4. **No complex privacy explanations in consumer-facing content.** Privacy is presented as simple controls, not legal frameworks. (T4_INFERRED)
5. **No AI hype without product grounding.** AI claims are tied to specific product capabilities, not abstract futurism. (T4_INFERRED)

## Steal Sheet — 3 Transferable Principles

**1. Use your data to tell human stories.** Year in Search transforms search query data into an emotional narrative about humanity's shared experience. The data is the story. Transferable: look at your product's usage data for human stories worth telling.

**2. Make AI concrete, not abstract.** Google's AI announcements always include live demos with specific use cases. "AI that helps you write emails" is more effective than "the future of AI" as a communication strategy. Transferable: anchor AI claims in specific, demonstrable user actions.

**3. Google Doodles are the most capital-efficient brand campaign ever.** A daily illustration on the world's most-visited page generates cultural affinity at near-zero marginal cost. Transferable: find the equivalent of the Doodle for your product — a small, daily creative gesture that humanizes the brand.
