## 3. Major Litigation

### 3.1 United States v. Google LLC (DOJ Antitrust — Search, 2020)

**Filed**: October 2020
**Court**: U.S. District Court for the District of Columbia
**Ruling (August 2024)**: Judge Amit Mehta found that Google maintained an illegal monopoly in the general search and general text advertising markets through exclusive distribution agreements (e.g., the Safari default search deal with Apple). (T1_OFFICIAL, court opinion)
**Remedies**: Remedies phase ongoing as of document date. Potential remedies discussed include prohibiting exclusive default search agreements, requiring data sharing, or structural remedies. (T3_SECONDARY_RELIABLE)
**Google's position**: Google has stated it will appeal the ruling. (T3_SECONDARY_RELIABLE)

### 3.2 United States v. Google LLC (DOJ Antitrust — Ad Tech, 2023)

**Filed**: January 2023
**Court**: U.S. District Court for the Eastern District of Virginia
**Nature**: DOJ alleges Google monopolized digital advertising technology markets through acquisitions and self-preferencing.
**Status**: Trial held September 2024; awaiting ruling as of document date.
**Source**: DOJ press releases; SEC 10-K FY2024 disclosures (T1_OFFICIAL)

### 3.3 European Commission — DMA and Competition

- **DMA Gatekeeper Designation**: Google designated as gatekeeper for Google Search, Google Play, Google Maps, Google Shopping, Chrome, Android, YouTube, Google Ads. (T1_OFFICIAL, EC)
- **Google Shopping Fine (2017)**: €2.42B fine for self-preferencing Google Shopping in search results. Upheld by ECJ in 2024. (T1_OFFICIAL)
- **Google Android Fine (2018)**: €4.34B fine for illegal restrictions on Android device manufacturers. (T1_OFFICIAL)
- **Google AdSense Fine (2019)**: €1.49B fine for abusive practices in online advertising. (T1_OFFICIAL)

### 3.4 Other Proceedings

- **Privacy class actions**: Multiple class actions regarding data collection practices in various jurisdictions (SEC 10-K FY2024, Item 3)
- **YouTube COPPA settlement (2019)**: $170M FTC settlement regarding children's privacy on YouTube (T1_OFFICIAL, FTC)
- **Various patent infringement cases**: Ongoing (SEC 10-K FY2024)

## 5. Project Risk Assessment

### 5.1 Google's Litigation Posture

**Assessment**: AGGRESSIVE-DEFENSIVE (T4_INFERRED)

Google actively defends its market position through litigation, lobbying, and regulatory engagement. Google's legal budget is among the largest in the technology industry. However, Google has not demonstrated a pattern of pursuing brand analysts or researchers for commentary based on public information.

### 5.2 Writing Risk Guide

#### SAFE TO WRITE
- Financial data from SEC filings
- Product descriptions from google.com and official documentation
- Summaries of court rulings and regulatory decisions with specific citations
- Market share estimates from reputable research firms, cited as "(estimated)"
- Descriptions of Google's stated strategy from earnings calls and official communications

#### REQUIRES HEDGING
| Topic | Approach |
|-------|----------|
| Antitrust proceedings | Cite specific court rulings and regulatory decisions; note appeals and ongoing proceedings |
| Data collection practices | Describe official privacy policies; do not characterize as "surveillance" |
| AI capabilities | Cite demonstrated capabilities from official demos; do not extrapolate |
| Market dominance | Use market share data with source citation; avoid "monopoly" as bare assertion (cite court finding specifically) |
| Competitive intent | Describe observable actions, not attributed motives |

#### DO NOT WRITE
- Assertions that Google's data practices are illegal beyond what courts/regulators have specifically ruled
- Claims about Google's internal deliberations or strategy not disclosed publicly
- Predictions about antitrust remedy outcomes
- Characterizations of Google's conduct using pejorative language not sourced from regulatory findings
- Reproducing substantial portions of Google's copyrighted content

All other antitrust commentary must distinguish between regulatory allegations and final judgments.
