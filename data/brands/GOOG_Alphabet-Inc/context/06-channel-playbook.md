## 3. Channel Prohibitions

### google.com
- **No advertising on the homepage.** The Google homepage has never displayed ads. The search bar is the product; ads appear in search results. (observed)
- **No promotional banners.** Exceptions: Doodles (cultural), and rare event announcements. (observed)

### YouTube (@Google)
- **No engagement with political controversies from the brand account.** (T4_INFERRED)
- **No low-quality production.** Google's YouTube content matches Apple-tier production quality for major campaigns. (observed)

### blog.google
- **No marketing hyperbole.** Blog posts are written in a conversational, factual tone — not advertising copy. (observed on blog.google)

### Google Cloud
- **No consumer-casual language.** Cloud content maintains enterprise-appropriate technical depth. (observed)

## 4. Crisis Response Protocol

### Scenario 1 — Major Service Outage (Grade 1)
**Google's pattern**: Real-time updates on Google Cloud Status Dashboard and Google Workspace Status Dashboard → Post-incident report within 48–72 hours → No consumer social media engagement during outage. (observed, T4_INFERRED)

### Scenario 2 — Antitrust/Regulatory Action (Grade 2)
**Google's pattern**: Official statement via blog.google or corporate communications → Legal team manages filings → Minimal social media commentary → Compliance implementation if required. (observed, DOJ and EU proceedings)

### Scenario 3 — AI Safety/Ethics Controversy (Grade 3)
**Google's pattern**: Internal review → blog.google statement if needed → Research publication addressing concerns → Google DeepMind safety publications. (T4_INFERRED)

## 5. Steal Sheet

**1. "The homepage is not a brochure."** Google.com proves that the most valuable homepage in the world can be nearly empty. Transferable: if your product is intuitive, your homepage should be the product, not a description of it.

**2. "Year in Search is the greatest brand campaign that contains zero product promotion."** The emotional resonance of showing what humanity searched for creates brand love without a single product mention. Transferable: create an annual brand moment that reflects your users' lives back to them.

**3. "Run a developer conference to set the industry agenda."** Google I/O shapes what developers build for the next year. The conference is not just marketing — it is strategic direction-setting for the platform ecosystem. Transferable: if you have a developer community, your conference should define what they build next.
