### Persona 1 — Destiny Williams
**"The Hustle-First Entrepreneur"**

| Field | Detail |
|-------|--------|
| Age | 26 |
| Ethnicity | Black American |
| Location | Atlanta, GA, USA |
| Occupation | Mobile hair braider + part-time Uber driver |
| Income | ~$32,000/year (variable) |
| Education | Some college (no degree) |
| Primary Device | Android smartphone |
| Primary Block Products | Cash App (banking + P2P), Square Reader (mobile payments) |

**Background:** Destiny runs her own hair braiding business out of clients' homes and a rented studio two days a week. She doesn't qualify for a traditional business checking account due to inconsistent income. She started using Cash App for P2P at 19, then discovered Square when a client asked to pay by card. She now uses both daily. She has no credit card and uses Cash App's free debit card as her primary financial instrument.

**Goals:**
- Accept card payments without losing margin to fees
- Build savings without minimum balance requirements
- Eventually open a proper salon with a business loan

**Pain Points:**
- Traditional banks treat variable income as a red flag
- High swipe fees from competitors eat into thin margins
- Distrust of financial institutions rooted in family history

**Brand Relationship with Block:**
- Highly loyal to Cash App; evangelizes it within her community
- Does not use TIDAL (streams on YouTube)
- Sees Square as a professional upgrade, not just a gadget

**Psychographic Tags:** Self-reliant, community-oriented, skeptical of institutions, aspirational, price-sensitive

## 5. Audience Journey Snapshot (Cash App)

```
AWARE → CONSIDER → TRIAL → ACTIVATE → LOYAL → ADVOCATE
  |          |         |         |          |         |
Peer       App      Free      First     Boost     Referral
referral   Store   download   send     activated  bonus
           review            ($1)     (cashback)  sent
```

**Key drop-off point:** Between TRIAL and ACTIVATE — users who download but never send money. Block addresses this with the $5 referral incentive program requiring both sender and receiver to transact.

## 7. Steal Sheets

### Steal Sheet 1 — Referral Mechanics That Require Transaction
**What Block does:** Cash App's referral bonus ($5–$15 per qualified referral) only pays out when BOTH the sender and the new user complete a transaction. This filters out idle sign-ups and creates immediate activation.
**How to steal it:** Restructure referral incentives to require a core product action (first transaction, first booking, first upload) rather than just registration. Increases activation rate and referral quality simultaneously.

### Steal Sheet 2 — Debit Card as Loyalty Program
**What Block does:** The Cash Card's "Boosts" system turns a debit card into a rotating merchant discount program. Users unlock 10–15% off at specific merchants, driving card-on-file loyalty without a points bureaucracy.
**How to steal it:** If your product has a payment layer, attach rotating, limited-time merchant partnerships to the card experience. Boosts create urgency (limited uses) and relevance (user-selected categories).

### Steal Sheet 3 — Audience Before Product in New Verticals
**What Block does:** TIDAL was acquired because Cash App already had a music-spending, artist-tipping user base. Block mapped the audience first, found the product second.
**How to steal it:** Before entering a new vertical, audit your existing user transaction data for latent demand. Who are your users paying outside your ecosystem? That gap is your next product.
