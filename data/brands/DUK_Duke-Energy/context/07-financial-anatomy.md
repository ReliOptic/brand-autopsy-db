## 4. Investment Structure

### 4.1 Capital Expenditure Plan

| Period | Planned CapEx | Focus | Source |
|--------|-------------|-------|--------|
| FY2024 | ~$11B | Grid modernization, renewables, nuclear uprates | (SEC 10-K FY2024) |
| FY2024–2028 (5-year plan) | ~$73B | Clean energy transition and grid infrastructure | (official, Duke Energy investor day 2024) |

Duke Energy's $73B five-year capital plan is one of the largest utility capital programs in the United States. This capital investment drives regulated rate base growth, which in turn supports earnings growth under the regulated utility model. (T4_INFERRED from regulated utility rate-base growth mechanism)

### 4.2 Rate Base Growth

Rate base is the capital investment on which Duke Energy earns a regulated return. As capital is invested and rate cases are filed and approved, rate base grows, supporting earnings growth. Duke Energy's regulated return on equity (ROE) is set by state public utility commissions. (T1_OFFICIAL, regulatory rate case disclosures)

## 7. Brand Valuation

Duke Energy's brand value is not independently assessed by Interbrand or Brand Finance in their major consumer brand rankings, as Duke Energy is a regulated utility with a captive customer base rather than a consumer-choice brand. Brand equity for Duke Energy is better measured through:

- **Regulatory relationship quality**: Rate case approval rates and authorized ROEs relative to requests (T4_INFERRED)
- **Customer satisfaction scores**: JD Power Utility Customer Satisfaction surveys; Duke Energy's rankings are publicly reported by JD Power annually (T3_SECONDARY_RELIABLE)
- **ESG investor ratings**: Bloomberg ESG scores, MSCI ESG ratings — relevant to institutional investor allocation decisions (T3_SECONDARY_RELIABLE)

## 9. Brand-Finance Nexus

**Signal 1 — Regulatory Goodwill as Earnings Enabler**
Duke Energy's ability to earn its authorized regulated ROE depends on maintaining regulatory relationships sufficient to support timely rate case approvals. Brand trust with regulators, communities, and policymakers directly affects earnings recovery. (T4_INFERRED)

**Signal 2 — Clean Energy Narrative and Valuation Premium**
Utilities with credible clean energy transition plans trade at valuation premiums relative to peers perceived as lagging the transition. Duke Energy's publicly stated commitments and capital plan are designed in part to support this valuation positioning. (T4_INFERRED; consistent with utility sector valuation research in T3_SECONDARY_RELIABLE sources)

**Signal 3 — Dividend Reliability as Brand Equity**
For income investors, the dividend is Duke Energy's most fundamental brand promise. 97+ years of consecutive dividends (estimated) is a brand asset with direct financial value — it supports a loyal investor base that provides relatively stable share price support compared to growth-oriented stocks. (T4_INFERRED)
