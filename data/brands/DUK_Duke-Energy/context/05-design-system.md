## Layout Principles

1. **Information Before Inspiration**: Duke Energy's layout hierarchy prioritizes functional information (outage map, bill payment, rate information) above brand aspiration. The utility customer's first need is service access, not brand engagement. (T4_INFERRED from observed duke-energy.com information architecture)

2. **Data Visualization for Complex Topics**: Clean energy transition progress, rate case impacts, and grid investment data are presented through charts and infographics that make complex information accessible without requiring regulatory expertise. (observed, Duke Energy sustainability report and investor materials)

3. **Safety First in Emergency Contexts**: Storm response and outage communications adopt a distinct visual mode — high-contrast, action-oriented, mobile-first — that overrides standard brand aesthetics in emergency situations. (T4_INFERRED from observed utility emergency communications)

4. **Regulatory Credibility Through Design Consistency**: Duke Energy's investor and regulatory communications maintain consistent, conservative design language that signals institutional credibility. Graphic novelty or design experimentation in this context would undermine the trust signal. (T4_INFERRED)

## Design Prohibitions

1. **Do not use Duke Energy's blue trademark color for competitive or adversarial purposes**: Duke's corporate blue identity is protected trade dress. (T4_INFERRED)
2. **No sensationalist imagery in safety communications**: Emergency content must be clear and actionable; dramatic or alarming imagery increases anxiety without aiding response. (T4_INFERRED from utility communications best practices)
3. **No unverified "100% renewable" visual claims**: Green imagery implying complete clean energy transition ahead of actual transition milestones creates regulatory and legal risk. (T4_INFERRED)
4. **No consumer brand visual aesthetics in investor materials**: Playful, consumer-oriented design in regulatory or investor contexts undermines institutional credibility. (T4_INFERRED)
5. **No competitor logos or imagery in any Duke Energy communications**: As a regulated utility, comparative advertising creates regulatory relationship risks. (T4_INFERRED)
6. **Maintain WCAG 2.1 AA accessibility standards**: Utility services are essential; accessibility for all customers including those with visual impairments is both a legal obligation and a brand responsibility. (T4_INFERRED from regulatory accessibility requirements)
