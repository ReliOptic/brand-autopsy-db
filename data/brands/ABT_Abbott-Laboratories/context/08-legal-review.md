## Major Litigation (Public Record)

### Infant Formula — Sturgis Facility / Similac Recall (2022)
The FDA inspected Abbott's Sturgis, Michigan infant formula manufacturing facility in early 2022 following reports of infant illness potentially linked to Cronobacter sakazakii contamination. Abbott voluntarily recalled certain Similac, Alimentum, and EleCare formulas. The facility was temporarily closed and subsequently remediated. Abbott reached a consent decree with FDA in September 2022 to resume production under enhanced oversight. (T1_OFFICIAL, FDA press releases 2022; T3_SECONDARY_RELIABLE, multiple news sources) Abbott disclosed litigation related to this matter in SEC 10-K filings. (T1_OFFICIAL, SEC 10-K FY2022, FY2023)

### FreeStyle Libre Patent Litigation
Abbott has engaged in patent litigation with Dexcom regarding CGM technology. The companies have entered into cross-licensing agreements resolving certain patent disputes. (T3_SECONDARY_RELIABLE, multiple news sources; verify current status) This is disclosed in Abbott's SEC litigation notes.

### Historical Litigation
Abbott's history includes various product liability cases across its medical device, diagnostics, and nutrition product lines. Material cases are disclosed in SEC 10-K filings under Legal Proceedings and Commitments and Contingencies. (T1_OFFICIAL, SEC 10-K FY2023)

## Regulatory Landscape

### FDA Oversight
- **Medical devices**: Abbott's devices require FDA 510(k) clearance or PMA approval. Post-market surveillance, MDR reporting, and recall authority apply.
- **In Vitro Diagnostics**: Alinity and other laboratory diagnostics require FDA clearance/approval and CLIA compliance.
- **Infant formula**: Subject to FDA regulation under 21 CFR Part 107 and the FDA's infant formula recall authority.
- **FreeStyle Libre**: Regulated as a Class II medical device; subject to ongoing post-market surveillance and MDR reporting obligations.

### International
- EMA oversight in European Union markets
- PMDA oversight in Japan
- ANVISA in Brazil; CDSCO in India
- ISO 13485 quality management system standards for medical device manufacturing globally

## Writing Risk Guide — Abbott Specific

### SAFE to write:
- "Abbott reported revenue of $20.0B in FY2023, as disclosed in its SEC 10-K."
- "Abbott voluntarily recalled certain Similac products in 2022 following FDA inspection findings, as documented in FDA press releases."
- "Abbott and Dexcom resolved certain CGM patent disputes through cross-licensing agreements, per public disclosures."
- "FreeStyle Libre achieved $5.3B in diabetes care revenue in FY2023, as disclosed in Abbott earnings materials."
- "Abbott has increased its dividend for 52+ consecutive years, as disclosed in company communications."
- "The FDA and Abbott entered into a consent decree in September 2022 regarding the Sturgis facility, per FDA public records."

### HEDGE before writing:
- Infant illness causation: "The FDA investigated reports of infant illness potentially associated with the Sturgis facility. Abbott recalled products and remediated the facility. Causation between the facility's products and specific illnesses was not publicly established through adjudicated findings as of this review."
- Market share claims: Label as estimated with source and date.
- CGM accuracy comparisons: Use only published, peer-reviewed head-to-head data with specific trial names.

### DO NOT WRITE:
- Any assertion that Abbott acted with disregard for infant safety — this is an intent claim unsupported by adjudicated findings.
- Claims that the 2022 recall products caused specific infant deaths — causation was not established in the publicly available record reviewed.
- Pejorative characterizations of Abbott's manufacturing practices beyond what FDA inspection records document.
- Unhedged claims about product accuracy without citing specific clinical trial data.
- Any assertion of fraud or criminal conduct not adjudicated by a court of competent jurisdiction.
