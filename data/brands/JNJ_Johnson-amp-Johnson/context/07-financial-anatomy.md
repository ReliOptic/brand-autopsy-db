### 4.3 Capital Expenditures

Johnson & Johnson's capital expenditures are disclosed in the cash flow statement of the 10-K.
JNJ invests in pharmaceutical manufacturing facilities, MedTech production infrastructure,
and R&D facilities globally. CapEx in recent years has been in the approximately $3–4B range
(estimated from publicly available earnings call commentary and 10-K cash flow disclosures;
exact figure in T1_OFFICIAL 10-K). As a pharmaceutical and medical device company, J&J is
moderately capital-intensive relative to pure-play software companies but less so than commodity
manufacturing businesses.

### 7.2 Stelara LOE: A Brand-Finance Risk Case Study

Stelara's anticipated revenue decline following the January 2025 U.S. biosimilar market
entry represents a real-time test of J&J's brand-finance resilience. The mechanism:

- Stelara FY2023 global sales: approximately $9.7B (T1_OFFICIAL, JNJ 10-K FY2023)
- U.S. biosimilar entry: expected January 2025 per settlement agreements
- J&J's management acknowledgment: publicly discussed on multiple earnings calls as a known
  headwind, with Tremfya commercial execution cited as the primary offset strategy
  (T2_PRIMARY_RELIABLE, JNJ earnings call transcripts)

The brand-finance implication: J&J's ability to maintain Innovative Medicine revenue growth
despite the Stelara LOE depends on whether Tremfya's brand authority with immunologists —
built through clinical data and physician education — is sufficient to capture patients who
might otherwise switch to biosimilar ustekinumab. This is a direct test of whether J&J's
institutional clinical credibility (Sage archetype brand value) translates into commercial
retention under competitive pressure. (T4_INFERRED)

## 8. Financial Risk Matrix

The following risks are disclosed in JNJ's 10-K, Part I, Item 1A (Risk Factors). Descriptions
below summarize publicly disclosed risk language. (T1_OFFICIAL, JNJ 10-K FY2024)

| # | Risk Category | Disclosed Risk Summary | Estimated Severity |
|---|---------------|------------------------|-------------------|
| 1 | **Talc/Asbestos Litigation** | J&J's 10-K discloses ongoing litigation related to talc-containing products. A proposed $8.9B settlement has been subject to legal proceedings. The financial and reputational exposure is the company's most prominent disclosed legal contingency. | Very High |
| 2 | **Stelara Biosimilar Erosion** | Loss of exclusivity for Stelara exposes approximately $9.7B in FY2023 revenue to biosimilar competition beginning 2025. Management has disclosed this as a known headwind. | High |
| 3 | **Product Liability / Recall Risk** | As a manufacturer of pharmaceutical products and medical devices, J&J is subject to ongoing product liability claims. A major recall or adverse safety signal for a high-revenue product would carry significant financial and brand impact. | High |
| 4 | **Pipeline Execution Risk** | A late-stage pipeline failure (Phase 3 study failure or FDA complete response letter) for a key asset (e.g., nipocalimab, next-generation oncology assets) would affect both near-term revenue expectations and investor confidence in the pipeline. | Medium-High |
| 5 | **MedTech Competitive Displacement** | Stryker's Mako robotic platform and Medtronic's surgical robotics programs create risk of orthopedic and surgical market share displacement before J&J's Ottava platform achieves scale. | Medium |
| 6 | **Regulatory / FDA Action Risk** | Drug or device regulatory actions (complete response letters, safety communications, recall orders) carry direct revenue and brand impact. J&J's pharmacovigilance infrastructure is designed to mitigate this risk. | Medium |
| 7 | **Currency / Geographic Exposure** | Approximately 40%+ of revenue is international; currency fluctuations can materially affect reported revenue and earnings. Management discusses constant currency growth rates in earnings communications. | Medium |
| 8 | **Intellectual Property Disputes** | Patent challenges to key pharmaceutical products (including inter partes review proceedings) or medical device IP disputes could affect exclusivity periods and revenue projections. | Medium |

## 9. Brand-Finance Nexus

### 9.1 Five Brand-Finance Signals

**Signal 1 — AAA Credit Rating Enables Favorable Capital Cost**
J&J's Aaa/AAA rating means the company borrows at rates unavailable to any healthcare
competitor. Lower cost of debt reduces the hurdle rate for capital deployment — whether M&A,
R&D investment, or manufacturing capacity. Brand-driven institutional trust directly subsidizes
capital access. (T4_INFERRED; rating T1_OFFICIAL)

**Signal 2 — Our Credo as Governance Premium**
Institutional investors increasingly assess governance frameworks in addition to financial
metrics. J&J's Credo — an 81-year-old patient-first obligation document invoked in board
decisions and crisis responses — provides a governance differentiator that reduces the perceived
risk of management acting contrary to stakeholder interests. (T4_INFERRED from ESG investment
framework context; Credo T1_OFFICIAL)

**Signal 3 — Dividend King Status Attracts Permanent Capital**
62+ consecutive years of dividend increases attracts a category of long-hold institutional
investors (income funds, retirement accounts, certain sovereign wealth funds) who provide
stable share price support and reduce earnings call volatility. The brand's consistency —
Credo-based, long-horizon thinking — directly supports the financial discipline that sustains
the dividend streak. (T4_INFERRED; dividend history T1_OFFICIAL for SEC-disclosed periods)

**Signal 4 — Darzalex Franchise Durability as Brand Proof**
Darzalex's estimated $11.5B in FY2024 sales (estimated) is not merely a commercial success
story — it is evidence that J&J's clinical development machine can produce and sustain
a best-in-class franchise in a competitive indication. This brand credibility in clinical
execution reduces pipeline discount applied by investors to J&J's next-generation assets.
(T4_INFERRED from investor communications context)

**Signal 5 — Post-Kenvue Focused Brand Commands Higher Multiple**
Healthcare conglomerates with consumer and institutional businesses historically trade at
lower valuation multiples than focused pharmaceutical or MedTech pure-plays. J&J's Kenvue
separation may support a re-rating of the remaining entity toward a higher innovation-focused
multiple over time, as the market prices the concentrated IM + MedTech portfolio without the
consumer health drag. (T4_INFERRED from comparable company analysis context; not an investment
recommendation)
