### 3.1 Talc/Asbestos Litigation — LTL Management LLC Proceedings

**Background**: Johnson & Johnson has faced litigation from claimants alleging that talc-based
products (historically including Johnson's Baby Powder) caused ovarian cancer and mesothelioma
due to alleged asbestos contamination. J&J has publicly denied that its talc products contain
asbestos or cause cancer. (T1_OFFICIAL, JNJ official statements; J&J has consistently disputed
liability in public filings and statements)

The extent and outcome of this litigation is subject to ongoing legal proceedings. The
factual record described below is drawn from publicly available court filings and SEC
disclosures. This project makes no assertion about the scientific or legal merits of either
side's position.

**Texas Two-Step / LTL Management Strategy**:
- In October 2021, J&J executed a corporate restructuring under Texas law (the "Texas Two-Step"),
  creating a new subsidiary, LTL Management LLC, which assumed the talc liabilities and
  subsequently filed for Chapter 11 bankruptcy protection in the U.S. Bankruptcy Court for
  the District of New Jersey. (T1_OFFICIAL, public court filings, PACER)
- The strategy sought to use the bankruptcy process to consolidate and resolve talc claims
  through a global settlement plan rather than continued individual litigation.
- The Third Circuit Court of Appeals dismissed the first LTL bankruptcy filing in January
  2023, ruling the case was not filed in good faith because LTL was not in financial distress
  at the time of the filing. (T1_OFFICIAL, Third Circuit opinion, January 30, 2023)
- LTL Management filed a second Chapter 11 petition shortly thereafter. The second filing was
  also dismissed by the Bankruptcy Court and affirmed on appeal. (T1_OFFICIAL, public court
  records)

**$8.9 Billion Settlement Proposal**:
- J&J announced a proposed $8.9 billion settlement offer to resolve existing and future
  talc-related claims, to be funded through a bankruptcy trust structure. (T2_PRIMARY_RELIABLE,
  JNJ press releases; T1_OFFICIAL, 8-K disclosures)
- As of publicly available information through Q1 2025, the settlement has faced challenges
  achieving the claimant vote thresholds required for bankruptcy court confirmation. The
  precise status is subject to ongoing court proceedings; refer to J&J's most recent 10-K
  and 8-K filings for current status. (T1_OFFICIAL)
- J&J has stated publicly that it believes the settlement represents a fair and efficient
  resolution. Claimant attorneys have publicly expressed opposition to aspects of the structure.
  (T3_SECONDARY_RELIABLE, Reuters, Bloomberg legal reporting)

**J&J's publicly stated position**: Johnson & Johnson has consistently stated, in public
filings, press releases, and court submissions, that its talc products do not contain asbestos
and do not cause cancer. The company has expressed that it believes the litigation is being
pursued through a fair process. (T1_OFFICIAL, JNJ 10-K disclosures and press statements)

**Financial disclosure**: J&J's 10-K discloses the talc litigation as a significant
contingency and provides information on the proposed settlement structure, accruals, and the
financial exposure as assessed by management. (T1_OFFICIAL, JNJ 10-K Commitments and
Contingencies note)

**Status as of document date**: Litigation and settlement proceedings ongoing. Refer to the
most recent JNJ 10-K and 8-K for current status. This document does not predict outcomes.

### 3.2 Risperdal / Invega Litigation

**Nature**: Johnson & Johnson and its subsidiary Janssen Pharmaceuticals faced litigation and
government investigations related to the marketing of Risperdal (risperidone) and Invega
(paliperidone) antipsychotic medications. Allegations in various proceedings included claims
related to off-label marketing and marketing practices.

**Settlements and resolutions (publicly disclosed)**:
- In 2013, J&J and Janssen agreed to pay approximately $2.2 billion to resolve federal and
  state investigations related to Risperdal and other drugs. (T1_OFFICIAL, DOJ press release,
  November 4, 2013; T3_SECONDARY_RELIABLE, Reuters)
- Additional state-level litigation and individual cases continued through subsequent years.
  J&J's 10-K filings in subsequent years disclosed the status of residual proceedings.
  (T1_OFFICIAL, 10-K legal proceedings disclosures)

**Status**: The major federal resolution was completed; residual state proceedings status
is disclosed in J&J's ongoing 10-K filings. (T1_OFFICIAL)

### 3.3 Pelvic Mesh Litigation

**Nature**: Johnson & Johnson's Ethicon subsidiary faced litigation from patients who received
transvaginal pelvic mesh implants for stress urinary incontinence and pelvic organ prolapse.
Claimants alleged adverse events including injury from the mesh devices.

**Settlements**: J&J resolved a substantial number of pelvic mesh claims and took related
charges disclosed in its 10-K filings. The company removed transvaginal mesh products from
the U.S. market. (T1_OFFICIAL, JNJ 10-K disclosures; T3_SECONDARY_RELIABLE, Reuters, Bloomberg)

**Status**: Ongoing residual litigation; current status disclosed in J&J's most recent 10-K,
Item 3. (T1_OFFICIAL)

### 3.4 Pharmaceutical Price Litigation / Antitrust Proceedings

**Nature**: Various government and private parties have filed lawsuits related to pharmaceutical
pricing, patent settlement agreements (so-called "pay-for-delay" or reverse payment settlements),
and drug distribution practices. (T1_OFFICIAL, J&J 10-K legal proceedings; T3_SECONDARY_RELIABLE,
press reports)

**Status**: Multiple proceedings in various stages; refer to 10-K Item 3 for current disclosure.
(T1_OFFICIAL)

### 3.5 Other Disclosed Legal Proceedings

Per J&J's 10-K disclosures (T1_OFFICIAL), additional disclosed litigation categories include:
- **Product liability claims** for various pharmaceutical products and medical devices
- **Patent disputes** related to pharmaceutical product exclusivity and device IP
- **Securities class actions** (various; typically resolved or dismissed)
- **Employment and labor matters**

### 4.3 Healthcare Fraud & Abuse Compliance

J&J's pharmaceutical operations are subject to the federal Anti-Kickback Statute (42 U.S.C.
§ 1320a-7b), which prohibits improper remuneration to healthcare professionals in connection
with Medicare/Medicaid-reimbursed products. The Sunshine Act (42 U.S.C. § 1320a-7h) requires
annual disclosure of transfers of value to physicians and teaching hospitals, publicly accessible
in the CMS Open Payments database. (T1_OFFICIAL, applicable statutes and CMS database)

J&J maintains a healthcare compliance program with publicly described elements in its Corporate
Governance / ESG disclosures. (T1_OFFICIAL, jnj.com compliance section)

### 5.4 Writing Risk Guide — Johnson & Johnson (MANDATORY)

> This is the most operationally critical section for day-to-day content creation.
> Every sentence written about Johnson & Johnson in this project must comply with these guidelines.
> The talc litigation section receives the highest-priority caution designation.

- Revenue, net income, margin, and segment data from J&J's SEC filings, cited as "(SEC 10-K FY20XX)"

#### TALC LITIGATION — MANDATORY PHRASINGS

The talc/asbestos litigation carries the highest writing risk for this project. The following
mandatory phrasings and prohibited expressions apply to all content in this database.

**PROHIBITED EXPRESSIONS (Never use these about the talc litigation)**:
- "J&J knew its talc contained asbestos" — makes a factual causation assertion not established by final court judgment
- "J&J hid evidence" — characterizes intent; not established by any final court judgment referenced here
- "J&J endangered consumers" — pejorative characterization as bare assertion
- "The Texas Two-Step was designed to cheat victims" — attributes malicious intent; not established
- "J&J's talc caused cancer" — scientific causation claim beyond the scope of this project
- Any phrasing that asserts J&J acted with wrongful or criminal intent without citing a specific final court judgment or regulatory order using those exact terms

**REQUIRED PHRASINGS (Use these when discussing the talc litigation)**:

| Topic | AVOID | USE INSTEAD |
|-------|-------|-------------|
| General characterization | "J&J's dangerous talc" | "Talc products that have been the subject of litigation, in which claimants have alleged..." |
| Texas Two-Step | "J&J used bankruptcy to escape accountability" | "J&J executed a corporate restructuring under Texas law, creating LTL Management LLC, which filed for Chapter 11 bankruptcy protection. The Third Circuit Court of Appeals dismissed the first filing in January 2023, ruling it was not filed in good faith as LTL was not in financial distress. (T1_OFFICIAL, Third Circuit opinion)" |
| Settlement proposal | "J&J's inadequate $8.9B offer" | "J&J has proposed an $8.9 billion settlement to resolve talc claims, as disclosed in its public filings (T1_OFFICIAL, JNJ 8-K). The settlement's status is subject to ongoing court proceedings." |
| J&J's position | "J&J denies responsibility" | "Johnson & Johnson has stated publicly and in its SEC filings that its talc products do not contain asbestos and do not cause cancer. The company has not acknowledged liability. (T1_OFFICIAL, JNJ 10-K, public statements)" |
| Claimant characterization | Any characterization of individual claimants | Avoid entirely; reference only "persons who filed claims" or "claimants" without characterizing their claims |
| Scientific merits | Any assertion about causation | "Scientific and legal questions regarding talc safety have been contested in litigation. This project does not assert a position on the scientific merits." |

#### DO NOT WRITE (High Legal / Project Integrity Risk)

- Any assertion that J&J engaged in fraud, deception, or criminal conduct without direct
  citation from a final court judgment or regulatory final order using those specific terms
- Characterizing J&J's conduct as "manipulative," "predatory," or "exploitative" as bare
  assertions without regulatory citation using those terms
- Claims about J&J executive knowledge, intent, or internal deliberations not disclosed in
  public filings or established by court judgment
- Reproducing substantial portions of J&J's annual reports, Credo text, or product
  documentation beyond brief citation with analysis
- Asserting outcomes for proceedings that have not reached final judgment (talc settlement,
  ongoing regulatory proceedings)
- Using J&J's logo in our database materials without transformative, clearly editorial context
- Repeating unverified social media claims about J&J's products or litigation as factual statements
- Any statement that could be construed as an assertion that J&J is "guilty" of any charge
  in a proceeding that has not reached final judgment
