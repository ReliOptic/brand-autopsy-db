## Competitive Architecture Overview

Johnson & Johnson's post-Kenvue competitive landscape operates across two distinct arenas:

1. **Innovative Medicine** — competing primarily against Pfizer, Roche/Genentech, AbbVie,
   Bristol-Myers Squibb, Eli Lilly, and Merck in oncology and immunology at the molecule,
   pipeline, and commercial execution levels.

2. **MedTech** — competing primarily against Medtronic, Abbott Laboratories, Stryker, Becton
   Dickinson, and Boston Scientific in surgical instruments, orthopedics, vision care, and
   cardiac electrophysiology at the product platform, surgeon relationship, and hospital
   contracting levels.

Unlike integrated pharma peers such as Pfizer or AbbVie, J&J has a dual commercial
infrastructure serving both therapeutic and device markets. This is simultaneously a complexity
cost and a diversification advantage: a decline in one segment (e.g., MedTech elective procedure
volumes during COVID-19) can be partially offset by performance in the other. (T4_INFERRED from
observed revenue patterns; T1_OFFICIAL for segment data, JNJ 10-K FY2024)

## Innovative Medicine Competitors

### Competitor 1 — Pfizer Inc. (PFE)

**Headquarters**: New York, NY
**FY2023 Total Revenue**: $58.5B (Pfizer 10-K FY2023, T1_OFFICIAL)
**Credit Rating (Moody's)**: A2 (T1_OFFICIAL, Moody's public rating)

**Overlapping therapeutic areas**: Oncology (multiple myeloma — Pfizer's Elrexfio in BCMA-targeted
therapy; prostate cancer — Pfizer's Xtandi co-promotion with Astellas); inflammation and
immunology (Pfizer's Xeljanz JAK inhibitor competes in the same rheumatoid arthritis and IBD
space as J&J's Remicade/biosimilar era, and the broader immunology landscape)

**Key competitive dynamic vs. JNJ**: Pfizer's post-COVID-19 financial profile was significantly
affected by the normalization of Paxlovid and Comirnaty revenues from 2022 pandemic-era peaks.
Pfizer's FY2023 10-K disclosed substantial revenue contraction as COVID product revenues
declined. (T1_OFFICIAL, Pfizer 10-K FY2023) J&J's revenue base has no equivalent COVID-driven
distortion — its FY2024 growth is organic through the Darzalex and Erleada franchises.
(T4_INFERRED from comparative financial profiles)

**Brand comparison**: Pfizer carries high public awareness amplified by COVID vaccine
prominence, but in the hematologic oncology prescriber audience, J&J's Darzalex franchise has
established a deeper clinical authority position in multiple myeloma specifically — an indication
where Pfizer's hematology portfolio is less prominent. (T4_INFERRED from publicly observable
FDA approval records and clinical practice guidelines)

### Competitor 2 — Roche Holding AG / Genentech (RHHBY)

**Headquarters**: Basel, Switzerland
**FY2023 Group Sales**: CHF 58.7B (Roche Annual Report FY2023, T1_OFFICIAL; approximately $65B
at 2023 average CHF/USD exchange rates, estimated)

**Overlapping therapeutic areas**: Hematologic oncology — Roche's portfolio includes MabThera/
Rituxan (CD20), Gazyva (obinutuzumab), and Polivy in diffuse large B-cell lymphoma and follicular
lymphoma. While Roche's anti-CD20 mechanism and J&J's Darzalex anti-CD38 mechanism operate at
different molecular targets, they compete for patient population in relapsed/refractory lymphomas
and the broader hematology treatment algorithm. Additionally, Roche's immunology portfolio
(Actemra) competes in the rheumatoid arthritis space adjacent to J&J's immunology franchise.

**Key competitive dynamic vs. JNJ**: Roche operates the world's largest oncology diagnostics
portfolio through Roche Diagnostics, enabling a companion diagnostics strategy that J&J does
not replicate at equivalent scale. This creates a potential competitive moat in precision oncology —
offering both the biomarker test and the matched therapeutic. (T4_INFERRED from publicly observable
Roche portfolio positioning)

**Brand comparison**: Roche communicates a "personalised healthcare" brand positioning that is
scientifically sophisticated but does not have a direct equivalent to J&J's Credo as a patient
obligation document. In the oncology prescriber audience, both brands carry high scientific
credibility through peer-reviewed clinical trial publication records. (T4_INFERRED from observed
brand communications at both companies)

### Competitor 3 — AbbVie Inc. (ABBV)

**Headquarters**: North Chicago, IL
**FY2023 Net Revenue**: $54.3B (AbbVie 10-K FY2023, T1_OFFICIAL)
**FY2024 Revenue**: Approximately $56.3B as reported in AbbVie's FY2024 earnings release
(T2_PRIMARY_RELIABLE, AbbVie Q4 FY2024 press release)

**Overlapping therapeutic areas**: Immunology is the direct head-to-head battleground.
AbbVie's Skyrizi (risankizumab, IL-23 inhibitor) and Rinvoq (upadacitinib, JAK1 inhibitor)
compete directly with J&J's Tremfya (guselkumab, IL-23) and the broader Stelara franchise
(ustekinumab, IL-12/23) in moderate-to-severe psoriasis, psoriatic arthritis, Crohn's disease,
and ulcerative colitis.

**The Stelara LOE confrontation**: J&J's Stelara generated approximately $9.7B in FY2023 global
sales (T1_OFFICIAL, JNJ 10-K FY2023 segment disclosures). Multiple biosimilar ustekinumab
manufacturers received FDA approval, with U.S. market entry beginning in January 2025 per
publicly reported settlement agreement terms with Amgen, Samsung Bioepis, and other biosimilar
developers. (T3_SECONDARY_RELIABLE, Reuters, Bloomberg reports on settlement timelines;
T1_OFFICIAL for FDA biosimilar approval records) AbbVie's Skyrizi is positioned to capture
switching immunology patients as Stelara's share erodes.

**Key competitive dynamic vs. JNJ**: AbbVie's commercial machine in immunology is among the
most effective in the industry — demonstrated by its management of the post-Humira LOE
transition, where Skyrizi and Rinvoq growth has partially offset Humira biosimilar erosion.
However, AbbVie's absence from MedTech means it carries higher concentration risk relative
to J&J's dual-segment model. (T4_INFERRED from public financial disclosures)

**Brand comparison**: AbbVie's brand identity is commercially aggressive and pipeline-narrative-
driven. It does not maintain an equivalent to J&J's Credo as a patient-obligation governance
document. The brand speaks in a more clinical-commercial register; J&J's Credo-anchored brand
carries a deeper institutional trust architecture. (T4_INFERRED from observed brand communications)

### Competitor 4 — Bristol-Myers Squibb (BMY)

**Headquarters**: New York, NY
**FY2023 Total Revenues**: $45.0B (BMY 10-K FY2023, T1_OFFICIAL)

**Overlapping therapeutic areas**: Multiple myeloma — BMS's Revlimid (lenalidomide, now generic),
Pomalyst (pomalidomide), and Abecma/Breyanzi (CAR-T therapies) compete with Darzalex in the
relapsed/refractory myeloma treatment algorithm. Oncology KOLs and tumor boards evaluate
Darzalex-based regimens against BMS's IMiD and CAR-T options across different patient profiles.

**Key competitive dynamic vs. JNJ**: BMS has invested heavily in cell therapy (Abecma, Breyanzi),
representing a next-generation treatment modality that J&J is also pursuing through Ciltacabtagene
autoleceucel (Carvykti, co-developed with Legend Biotech). Both companies are in the CAR-T
space in myeloma, creating direct head-to-head competition at the frontier of treatment. The
relative clinical positioning of Darzalex-based combinations versus CAR-T approaches across
different patient lines is an active area of clinical investigation as of 2024–2025. (T4_INFERRED
from publicly observable clinical landscape; specific trial outcomes subject to independent
clinical assessment)

## MedTech Competitors

### Competitor 5 — Medtronic plc (MDT)

**Headquarters**: Dublin, Ireland (operational: Minneapolis, MN)
**FY2024 Revenue (fiscal year ending April 2024)**: $32.4B (Medtronic 10-K FY2024, T1_OFFICIAL)

**Overlapping segments**: Surgical robotics — Medtronic's Hugo robotic-assisted surgery system
competes with J&J's Ottava platform (in development/early commercialization as of 2024) in the
soft tissue laparoscopic surgery market where Intuitive Surgical's da Vinci has dominant
installed base. Cardiac electrophysiology — Medtronic's mapping and ablation platforms compete
with J&J's Biosense Webster CARTO electrophysiology system and its pulsed field ablation (PFA)
catheter development programs.

**Key competitive dynamic vs. JNJ**: Medtronic's established leadership in cardiac rhythm
management and its early surgical robotics investment creates first-mover dynamics in segments
where J&J's Ottava is still scaling commercially. The race to establish robotic surgery platform
ecosystems — where the installed base drives recurring instrument revenue — favors incumbency.
(T3_SECONDARY_RELIABLE, Bloomberg industry coverage; T4_INFERRED for competitive timing)

**Brand comparison**: Medtronic's brand mission ("To alleviate pain, restore health, and extend
life") occupies similar Caregiver archetype territory as J&J's Credo, differentiated primarily
by sub-segment specialization rather than brand architecture approach. Both brands speak to
clinical outcomes and patient impact. (T4_INFERRED)

### Competitor 6 — Stryker Corporation (SYK)

**Headquarters**: Kalamazoo, MI
**FY2023 Net Sales**: $20.5B (Stryker 10-K FY2023, T1_OFFICIAL)

**Overlapping segments**: Orthopedics is the direct competition arena. J&J's DePuy Synthes
franchise — spanning hip replacement, knee replacement, spine, and trauma fixation — competes
against Stryker's orthopedics division in the same hospital procurement and surgeon preference
decisions. Stryker's Mako robotic joint replacement system has gained significant adoption, with
over 1,600 systems installed globally as of Stryker's publicly reported investor data.
(T3_SECONDARY_RELIABLE, Stryker investor presentations) J&J's VELYS robotic-assisted solution
for knee arthroplasty represents its competitive response in robotic orthopedics.
(T1_OFFICIAL, jnj.com MedTech product pages)

**Key competitive dynamic vs. JNJ**: The orthopedics robotics race between Stryker's Mako and
J&J's VELYS is a competitive dynamic with long-term implications: hospitals that standardize
on one platform tend to expand their use of that vendor's implants over time, creating a
flywheel between robotic platform adoption and implant market share. (T4_INFERRED from
publicly observable orthopedic market dynamics)

### Competitor 7 — Abbott Laboratories (ABT)

**Headquarters**: North Chicago, IL
**FY2023 Reported Sales**: $20.0B (excluding COVID testing, estimated from Abbott 10-K FY2023,
T1_OFFICIAL)

**Overlapping segments**: Electrophysiology — Abbott's EnSite mapping system and cardiac ablation
catheter business compete directly with J&J's Biosense Webster CARTO mapping platform in the
cardiac electrophysiology laboratory. Both companies are investing in pulsed field ablation (PFA)
technology for atrial fibrillation treatment, a rapidly growing procedural category driven by
the global prevalence increase of AF. (T3_SECONDARY_RELIABLE, industry research reports)

## Key Competitive Threat: Stelara Biosimilar Erosion (2025–2027)

The highest-priority disclosed competitive threat in J&J's FY2024 communications is the
loss of exclusivity for Stelara (ustekinumab), one of the company's largest-revenue products.
Multiple biosimilar ustekinumab manufacturers received FDA approval. U.S. market entry was
expected beginning January 2025 per settlement agreements. (T3_SECONDARY_RELIABLE, Reuters,
Bloomberg; T1_OFFICIAL for FDA approval records at fda.gov/drugs)

J&J's management has publicly acknowledged this headwind and positioned Tremfya (guselkumab,
selective IL-23p19 inhibitor) as the commercial successor in psoriasis and expanding IBD
indications. Tremfya holds patent exclusivity through the late 2020s and offers a differentiated
IL-23-specific mechanism versus Stelara's combined IL-12/23 inhibition. (T1_OFFICIAL, JNJ
10-K FY2024 pipeline disclosures; T4_INFERRED for commercial offset assessment)

The immunology competitive landscape intensified significantly between 2022 and 2025, with
AbbVie's Skyrizi, Eli Lilly's Taltz (ixekizumab) and Omvoh (mirikizumab), UCB's Bimzelx
(bimekizumab), and biosimilar ustekinumab from Amgen, Samsung Bioepis, and others all competing
for the psoriasis and IBD patient population. J&J's Tremfya commercial execution in this
environment represents the critical near-term test of the Innovative Medicine segment's ability
to sustain immunology franchise revenue through LOE transitions. (T4_INFERRED from competitive
landscape analysis of publicly available information)

## Three Observations on Competitive Brand Positioning

**Observation 1 — The Credo moat is non-replicable but also requires visible behavioral proof.**
No competitor can credibly adopt a document analogous to J&J's 1943 Credo without decades of
operational history substantiating its application under real adversity. However, the talc
litigation's procedural strategy (discussed in Layer 08) creates a brand tension: stakeholders
who know the Credo may assess whether specific litigation strategies align with its first
obligation to patients. This tension is uniquely a J&J brand challenge. (T4_INFERRED)

**Observation 2 — Dual-segment model creates brand scope competitors cannot match individually.**
J&J's combination of a leading multiple myeloma franchise (Darzalex), a leading cardiac
electrophysiology platform (Biosense Webster CARTO), and a leading orthopedic implant system
(DePuy Synthes) across a single masterbrand creates enterprise relationship value that no single
pharma or single device company can replicate. The challenge is that J&J's individual product
brands often communicate more prominently in clinical contexts than the J&J masterbrand umbrella.
(T4_INFERRED from observed brand architecture in medical marketing)

**Observation 3 — AAA credit rating functions as a procurement brand signal.**
Hospital systems and healthcare system CFOs evaluating 5-year vendor contracts implicitly
consider counterparty financial durability. J&J's Aaa/AAA credit rating — one of two U.S.
corporations at this level — provides a brand signal of permanence that no commercial competitor
can match at the same credit quality level. This is not a clinical brand dimension; it is a
procurement and institutional brand dimension that reinforces long-term contracting decisions.
(T4_INFERRED; rating facts T1_OFFICIAL)
