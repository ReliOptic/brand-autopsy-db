## 1. Channel Strategy Overview

Johnson & Johnson's post-Kenvue channel strategy reflects the brand's institutional, B2B, and
B2P communications mandate. With the consumer health audience transferred to Kenvue, J&J's
channel mix has shifted toward professional and institutional registers. The company no longer
needs to win at retail point-of-purchase or consumer social media scale. Its channel investment
focuses on: (a) HCP (healthcare professional) engagement in medical and scientific contexts,
(b) investor and financial media communications, (c) regulatory and policy stakeholder engagement,
and (d) patient/caregiver direct engagement for serious disease areas.

The brand's Credo obligation — patients first, then employees, then communities, then shareholders —
maps approximately to the channel priority order: clinical/patient channels serve the first
obligation; investor relations channels serve the fourth. Each channel tier has a distinct
formality register, legal compliance framework, and audience expectation set. (T4_INFERRED;
T1_OFFICIAL for Credo source)

## 6. Channel-Specific Compliance Requirements

### Investor Relations (investor.jnj.com / SEC filings)

- **No promotional language** in any document filed with the SEC. Earnings releases and 10-K/10-Q
  filings must comply with Regulation FD and applicable SEC disclosure rules. (T1_OFFICIAL, SEC)
- **Forward-looking statements** require explicit safe harbor language per the Private Securities
  Litigation Reform Act (PSLRA). All forward-looking statements in earnings calls, presentations,
  and press releases must include safe harbor disclaimers. (T1_OFFICIAL, SEC requirements)
- **No selective disclosure**: material non-public information must be disclosed publicly before
  or simultaneously with any selective communication to analysts or investors. (T1_OFFICIAL,
  Regulation FD, 17 CFR § 243)
- **Earnings call transcripts** are publicly accessible via IR website and third-party financial
  data services; content is T2_PRIMARY_RELIABLE when cited with speaker attribution.

### Healthcare Professional (HCP) Digital Channels (Janssen.com, HCP sections of jnj.com)

- **Promotional content for pharmaceutical products** directed at HCPs is subject to FDA
  regulations governing prescription drug promotion (21 CFR Part 202). Claims must be consistent
  with the FDA-approved prescribing information (label). (T1_OFFICIAL, FDA regulations)
- **Off-label promotion is prohibited**: HCP-directed content must remain within the scope of
  FDA-approved indications. (T4_INFERRED from applicable FDA promotional guidance)
- **Required promotional disclosures** include full prescribing information reference, indication
  statement, and important safety information (ISI) in appropriate contexts. (T4_INFERRED)
- **Medical Education vs. Promotion**: J&J's medical affairs function engages in non-promotional
  medical education. Content produced by medical affairs is distinct from promotional materials
  under applicable frameworks. (T4_INFERRED from standard pharma medical affairs practice)

### Direct-to-Consumer (DTC) Advertising (US Market)

- **DTC pharmaceutical advertising** for prescription products in the United States is regulated
  by the FDA's Office of Prescription Drug Promotion (OPDP). Major fair balance disclosures
  (risks, side effects) are required in broadcast DTC ads. (T1_OFFICIAL, FDA OPDP guidance)
- **J&J's immunology DTC campaigns** (Tremfya, Stelara historically) include fair balance
  disclosures consistent with FDA requirements. (T4_INFERRED from observed DTC advertising patterns)
- **No DTC advertising for medical devices**: MedTech products are not typically promoted
  through mass consumer DTC campaigns; MedTech marketing channels are professional/institutional.
  (T4_INFERRED)

## 7. Crisis Communications Protocol — Channel-Specific

### Scenario 1 — FDA Safety Communication or Drug Recall

**Channel sequence:**
1. Legal and regulatory review: what can be publicly stated and when (T+0 to T+12h)
2. 8-K filed with SEC if material; press release on investor.jnj.com (T+12–24h)
3. Janssen.com / HCP communication update with prescribing guidance (T+24–48h)
4. CarePath patient notification program activated for affected therapy patients (T+48–72h)
5. Media inquiry response: factual, citing regulatory body communications (ongoing)

### Scenario 2 — Talc/Asbestos Litigation Development

**Channel sequence:**
1. Legal review: assess materiality for SEC disclosure purposes
2. If material: 8-K filed; controlled statement citing the proceeding, J&J's position, and
   next steps — no characterization of claimants or outcome predictions
3. IR website update: legal proceedings section updated per 10-K Item 3 standards
4. Social channels: no speculative commentary; factual reference to official disclosures only
5. See Layer 08, Section 5.4 Writing Risk Guide for required phrasings

### Scenario 3 — Pipeline Failure (Phase 3 Study Negative)

**Channel sequence:**
1. Press release on investor.jnj.com disclosing the data (T1_OFFICIAL, material event)
2. 8-K filed if financial materiality threshold reached
3. Investor briefing: provide scientific context without minimizing the result
4. Twitter/X: factual announcement citing the press release
5. LinkedIn: CEO/CSO comment providing pipeline context (remaining assets, strategic implications)
6. Medical affairs: communicate to KOLs and investigator network

## 9. Steal Sheet — 3 Transferable Principles

**1. Build channel architecture around your stakeholder hierarchy, not your marketing calendar.**
J&J's channel map mirrors the Credo's priority order: patient channels (CarePath, jnj.com patient
sections) serve the first obligation; HCP/medical affairs channels serve the prescriber; IR
channels serve the shareholder. Each channel has a specific compliance framework and audience
expectation. Transferable structure: audit your channel mix and ask which stakeholder obligation
each channel primarily serves. Channels that serve no clear stakeholder need can be deprioritized;
channels that serve underserved stakeholders can be invested in.

**2. Medical congresses are your highest-return content investment if your brand has clinical
evidence to present.**
For companies in healthcare, MedTech, diagnostics, or any evidence-based industry, the annual
major professional congress is the most concentrated audience of qualified decision-makers
available at any single event. J&J invests in satellite symposia, poster sessions, and KOL
briefings at every major relevant congress because these are the venues where clinical credibility
is established with prescribers. Transferable structure: identify the two or three annual
conferences where your target audience is most concentrated and build a content presence that
goes beyond a booth — symposia, papers, panel moderation, sponsored education.

**3. Treat your regulatory filing disclosures as primary content, not compliance obligations.**
JNJ's 10-K, 10-Q, and 8-K filings are the highest-authority, most trusted content the brand
produces. Financial analysts, investigative journalists, and sophisticated investors treat these
documents as the primary truth source for everything the company claims in marketing. Transferable
structure: treat your terms of service, financial disclosures, privacy policy, and compliance
documents as brand content. The quality of language, specificity of commitment, and internal
consistency across your formal documents and your marketing copy is a signal of institutional
integrity that sophisticated audiences notice and remember.
