## Audience Architecture Overview

Johnson & Johnson's post-Kenvue audience structure is fundamentally B2B and B2P
(business-to-professional and business-to-patient). The August 2023 separation of consumer health
(now Kenvue, ticker KVUE) removed J&J's mass-consumer audience — the parents buying Band-Aids and
Tylenol — and concentrated the remaining brand on institutional and clinical audiences. This is a
structurally significant shift in brand communications strategy: J&J no longer needs to speak to
the general public about household products. It speaks to payers, prescribers, procurement
officers, patients with serious diseases, and the capital markets. (T1_OFFICIAL, JNJ 10-K FY2024;
T4_INFERRED for strategic communications implication)

Post-Kenvue, J&J's revenue is generated across two segments:
- **Innovative Medicine**: reported segment sales disclosed in JNJ 10-K FY2024 (T1_OFFICIAL)
- **MedTech**: reported segment sales disclosed in JNJ 10-K FY2024 (T1_OFFICIAL)

FY2024 total adjusted operational sales were approximately $88.8B as reported in J&J's FY2024
earnings release. (T2_PRIMARY_RELIABLE, JNJ Q4 FY2024 earnings press release)

Each segment serves distinct primary audiences with different decision-making structures,
buying timelines, and brand trust requirements.

## Primary Audience 1 — Oncologists & Hematologists (Innovative Medicine)

**Profile**: Specialist physicians managing hematologic malignancies (multiple myeloma, B-cell
lymphomas, prostate cancer) and immunology conditions. Academic and community oncology settings.
Leading institutions include major NCI-designated cancer centers and community oncology networks.

**Why they matter**: Prescribers of Darzalex (daratumumab), J&J's highest-revenue product.
Darzalex's multiple myeloma franchise spans front-line through relapsed/refractory settings,
with FDA approvals across multiple combination regimens. (T1_OFFICIAL, FDA.gov NDA approval
records) Erleada (apalutamide) for prostate cancer adds a second oncology prescriber audience
in urology and oncology. (T1_OFFICIAL, FDA.gov)

**Decision-making criteria**:
- Clinical trial data quality (Phase 3 overall survival, progression-free survival outcomes)
- FDA-approved label scope (breadth of approved combinations and lines of therapy)
- Patient support program infrastructure (prior authorization support, nursing programs)
- Peer-reviewed publication record and Key Opinion Leader endorsement at ASCO, ASH, EHA
- Reimbursement landscape (Medicare Part B, commercial payer coverage)

**JNJ's brand signal to this audience**: Scientific authority built through the depth of clinical
evidence supporting Darzalex across its approved indications and the company's presence at every
major hematology congress. (T4_INFERRED from observed JNJ medical affairs activities)

**Pain points addressed**: Complexity of frontline multiple myeloma treatment selection; need for
combination regimens with established long-term safety profiles; patient support infrastructure
for subcutaneous administration programs.

## Primary Audience 2 — Hospital Systems & IDN Procurement Officers (MedTech)

**Profile**: Value Analysis Committees (VACs), supply chain directors, and C-suite procurement
leadership at Integrated Delivery Networks (IDNs), academic medical centers, and ambulatory
surgical centers. Buyers of DePuy Synthes orthopedic implants, Ethicon surgical instruments
and stapling systems, Biosense Webster cardiac electrophysiology catheters, and J&J Vision
surgical and consumer contact lens products.

**Scale context**: The U.S. hospital market includes approximately 6,100 hospitals (American
Hospital Association data, T3_SECONDARY_RELIABLE). JNJ's MedTech segment serves a significant
proportion of these facilities through direct sales forces and distributor networks.

**Decision-making criteria**:
- Total cost of care (device cost + procedure time + complication rate + revision rate)
- Clinical outcomes data (published registries, real-world evidence, registry data)
- Contract pricing via Group Purchasing Organizations (GPOs) such as Premier and Vizient
- Surgeon preference and training investment required for platform adoption
- Service and support infrastructure (field clinical specialists, technical support)
- Capital equipment economics (robotic platform acquisition or rental models)

**JNJ's brand signal to this audience**: Institutional reliability. A hospital system standardizing
on DePuy Synthes hip implant systems or Ethicon stapling platforms enters a multi-year contract
relationship with training, inventory, and workflow dependencies. The J&J brand's 138-year
institutional track record and AAA credit rating reduce perceived counterparty risk in these
high-commitment procurement decisions. (T4_INFERRED; AAA rating T1_OFFICIAL)

**Pain points addressed**: Rising procedural volume pressure, surgeon efficiency demands,
value-based contracting requirements from CMS and commercial payers, robotic surgery platform
adoption decisions (VELYS for orthopedics, Ottava in development for general surgery).

## Primary Audience 3 — Patients with Serious Immunological & Oncological Disease

**Profile**: Adults living with moderate-to-severe plaque psoriasis, psoriatic arthritis,
Crohn's disease, ulcerative colitis (Tremfya, Stelara indications), multiple myeloma, and
castration-resistant or metastatic prostate cancer (Darzalex, Erleada indications). Patients
navigating complex, long-duration treatment decisions in partnership with specialist physicians.

**Engagement model**: Direct-to-patient advertising for immunology brands is permitted under
FDA regulations in the United States. J&J's Tremfya and the Stelara portfolio have utilized
direct-to-consumer campaigns targeting patients who may not yet be on biologic therapy.
Patient engagement also occurs via J&J's CarePath patient support program, which provides
insurance navigation assistance, co-pay support, and adherence resources. (T1_OFFICIAL,
jnj.com/carepath; observed on jnj.com)

**Decision-making criteria at patient level**:
- Efficacy in symptom control (skin clearance rates, remission rates in IBD, MRD negativity
  in multiple myeloma)
- Safety profile (adverse event frequency, long-term safety data from registries)
- Administration convenience (subcutaneous self-injection vs. IV infusion, dosing frequency)
- Insurance coverage and out-of-pocket cost management (co-pay programs)
- Physician recommendation strength

**JNJ's brand signal to this audience**: The Credo's first obligation — "patients, doctors and
nurses, to mothers and fathers and all others who use our products and services." CarePath and
comparable patient support programs translate this Credo language into operational patient
infrastructure. (T1_OFFICIAL, Our Credo; T4_INFERRED for Credo-program connection)

## Primary Audience 4 — Institutional Investors & Capital Markets

**Profile**: Long-only institutional equity funds, healthcare sector ETFs (XLV), dividend-focused
income investors, ESG-screened funds. J&J is a component of the Dow Jones Industrial Average,
S&P 500, and multiple healthcare sector indices. As of early 2025, market capitalization is
in the approximately $370–$400B range (estimated, publicly observable market data).

**Why they matter**: J&J's AAA credit rating (Moody's Aaa / S&P AAA — one of only two U.S.
corporations at this level alongside Microsoft, as of publicly available agency ratings),
62+ consecutive years of dividend increases qualifying it as a Dividend King, and the post-Kenvue
strategic clarity make it a core holding in institutional healthcare portfolios. (T1_OFFICIAL,
Moody's and S&P public ratings; T3_SECONDARY_RELIABLE for Dividend King characterization)

**Decision-making criteria for this audience**:
- EPS growth trajectory and guidance credibility (operational sales growth excluding Kenvue effects)
- Pipeline depth and near-term catalyst timeline (Phase 3 data readouts, FDA PDUFA dates)
- Talc litigation resolution trajectory and financial exposure quantification
- Stelara loss-of-exclusivity headwind management and commercial offset execution
- MedTech recovery from elective procedure volume disruptions
- Capital allocation strategy (dividends, buybacks, M&A)

**JNJ's brand signal to this audience**: Financial durability and governance discipline. The AAA
credit rating, Dividend King status, and Credo as governance framework collectively position J&J
as the healthcare sector's most institutionally durable large-cap investment. (T4_INFERRED from
observed investor relations positioning)

## Secondary Audience 1 — Regulatory Agencies (FDA, EMA, PMDA, National Health Authorities)

**Profile**: U.S. Food and Drug Administration (Center for Drug Evaluation and Research; Center
for Devices and Radiological Health), European Medicines Agency (EMA), Japanese Pharmaceuticals
and Medical Devices Agency (PMDA), and equivalent national agencies in 150+ markets.

**Relationship type**: Regulated-entity relationship, not a customer relationship. However, the
quality of J&J's regulatory relationship — built through decades of clinical rigor, transparent
adverse event reporting, and high-quality submission packages — constitutes a brand asset that
affects approval timelines and label breadth. (T4_INFERRED)

**JNJ's brand signal**: Scientific credibility and regulatory transparency. Agencies assess
submission quality, pharmacovigilance responsiveness, and post-market commitment. J&J's global
regulatory affairs infrastructure is built for this audience's expectations. (T4_INFERRED)

---

## Secondary Audience 2 — Surgeons & Interventional Specialists (MedTech)

**Profile**: Orthopedic surgeons (DePuy Synthes), general and laparoscopic surgeons (Ethicon),
cardiac electrophysiologists (Biosense Webster), ophthalmic surgeons (J&J Vision Surgical /
TECNIS IOL), and cataract surgery specialists. These are the end users of MedTech products whose
clinical preferences heavily influence hospital procurement and standardization decisions.

**Engagement model**: Clinical education, proctoring programs, cadaveric skills labs, and
surgical technique development. Transfers of value from medical device companies to healthcare
professionals are subject to Sunshine Act (Open Payments) disclosure requirements. (T1_OFFICIAL,
CMS Open Payments database)

**JNJ's brand signal**: Clinical innovation combined with training infrastructure. J&J's
educational programs for surgical technique and device adoption represent brand investment that
deepens the surgeon relationship over time. (observed on jnj.com MedTech pages; T4_INFERRED)

---

## Secondary Audience 3 — Commercial Payers & Pharmacy Benefit Managers (Innovative Medicine)

**Profile**: Major commercial health insurance plans, Pharmacy Benefit Managers (Express Scripts /
Evernorth, CVS Caremark, OptumRx), and government payers (Medicare Part B/D, Medicaid, VA/DoD).
These entities negotiate formulary placement, prior authorization criteria, and rebate agreements
for J&J's biologic and specialty pharmaceutical products.

**Why they matter**: Access to this audience determines the real-world prescribability of J&J's
drugs. A biologic with strong clinical data but poor formulary position reaches fewer patients.
J&J's market access function engages payers with health economics and outcomes research (HEOR)
data demonstrating total cost of care value. (T4_INFERRED from standard pharmaceutical market
access model)

**JNJ's brand signal**: Evidence of total cost reduction. Payers respond to economic evidence
that a drug reduces hospitalization rates, emergency visits, or surgical interventions. J&J's
HEOR function builds this case for each major asset. (T4_INFERRED)

## Audience Shift: Pre- vs. Post-Kenvue

The Kenvue separation in August 2023 represents the most significant audience restructuring
in J&J's modern history. Prior to the separation, J&J maintained a dual identity as both a
consumer household brand and an institutional healthcare enterprise. The post-Kenvue J&J has
focused primarily on professional, institutional, and B2P healthcare audiences.
(T1_OFFICIAL, JNJ 10-K FY2024, strategic rationale disclosures)

| Audience Type | Pre-Kenvue (JNJ) | Post-Kenvue (JNJ) | Post-Kenvue (Kenvue) |
|--------------|------------------|-------------------|----------------------|
| General consumers | Yes (Band-Aid, Tylenol, Neutrogena) | No | Yes |
| Retail pharmacy buyers | Yes | No | Yes |
| Oncology / hematology prescribers | Yes | Yes | No |
| Hospital procurement | Yes | Yes | No |
| Institutional investors | Yes | Yes | Separate entity |
| Patients with serious disease | Yes | Yes | No |

*Sources: T1_OFFICIAL, JNJ 10-K FY2024; Kenvue 10-K FY2023*

This separation clarifies J&J's brand communication task: it no longer needs to earn the
trust of parents buying consumer health products. It needs to maintain the trust of oncologists,
cardiac surgeons, hospital CFOs, and the FDA. These audiences require a fundamentally different
brand register — one built on clinical evidence, institutional track record, and financial
durability rather than emotional warmth and consumer familiarity. (T4_INFERRED)
