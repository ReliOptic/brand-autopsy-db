## IP Portfolio Overview

Amgen's intellectual property position is central to its commercial viability. Key elements of the portfolio as disclosed in SEC filings:

**Patents**: Amgen holds thousands of patents globally covering composition of matter for biologics, manufacturing processes, formulations, and methods of treatment. (SEC 10-K FY2023) The scope and duration of patent protection varies by product and geography.

**Key patent situations**:
- **Enbrel (etanercept)**: Amgen's US patent position has been maintained through a licensing arrangement with Pfizer/Wyeth (co-owner of Enbrel in the US). This arrangement has functionally delayed US biosimilar entry despite European biosimilar approvals. Patent expiry dynamics are a material disclosure in Amgen's 10-K. (SEC 10-K FY2023)
- **Prolia/XGEVA (denosumab)**: Patents covering composition of matter and methods of treatment are scheduled to expire in the late 2020s, representing a significant loss-of-exclusivity event. (SEC 10-K FY2023)
- **Repatha (evolocumab)**: The PCSK9 inhibitor class has been subject to significant patent litigation. The Federal Circuit's 2019 ruling narrowing Amgen's PCSK9 antibody genus claims against Sanofi/Regeneron was a significant adverse outcome for Amgen's patent position. (T3_SECONDARY_RELIABLE, Federal Circuit decision 2019; T4_INFERRED for brand impact assessment)

**Trademarks**: Amgen holds registered trademarks for all major product names (Enbrel, Prolia, XGEVA, Repatha, Otezla, Kyprolis, Blincyto, Nplate, and others) as well as the Amgen corporate mark. (T4_INFERRED from standard pharmaceutical trademark practice; SEC 10-K FY2023 IP section)

**Biosimilar patents**: Amgen's biosimilar products (Amgevita, Kanjinti, Mvasi, Riabni, and others) operate under agreements with the reference product sponsors following the Biologics Price Competition and Innovation Act (BPCIA) patent dance process. (SEC 10-K FY2023)

## Major Litigation

### Matter 1: PCSK9 Antibody Patent Litigation — Amgen vs. Sanofi/Regeneron

**Status**: Concluded at Federal Circuit level (adverse to Amgen); Supreme Court petition addressed.

**Facts**: Amgen sued Sanofi and Regeneron alleging infringement of broad genus patents covering PCSK9 antibodies. The Federal Circuit ruled in 2019 that Amgen's claims were not enabled under 35 U.S.C. § 112 because the patent specification did not enable one of ordinary skill to make and use the full scope of the claimed genus. The Supreme Court affirmed in May 2023 in a unanimous decision. (T3_SECONDARY_RELIABLE, Federal Circuit and Supreme Court public records; T1_OFFICIAL, Supreme Court opinion Amgen Inc. v. Sanofi, 598 U.S. ___, 2023)

**Brand impact**: The Supreme Court loss removes a legal barrier that had constrained Praluent's commercial development. This may intensify competition in the PCSK9 class, potentially affecting Repatha's revenue trajectory. (T4_INFERRED)

### Matter 2: Biosimilar Patent Disputes

**Context**: Amgen is simultaneously a defendant in biosimilar patent cases (where competitors challenge its innovator patents) and a plaintiff (where Amgen's biosimilar products face challenges from innovator manufacturers).

**Enbrel-related**: Samsung Bioepis and Pfizer have pursued Enbrel biosimilar development in Europe successfully. US biosimilar entry has been delayed by the patent/licensing landscape. (T3_SECONDARY_RELIABLE, multiple press reports; SEC 10-K FY2023 legal proceedings)

**Amgen biosimilar as plaintiff**: Amgen has been involved in BPCIA patent dance proceedings for its own biosimilars (Mvasi, Kanjinti) against Genentech/Roche. Multiple disputes were resolved through settlement or litigation. (SEC 10-K FY2023 legal proceedings)

---

### Matter 3: Horizon Therapeutics Acquisition Regulatory Review

**Status**: Completed (FTC investigation resolved).

**Facts**: The FTC investigated the proposed $28B acquisition of Horizon Therapeutics under antitrust law. The FTC filed a complaint in May 2023 alleging the acquisition would enable Amgen to use its commercial relationships with PBMs to disadvantage competing products in the rare disease market. The FTC and Amgen reached a consent agreement in September 2023 that imposed behavioral remedies without blocking the transaction. (T1_OFFICIAL, FTC press release September 1, 2023; SEC 8-K filings)

**Brand impact**: The FTC consent order imposed restrictions on how Amgen may contract for Horizon products with PBMs. This is a material commercial constraint disclosed in Amgen's SEC filings. (SEC 10-K FY2023)

## Regulatory Landscape

**FDA oversight**: Amgen's products are regulated as biologics (BLA pathway) and, for Otezla, as a small-molecule drug (NDA pathway). Amgen maintains an active FDA relationship through periodic inspections of its manufacturing facilities, PDUFA submissions, post-marketing commitment fulfillment, and REMS program administration. (SEC 10-K FY2023)

**Inflation Reduction Act (IRA)**: The IRA authorizes Medicare to negotiate prices for selected high-expenditure drugs. Repatha was selected for the first cycle of Medicare drug price negotiation (drugs to take negotiated prices beginning 2026). This is a material financial event disclosed by Amgen and represents a new regulatory dimension affecting pricing strategy. (SEC 10-K FY2023; T3_SECONDARY_RELIABLE, CMS published drug list)

**EU regulatory environment**: Amgen's biologics are subject to EMA review and periodic re-assessment. European pricing and reimbursement negotiations are conducted at member state level and represent a separate regulatory track from FDA/CMS. (T4_INFERRED from pharmaceutical regulatory landscape)
