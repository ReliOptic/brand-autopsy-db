## Content Pillars

Amgen operates a dual-audience content strategy: scientific/clinical content for healthcare professionals and payers, and patient-narrative content for public-facing channels. The two tracks use different voices but share a common foundation: factual grounding.

| Pillar | Share (estimated) | Description |
|--------|-------------------|-------------|
| **Science & Pipeline** | ~30% | Mechanism of action explanations, Phase II/III trial results, peer-reviewed publication summaries, scientific symposium coverage. Primary audience: oncologists, rheumatologists, cardiologists, research community. (observed on amgen.com/science) |
| **Patient Stories** | ~25% | First-person narratives from patients living with rheumatoid arthritis, multiple myeloma, osteoporosis, and cardiovascular disease. Always paired with clinical context. Primary audience: patients, caregivers, general public. (observed on amgen.com/stories) |
| **Corporate Purpose & Mission** | ~20% | "To serve patients" mission expression, employee stories, community investment, Amgen Foundation updates, CEO communications. (official, amgen.com/about) |
| **Sustainability & ESG** | ~15% | Environmental progress, diversity and inclusion reporting, access and affordability programs. Tied to formal ESG/sustainability report publication cycle. (official, Amgen ESG Report FY2023) |
| **Investor & Financial** | ~10% | Earnings commentary, pipeline milestones, R&D investment rationale. Primary audience: institutional investors and analysts. (official, amgen.com/investors) |

## Hook Patterns

### Hook 1: The Patient Outcome Hook
Opens with a specific patient's measurable outcome before any product mention.
> "Before Enbrel, getting out of bed took Sarah forty-five minutes. Now she coaches her daughter's soccer team."
The product is named in sentence three or later; the patient's life change is the lead. (observed on amgen.com/stories pattern)

### Hook 2: The Mechanism Hook (HCP-Targeted)
Opens with a molecular mechanism question that frames clinical unmet need.
> "What happens when your immune system's LDL-clearing pathway is blocked by excess PCSK9? For millions of high-risk patients, statins alone are not enough."
Used in CME materials, medical journal advertising, and HCP portal content. (T4_INFERRED from observed HCP-targeted pharmaceutical content patterns)

### Hook 3: The Data-First Hook
Opens with a specific trial result or statistic without emotional context.
> "In the FOURIER cardiovascular outcomes trial, Repatha reduced the risk of myocardial infarction by 27%."
Used in detail aid materials, formulary dossiers, and payer-facing content. (T4_INFERRED from pharmaceutical detail aid patterns; FOURIER data: SEC 10-K FY2023 reference; NEJM 2017)

### Hook 4: The Scale Hook
Opens with a statement of patient population size to establish the magnitude of unmet need.
> "More than 1.5 million Americans live with multiple myeloma or its precursor conditions. For many, treatment options have historically been limited."
Used in disease awareness campaigns and corporate advocacy content. (T4_INFERRED; epidemiology figures are estimated from published SEER data)

### Hook 5: The Heritage Hook
Opens with a founding-era reference to establish scientific credibility through longevity.
> "When Amgen's scientists produced the first recombinant human erythropoietin in 1983, it opened a door that still stands: the possibility that biology itself could be the medicine."
Used in corporate anniversary communications and investor day materials. (T4_INFERRED from observed corporate narrative patterns)

> "Request medical information about [Product Name]."
Used on HCP-gated portal pages. Compliance-driven; never uses urgency language. (observed on amgen.com HCP section)

## Content Prohibitions

1. **Unqualified efficacy claims**: Any statement suggesting a product works without citing the specific clinical trial, indication, and patient population. Regulatory requirement and brand standard. (T4_INFERRED from FDA promotional materials guidance)
2. **Off-label promotion**: Content suggesting use of a product outside its FDA-approved label. Strict regulatory prohibition with significant legal exposure. (official, FDA promotional guidelines)
3. **Comparative safety superiority without head-to-head data**: Stating any Amgen product is "safer" than a named competitor without approved head-to-head clinical data. (T4_INFERRED from pharmaceutical promotional standards)
4. **Patient testimonials without IRB/regulatory review**: Patient stories on promotional channels require compliance review to ensure they do not constitute unsubstantiated efficacy claims. (T4_INFERRED from FDA guidance on testimonials in pharma advertising)
5. **Pipeline product promotion**: Investigational compounds may not be promoted to the public or healthcare professionals outside of approved clinical trial communication contexts. (official, FDA IND regulations)
6. **Pricing discussions in clinical content**: Mixing clinical efficacy messaging with pricing discussions in the same content unit undermines both the scientific credibility and the commercial positioning. (T4_INFERRED from observed pharmaceutical content strategy)

## Steal Sheet — 3 Transferable Principles

**1. The dual-track content architecture: expert channel + human channel.**
Amgen runs two parallel content tracks — one for clinical experts (data-dense, peer-to-peer) and one for patients/public (story-led, empathy-first). They never cross-contaminate: clinical jargon does not appear in patient content; patient anecdote does not appear in formulary dossiers. Transferable structure: any B2B2C company (insurtech, legal tech, medtech) benefits from building explicit content tracks for the buyer and the user — separate voices for separate decision-makers.

**2. Anchor patient stories in outcomes data, not sentiment.**
Amgen's most effective patient content pairs an emotional narrative with a verifiable clinical outcome. The story provides the hook; the data provides the credibility. Without data, the story is advertising. Without story, the data is noise. Transferable structure: in any category where the product delivers measurable results, pair every user story with a specific, verifiable metric.

**3. Compliance constraints are a brand voice filter, not a creative obstacle.**
The FDA's requirement for fair balance, clear indication statements, and risk disclosure forces Amgen's content to be unusually precise and factual. This precision — which competitors must also meet — paradoxically creates trust with physicians and sophisticated patients. The regulatory constraint produces a more credible voice than an unconstrained promotional voice would. Transferable principle: industries with mandatory disclosure requirements (finance, legal, insurance, healthcare) can convert those disclosures from fine-print obligations into brand transparency signals.
