FICO has delivered steady revenue growth across the five-year period, accelerating to +9.9% in FY2025. Revenue growth in FY2025 was the strongest since FY2022, reflecting the pricing power of the FICO Score franchise and ongoing Software segment development. (T4_INFERRED based on publicly disclosed segment trends)

### 2.2 Revenue Segment Note

FICO's Scores segment derives revenue primarily from business-to-business sales of credit scores to lenders, mortgage originators, and financial institutions. The Software segment includes platform-based analytics and decision management solutions. Segment-level breakdowns are disclosed in the 10-K MD&A; analysts should consult the full filing for current segment revenue split. (T1_OFFICIAL)

## 8. Brand-Finance Nexus

Based on publicly available financial data and FICO's disclosed business model (T4_INFERRED where noted):

**Score Franchise Pricing Power**: The operating margin expansion from 21.8% (FY2021) to 42.5% (FY2025) on relatively modest revenue growth (+30% over five years) suggests that FICO's Scores segment — which prices based on transaction volume and score type — has benefited from pricing adjustments rather than volume growth alone. This pricing dynamic is the subject of the disclosed antitrust litigation; analysis should note this context.

**Leverage-Funded Buyback Strategy**: FICO's combination of negative shareholders' equity, $2.2B in long-term debt, and $2.6B in three-year buybacks represents a deliberate financial engineering strategy that maximizes per-share value metrics. This strategy is high-risk if revenue growth stalls, as debt service requires sustained cash generation. (T4_INFERRED)

**Software Segment Brand Investment**: R&D investment of ~$160M (10.6% of revenue) in FY2025 indicates continued investment in FICO Platform, the company's cloud-native decisioning platform. The 10-K explicitly names the software platform strategy as both a growth driver and a risk factor, suggesting the market has not yet fully validated the transition. (T4_INFERRED)

**Asset-Light Model**: CapEx below 0.5% of revenue in FY2024–FY2025 confirms an almost entirely software and data-driven business model with minimal physical capital requirements.
