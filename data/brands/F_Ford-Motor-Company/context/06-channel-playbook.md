## 3. Key Channel Deep Dives

### 3.1 Ford Dealer Network — The Revenue Engine

The Ford dealer network is the primary transaction channel for retail vehicle sales. Ford's
relationship with its dealer network is a franchise model governed by dealer agreements, with
approximately 3,000 U.S. Ford franchised dealers as of recent disclosures. (estimated from
industry sources; exact count subject to change)

**Key characteristics**:
- Dealers are independent businesses operating under Ford franchise agreements; Ford cannot
  dictate retail pricing (MSRP guidance is not binding on dealers). (T4_INFERRED from
  U.S. franchise dealer law)
- Ford has explored agency and direct-sale models for EV products (citing Tesla's direct
  model as context), but has faced legal constraints in U.S. states that prohibit direct
  manufacturer sales. (T3_SECONDARY_RELIABLE, automotive press coverage, 2022–2024)
- The dealer network provides the national service coverage that Ford Pro leverages as a
  competitive moat vs. Tesla and Rivian in commercial fleet — service accessibility in
  non-major metropolitan markets. (T4_INFERRED)
- FordPass Rewards is the loyalty mechanism that connects the dealer transaction to ongoing
  Ford-brand digital engagement. (official, FordPass product pages)

### 3.2 YouTube — The Capability Showcase

Ford's YouTube presence functions as a long-form capability demonstration platform where
the brand proves claims rather than asserts them.

**Key content categories observed on Ford's YouTube channels**:
- F-150 and Super Duty capability tests (towing, payload, off-road)
- F-150 Lightning real-world range and Pro Power Onboard demonstrations
- Bronco off-road trail capability documentation
- Mustang and Mustang Mach-E performance content
- Heritage series (Ford history, model anniversary content)
- Ford Pro Intelligence and fleet technology demonstrations

YouTube is particularly important for the truck-buyer purchase journey: research indicates
that truck buyers conduct extensive online research including professional review videos and
owner testimonials before purchase. (T3_SECONDARY_RELIABLE, automotive research behavioral
data from sources including Cox Automotive annual studies)

### 3.3 Ford Newsroom (media.ford.com) — The Official Record

The Ford Newsroom serves as the company's official press communication hub, functioning as
the primary source for journalists, analysts, and researchers seeking verified, attributable
Ford statements.

**Content categories**:
- Vehicle launch press releases (specifications, pricing, availability)
- Executive statements and CEO Jim Farley communications
- Ford Pro announcements (fleet contracts, software launches, charging infrastructure)
- Recall and safety communications (coordinated with NHTSA disclosures)
- Financial results press releases (aligned with SEC filings and earnings calls)
- Partnership and strategic announcements

For this project: Ford Newsroom content is treated as T1_OFFICIAL or T2_PRIMARY_RELIABLE
depending on whether it is a direct company statement or an attributed executive quote.

## 5. Ford Pro — Dedicated B2B Channel Architecture

Ford Pro represents the most structurally distinct channel segment in Ford's portfolio.
Its channel architecture is explicitly separated from consumer channels:

| Element | Ford Pro Approach |
|---------|------------------|
| **Website** | fordpro.com — separate domain with commercial-only content and CTA architecture |
| **Sales team** | Dedicated Ford Pro commercial sales representatives (separate from retail dealer sales) |
| **Service** | Ford Pro Commercial Vehicle Centers — dedicated commercial service lanes at participating dealers; priority service for fleet customers (official, Ford Pro) |
| **Software** | Ford Pro Intelligence — telematics, vehicle health monitoring, fleet management, EV charging management (official, Ford Pro Intelligence product pages) |
| **Charging infrastructure** | Ford Pro EvSE — commercial charging hardware for fleet depot charging; managed charging software (official, Ford Pro) |
| **Government/Fleet RFP support** | Dedicated government and national accounts team for public sector fleet procurement (T4_INFERRED from Ford Pro organizational structure) |

## 6. Channel-Specific Prohibitions

### Ford.com / Consumer Channels
- **No promotional pricing as the primary hero message.** Value proposition leads; pricing
  appears in configurator context, not brand advertising creative. (T4_INFERRED)
- **No competitor disparagement by name without metric backing.** "Best-selling" is permissible
  with sourcing; "better than GM" without a specific metric is not Ford's style. (T4_INFERRED)

### FordPro.com / B2B Channels
- **No consumer brand voice.** "Built Ford Tough" is consumer language; Ford Pro speaks
  in TCO, uptime, and ROI terms. Mixing these voices creates brand confusion for commercial
  buyers. (T4_INFERRED)
- **No unverified fleet case study claims.** Customer testimonials require documented
  attribution and permission. (T4_INFERRED)

### YouTube
- **No production capability claims that exceed EPA/SAE certified figures.** Towing and payload
  demonstrations must be consistent with published specifications. Exaggerated capability
  claims in video create NHTSA and FTC exposure. (T4_INFERRED; T1_OFFICIAL regulatory context)

### IR / SEC Channels
- **No marketing language in financial communications.** "Built Ford Tough" does not appear
  in the 10-K. (T1_OFFICIAL, SEC filing standards)
- **No forward-looking statements without safe harbor disclaimers.** Required by SEC rules.
  (T1_OFFICIAL)
- **No social media used for material non-public information disclosure.** SEC Regulation FD
  requires simultaneous disclosure through approved channels. (T1_OFFICIAL)

### FordPass App
- **No advertising of competitor products or services within the app.** The app is a Ford
  ecosystem retention tool; cross-selling must remain within the Ford portfolio. (T4_INFERRED)

## 7. Crisis Response Protocol

### Scenario 1 — Vehicle Safety Recall

**Response sequence:**
1. Legal and engineering assessment of scope (T+0 to T+48h prior to public disclosure)
2. NHTSA coordination and official recall submission (triggers public disclosure obligation)
3. Ford Newsroom press release simultaneously with NHTSA posting
4. Owner notification letters (U.S. postal + email for registered owners)
5. Dealer network briefing for remedy scheduling
6. Status updates on Ford Newsroom until remedy completion rate targets met
7. SEC 8-K if recall meets materiality thresholds for disclosure

Note: Ford has managed multiple significant recall events (e.g., Takata airbag recall,
various powertrain recalls). Public recall records are accessible via NHTSA's public
database at nhtsa.gov. (T1_OFFICIAL, NHTSA public records)

### Scenario 2 — EV / Lightning Performance Shortfall vs. Advertised Specs

**Response sequence:**
1. Technical investigation to establish factual basis
2. If EPA/SAE spec issue: immediate correction of marketing claims to match certified figures
3. OTA update deployment if software-addressable
4. FordPass app notification to Lightning owners
5. Ford Newsroom statement if media coverage reaches material level
6. Ford Pro outreach if commercial Lightning fleet customers are affected

### Scenario 3 — Segment Financial Performance Criticism (Model e Losses)

**Response sequence:**
1. Respond through IR channel with Q&A context at earnings call (CEO/CFO level)
2. Reference SEC-disclosed segment financials as the authoritative record
3. CEO Jim Farley has publicly contextualized Model e losses as planned investment with
   disclosed milestones for profitability — maintain this framing consistently across
   investor and media communications. (T2_PRIMARY_RELIABLE, Farley earnings call statements)
4. Do not defend or minimize through brand marketing channels; this is an IR/investor
   communications matter.

## 8. Steal Sheet — 3 Transferable Principles

**1. Separate commercial and consumer channel architecture completely — don't stretch one brand.**
Ford Pro and Ford Blue serve buyers with incompatible decision frameworks. Ford Pro buyers
evaluate TCO; Ford Blue buyers evaluate capability and identity. A single website, single
sales team, and single CTA architecture cannot optimally serve both. Transferable structure:
if your product serves both enterprise/commercial and individual consumer buyers, invest in
separated digital properties, dedicated sales teams, and segment-specific content — even if
the underlying product is similar.

**2. The dealer / service network is a channel asset that digital-native competitors cannot
rapidly replicate.**
Approximately 3,000 U.S. Ford dealers represent a service accessibility infrastructure that
Tesla and Rivian have not matched in non-major metropolitan areas. For commercial fleet
buyers whose vehicles need service while on route, this density is a purchase criterion that
digital-first competitors cannot address. Transferable structure: identify your physical or
human infrastructure assets (support team coverage, regional offices, certified partner network)
and quantify their geographic density vs. digital-only competitors. Infrastructure as a channel
advantage is frequently undervalued in brand communications.

**3. Use the earnings call as a brand communication channel — CEO voice matters.**
Jim Farley's statements on Ford Pro's margin profile, Model e's investment thesis, and
Ford Blue's ICE durability have become cited reference points in automotive industry analysis.
The earnings call is among the most-cited communication venues in the automotive sector.
Transferable structure: CEO/founder commentary on earnings calls and investor days reaches
B2B buyers, commercial fleet managers, and enterprise decision-makers who read financial
press. These audiences are also customers or potential customers. Treat investor communications
as a brand channel, not just a compliance exercise.
