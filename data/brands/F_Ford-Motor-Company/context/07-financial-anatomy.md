## 2. Revenue Anatomy

### 2.1 Five-Year Revenue Trend

| Fiscal Year | Total Revenue | YoY Change | Source |
|-------------|---------------|------------|--------|
| FY2020 | $127.1B | -18.4% | (SEC 10-K FY2020) |
| FY2021 | $136.3B | +7.2% | (SEC 10-K FY2021) |
| FY2022 | $158.1B | +16.0% | (SEC 10-K FY2022) |
| FY2023 | $176.2B | +11.5% | (SEC 10-K FY2023) |
| FY2024 | ~$185B | +5.0% (estimated) | (T3_SECONDARY_RELIABLE, Ford Q4 FY2024 earnings release; SEC 10-K FY2024) |

The FY2020 revenue decline (-18.4%) reflects COVID-19 pandemic production shutdowns and
dealer closures. Ford's 10-K for FY2020 describes the significant operational disruption from
the pandemic. FY2022–FY2024 revenue growth has been driven primarily by vehicle pricing power
(average transaction prices increased industry-wide post-2021 supply chain constraints) and
the strong performance of Ford Pro, Ford's highest-margin segment. (SEC 10-K filings; T4_INFERRED
for driver attribution)

Note: FY2024 final figure is based on Ford's earnings release communications and preliminary
reports; verify against the filed FY2024 10-K for the definitive figure.

### 2.2 Segment Revenue Breakdown

Ford's three-segment structure (Ford Blue, Ford Model e, Ford Pro) was introduced in March 2022
at a capital markets day presentation and reflected in Ford's annual reporting from FY2022
onward. This segmentation represents a significant disclosure enhancement that allows external
stakeholders to evaluate each business unit's economics independently. (official, Ford Motor
Company investor presentations; SEC 10-K FY2022 and subsequent)

| Segment | FY2023 Revenue | % of Total | Adj. EBIT | Source |
|---------|---------------|------------|-----------|--------|
| Ford Blue (ICE vehicles) | ~$100B+ | ~57% (estimated) | Positive, margin undisclosed separately | (SEC 10-K FY2023; T4_INFERRED) |
| Ford Pro (commercial) | ~$64B | ~36% (estimated) | Highest margin segment; ~$7B+ EBIT (estimated) | (SEC 10-K FY2023 segment disclosures; T4_INFERRED) |
| Ford Model e (EV) | ~$7B | ~4% (estimated) | -$4.7B EBIT loss | (SEC 10-K FY2023) |
| Ford Credit (financial services) | Disclosed separately | — | Positive contribution | (SEC 10-K FY2023) |

**Key observations**:

- Ford Pro's revenue of approximately $64B in FY2023 represents a commercial fleet business
  that, if standalone, would rank among the largest commercial vehicle businesses globally.
  Its EBIT margin significantly exceeds the Ford consolidated average. (T4_INFERRED from
  segment EBIT disclosures in SEC 10-K FY2023)
- Ford Model e's loss of approximately $4.7B in FY2023 EBIT (SEC 10-K FY2023) represents
  Ford's stated investment in EV transition. CEO Jim Farley has publicly stated that Model e
  profitability is a milestone-gated target. (T2_PRIMARY_RELIABLE, Farley earnings statements)
- Ford Blue (ICE vehicles, including F-Series, Explorer, Mustang, Bronco) generates the bulk
  of Ford's vehicle revenue and remains the cash generation engine that funds Model e
  investment. (T4_INFERRED from segment structure)

| Segment | Adjusted EBIT | Margin (est.) | Source |
|---------|--------------|---------------|--------|

The segment margin divergence is the defining financial characteristic of Ford's current
business structure. Ford Pro's ~11% Adjusted EBIT margin (estimated) significantly exceeds the

(T4_INFERRED based on segment disclosures and industry benchmarking)

This divergence has major brand-strategy implications: Ford Pro's margin profile suggests
(T4_INFERRED) that commercial fleet buyers are less price-sensitive than retail consumers,
that Ford's fleet relationships carry meaningful switching costs, and that software/service
revenue from Ford Pro Intelligence may be contributing incremental high-margin revenue.

## 5. Capital Allocation

### 5.1 Shareholder Returns

Ford restored its regular quarterly dividend in 2021 after suspending it in 2020 during the
COVID-19 pandemic. Ford has also issued supplemental dividends tied to Ford Pro's strong
performance.

| Mechanism | FY2023 | Source |
|-----------|--------|--------|
| Regular Dividends | $0.15 per share quarterly (annualized $0.60/share) | (official, Ford dividend announcements; T1_OFFICIAL) |
| Supplemental Dividend | $0.18 per share special dividend declared February 2024 for FY2023 performance | (official, Ford 8-K dividend announcement) |
| Share Repurchases | Ford initiated a buyback program; scale disclosed in SEC filings | (SEC 10-K FY2023 cash flow statement) |
| Ford Credit dividends to parent | Ford Motor Credit Company contributes cash to the parent company | (SEC 10-K FY2023 consolidated cash flow) |

The issuance of supplemental dividends tied to Ford Pro's strong performance represents an
explicit brand-strategy signal: Ford Pro's commercial success is being returned to shareholders
in a distinct, identifiable capital allocation mechanism. (T4_INFERRED from capital allocation
pattern analysis)

### 5.2 Ford Motor Credit Company

Ford Motor Credit Company (FMCC) is a wholly owned subsidiary providing vehicle financing,
leasing, and dealer floor plan financing. FMCC is a material component of Ford's financial
profile and is separately capitalized with its own debt ratings.

| Item | Description | Source |
|------|-------------|--------|
| Role | Consumer auto financing; dealer inventory financing; fleet financing | (SEC 10-K FY2023, Ford Credit segment) |
| Assets | ~$140B+ (estimated, finance receivables + operating leases) | (SEC 10-K FY2023 Ford Credit balance sheet) |
| Revenue contribution | Disclosed separately in Ford's segment reporting | (SEC 10-K FY2023) |
| Brand role | Ford Credit financing is the primary mechanism by which most retail Ford buyers finance their purchase | (T4_INFERRED) |

FMCC's captive financing capability allows Ford to offer competitive financing rates as a
sales incentive and provides a recurring revenue stream tied to the vehicle installed base.
(T4_INFERRED from captive finance business model analysis)

### 5.3 EV Joint Ventures — BlueOval SK

Ford has committed substantial capital to battery manufacturing joint ventures with SK On
(South Korean battery manufacturer):

- **BlueOval City** (Stanton, Tennessee): EV truck and battery manufacturing facility;
  capital commitment disclosed as part of Ford's broader $50B+ EV investment program
  (2022 announcement) (official, Ford press releases)
- **BlueOval SK Battery Park** (Glendale, Kentucky): Two battery plants
- Total Ford EV investment commitment: announced at $50B+ through 2026 at the time of the
  March 2022 capital markets day; subsequently moderated as Ford adjusted EV scaling timelines
  (T2_PRIMARY_RELIABLE, Jim Farley investor communications; T3_SECONDARY_RELIABLE, press
  coverage of 2024 investment pace adjustments)

## 8. Financial Risk Matrix

The following risks are disclosed in Ford's FY2023 10-K, Part I, Item 1A (Risk Factors).

| # | Risk Category | Disclosed Risk Summary | Severity (estimated) |
|---|---------------|------------------------|----------------------|
| 1 | **EV Transition Execution** | Ford has committed substantial capital to EV programs. If EV demand growth is slower than planned, capital invested in EV manufacturing may not generate expected returns. The 10-K specifically addresses EV execution risk. | High |
| 2 | **Supply Chain Concentration** | Semiconductor shortages (which severely affected Ford in 2021–2022) and battery material supply chains represent ongoing risks disclosed in the 10-K. Ford's reliance on certain single-source or limited-source suppliers is identified. | High |
| 3 | **Quality and Warranty Costs** | Ford discloses significant warranty and recall-related charges in its financial statements. Quality-related costs are a material line item that can affect profitability significantly in any given period. | High |
| 4 | **UAW Labor Relations** | Ford's workforce includes UAW-represented employees. The 2023 UAW strike resulted in a new four-year contract with materially higher labor costs, as disclosed in Ford's post-strike communications and 10-K. (T2_PRIMARY_RELIABLE, Ford post-strike announcements) | High |
| 5 | **Macroeconomic / Interest Rate** | Vehicle purchase is interest-rate sensitive. Rising rates increase monthly payments and reduce demand for new vehicles. Ford Credit's profitability is also affected by rate environment. | Medium-High |
| 6 | **Geographic Concentration (U.S.)** | Ford's revenue is approximately 74% U.S.-sourced (estimated). A U.S. economic downturn has outsized impact relative to more globally diversified OEMs. | Medium-High |
| 7 | **China Market Exposure** | Ford's China operations have faced competitive pressure from domestic EV manufacturers. The 10-K identifies China as a market with heightened competitive and regulatory risk. | Medium |
| 8 | **Ford Credit Counterparty Risk** | Ford Motor Credit Company's portfolio of consumer and dealer financing is exposed to default risk in economic downturns. The 2008–2009 financial crisis significantly affected auto finance portfolios industry-wide. | Medium |

## 9. Brand-Finance Nexus

### 9.1 Five Brand-Finance Signals

**Signal 1 — F-Series Pricing Power**
Average transaction prices for F-Series trucks have risen materially over the 2020–2024
period, reflecting both industry-wide pricing power post-supply constraint and the brand's
ability to sustain price increases in its most loyal customer segment. Premium trim levels
(F-150 Lariat, King Ranch, Platinum, Limited) command prices in the $60,000–$90,000 range
and have grown as a share of F-Series mix. (T3_SECONDARY_RELIABLE, Edmunds transaction price
data; T4_INFERRED for mix shift observation)

**Signal 2 — Ford Pro Margin Premium Reflects B2B Switching Costs**
Ford Pro's superior margin vs. Ford Blue suggests (T4_INFERRED) that commercial fleet
customers assign a premium to Ford's service network density, Ford Pro Intelligence software
integration, and demonstrated uptime reliability — switching costs that brand loyalty has
accumulated over fleet relationships measured in years and decades.

**Signal 3 — Supplemental Dividend as Brand Proof Point**
Ford's decision to issue supplemental dividends tied explicitly to Ford Pro's performance
communicates to investors that the commercial segment's margin profile is sustainable and
distinct from the EV investment cycle. The dividend mechanism converts segment brand strength
into a directly observable shareholder return signal. (T4_INFERRED from capital allocation
pattern analysis)

**Signal 4 — Model e Losses as Strategic Investment, Not Brand Deterioration**
The existence of ring-fenced Model e losses in Ford's segment reporting prevents EV investment
costs from obscuring Ford Blue's and Ford Pro's underlying profitability. Brand equity in
Ford Blue (the heritage truck franchise) is not financially contaminated by EV transition costs
because the segment structure creates transparency. (T4_INFERRED)

**Signal 5 — Heritage Durability in Warranty Reserve**
Ford's warranty and recall provisions (a material line item in Ford's financials) reflect the
long service lives of its vehicles — particularly work trucks that may remain in service for
200,000+ miles. The long vehicle life span is simultaneously a quality indicator and a
per-vehicle warranty cost driver. Brands whose customers keep vehicles for 10+ years are brands
whose products are trusted; the warranty provision is the financial expression of that trust.
(T4_INFERRED from warranty cost analysis)

## Steal Sheet — 3 Transferable Principles

**1. Ring-fence your loss-making growth investment from your profitable core — then disclose both.**
Ford's three-segment P&L structure allows investors to evaluate Ford Pro's ~11% Adj. EBIT margin
independently from Model e's -$4.7B loss. Without this transparency, the EV losses would obscure
the core business's health. Transferable structure: if you are simultaneously running a profitable
core business and funding a loss-making growth initiative, create segment-level financial reporting
that lets stakeholders evaluate each independently. Opacity about investment-phase losses erodes
confidence; transparent disclosure with articulated milestones builds it.

**2. Use a captive finance subsidiary to lower your customer's effective price without discounting
your brand.**
Ford Motor Credit Company allows Ford to offer competitive financing rates as a sales incentive —
effectively lowering the customer's monthly cost without reducing the vehicle's sticker price or
perceived value. The MSRP is preserved; the financing makes it accessible. Transferable structure:
in high-ticket categories with long purchase decision cycles (B2B SaaS multi-year contracts,
enterprise hardware, capital equipment), consider whether a financing, leasing, or payment
structure can lower the buyer's effective cost of entry without requiring you to reduce list
pricing.

**3. Issue supplemental dividends tied to a specific segment's performance to make that segment's
success tangible to investors.**
Ford's supplemental dividend explicitly tied to Ford Pro's FY2023 performance converted an
accounting disclosure into a shareholder return event. The dividend made Ford Pro's value
concrete and shareholder-relevant in a way that a segment revenue line in a 10-K cannot.
Transferable structure: find mechanisms to make your highest-performing business unit's success
directly tangible to stakeholders — whether through bonuses tied to specific product lines,
customer-visible pricing structures, or investor return mechanisms linked to specific
business unit performance.
