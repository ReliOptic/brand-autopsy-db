## Major Litigation & Regulatory History

### 2008 Financial Crisis / Countrywide Acquisition (2012–2014)
- **Nature**: DOJ and state AG investigations into mortgage origination and securitization practices of Countrywide Financial (acquired by BofA in 2008)
- **Outcome**: BofA reached a $16.65 billion settlement with the DOJ in August 2014 — one of the largest civil settlements in U.S. corporate history at the time (T1_OFFICIAL, DOJ press release August 21, 2014)
- **Project writing note**: This settlement is a matter of public record. Always attribute to DOJ announcement. Do not characterize the underlying conduct beyond what DOJ settlement documents state. Countrywide was a separate company acquired by BofA; distinguish clearly.

### Merrill Lynch Acquisition Disclosures (SEC Action, 2010)
- **Nature**: SEC alleged BofA failed to disclose Merrill Lynch's bonus payments and losses to shareholders before the merger vote
- **Outcome**: BofA paid $150 million to resolve SEC charges without admitting or denying findings (T1_OFFICIAL, SEC Litigation Release, February 4, 2010)
- **Project writing note**: Use SEC release language. Do not characterize as fraud without quoting the specific regulatory finding.

### CFPB Actions (various)
- **2014**: CFPB ordered BofA to pay $727 million for illegal credit card practices (T1_OFFICIAL, CFPB press release April 9, 2014)
- **2023**: CFPB ordered BofA to pay $250 million for allegedly opening unauthorized accounts, withholding credit card rewards, and double-charging fees (T1_OFFICIAL, CFPB press release July 11, 2023)
- **Project writing note**: Quote CFPB press release language specifically. Do not characterize conduct beyond what CFPB public orders state.

### Overdraft Fee Litigation (ongoing class actions)
- Multiple class action lawsuits regarding overdraft fee practices; various states (T3_SECONDARY_RELIABLE)
- BofA eliminated NSF fees and reduced overdraft fees in 2022 (official, BofA press release January 11, 2022)

## PROJECT RISK ASSESSMENT

### Litigation Posture
BofA has extensive litigation history and a large, sophisticated legal function. The combination of the Countrywide legacy, multiple CFPB actions, and ongoing class actions makes BofA one of the higher-risk subjects for factual characterization. **Writing risk: HIGH**. Every regulatory claim must be sourced to the specific agency action.

### Data Collection Legality
Project uses only public filings and communications. No customer data collected. **Risk: LOW**.

### Fair Use Assessment
Educational analysis of BofA's brand, business model, and public regulatory history is fair use. **Status: FAVORABLE** with rigorous source attribution.

## Writing Risk Guide

### SAFE — Use Freely
- "Bank of America reported net income of approximately $26.5 billion in FY2023. (SEC 10-K FY2023)"
- "The DOJ announced a $16.65 billion settlement with Bank of America in August 2014. (T1_OFFICIAL, DOJ press release)"
- "The CFPB ordered Bank of America to pay $250 million in July 2023. (T1_OFFICIAL, CFPB press release)"
- "Bank of America's Preferred Rewards program offers interest rate boosts and fee waivers. (official, bankofamerica.com)"
- "BofA announced it would eliminate NSF fees in January 2022. (official, BofA press release)"

### HEDGE — Use with Citation
- Claims about BofA's current credit quality or loan performance: cite specific filing quarter and date
- Claims about Countrywide conduct: clearly attribute to Countrywide pre-acquisition; note BofA inherited liability
- Comparisons to peer banks: use specific metrics and cite sources
- Forward-looking financial projections: label as estimates; cite analyst consensus if used

### DO NOT WRITE
- Do not characterize BofA's mortgage securitization practices as fraudulent beyond what DOJ settlement documents specifically state — the settlement involved civil charges without admission of wrongdoing
- Do not attribute the Countrywide conduct to BofA employees — Countrywide was a separate company pre-acquisition
- Do not state that specific BofA executives committed crimes without citation to criminal conviction records
- Do not use "systematic fraud," "criminal enterprise," or similar characterizations without citation to criminal conviction (none exists for BofA as an institution)
- Do not republish customer account data or reference specific customer financial situations
