# 05 — Design System
## Bristol Myers Squibb (BMY)
**Sector:** Health Care — Pharmaceuticals
**Key Products:** Opdivo (nivolumab), Eliquis (apixaban)

---

## 1. Design Philosophy

BMY's visual identity operates across three distinct but coherent design registers:

1. **Corporate Masterbrand:** Institutional authority, trustworthiness, scientific gravitas
2. **Opdivo Brand:** Strength, momentum, warmth — the "prevail" visual language
3. **Eliquis Brand:** Clarity, reassurance, life continuity — protection without fear

All three registers share a commitment to **clarity over decoration** — design serves communication, not aesthetics for its own sake. In a regulated environment where every visual element may be reviewed by legal and regulatory teams, visual restraint is not a limitation — it is a strategic asset.

## 3. Typography System

### Corporate Masterbrand Typography

| Role | Typeface | Weight | Style | Usage |
|------|---------|--------|-------|-------|
| Display Headline | Georgia (or custom serif) | Bold | Italic available | Homepage heroes, annual report headers (estimated) |
| Primary Headline | Helvetica Neue / Arial | Bold (700) | Upright | Section headers, product names (estimated) |
| Body Copy | Helvetica Neue / Arial | Regular (400) | Upright | All running text, patient materials (estimated) |
| Data / Tables | Roboto Mono / Courier | Regular | Monospace | Clinical data tables, numeric data (estimated) |
| Legal / Fair Balance | Arial | Regular (400) | Small caps optional | Required safety language, footnotes (estimated) |

### Opdivo Typography Notes
- Display type tends toward bold serif for authority and emotional weight
- Patient testimonials often set in a slightly warmer, humanist sans-serif
- "PREVAIL" typography treatment: all-caps, tracked, dominant — a visual declaration

### Eliquis Typography Notes
- Clean sans-serif dominant — readability and approachability prioritized
- Clinical data sections use tabular numerals for alignment precision
- Spanish-language materials maintain same typeface; line spacing increased for readability

## 4. Imagery & Photography Style

### Corporate Photography Principles
- **Real people only** — stock photography with obvious staging is explicitly avoided in guidelines
- **Diversity mandate:** Every major campaign and HCP collateral must reflect racial, gender, age, and geographic diversity
- **Scientist portraiture:** Modern, well-lit labs; scientists engaged in genuine work (not posed pipette shots)
- **Patient photography:** Natural light, authentic environments (home, outdoors, community settings)
- **Emotion range:** Full spectrum — not only "smiling survivor" — includes contemplative, determined, hopeful

### Opdivo Photography Language
- **Landscapes:** Wide-angle natural environments (mountains, coastlines, forests) — scale suggests possibility
- **Motion:** Subjects in movement — walking, running, gardening — reinforcing the "prevailing" concept
- **Color grading:** Warm, golden hour tones; slight desaturation on clinical/hospital settings to maintain authenticity
- **Patient diversity:** EXPLICIT requirement — Opdivo's survivor campaigns have faced scrutiny for underrepresenting Black and Hispanic patients; corrective direction now mandates diverse representation in hero imagery

### Eliquis Photography Language
- **Lifestyle continuity:** Subjects doing what they love — dancing, traveling, playing with grandchildren
- **AFib patient profile:** Primarily 55–75 age range; active, engaged in life
- **Color grading:** Clean, bright, optimistic — healthcare anxiety deliberately avoided
- **Family inclusion:** Caregiver and family member presence in lifestyle imagery reinforces "protection" narrative

## 5. Iconography & Illustration

### Scientific Iconography
- Molecular structures and DNA helixes: used sparingly as scientific credibility signals
- Cell mechanism illustrations: PD-1/PD-L1 pathway diagrams are core Opdivo visual assets
- Heart/circulatory system icons: Eliquis uses clean, schematic cardiovascular iconography
- Style: flat vector, not 3D renders — accessibility and print reproduction quality

### Data Visualization Principles
- Kaplan-Meier survival curves: standardized presentation format; BMY navy vs. competitor gray
- Bar charts: always labeled with exact values; no chart junk
- Forest plots: clean white background; confident intervals clearly marked
- Infographics: 3-step maximum for patient-facing; unlimited depth for HCP materials

## 6. Layout & Grid System

### HCP Materials (Print + Digital)
- **Grid:** 12-column; 3 or 4 column content layout standard
- **Hierarchy:** Headline > Subhead > Body > Footnote/Fair Balance (strict size differentiation)
- **Fair Balance Rule:** Safety information must occupy minimum 1/3 page real estate on DTC print; equal time on broadcast
- **White space:** Generous — crowded layouts signal low trust in regulated pharma design
- **Data tables:** Full-bleed width; alternating row shading for readability

### Patient / DTC Materials
- **Grid:** Simplified 2-column or single-column; mobile-first for digital
- **Reading level:** Design supports 6th-grade reading level target — short paragraphs, bullet points
- **CTA placement:** Above the fold always; "Ask your doctor about [Brand]" or "Visit [URL]"
- **Accessibility:** WCAG 2.1 AA minimum; large type options for elderly patient materials

---

## 7. Motion & Animation Guidelines

### Mechanism of Action Animations
- Duration: 60–90 seconds standard; 30-second cut for digital ads
- Style: Clean 3D cell biology animation — not cartoonish; photorealistic lighting
- Narration: Medical narrator voice (authoritative but not robotic); patient narration for DTC cuts
- Music: Understated orchestral or ambient — never triumphant fanfare in HCP versions

### DTC Broadcast Motion
- Opdivo: Cinematic quality; natural light; golden hour color grading; music builds to hopeful resolution
- Eliquis: Lifestyle B-roll; warm color palette; music upbeat but not aggressive; ends on connection (family moment)
- Pacing: Slower than FMCG advertising — serious disease audiences need processing time

## 8. Digital Design System

### Web & Portal Design
- **HCP Portal (bms.com/healthcare-providers):** Dark navy header, white body, teal accents; data-table heavy
- **Patient Site (bms.com):** Lighter palette, larger type, lifestyle imagery dominant
- **Opdivo.com:** Teal primary; patient story carousel; "Find a doctor" CTA prominent
- **Eliquis.com:** Blue primary; AFib risk calculator tool; copay assistance calculator embedded

### Component Standards (estimated based on pharma DX patterns)
- Button: Primary = filled brand color; Secondary = outline; Disabled = gray
- Cards: White background, 4px border-radius, subtle drop shadow (0 2px 8px rgba(0,0,0,0.12))
- Forms: Full-width inputs; inline validation; error states in red with icon
- Navigation: Sticky header; hamburger on mobile; mega-menu for product section depth

## 10. Steal Sheets — Design System

### Steal Sheet 1: Regulated-Environment White Space as Trust Signal
**What to steal:** BMY's deliberate use of generous white space in HCP materials is not a design luxury — it is a trust mechanism. In pharmaceutical design, crowded layouts signal either information overload or an attempt to hide something. Brands in regulated industries should use white space as a primary credibility tool, not sacrifice it for more content.

### Steal Sheet 2: Compound Color Identity (Corporate + Product)
**What to steal:** BMY maintains a coherent corporate navy identity while allowing product brands (Opdivo teal, Eliquis blue) to develop distinct personalities. This compound color architecture lets each product build its own recognizable identity while remaining legible as a BMY product. Applicable to any multi-product company that needs brand architecture without visual chaos.

### Steal Sheet 3: Survival Curve as Brand Asset
**What to steal:** Kaplan-Meier survival curves — standard clinical data charts — have become a visual brand signature for Opdivo in HCP contexts. When your data is good, make the data visual the centerpiece of your design system. The survival curve that curves upward IS the brand promise, made visible. Turn your best clinical chart into a design element.
