## Ideal Customer Profile (ICP)

| Dimension | Profile |
|-----------|---------|
| **Core Demographics** | Ages 35–65, household income $200K+ (estimated), urban or suburban residence, college-educated or higher, skewing slightly male in gaming segments but increasingly balanced in resort/dining segments |
| **Occupation segments** | C-suite executives, business owners, high-net-worth retirees, finance and legal professionals, entertainment industry professionals, luxury travel enthusiasts, high-stakes gaming patrons |
| **Platform behavior** | Wynn Rewards members engaging through wynnresorts.com direct booking; mobile app for reservations and gaming activity tracking; high-value guests managed through dedicated VIP host relationships rather than digital self-service (T4_INFERRED from observed resort operations) |
| **Psychographic profile** | Values aesthetic quality over quantity of amenities; treats travel as self-expression rather than utility; defines luxury by sensory detail (thread count, plating presentation, lobby scent) rather than brand logos; seeks environments that reflect their self-image as discerning and successful |
| **Purchase triggers** | Special occasion travel (anniversaries, milestone birthdays), business travel to Las Vegas with upgrade intent, Macau gaming trips for high-net-worth Asian clientele, loyalty program tier status pursuit, word-of-mouth from peers in similar income brackets (T4_INFERRED) |
| **Price elasticity** | Low — Wynn's core customer segment selects on experience quality, not rate comparison. Room rates of $300–$1,500+ per night for standard to suite categories do not trigger price-shopping behavior in the target segment (estimated, based on publicly listed rates on wynnresorts.com) |
| **Geography** | Las Vegas properties: primarily domestic US (California, Texas, New York, Florida feeder markets), with international leisure travelers; Macau properties: mainland China (Guangdong province as primary feeder), Hong Kong, Southeast Asia (SEC 10-K FY2023, geographic revenue disclosure) |

## Core Personas

### Persona 1 — Richard, High-Net-Worth Gaming Patron

- **Age / Occupation**: 52, managing partner at a private equity firm, based in Los Angeles
- **Travel pattern**: Visits Wynn Las Vegas 6–10 times per year for weekend stays; has a dedicated casino host
- **Average trip spend**: $15,000–$50,000+ across gaming, dining, and suite accommodation (estimated for high-value patron segment)
- **Core pain**: Demands recognition and seamless service — does not want to wait in any line, explain preferences to new staff, or be treated as anonymous. The casino host relationship is the single most important service element; losing this relationship to a competitor would trigger switching.
- **Wynn selection rationale**: The quality of the gaming floor environment (lower noise, higher aesthetic standard than competitors), the dedicated VIP salon experience, and the caliber of dining options that provide non-gaming entertainment during the trip. (T4_INFERRED from luxury gaming patron behavioral patterns)
- **Brand relationship**: Does not engage with Wynn on social media; relationship is entirely through the casino host, direct mail invitations, and Wynn Rewards tier benefits. This persona's loyalty is to the host and the property experience, not the brand's digital presence.
- **Churn risk**: If a dedicated casino host leaves Wynn for MGM or Sands and brings the relationship with them. Casino host mobility is the single highest churn vector in this segment. (T4_INFERRED)
- **Media touchpoints**: Private invitations, direct mail, casino host phone calls, peer recommendations within high-net-worth social circles

### Persona 2 — Jennifer and Mark, Luxury Leisure Couple

- **Ages / Occupation**: 44 and 46, dual-income professionals (she is a dermatologist, he is a tech VP), based in San Francisco
- **Travel pattern**: 1–2 Las Vegas trips per year as part of a broader luxury travel portfolio that includes Four Seasons, Aman, and Ritz-Carlton properties globally
- **Average trip spend**: $3,000–$8,000 across suite accommodation, spa, fine dining, and entertainment (estimated)
- **Core pain**: Las Vegas is associated with mass-market tourism, which conflicts with their self-image as discerning travelers. They need a Las Vegas property that feels like it exists above the Strip — a resort that could be in any luxury market worldwide but happens to be in Nevada.
- **Wynn selection rationale**: The property's design quality, the fine dining restaurants, the spa and pool environment, and the perception that Wynn is the refined option on the Strip. This couple does not gamble significantly; the casino floor is background, not destination. (T4_INFERRED)
- **Brand relationship**: Follows @wynnlasvegas on Instagram; books through wynnresorts.com directly or via luxury travel advisor; has enrolled in Wynn Rewards but uses it primarily for dining and spa benefits rather than gaming tier status.
- **Churn risk**: If the Bellagio (MGM) or a new luxury non-gaming resort opens on the Strip that delivers comparable or higher aesthetic quality. Also at risk if Wynn's public brand perception is damaged by negative press coverage. (T4_INFERRED)
- **Media touchpoints**: Instagram, luxury travel publications (Conde Nast Traveler, Travel + Leisure), travel advisor recommendations, peer word-of-mouth

### Persona 3 — David, MICE / Convention Organizer

- **Age / Occupation**: 38, senior events manager at a Fortune 500 technology company, based in Austin
- **Travel pattern**: Books Wynn for 2–4 corporate events per year ranging from executive retreats (50 guests) to product launch receptions (300+ guests)
- **Average event spend**: $50,000–$500,000+ per event across room blocks, F&B, AV, and meeting space (estimated for corporate event segment)
- **Core pain**: Needs a venue that impresses senior leadership and clients while providing reliable AV infrastructure, flexible meeting space, and seamless event logistics. The venue is a brand statement for his company — choosing a generic convention hotel signals budget thinking.
- **Wynn selection rationale**: The Wynn Las Vegas meeting and convention space (approximately 200,000 sq ft, official wynnresorts.com) combined with the resort environment creates a venue that serves both business function and executive entertainment. Post-meeting transitions from conference rooms to fine dining to entertainment are seamless within a single property. (official, wynnresorts.com event facilities)
- **Brand relationship**: Engages through the Wynn group sales team; evaluates based on event execution quality and post-event feedback from attendees. Brand loyalty is tied to the group sales team's responsiveness and execution reliability.
- **Churn risk**: If Wynn's convention facilities fall behind competitors in AV technology, or if room block pricing becomes uncompetitive with Bellagio or Venetian for equivalent quality. (T4_INFERRED)
- **Media touchpoints**: Event industry publications, site visit evaluations, RFP processes, LinkedIn professional networks

### Persona 4 — Wei, Macau Premium Mass Gaming Patron

- **Age / Occupation**: 48, manufacturing company owner based in Guangdong Province, China
- **Travel pattern**: Visits Macau 8–15 times per year; alternates between Wynn Macau, Wynn Palace, and competitor properties based on promotional offers and host relationships
- **Average trip spend**: HKD 200,000–1,000,000+ per trip (estimated, approximately $25,000–$130,000 USD) across gaming, dining, and accommodation
- **Core pain**: Expects VIP treatment commensurate with gaming volume — private gaming salons, complimentary suite upgrades, dedicated Mandarin-speaking hosts, and dining reservations at peak times without wait. The competitive intensity in Macau means this patron receives competing offers from Sands China, Galaxy Entertainment, Melco Resorts, and MGM China simultaneously.
- **Wynn selection rationale**: Wynn Palace's property quality and the perceived prestige of the Wynn brand in the Macau market. The floral installations, the Performance Lake, and the SkyCab gondola experience at Wynn Palace create a resort environment that this patron associates with face and status — important social currency in business entertaining contexts. (T4_INFERRED)
- **Brand relationship**: Managed entirely through VIP host relationships. Digital engagement through WeChat is the primary communication channel. (T4_INFERRED)
- **Churn risk**: Highest of all personas. Macau gaming patrons exhibit lower brand loyalty than Las Vegas patrons due to the geographic concentration of competing properties (all major competitors within a 15-minute drive). Host mobility and promotional offer competition are constant switching pressures. (T4_INFERRED)
- **Media touchpoints**: WeChat, VIP host direct communication, peer networks within business communities, property reputation within Guangdong business circles

| **Budget traveler** | Searches Las Vegas on price comparison sites; maximum nightly rate tolerance under $150 | Wynn's room rates and F&B pricing are structurally incompatible with budget-conscious decision-making. No discount positioning exists. |
| **Bachelor/bachelorette party group** | Priority is nightclub access, pool party atmosphere, and group entertainment | Wynn's environment prioritizes aesthetic refinement over high-energy group entertainment. The brand actively avoids association with party tourism. (T4_INFERRED) |
| **Convention-only attendee** | Attends a conference at Wynn but stays at a cheaper hotel off-Strip; evaluates Wynn only as a meeting venue | The brand's value proposition requires immersion in the resort experience. ## Steal Sheet — 3 Transferable Principles

**1. Build the relationship layer that digital cannot replicate.**
Wynn's highest-value customer relationships are managed by human casino hosts, not by CRM automation. The host remembers the patron's preferred suite, dining preferences, and gaming patterns — and acts on that knowledge in real time. Transferable structure: for any business with a high-LTV customer segment, assign dedicated human relationship managers to the top 5–10% of customers. CRM tools support the relationship; they do not replace it. The human touch at scale is expensive, but the retention economics in high-value segments justify the cost because acquisition of replacement customers at the same value tier is significantly more expensive.

**2. Let the physical space filter the audience.**
Wynn's lobby, common areas, and restaurant environments are designed to communicate a quality standard without ever stating it explicitly. The physical space self-selects the target audience. Transferable structure: design your retail environment, office reception, or digital interface to reflect the aesthetic standards of your target customer. The customer who feels at home is the right customer. The customer who feels out of place will self-select out — which is the intended outcome.

**3. Segment by relationship depth, not by demographic.**
Wynn's most valuable distinction is not between age groups or income brackets but between transactional visitors (one-time, price-compared booking) and relational patrons (repeat visitors with host relationships). The relational patron at $200K annual gaming value is worth more than 1,000 transactional visitors. Transferable structure: build your segmentation around relationship depth indicators (visit frequency, direct booking rate, response to personalized outreach) rather than demographic proxies. Behavioral loyalty is a stronger predictor of lifetime value than income bracket.
