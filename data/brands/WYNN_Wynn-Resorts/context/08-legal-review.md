## 3. Major Litigation

> All descriptions below are factual summaries of publicly disclosed legal proceedings.

### 3.1 Steve Wynn Sexual Misconduct Allegations & Departure (2018)

**Timeline**: In January 2018, the Wall Street Journal published allegations of sexual misconduct by Steve Wynn, the company's founder, chairman, and CEO. (T3_SECONDARY_RELIABLE, WSJ, January 26, 2018)

**Outcome**: Steve Wynn resigned as chairman and CEO on February 6, 2018. He subsequently sold his entire equity stake in the company (approximately 12% of outstanding shares). (T1_OFFICIAL, 8-K filing, February 2018; T3_SECONDARY_RELIABLE)

**Regulatory Consequences**:
- The Massachusetts Gaming Commission conducted an investigation and in April 2019 issued a report finding that Wynn Resorts had failed to investigate allegations known to company executives. The MGC imposed a $35.5 million fine on the company — the largest in Massachusetts gaming regulatory history at that time. (T1_OFFICIAL, MGC press release, April 2019)
- The Nevada Gaming Control Board conducted a parallel investigation. In February 2019, the NGCB issued a $20 million fine to Wynn Resorts for failing to investigate allegations against Steve Wynn. (T1_OFFICIAL, NGCB proceedings)
- Steve Wynn was found "unsuitable" by the Nevada Gaming Commission in October 2019. (T1_OFFICIAL, NGC proceedings)
- The Macau government conducted its own assessment but took no separate public enforcement action against the company entity. (T3_SECONDARY_RELIABLE)

**Impact on Brand**: The company undertook a comprehensive corporate governance overhaul, including new board members, an enhanced compliance program, and the appointment of Craig Billings as CEO (effective January 2022, succeeding Matt Maddox who led the post-Wynn transition). (T1_OFFICIAL, SEC filings)

### 3.2 Macau Gaming Concession Renewal (2022–2023)

**Context**: Wynn Macau Limited's original gaming concession expired in June 2022. The Macau SAR government conducted a public tender process for new 10-year concessions under revised gaming law (Law No. 7/2022).

**Outcome**: Wynn Macau was awarded a new 10-year gaming concession effective January 1, 2023, as one of six concessionaires (alongside SJM Holdings, Galaxy Entertainment, Sands China, MGM China, and Melco Resorts). (T1_OFFICIAL, Macau SAR Government press releases, 2022)

**Conditions**: The new concession includes requirements for minimum investment commitments, non-gaming development, support for local SMEs, and social responsibility programs. The Macau government retains the right to terminate concessions for material breach. (T1_OFFICIAL, Macau gaming law; SEC 10-K FY2023 risk factors)

**Financial Commitment**: Wynn Macau committed to investing approximately MOP $12.8B (~$1.6B USD) over the 10-year concession period in non-gaming and other development projects. (T1_OFFICIAL, disclosed in concession tender documents; T3_SECONDARY_RELIABLE)

### 3.3 Derivative Litigation Related to Steve Wynn Allegations

Multiple shareholder derivative lawsuits were filed following the 2018 misconduct allegations, alleging the board failed in its oversight responsibilities. Several cases were consolidated. Wynn Resorts disclosed these proceedings in its 10-K filings. Settlement of the primary derivative action was reached, with terms disclosed in SEC filings. (T1_OFFICIAL, SEC 10-K disclosures)

### 3.4 Other Proceedings

- Wynn's 10-K discloses various routine litigation matters, including personal injury claims, employment disputes, and contract disputes incidental to the company's operations. (T1_OFFICIAL, SEC 10-K FY2023, Item 3)
- The company has disclosed legal proceedings in Macau relating to junket operator debts and gaming patron disputes. (T1_OFFICIAL, SEC 10-K FY2023)

## 4. Regulatory Landscape

### 4.1 Gaming Regulatory Framework

Gaming is among the most heavily regulated industries globally. Wynn operates under overlapping regulatory regimes:

| Jurisdiction | Regulator | License Type | Status |
|-------------|-----------|-------------|--------|
| **Nevada** | Nevada Gaming Commission / Nevada Gaming Control Board | Nonrestricted gaming license | Active; ongoing suitability requirements (T1_OFFICIAL) |
| **Massachusetts** | Massachusetts Gaming Commission | Category 1 resort casino license | Active; Encore Boston Harbor (T1_OFFICIAL) |
| **Macau SAR** | DICJ (Gaming Inspection and Coordination Bureau) | Gaming concession (10-year, effective Jan 2023) | Active; one of six concessionaires (T1_OFFICIAL) |
| **UAE (Ras Al Khaimah)** | RAK Department of Entertainment & Gaming Regulation | Integrated resort gaming license (new regime) | Awarded; Wynn Al Marjan Island under development (T3_SECONDARY_RELIABLE) |

### 4.2 Key Regulatory Risks

**Suitability Requirements**: All gaming jurisdictions require company directors, officers, and significant shareholders to undergo suitability determinations. Loss of individual suitability can require separation from the company; loss of corporate suitability can result in license revocation. (T1_OFFICIAL, SEC 10-K FY2023 risk factors)

**Macau Policy Risk**: The Macau government's 2022 gaming law revision strengthened regulatory authority over concessionaires, including satellite casino elimination, increased government oversight of VIP gaming, and investment requirements. Changes in Chinese government policy regarding Macau gaming, cross-border currency controls, or travel restrictions can materially impact operations. (T1_OFFICIAL, Macau gaming law; SEC 10-K FY2023)

**Anti-Money Laundering**: Gaming operators face stringent AML/KYC requirements in all jurisdictions. The elimination of the traditional Macau junket operator system (following the arrest and conviction of junket operator Alvin Chau of Suncity Group in 2021–2022) has fundamentally restructured Macau's VIP gaming segment. (T3_SECONDARY_RELIABLE, widely reported)

### 4.3 Other Regulatory Areas

| Area | Applicable Framework |
|------|---------------------|

### 5.4 Writing Risk Guide — Wynn Resorts

#### SAFE TO WRITE

- Revenue, EBITDA, net income from SEC filings with citation
- Property descriptions based on wynnresorts.com
- Gaming concession details from public Macau government records
- Regulatory proceedings and fines from public commission records (NGC, MGC)
- Factual timeline of Steve Wynn departure citing 8-K and public records
- Competitor comparisons using disclosed financial metrics

#### REQUIRES CAREFUL HEDGING

| Topic | Required Approach |
|-------|-------------------|
| Steve Wynn misconduct allegations | "Allegations were reported by [source]" — describe company response factually, do not characterize conduct |
| Macau political risk | Cite 10-K risk factors; do not editorialize on China-Macau relations |
| VIP gaming / junket system | Describe regulatory changes factually; do not imply money laundering without regulatory citation |
| Competitive comparisons | Metric-based only (ADR, revenue, EBITDA margin); no qualitative superiority claims |
| Corporate governance | Describe reforms factually; do not characterize prior governance as negligent without citing the specific regulatory findings |
| UAE expansion | Describe disclosed plans; do not predict success or failure |

#### DO NOT WRITE

- Assertions about Steve Wynn's personal conduct beyond what is stated in regulatory findings and court records
- Characterizing Wynn's pre-2018 corporate culture with pejorative terms not used by regulators
- Claims about VIP player identities, gambling habits, or financial capacity
- Predictions about Macau regulatory outcomes or Chinese government policy intentions
- Assertions about the UAE gaming market's success or failure
- Reproducing Wynn's property photography, marketing copy, or design materials beyond fair use summary
