## 4. Channel-Specific Prohibitions

### wynnresorts.com
- **No discount pricing in hero positions.** Promotional rates exist in dedicated "Offers"
  sections but are never featured as hero content. The property experience is always the
  primary visual. (observed on wynnresorts.com)
- **No competitor property references.** wynnresorts.com does not reference MGM, Caesars,
  Sands, or any competitor by name. (observed on wynnresorts.com)
- **No pop-up modals with urgency messaging.** Exit-intent popups and countdown timers are
  not observed. (observed on wynnresorts.com)

### Instagram (@wynnlasvegas)
- **No text-heavy graphics.** Posts are photography-dominant; graphic design cards with
  promotional text are not observed. (observed on @wynnlasvegas)
- **No mass-market hashtags.** Tags like #Vegas, #VegasBaby, #PartyInVegas are inconsistent
  with brand positioning. Brand-specific hashtags only. (T4_INFERRED)
- **No reposting of low-quality guest content.** Guest content featured by the brand account
  must meet a minimum photographic quality standard. (T4_INFERRED from observed curation patterns)

### YouTube
- **No clickbait thumbnails or titles.** Titles are descriptive and brand-consistent. No
  "YOU WON'T BELIEVE THIS HOTEL ROOM" style engagement-bait. (observed on Wynn YouTube)
- **No unfinished or under-construction property footage in marketing context.** Development
  updates exist in investor communications, not marketing content. (T4_INFERRED)

### WeChat
- **No broadcast-style mass messaging.** WeChat communications in the Macau market are
  personalized through VIP host relationships, not mass marketing blasts. (T4_INFERRED)

### LinkedIn
- **No consumer promotional content.** LinkedIn posts focus on corporate updates, ESG,
  talent, and financial results. Room rate promotions are inconsistent with the professional
  channel. (observed on Wynn Resorts LinkedIn)

### Email / Direct Mail
- **No purchased-list cold outreach.** Email communications go to Wynn Rewards members and
  past guests. Third-party list acquisition for cold email is inconsistent with luxury
  brand positioning. (T4_INFERRED)
- **No excessive frequency.** VIP patrons receive personalized communications from their
  host; non-VIP Rewards members receive periodic (not daily) promotional emails. (T4_INFERRED)

## 5. Crisis Response Protocol

Wynn's crisis communication approach reflects the unique regulatory environment of the gaming
industry, where crises can trigger regulatory action (license review, fine, restriction) in
addition to reputational damage. (T4_INFERRED)

### Scenario 1 — Regulatory / Gaming License Issue (Grade 1: Immediate)

**Trigger examples**: Gaming commission investigation, license renewal challenge, regulatory
fine, Macau concession compliance issue.

**Response sequence**:
1. **T+0 to T+24h**: Legal review of all public communications; no public statement beyond
   confirmation of cooperation with regulators.
2. **T+24h**: Official statement on Wynn Newsroom/IR page. Factual, brief, emphasizing
   cooperation with regulatory authorities.
3. **T+48h**: SEC disclosure (8-K) if material; investor relations proactive outreach to
   major analysts and institutional holders.
4. **Ongoing**: Compliance updates published through official channels as proceedings develop.

**Channel assignments**: Newsroom/IR (primary); SEC filings (mandatory); all consumer social
channels: no engagement.

### Scenario 2 — Guest Safety Incident (Grade 2: 48-Hour Response)

**Response sequence**: Property-level incident response; corporate communication if media
coverage escalates; guest services remediation. Newsroom statement only if incident reaches
national media coverage threshold.

### Scenario 3 — Social Media Controversy (Grade 3–4: Monitor)

**Default response**: Monitor without engagement. Wynn's social media accounts do not engage
with negative commentary or controversy. Silence is the default channel strategy for social
media criticism. (T4_INFERRED from observed gaming industry communication patterns)

## 6. Steal Sheet — 3 Transferable Principles

**1. "The booking engine is inside the beauty."**
Wynn's website does not separate "inspirational content" from "booking functionality." The
property photography, the dining descriptions, the entertainment listings — every piece of
content on wynnresorts.com is one click from a booking action. The beauty IS the funnel. Brands
that separate their "brand storytelling" site from their "commerce site" create friction.
The steal: embed conversion paths directly within your highest-quality content. Do not make
the customer navigate from inspiration to transaction — the inspiration IS the transaction page.

**2. "WeChat is not social media; it is CRM."**
In the Macau market, WeChat functions as Wynn's primary VIP relationship management tool —
not as a social media broadcasting platform. The channel is used for one-to-one host-patron
communication, not one-to-many marketing. The steal: for any business serving international
high-value customers, identify the messaging platform native to that market and use it as a
relationship tool, not an advertising channel. WeChat in China, LINE in Japan, WhatsApp in
Europe — the platform is the medium for the host-guest relationship.

**3. "Let corporate events seed leisure bookings."**
Wynn's convention and MICE business functions as a customer acquisition channel for the leisure
business. A corporate attendee who experiences the property during a conference becomes a
future leisure guest. The steal: if you have a B2B and a B2C business, design the B2B
experience to showcase the B2C offering. The B2B customer who falls in love with your product
during a business context becomes your highest-conversion B2C lead — because they have already
experienced the product at full quality.
