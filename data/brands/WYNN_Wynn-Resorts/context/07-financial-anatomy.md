## 2. Revenue Anatomy

### 2.1 Five-Year Revenue Trend

| Fiscal Year | Total Operating Revenue | YoY Change | Source |
|-------------|------------------------|------------|--------|
| FY2019 | $6.61B | — | (SEC 10-K FY2019) |
| FY2020 | $2.10B | −68.2% | (SEC 10-K FY2020) |
| FY2021 | $3.76B | +79.3% | (SEC 10-K FY2021) |
| FY2022 | $3.76B | −0.1% | (SEC 10-K FY2022) |
| FY2023 | $7.13B | +89.5% | (SEC 10-K FY2023) |

FY2020 represents the full impact of COVID-19 pandemic shutdowns across all three operating regions. Las Vegas properties closed from March to June 2020; Macau properties experienced multiple periods of restricted or zero operations through 2022 due to China's zero-COVID policy. (SEC 10-K FY2020, FY2022) FY2023 marks the first full year of unrestricted operations across all properties since FY2019, with Macau's recovery driving the majority of the revenue rebound. (SEC 10-K FY2023)

### 2.2 Segment Revenue Breakdown (FY2023)

| Segment | Operating Revenue | % of Total | YoY Change | Source |
|---------|-------------------|-----------|------------|--------|
| Wynn Macau (incl. Wynn Palace) | $3.74B | 52.5% | +301.5% | (SEC 10-K FY2023) |
| Las Vegas Operations (Wynn/Encore) | $2.32B | 32.5% | +5.6% | (SEC 10-K FY2023) |
| Encore Boston Harbor | $0.87B | 12.2% | +7.9% | (SEC 10-K FY2023) |
| Corporate & Other | $0.20B | 2.8% | — | (SEC 10-K FY2023) |
| **Total** | **$7.13B** | **100%** | **+89.5%** | (SEC 10-K FY2023) |

Key observations:
- Macau operations accounted for over half of total revenue in FY2023, reflecting the full post-COVID reopening of Macau's gaming market after China lifted travel restrictions in January 2023. (SEC 10-K FY2023)
- Las Vegas operations delivered steady single-digit growth, indicating the property's premium positioning continues to attract high-end customers in a competitive market. (T4_INFERRED)
- Encore Boston Harbor, which opened in June 2019, has stabilized as the third revenue pillar. (SEC 10-K FY2023)

## 4. Investment Structure

### 4.1 Capital Expenditures

Wynn is a capital-intensive business. Properties are destination resorts requiring continuous investment in renovation, expansion, and guest experience enhancement.

| Metric | FY2023 | FY2022 | Source |
|--------|--------|--------|--------|
| Capital Expenditures | ~$0.57B | ~$0.44B | (SEC 10-K FY2023, cash flow statement) |
| CapEx as % of Revenue | ~8.0% | ~11.7% | (estimated) |

Notable capital projects disclosed in filings include Wynn Al Marjan Island (UAE), a ~$3.9B integrated resort development expected to open in 2027, representing Wynn's geographic expansion beyond Las Vegas, Macau, and Boston. (official, Wynn Resorts press releases, 2022–2023; SEC 10-K FY2023 risk factors)

### 4.2 SG&A and Pre-Opening

Wynn does not separately report R&D (hospitality companies typically do not maintain formal R&D line items). SG&A, pre-opening costs, and depreciation are disclosed in the 10-K income statement. (SEC 10-K FY2023)

## 8. Brand-Finance Nexus

### 8.1 Five Brand-Finance Signals

**Signal 1 — ADR Premium**
Wynn Las Vegas average daily room rate (ADR) consistently ranks among the highest on the Las Vegas Strip. FY2023 ADR was approximately $430–$450 (estimated from disclosed occupancy and room revenue data). This premium over market average reflects brand-driven pricing power. (T4_INFERRED)

**Signal 2 — Non-Gaming Revenue Mix**
At ~41% of total revenue, Wynn's non-gaming mix exceeds many Macau-focused peers. This suggests the brand attracts guests willing to spend on dining, entertainment, and rooms — not solely gaming. Higher non-gaming mix typically indicates stronger brand equity. (T4_INFERRED)

**Signal 3 — Macau Concession Renewal**
The 10-year concession renewal in January 2023 (Macau SAR Government, official) validates the Wynn brand's standing with Macau regulators. Concession renewal is not guaranteed and reflects the government's assessment of operator quality and investment commitment. (T1_OFFICIAL)

**Signal 4 — Return to Profitability**
The FY2023 return to ~$1.2B operating income after three years of losses demonstrates the brand's resilience — demand rebounded rapidly once travel restrictions were lifted, suggesting brand loyalty survived the COVID-19 hiatus. (T4_INFERRED from SEC filing data)

**Signal 5 — UAE Expansion License**
Wynn's selection for the first integrated resort gaming license in the UAE represents brand credibility in a new jurisdiction. The UAE's choice of Wynn over other global gaming operators signals brand strength in luxury hospitality. (T4_INFERRED; official, Wynn Resorts press releases)
