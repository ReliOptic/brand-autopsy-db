## Content Pillars

Wynn's content output across all channels maps to five distinct pillars. Unlike technology brands that lead with product capabilities, Wynn leads with environmental beauty and sensory experience. Specifications (room square footage, gaming floor size) are subordinate to atmosphere. (T4_INFERRED from systematic observation of wynnresorts.com and Wynn social media accounts)

| Pillar | Estimated Share | Core Message | Primary Formats |
|--------|----------------|--------------|-----------------|
| **1. Visual Luxury & Property Beauty** | ~35% | "The space itself is the experience." Property photography, architectural details, floral installations, the Lake of Dreams, the Performance Lake — the environment is the hero. | Full-bleed property photography, drone cinematography, time-lapse seasonal installations, Instagram feed curation |
| **2. Culinary Excellence** | ~25% | "Dining as art, not amenity." Michelin-level restaurants, celebrity chef partnerships, ingredient sourcing stories, plating photography. | Chef profile videos, dish photography (editorial quality), restaurant reveal campaigns, menu launch content |
| **3. Entertainment & Cultural Programming** | ~15% | "Performances and experiences curated for the discerning audience." Shows, residencies, nightlife, golf course, spa experiences. | Event photography, performer partnerships, behind-the-scenes content, experience packages |
| **4. Guest Experience & Lifestyle** | ~15% | "Your stay is a story worth telling." Pool scenes, spa moments, suite reveals, guest-perspective narratives. | Suite walkthrough videos, pool and spa photography, lifestyle vignettes, user-generated guest content reposting |
| **5. Corporate & Investor Communications** | ~10% | "Responsible luxury with a forward vision." ESG reporting, community investment, Macau development updates, financial results. | Press releases (wynnresorts.com/investors), earnings call transcripts, sustainability reports, development project announcements |

Pillar weighting estimates are based on observed content distribution across Wynn's official channels; Wynn does not publish an official content strategy breakdown. All percentages are (estimated).

## Hook Patterns

Wynn's content openings follow identifiable recurring structures. Six patterns appear consistently across channels.

### Hook 1 — Establishing Shot (Cinematic Property Reveal)

The content opens with a wide-angle or aerial view of the property — the curved bronze facade of Wynn Las Vegas, the lake approach to Wynn Palace, or a sunset over the Las Vegas Strip with Wynn towers prominent.

- **Example**: Wynn Palace marketing videos open with a drone sweep across the Performance Lake toward the facade, establishing grandeur before any interior detail appears. (observed on wynnresorts.com and YouTube)
- **Mechanism**: Scale and beauty are communicated before any words are spoken. The viewer's first impression is awe, not information.
- **Applicable contexts**: Property marketing, seasonal campaigns, new guest acquisition content.

### Hook 2 — Detail Close-Up (Sensory Immersion)

Opens on an extreme close-up of a material, texture, or culinary element — a linen fold, a cocktail garnish, a floral petal, a gold fixture — before pulling back to reveal the full context.

- **Example**: Restaurant marketing content opens on a chef's hands plating a dish — the micro-herbs, the sauce drizzle, the steam — before revealing the dining room environment. (observed on Wynn Instagram and wynnresorts.com dining pages)
- **Mechanism**: Forces the viewer to engage with sensory detail before understanding the commercial context. The quality of the detail sells the quality of the whole.
- **Applicable contexts**: Dining content, spa content, suite reveals, material quality storytelling.

### Hook 3 — Transformation Sequence (Day-to-Night)

Content shows the same space or experience transforming from daytime to evening — the pool at golden hour, the lobby as light shifts, the gaming floor as energy builds.

- **Example**: Wynn Las Vegas pool content shows the transition from serene daytime lounging to illuminated evening atmosphere. (observed on Wynn social media accounts)
- **Mechanism**: Communicates that the property offers multiple experiences within a single stay. Daytime and nighttime Wynn are different products — doubling the perceived value.
- **Applicable contexts**: Seasonal campaigns, package promotions, multi-day stay encouragement.

### Hook 4 — Authority Introduction (Chef / Designer / Curator)

Opens with a person of authority — a chef, an art curator, a floral designer — speaking about their craft within the Wynn context. The authority figure is the hook; the property is the platform.

- **Example**: Content featuring Wynn's Director of Floral and Design discussing the creative process behind the lobby installations. (T4_INFERRED pattern from observed luxury hospitality content strategies)
- **Mechanism**: Transfers the authority figure's credibility to the property. If a Michelin-starred chef chooses Wynn as their platform, the property inherits their standard.
- **Applicable contexts**: Restaurant launches, entertainment residency announcements, art and design content.

### Hook 5 — Guest Point-of-View (Arrival Experience)

Content shot from the guest's perspective — entering the porte-cochere, walking through the lobby, the elevator ride to the suite floor, the suite door opening to a Strip view.

- **Example**: Suite reveal videos that begin with the guest's hand on the door handle and end with the curtains opening to a floor-to-ceiling Las Vegas panorama. (observed on Wynn YouTube and Instagram Reels)
- **Mechanism**: The viewer mentally rehearses their own arrival. By the time the video ends, they have already imagined themselves in the suite.
- **Applicable contexts**: Suite marketing, new guest acquisition, booking page support content.

### Hook 6 — Seasonal Installation Reveal

Opens with the previous season's installation being deconstructed, then reveals the new installation in time-lapse or dramatic unveiling.

- **Example**: Wynn's lobby floral installations change seasonally; reveal content documents the installation process and the finished result. (observed on wynnresorts.com and social media)
- **Mechanism**: Creates recurring appointment viewing — followers return to see each season's new installation. The change itself is the content event.
- **Applicable contexts**: Seasonal campaigns, loyalty program engagement, social media recurring series.

## CTA Patterns

Wynn's calls to action are more direct than Apple's but less aggressive than mass-market hospitality brands. The tone is invitation, not urgency. (observed on wynnresorts.com)

| CTA Type | Wynn Pattern | Official Example | What Wynn Does Not Use |
|----------|-------------|-----------------|------------------------|
| **Room booking** | "Reserve" or "Book Now" — direct but not urgent | "Reserve Your Stay" (observed on wynnresorts.com) | "Book before rooms sell out!" / "Limited availability!" |
| **Dining** | "Make a Reservation" — service-oriented language | "Make a Reservation" (observed on wynnresorts.com dining pages) | "Don't miss out on this dining experience!" |
| **Information** | "Explore" or "Discover" — curiosity-driven | "Explore Wynn Las Vegas" (observed on wynnresorts.com) | "Learn more about our deals!" |
| **Offers** | "View Offers" — neutral presentation | "View Offers" (observed on wynnresorts.com) | "FLASH SALE! 40% OFF!" |
| **Entertainment** | "Get Tickets" — transactional, clear | "Get Tickets" (observed on wynnresorts.com entertainment pages) | "Buy Now Before They're Gone!" |

**Core CTA principle**: The invitation is extended; the guest decides. Wynn's CTAs assume the audience is already interested and does not need to be pressured. (T4_INFERRED from systematic observation of wynnresorts.com)

## Tone & Mood Guide

### Situation 1 — Property Marketing (Hero Content)

**Mood**: Aspirational reverence — the property is presented as a place that exists at a standard above everyday life.

- Sentence length: Medium to long; flowing, descriptive sentences preferred over staccato fragments
- Vocabulary: "exquisite," "incomparable," "curated," "award-winning," "destination"
- Pacing: Slow, deliberate; allows the reader to linger on each sensory detail
- Competitor mention: Never — Wynn does not acknowledge competitors in marketing content (observed on wynnresorts.com)
- Photography: Full-bleed, high-resolution, warm lighting, minimal post-production manipulation

**Example copy pattern**: "[Property name]. [Sensory description]. [Emotional promise.]"
"Wynn Las Vegas. Where every detail has been considered, and every experience has been crafted to exceed expectation." (T4_INFERRED, based on observed copy patterns)

---

### Situation 2 — Dining Content

**Mood**: Artisanal precision — the food is treated as art, and the chef is treated as an artist.

- Sentence length: Medium; detail-rich but not flowery
- Vocabulary: "crafted," "seasonal," "sourced," "technique," "presentation"
- Photography: Overhead plating shots, side-angle with shallow depth of field, chef action shots with motion blur
- Copy structure: Ingredient provenance + preparation method + sensory outcome

---

### Situation 3 — Financial / Investor Communications

**Mood**: Institutional clarity — precise, factual, zero marketing language.

- Sentence length: Standard business prose; complex sentences with appropriate qualification
- Vocabulary: Financial terminology; "revenue," "EBITDA," "occupancy rate," "segment"
- Sourcing: All claims attributed to specific SEC filings or official company disclosures
- Channel: Wynn Newsroom (wynnresorts.com), SEC EDGAR filings, earnings call transcripts

## Content Prohibitions

### Copy-Level Prohibitions

1. **Price-comparison language**: "More affordable than," "best value," "compare our rates" — Wynn does not position on price relative to competitors. (T4_INFERRED)
2. **Urgency and scarcity language**: "Limited time," "selling fast," "only X rooms left" — pressure tactics are inconsistent with luxury hospitality positioning. (T4_INFERRED)
3. **Party and nightlife excess language**: "Wild night," "party destination," "let loose" — Wynn positions entertainment as refined, not hedonistic. (T4_INFERRED)
4. **Gambling addiction triggers**: "Win big," "hot streak," "lucky night" — responsible gaming guidelines and brand positioning both prohibit promotional gambling language. (T4_INFERRED, consistent with Nevada Gaming Commission advertising guidelines)
5. **Mass-market superlatives**: "Biggest casino," "most rooms," "cheapest rates" — scale claims position against Wynn's intimate luxury thesis. (T4_INFERRED)
6. **Direct competitor naming**: MGM, Caesars, Sands are not named in Wynn marketing content. (observed on wynnresorts.com)
7. **Stock photography**: All imagery on wynnresorts.com appears to be property-specific photography, not generic stock images. (observed on wynnresorts.com)

### Visual-Level Prohibitions

1. **Cluttered compositions**: Wynn photography favors clean compositions with generous negative space. Busy, multi-element frames are not observed. (observed on wynnresorts.com)
2. **Cold or harsh lighting**: Warm, golden-hour quality lighting dominates Wynn property photography. Fluorescent or harsh flash photography is not used. (observed on wynnresorts.com)
3. **Visible gaming addiction signage in marketing**: While responsible gaming disclosures are required by regulation, marketing photography does not feature responsible gaming signage prominently. (T4_INFERRED)
4. **Text-heavy social media graphics**: Wynn's social media posts are image-led; text overlays are minimal or absent. (observed on Wynn Instagram)

## Steal Sheet — 3 Transferable Principles

**1. Make the environment the content, not the backdrop for content.**
Wynn's most effective content is property photography — the building itself, the lobby, the pool, the restaurant interior. The environment is not a setting for a marketing message; the environment IS the marketing message. Transferable structure: if your physical space or product design is beautiful, photograph it with the care you would give a fashion editorial. Let the space speak. The highest-converting hotel photography is not a smiling couple on a bed — it is an empty suite at golden hour with the curtains open. The viewer imagines themselves into the space.

**2. Use culinary content as an aspirational gateway.**
Wynn invests disproportionately in dining content relative to room content. The reason: a $150 dinner is an accessible entry point to the Wynn brand that a $500+ room night is not. The diner who experiences Wynn through a restaurant becomes a future hotel guest. Transferable structure: identify the lowest-price-point product or service in your premium brand and invest marketing content there. It is the gateway drug for the full-price experience.

**3. Create appointment content through seasonal change.**
Wynn's seasonal lobby installations create recurring content events — guests and followers return to see each new installation. This is content that regenerates itself on a predictable schedule. Transferable structure: build at least one annually recurring content event that your audience expects and anticipates. The recurring event creates habitual engagement that one-off campaigns cannot.
