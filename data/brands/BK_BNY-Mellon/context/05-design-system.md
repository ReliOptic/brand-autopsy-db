# 05 — Design System
## BNY Mellon (BK) | Financials — Custody & Asset Servicing

---

## 1. Design Philosophy

BNY Mellon's visual identity operates under a doctrine of **quiet authority** — a design language that communicates permanence, precision, and institutional gravitas without ornament. The brand does not compete on visual excitement; it competes on the confidence that comes from restraint and clarity.

Three design principles govern all brand expression:

1. **Structural clarity over decoration** — every visual element earns its place by communicating something, not by making the design "richer"
2. **Data made legible** — BNY Mellon handles the world's most complex financial data; the design system must make complexity navigable, not impressive
3. **Institutional weight** — spacing, typography scale, and color weight should convey the gravity of a $49.5T custodian, not the lightness of a fintech startup

### Color Usage Rules
- Navy-on-white and white-on-navy are the two primary reading combinations; both pass WCAG AA (4.5:1 minimum)

- Data red and data green are reserved exclusively for performance directionality — never decorative
- No gradients in the primary brand system; flat color only (gradients permitted in data visualization only)

## 3. Typography

### Type Hierarchy

| Level | Role | Typeface | Weight | Size Range | Label |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| Display | Campaign headlines, hero statements | Publico Headline (or Guardian Egyptian) | Bold | 48–72pt | (estimated) |
| H1 | Section primary headers | Publico Text / Mercury Text | Semibold | 32–48pt | (estimated) |
| H2 | Sub-section headers | Publico Text | Medium | 24–32pt | (estimated) |
| H3 | Content headers, card titles | BNY Mellon Sans (custom grotesque) or Helvetica Neue | Medium | 18–24pt | (estimated) |
| Body | Running text, white papers, articles | BNY Mellon Sans / Helvetica Neue | Regular | 14–16pt | (estimated) |
| Caption | Footnotes, data labels, image credits | BNY Mellon Sans | Regular | 10–12pt | (estimated) |
| Data | Chart labels, table headers, numerical data | Monospace companion (Roboto Mono equivalent) | Regular | 10–14pt | (estimated) |

### Typography Rules
- Line height: 1.5x for body; 1.2x for display/headers
- Tracking: -1 to 0 for display; 0 for body; +20 for all-caps labels
- Never set body copy in all-caps
- Numerals: tabular (lining) figures mandatory in all data contexts
- Sentence case for all body headers; title case permitted for formal document titles only

## 4. Logo System

### Primary Mark
- **Full wordmark:** "BNY Mellon" in custom typeface — a serif-influenced grotesque with slightly elevated x-height signaling legibility at scale
- **Abbreviated mark:** "BNY" — introduced in recent brand communications for digital/social contexts; increasing in usage as of 2023–2024
- **Icon mark:** Abstract interlocking arc device representing interconnected financial flows; used as standalone only at large scale or on dark backgrounds
- **Minimum size:** Wordmark minimum 80px wide on digital; 25mm on print
- **Clear space:** 1x the cap-height of the wordmark on all sides

### Logo Color Variants
| Variant | Background | Usage |
|---|---|---|
| Navy wordmark | White / light | Standard print and digital |
| White wordmark | Navy / dark | Dark backgrounds, event materials |
| Gold wordmark | Navy | Premium materials, award contexts |
| Monochrome black | White | Legal documents, black-and-white print |

### Logo Don'ts
- Do not recolor the wordmark in any color outside the approved variants
- Do not stretch, condense, or alter letterform proportions
- Do not use the icon mark alone at small sizes (below 40px)
- Do not place the wordmark on busy photographic backgrounds without a clear zone overlay

## 5. Iconography

### Icon Style Principles
- **Stroke-based line icons** — 2px stroke weight at 24px base grid; not filled icons
- Corner radius: 2px for rectangular elements; fully rounded for circular elements
- Single-weight stroke — no combination of thin and thick strokes in one icon
- Icons communicate function, not decoration; no illustrative or metaphorical icons in UI

### Icon Categories in Use
| Category | Examples |
|---|---|
| Navigation & UI | Home, settings, search, filter, download, external link |
| Financial Operations | Settlement, reconciliation, position, transaction, transfer |
| Data & Analytics | Chart line, chart bar, table, filter, sort, export |
| Security & Compliance | Lock, shield, verification check, alert, audit |
| Global & Network | Globe, map pin, network node, connection |
| Document | Report, PDF, brief, contract, ledger |

## 6. Photography & Imagery

### Photography Style
- **Architecture:** International financial district exteriors and interiors — glass, steel, structural geometry. Conveys permanence and global reach.
- **Professionals in motion:** Diverse institutional professionals in candid-but-composed contexts — walking through trading floors, reviewing screens, in boardroom dialogue. Not posed studio portraiture.
- **Data visualization overlays:** Photography overlaid with translucent data grids, network connection graphics, or subtle HUD-style analytics elements. Bridges the physical and digital brand.
- **City and market photography:** Aerials and street-level views of financial districts — New York, London, Hong Kong, Singapore, Dubai. Reinforces global network positioning.

### Photography Rules
- Color tone: slightly desaturated with cool temperature bias; no warm Instagram-style processing
- No stock-photo warmth (smiling people looking at camera, handshakes, generic office scenes)
- Diversity requirement: photography must represent gender, racial, age, and geographic diversity across the institutional professional range
- Photography never used as decoration; always paired with substantive copy context

### Illustration & Graphic Style
- Data visualization is the primary illustration vehicle — not conceptual illustration
- Structural diagrams (custody workflow, network maps, architecture diagrams) use the brand color system with clean line weights
- No mascots, no cartoon elements, no isometric illustration in the primary brand system
- Abstract brand patterns (arc device derivatives) permitted as texture in large-format materials

## 7. Layout & Grid System

### Grid Principles
- **12-column grid** for digital; **8-column grid** for print
- Column gutter: 24px digital / 5mm print
- Outer margin: 80px desktop / 16px mobile / 25mm print
- Maximum content width: 1200px on desktop

### Layout Hierarchy Rules
- White space is a brand asset — generous margins signal confidence and readability
- No more than 2 typeface weights per layout zone
- Data tables and charts always full-width within their column allocation
- Sidebar content (callouts, statistics, pull quotes) set at 30% column width maximum

### Key Layout Templates
| Template | Use Case | Grid |
|---|---|---|
| White Paper | Long-form research and regulatory content | 2-column (text + sidebar) |
| Product Sheet | Custody service capability one-pager | Single column with data callouts |
| Data Dashboard | Workbench UI, client portal, analytics | 12-column modular |
| Event Presentation | SIBOS, SIFMA, Finadium keynotes | 16:9 widescreen; full-bleed photography |
| Email | Client communications, regulatory briefs | Single column; 600px max width |

---

## 8. Motion & Animation (Digital)

- **Easing:** Ease-in-out (cubic-bezier); never linear or bouncy
- **Duration:** UI transitions 150–250ms; data visualization reveals 400–800ms; page transitions 300ms
- **Motion principles:** Data should "build" into charts (line drawing, bar rising) — never appear all at once; element entrances use fade + subtle upward translate only
- **No autoplay video** in professional content contexts; video always user-initiated
- **Looping animations:** Permitted only for data flow diagrams and network visualizations; maximum 4-second loop; no more than 30% of viewport area in motion simultaneously

---

## 9. Accessibility Standards

| Standard | Requirement |
|---|---|
| Color contrast | WCAG AA minimum (4.5:1 body; 3:1 large text) for all text on background combinations |
| Focus indicators | Visible 2px focus ring in BNY Gold (`#C9A84C`) on all interactive elements |
| Alt text | Mandatory on all non-decorative imagery |
| Typography minimum | 14px minimum body text on all digital surfaces |
| Data tables | Full header row markup; sortable columns keyboard-accessible |
| PDF documents | Tagged PDF structure for screen reader compatibility on all public documents |

## 11. Steal Sheets (Design Layer)

### Steal Sheet 1 — "Restraint as Luxury Signal"
**What BNY Mellon does:** Generous white space, limited color palette, and structural typography communicate premium institutional positioning without a single word. The design says "we do not need to impress you."
**How to steal it:** In premium B2B markets, reduce visual noise. Cut decorative elements by 30%. Increase white space by 40%. Restraint signals confidence; busyness signals insecurity.

### Steal Sheet 2 — "Data Visualization as Brand Differentiation"
**What BNY Mellon does:** The Workbench platform's chart library and data visualization style IS the brand in operational contexts. A client spending 6 hours per day in Workbench experiences the brand through data design, not logo placement.
**How to steal it:** If your product has an operational interface (dashboard, portal, analytics), treat that interface as your primary brand expression. Invest in the design system for data visualization first — it has more daily brand contact hours than any campaign.

### Steal Sheet 3 — "The Color Moat"
**What BNY Mellon does:** Navy + Gold is a differentiated combination in custody (where most competitors are navy + white or navy + red). The gold creates warmth and distinction that competitors cannot easily adopt without looking derivative.
**How to steal it:** In any category where 3–5 dominant players share similar color palettes, the brand that claims a distinctive secondary accent creates a visual moat. Find the color your category has abandoned and own it before a competitor does.
