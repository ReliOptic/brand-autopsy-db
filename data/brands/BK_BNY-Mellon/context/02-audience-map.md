# 02 — Audience Map
## BNY Mellon (BK) | Financials — Custody & Asset Servicing

---

## 1. Audience Architecture Overview

BNY Mellon's custody and asset servicing audience is entirely B2B and B2I (business-to-institution). There are no retail consumers. Decision-making is long-cycle (12–36 months for custody mandates), consensus-driven (procurement committees, C-suite sign-off, legal review), and relationship-dependent.

```
PRIMARY AUDIENCES
├── Asset Owners (Pension Funds, SWFs, Endowments, Insurance Cos)
├── Asset Managers (Global & Regional Fund Managers)
└── Financial Intermediaries (Banks, Broker-Dealers, Prime Brokers)

SECONDARY AUDIENCES
├── Regulators & Policymakers (indirect; compliance positioning)
├── Technology & Fintech Partners (API/ecosystem integrations)
└── Talent Market (institutional finance operations professionals)
```

## 7. Steal Sheets (Audience Layer)

### Steal Sheet 1 — "The Conflict-Free Custodian" Positioning
**What BNY Mellon owns:** The implicit message to asset managers that BNY Mellon does not manage competing investment products at scale (unlike JPMorgan or Goldman custody arms) creates a conflict-of-interest advantage.
**How to steal it:** If your firm's business model is structurally free of conflicts common in your category, make that neutrality explicit in all audience communications. Structural honesty is a positioning lever.

### Steal Sheet 2 — "Buying Committee Content Mapping"
**What BNY Mellon does well:** Each buyer role in the custody committee receives tailored content — COO gets SLA documentation, CTO gets API developer docs, CIO gets thought leadership. Content is not one-size-fits-all.
**How to steal it:** Map every piece of brand content to a specific buying committee role. If a piece of content could be written for anyone, it is written for no one.

### Steal Sheet 3 — "The Transition Risk Frame"
**What BNY Mellon owns:** Custody transitions are genuinely painful (12–18 months of parallel running, settlement risk, data migration). BNY Mellon's methodology documentation and dedicated transition management teams address the #1 reason clients stay with an inferior custodian: fear of switching.
**How to steal it:** In any category where switching costs are high, produce detailed, credible "transition/migration methodology" content. Make the fear of moving TO you smaller than the fear of staying with a competitor.
