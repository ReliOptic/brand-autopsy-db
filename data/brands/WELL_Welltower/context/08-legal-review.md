## 3. Major Litigation and Regulatory

### 3.1 Operator-Related Legal Exposure

Welltower's primary litigation exposure is indirect — through the operations of its tenant-operators. Senior housing and post-acute care facilities face regulatory scrutiny, resident injury claims, and staffing-related litigation. While operators bear direct liability under most lease structures, Welltower can face:

- **Premises liability**: As property owner, Welltower may be named in personal injury claims arising from property conditions. (T4_INFERRED from REIT industry legal dynamics)
- **Operator default proceedings**: When operators experience financial distress, lease restructuring or operator transitions can involve legal proceedings. (SEC 10-K FY2023, risk factors)

### 3.2 ProMedica Relationship

Welltower's relationship with ProMedica Health System has been a notable operational challenge. ProMedica operates a significant portfolio of senior housing and post-acute care properties for Welltower. Operational and financial challenges at ProMedica-operated properties have been discussed by Welltower management on earnings calls and are reflected in portfolio restructuring activities. (T2_PRIMARY_RELIABLE, earnings call commentary; SEC 10-K FY2023)

Welltower has been transitioning properties away from underperforming operators, including portions of the ProMedica portfolio, to stronger operators as part of its active portfolio management strategy. (T2_PRIMARY_RELIABLE)

### 3.3 COVID-19 Related Litigation

The senior housing industry faced litigation related to COVID-19 outbreaks in care facilities nationally. Welltower's 10-K discloses COVID-related litigation risks. Many states enacted liability protection legislation for healthcare facilities during the pandemic, though the scope and duration of these protections vary by jurisdiction. (SEC 10-K FY2022, FY2023; T3_SECONDARY_RELIABLE)

### 3.4 SEC and Securities Matters

Welltower's 10-K discloses various routine legal proceedings. As a publicly traded REIT, the company is subject to standard securities litigation risks including class action claims. (SEC 10-K FY2023, Item 3)

## 4. Regulatory Landscape

### 4.1 Healthcare Regulatory Framework

Welltower's operators are subject to extensive healthcare regulation that directly impacts property-level economics:

| Regulator | Area | Impact on Welltower |
|-----------|------|---------------------|
| **CMS** | Medicare/Medicaid reimbursement rates for skilled nursing | Reimbursement changes affect operator profitability and ability to pay rent (SEC 10-K) |
| **State Health Departments** | Facility licensing, certificate of need, staffing ratios | Licensing requirements affect property value and operator viability (SEC 10-K) |
| **State Attorneys General** | Elder care quality investigations | Investigations can result in operator sanctions affecting Welltower properties (T4_INFERRED) |
| **OSHA** | Workplace safety in care facilities | Compliance costs borne by operators (T4_INFERRED) |
| **HHS/OCR** | HIPAA compliance for healthcare data | Applies to operators; data breach at a Welltower property creates reputational risk (T4_INFERRED) |

### 4.2 REIT Regulatory Requirements

| Requirement | Rule | Impact |
|-------------|------|--------|
| **Income distribution** | Distribute ≥90% of taxable income as dividends | Limits retained earnings; requires external capital for growth (IRC Section 856, T1_OFFICIAL) |
| **Asset tests** | ≥75% of assets must be real estate-related | Constrains diversification into non-real-estate businesses (T1_OFFICIAL) |
| **Income tests** | ≥75% of gross income from real estate sources | Limits non-rental revenue activities (T1_OFFICIAL) |
| **Ownership tests** | No 5 individuals may own >50% of shares | Monitored through share ownership limitations (T1_OFFICIAL) |

### 4.3 International Regulatory

| Jurisdiction | Key Regulatory Areas |
|-------------|---------------------|
| **United Kingdom** (~15% of portfolio) | CQC (Care Quality Commission) oversight of care homes; UK planning law; FCA for debt issuance (T4_INFERRED) |
| **Canada** (~10% of portfolio) | Provincial health ministry licensing; CCPF standards for long-term care; CMHC for mortgage insurance (T4_INFERRED) |

### 5.4 Writing Risk Guide — Welltower

#### SAFE TO WRITE
- FFO, NOI, revenue, and financial data from SEC filings
- Portfolio composition and property counts from supplemental data
- Operator names that are publicly disclosed in filings
- Dividend history from IR disclosures
- Occupancy statistics from earnings releases
- Credit ratings from public agency publications

#### REQUIRES CAREFUL HEDGING
| Topic | Required Approach |
|-------|-------------------|
| Operator performance | "Welltower has disclosed..." — do not characterize operators as failing or negligent |
| Senior care quality | Cite CMS ratings or state inspection data; do not make quality claims without sourced evidence |
| COVID-19 impact on residents | Factual, statistical only; no attribution of blame for outcomes |
| ProMedica relationship | Describe disclosed portfolio actions; do not characterize the relationship with pejorative terms |
| Occupancy projections | "Management has indicated..." — do not predict recovery timeline |

#### DO NOT WRITE
- Claims about care quality at specific Welltower properties without CMS or regulatory citation
- Attribution of resident health outcomes to Welltower's ownership decisions
- Characterization of operator relationships as exploitative or negligent
- Resident or patient identifying information
- Predictions about CMS reimbursement rate changes
- Assertions about care facility conditions not based on public regulatory records
