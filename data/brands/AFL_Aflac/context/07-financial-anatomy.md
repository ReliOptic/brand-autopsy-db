## Brand Valuation

Aflac's brand value is estimated at approximately $4-6B by major brand valuation firms. (T3_SECONDARY_RELIABLE, based on published brand valuation reports; note that methodologies vary) The brand's value derives from:

1. **Consumer recognition**: 90%+ aided awareness in the U.S., driven by 20+ years of Aflac Duck advertising
2. **Category ownership**: "Supplemental insurance" and "Aflac" are nearly synonymous in the U.S. market
3. **Japan market position**: One in four Japanese households has an Aflac policy (estimated)
4. **Distribution moat**: Employer enrollment infrastructure and broker relationships create recurring access to consumers

**Brand-to-financial link**: Aflac's brand awareness directly reduces customer acquisition costs. At open enrollment, an employee who recognizes the Aflac name requires less education and is more likely to enroll than an employee presented with an unknown carrier. This awareness-to-enrollment conversion efficiency is the financial return on decades of Duck advertising investment. (T4_INFERRED)

## Brand-Finance Nexus

1. **Brand awareness is the underwriting margin**: Aflac's U.S. benefit ratio (~48%) means more than half of premium revenue is retained after claims. This margin funds the $300-400M annual advertising spend that sustains the brand awareness that drives the next cycle of enrollment. The brand is self-funding. (T4_INFERRED)

2. **Share repurchase amplifies per-share brand value**: By retiring ~50% of shares outstanding over 13 years, Aflac has doubled the brand value per share even without proportional revenue growth. Each remaining share represents a larger claim on the brand's earnings power. (T4_INFERRED)

3. **Yen exposure is the brand's hidden cost**: When the yen weakens, Aflac Japan's premiums translate to fewer USD. This currency effect can mask strong operational performance, creating a disconnect between the brand's market-level strength and its reported financial results. (T4_INFERRED)

4. **Japan persistency is the financial flywheel**: Aflac Japan's >90% persistency rate means that the cost of acquiring a policyholder is amortized over many years of premium payments. The longer a policyholder stays, the more profitable that policy becomes — and the more the initial brand investment (awareness → enrollment) pays off. (T4_INFERRED)

5. **Dividend Aristocrat status is a financial brand**: 41 consecutive years of dividend increases makes Aflac a "must-own" for income-focused institutional investors. This financial brand (reliable capital return) complements the consumer brand (reliable insurance coverage) and supports the stock's valuation multiple. (T4_INFERRED)
