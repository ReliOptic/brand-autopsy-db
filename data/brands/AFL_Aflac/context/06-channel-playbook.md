## Channel Prohibitions

1. **Do not use duck humor in claims or policyholder service communications**: When a policyholder is filing a claim after a cancer diagnosis or accident, the duck's levity is inappropriate. Claims and service channels maintain the Caregiver register exclusively. (T4_INFERRED)

2. **Do not publish medical advice or health recommendations on any channel**: Aflac is a financial product company, not a healthcare provider. Wellness content is limited to general awareness (cancer awareness month, preventive screening reminders) and must include disclaimers directing readers to consult healthcare professionals. (T4_INFERRED)

3. **Do not disclose policyholder information or claim details on social media**: Even in response to public complaints on Twitter/X, policyholder details must never be discussed publicly. Direct the conversation to private channels immediately. (T4_INFERRED)

4. **Do not use LinkedIn for duck personality content**: LinkedIn serves the B2B/employer/broker audience. Duck campaigns live on TV, Twitter/X, Instagram, and YouTube. Mixing channels confuses the dual-audience strategy. (T4_INFERRED)

5. **Do not make claims speed promises outside of One Day Pay eligibility criteria**: Social posts or advertisements suggesting all claims are paid in one day without the eligibility context create regulatory risk and consumer disappointment. (T4_INFERRED)

## Crisis Protocol

### Scenario 1: Claims Processing Failure or Denial Controversy

**Trigger**: Media or social media attention on claims denials, processing delays, or a policyholder who was denied coverage they expected.

**Protocol**:
- **Immediate**: Legal and communications review of the specific case. Determine whether the denial was policy-appropriate or an error.
- **Social media**: Do not engage publicly with case details. Respond with: "We take every claim seriously. Please DM us your policy number so we can review your case personally." Escalate to dedicated claims resolution team.
- **aflac.com**: If systemic, publish a customer-facing FAQ addressing the issue transparently.
- **Media**: Prepare a holding statement: "Aflac is committed to paying claims promptly and fairly. We are reviewing this matter and will work directly with the policyholder."
- **Tone**: Empathetic, accountability-forward, specific about resolution steps.

### Scenario 2: Aflac Duck Campaign Controversy

**Trigger**: An Aflac Duck advertisement generates public backlash — perceived insensitivity, cultural misstep, or controversy involving a spokesperson.

**Protocol**:
- **Within 4 hours**: Assess the nature and scale of the backlash. Determine if the concern is legitimate or amplified by a small group.
- **Social media**: Pause all scheduled duck content. Do not delete the original content unless legal advises. Acknowledge the concern: "We hear you. We are reviewing this content and take your feedback seriously."
- **If legitimate concern**: Pull the ad, issue a brief public apology, redirect marketing to Caregiver-focused content.
- **Television**: Contact media buyers to pause the spot rotation.
- **Tone**: Humble, responsive, not defensive.

### Scenario 3: Japan Regulatory or Distribution Partner Issue

**Trigger**: Japanese regulators (FSA) issue a warning, Japan Post partnership terms change, or a compliance issue surfaces in Japan operations.

**Protocol**:
- **SEC filing**: 8-K if material to financial results.
- **Investor relations**: Proactive communication to analysts with context and expected impact.
- **Japan-specific channels**: Official statement on aflac.co.jp per FSA guidance requirements.
- **U.S. social channels**: Do not proactively discuss unless media coverage makes it necessary. If asked, direct to official statement.
- **Tone**: Regulatory-compliant, factual, forward-looking on remediation.

## Steal Sheet — 3 Transferable Principles

**1. Split the mascot channel from the product channel.**
Aflac runs the duck on TV, Twitter, and Instagram for awareness. LinkedIn and the broker channel handle B2B product conversations. The two never cross. Transferable structure: if your brand has a personality-driven awareness vehicle and a professional sales channel, assign each to separate platforms and maintain strict tone separation.

**2. Use open enrollment as your annual demand spike.**
Aflac's entire marketing calendar builds toward Q4 open enrollment season. TV spend increases, enrollment coordinators are deployed, and employer communications intensify. Transferable insight: identify the annual decision window in your industry (budget season, contract renewal, regulatory deadline) and concentrate your marketing investment around it rather than spreading evenly across the year.

**3. Let CSR build the trust that product marketing cannot.**
Aflac's childhood cancer initiatives (Aflac Cancer Center, My Special Aflac Duck) build emotional trust that no product advertisement can replicate. When a consumer associates Aflac with "the company that gives comfort ducks to kids with cancer," the brand has earned permission to ask for their insurance business. Transferable structure: choose a CSR initiative that is authentically connected to your product's domain (Aflac insures health; Aflac supports children's health) and invest enough to make it visible, not just checkbox-level.
