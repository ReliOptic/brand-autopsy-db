## Major Litigation

### Overview

Aflac maintains a relatively clean litigation profile compared to other large insurers. The company's SEC 10-K FY2023 Risk Factors disclose standard insurance industry litigation risks but do not identify any single pending case as material to the company's financial condition. (SEC 10-K FY2023)

### Notable Matters

| Matter | Description | Status | Source |
|--------|-------------|--------|--------|
| **Routine claims litigation** | Individual policyholder disputes regarding claims denials or benefit calculations. Normal course of business for any insurer. | Ongoing, not individually material | SEC 10-K FY2023 |
| **Sales practice inquiries** | Periodic regulatory inquiries into sales practices of independent agents. Insurance regulators routinely examine agent conduct. | No material outstanding actions disclosed | SEC 10-K FY2023 |
| **Japan regulatory compliance** | Aflac Japan operates under the supervision of Japan's Financial Services Agency (FSA). Regulatory examinations are periodic and standard. | Compliant; no material adverse findings disclosed | SEC 10-K FY2023 |
| **2011 investment loss controversy** | Historical matter — Aflac disclosed significant losses on European sovereign debt and banking investments during the 2011 eurozone crisis. This led to executive changes and portfolio rebalancing. | Resolved; portfolio restructured | T3_SECONDARY_RELIABLE, historical reporting |

**Litigation posture interpretation**: Aflac's litigation risk profile is LOW relative to other large insurers. The company does not face mega-litigation comparable to asbestos claims, PFAS liability, or multi-billion-dollar class actions. The primary legal risks are routine claims disputes, agent conduct supervision, and regulatory compliance in two jurisdictions (U.S. state regulators and Japan FSA). (T4_INFERRED)

### Writing Risk Guide

#### SAFE — Can write without qualification

- Revenue, margin, and financial metrics sourced from SEC filings with fiscal year citation
- Number of policyholders, market share estimates attributed to source
- Observable brand elements (Aflac Blue color, typography, Duck character description)
- Historical facts (founding date, market entry dates, campaign launch dates)
- Direct quotes from official Aflac communications with attribution
- Description of the Aflac Duck campaign as an advertising phenomenon

#### HEDGE — Write with attribution and qualifying language

- Brand awareness estimates: "Aflac's aided brand awareness is estimated to exceed 90% among U.S. adults (estimated, based on publicly discussed Aflac marketing data)"
- Competitive position claims: "Aflac appears to be the largest provider of supplemental insurance in the United States based on premium volume (SEC 10-K FY2023)"
- Japan market dominance: "Approximately one in four Japanese households is estimated to hold an Aflac policy (estimated, based on publicly available market data)"
- Consumer perception: "Analysis suggests that Aflac's brand is primarily associated with supplemental insurance accessibility (T4_INFERRED)"

#### DO NOT WRITE — Prohibited content

- Any assertion that Aflac denies legitimate claims as a business practice
- Any claim about Aflac agent misconduct without citing specific regulatory findings
- Any characterization of Aflac's Japan operations as exploitative of elderly consumers
- Any suggestion that Aflac's investment portfolio carries hidden or undisclosed risks
- Any reproduction of the Aflac Duck character design (description is permitted; visual reproduction is not)
- Any statement about Aflac's internal pricing or actuarial decisions not disclosed in public filings
