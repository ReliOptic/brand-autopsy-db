## 4. Channel-Specific Prohibitions

### TV Advertising
- **No fear-based scenarios.** The duck provides levity; medical emergencies are implied, not shown graphically. (T4_INFERRED)
- **No competitor naming.** The duck quacks "Aflac," not comparisons. (observed)

### aflac.com
- **No auto-playing video on product pages.** Trust requires user control. (T4_INFERRED)
- **No promotional countdown timers.** Insurance is not a flash sale. (T4_INFERRED)

### LinkedIn
- **No duck-heavy content.** LinkedIn audience expects professional, data-driven content. The duck appears minimally or not at all. (T4_INFERRED)

### Workplace Enrollment
- **No high-pressure sales tactics.** Benefits coordinators are trained to educate, not pressure. Aflac's voluntary model depends on employee trust. (T4_INFERRED)

## 5. Crisis Response Protocol

### Scenario 1 — Claims Processing Failure (Grade 1)
**Trigger**: Systemic claims delay or denial affecting multiple policyholders.
**Response**: Internal investigation → Public acknowledgment via aflac.com → Direct outreach to affected policyholders → Expedited claims processing → State insurance commissioner communication if regulatory inquiry initiated.

### Scenario 2 — Duck Campaign Controversy (Grade 3)
**Trigger**: A duck commercial perceived as insensitive to a particular community or situation.
**Response**: Monitor social sentiment velocity → If media pickup: pause ad rotation → Release brief statement acknowledging concern → Modify or retire specific creative → Resume with alternative creative.

### Scenario 3 — Data Breach (Grade 1)
**Trigger**: Unauthorized access to policyholder personal or health information.
**Response**: Immediate regulatory notification per state breach notification laws → Policyholder notification within required timeframes → Offer credit monitoring → Publish incident details on aflac.com → Executive statement.

## 6. Steal Sheet

**1. "Make the mascot the media strategy."**
The Aflac Duck is not merely a character in commercials — it IS the media strategy. The duck generates earned media through cultural appearances, memes, and social sharing that would cost millions in paid media. Transferable structure: invest in a brand asset that generates its own media coverage rather than depending entirely on paid distribution.

**2. "Seasonal concentration creates campaign efficiency."**
Aflac concentrates marketing spending around Q4 open enrollment season, when purchase decisions actually happen. This avoids the waste of year-round spending in a category where the purchase window is narrow. Transferable insight: identify when your customer actually makes decisions and concentrate spend there.

**3. "Zero-cost-to-employer as distribution unlock."**
The voluntary benefits model makes Aflac a free addition to the employer's benefits package. This removes the employer's cost objection entirely, making the distribution decision frictionless. Transferable structure: design your B2B2C offering so the business intermediary bears zero direct cost.
