# 02 — Audience Map
## Ares Management Corporation

---

## 1. Audience Architecture Overview

Ares operates in a B2B2C structure with a primary institutional audience and a growing accredited/semi-institutional tier. The audience map reflects this layered reality: sovereign funds and pension allocators at the top, family offices and wealth platforms in the middle, and emerging retail-adjacent segments at the base.

---

## 2. Primary Audience Segments

### Segment A: Institutional Allocators
- Pension funds, sovereign wealth funds, endowments, foundations, insurance general accounts
- AUM typically $1B+ under management
- Decision cycle: 12–24 months; committee-driven
- Key need: Benchmark-beating risk-adjusted returns; capital preservation; regulatory-compliant vehicles

### Segment B: Private Wealth / Family Office
- Ultra-high-net-worth individuals; single- and multi-family offices
- $25M–$500M investable assets
- Decision cycle: 3–12 months; advisor-influenced or direct
- Key need: Diversification beyond public markets; income yield; portfolio resilience

### Segment C: Financial Intermediaries
- Registered investment advisors (RIAs), wirehouse advisors, private bank platforms (Merrill, Morgan Stanley, UBS)
- $5M–$50M client relationships
- Key need: Product shelf access; due diligence support; client-facing materials

### Segment D: Corporate / Strategic Partners
- Middle-market and upper-middle-market companies seeking debt or equity capital
- Revenue $50M–$2B
- Key need: Flexible capital solutions; speed to close; relationship-driven underwriting

### Segment E: Internal & Talent Audience
- Investment professionals, operating executives, back-office talent
- Competitive with KKR, Blackstone, Apollo, Carlyle for top MBA/law graduates
- Key need: Meritocratic carry, intellectual rigor, institutional prestige

## 6. Steal Sheets — Audience Layer

### Steal Sheet 1: The "Institutional Economics for Everyone" Unlock
**What Ares owns:** Co-investment economics at institutional rates regardless of LP size.
**How to steal it:** Build a formal co-investment program with published criteria. Communicate that family offices and smaller LPs get the same co-invest terms as $10B pension funds. Make this a public differentiator.

### Steal Sheet 2: The "Advisor Confidence Kit"
**What Ares owns:** Awareness that advisors are the gatekeeper to the wealth channel — and they need tools, not just pitches.
**How to steal it:** Develop a library of client-facing one-pagers, explainer videos under 3 minutes, and pre-approved email templates for RIAs. Call it a "confidence kit." Own the advisor enablement category.

### Steal Sheet 3: The "Digital-Native LP Portal" Expectation
**What Ares owns:** Recognition that next-generation allocators (tech-founder family offices, younger CIOs) expect real-time data access.
**How to steal it:** Build and market a self-service LP data portal. Publish the capability prominently. Let the technology be a brand signal that says "we are building for the next 20 years of LP relationships, not the last 20."
