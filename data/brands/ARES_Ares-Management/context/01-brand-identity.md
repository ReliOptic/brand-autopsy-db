## 2. Mission & Purpose Statement

**Official:** Ares Management is a leading global alternative investment manager delivering superior risk-adjusted returns through disciplined investment processes across credit, private equity, and real assets.

**Decoded intent:** Ares positions itself as the institutional-grade operator — the firm that professionals trust not because it shouts loudest, but because its track record speaks with quiet authority. Its brand promise is not transformation; it is reliability at scale.

---

## 3. Vision Statement

To be the world's most trusted alternative asset manager — expanding access to institutional-quality investing while maintaining the credit discipline and deal selectivity that built the franchise.

## 6. Tone of Voice

**Primary tone:** Measured authority — the voice of the senior credit analyst who has seen every cycle.

**Secondary tone:** Institutional partnership — "we are in this with you" without sycophancy.

**Tertiary tone:** Operational precision — numbers before narratives.

### Banned Words (never use in Ares-aligned content)

1. "Disruptive" — antithetical to the stability brand
2. "Guaranteed" — regulatory and credibility risk
3. "Explosive growth" — incompatible with credit conservatism
4. "Revolutionary" — Ares operates on evolution, not revolution
5. "Game-changing" — diminishes earned authority
6. "Exciting opportunity" — retail vernacular; beneath institutional tone
7. "Passive income" — retail/influencer language
8. "Risk-free" — factually wrong; legally dangerous

### Required Words / Phrases (always available)

1. "Risk-adjusted returns"
2. "Disciplined underwriting"
3. "Institutional-quality"
4. "Across the capital structure"
5. "Long-term value creation"
6. "Direct lending"
7. "Downside protection"
8. "Credit cycle"
9. "Portfolio construction"
10. "LP alignment"

## 10. Steal Sheets — Brand Identity Layer

### Steal Sheet 1: The "Credit DNA" Positioning Lock
**What Ares owns:** The narrative that credit discipline is an innate organizational skill, not a product feature.
**How to steal it:** Build a founding-story content series that traces every strategic decision back to credit-first thinking. Show that PE and real assets decisions are filtered through a credit lens.

### Steal Sheet 2: The "Cycle Survivor" Credibility Marker
**What Ares owns:** Implicit authority from having managed through the 2001 downturn, 2008 GFC, 2020 COVID shock, and 2022 rate spike.
**How to steal it:** Publish a "Through the Cycles" retrospective. Name the years. Show the NAV charts. Let the data carry the brand.

### Steal Sheet 3: The "One Platform" Cross-Sell Signal
**What Ares owns:** Messaging that multiple business lines (credit, PE, real assets) share underwriting discipline and deal flow.
**How to steal it:** Use unified case studies that show how a single corporate relationship spawned a leveraged loan, a direct equity co-investment, and a real estate debt facility — proving platform value over single-strategy competitors.
