# 04 — Content DNA
## Ares Management Corporation

---

## 1. Content Philosophy

Ares content operates on a single governing principle: **data earns trust; narrative sustains it.** Every content asset — whether a 60-page LP quarterly report or a two-paragraph LinkedIn post — must anchor to verifiable performance data before making any qualitative claim.

The brand does not use content to entertain. It uses content to reduce uncertainty in the minds of allocators, advisors, and borrowers. Content that cannot answer "what does this tell me about risk and return?" has no place in the Ares content system.

---

## 2. Content Pillars

### Pillar 1 — Credit Market Intelligence
**Purpose:** Demonstrate that Ares has a distinct and differentiated view of credit markets — not consensus, not generic macro commentary.
**Formats:** Quarterly credit outlook, sector-specific memos, spread analysis, dislocation opportunity notes
**Proof point:** "We called the 2022 direct lending vintage as the best entry point in a decade — in Q1 2022."

### Pillar 2 — Portfolio Transparency
**Purpose:** Build LP trust through disclosure depth that exceeds industry norms.
**Formats:** Quarterly LP reports with loan-level attribution, loss rate tables, sector exposure breakdowns, weighted average yield reporting
**Proof point:** ARCC (Ares Capital Corp) is one of the most extensively disclosed BDCs in the market.

### Pillar 3 — Middle Market Ecosystem
**Purpose:** Anchor Ares as the essential capital provider to the backbone of the US and European economy.
**Formats:** Portfolio company spotlights, deal case studies (anonymized or named), economic impact data, job creation metrics
**Proof point:** "Our portfolio companies employ over [X] people across [Y] sectors."

### Pillar 4 — Thought Leadership on Alternative Credit Structure
**Purpose:** Own the intellectual conversation about CLO structures, covenant packages, direct lending underwriting, and private credit regulation.
**Formats:** White papers, regulatory comment letters, academic co-authored research, conference presentations
**Proof point:** Ares partners frequently present at IMN, SFVegas, SuperReturn as subject matter authorities.

### Pillar 5 — Wealth Channel Education
**Purpose:** Translate institutional credit expertise into accessible language for RIAs and financial advisors.
**Formats:** Advisor CE webinars, plain-language fund explainers, FAQ decks, short-form video (under 3 minutes)
**Proof point:** Non-traded BDC distribution through 1,000+ advisor relationships.

## 4. Narrative Frameworks

### Framework A: The Dislocation Opportunity Thesis
**Structure:** (1) Market observation — spread widening or credit tightening, (2) Historical precedent — what happened in prior analogous periods, (3) Ares positioning — how the portfolio is positioned to capture or protect, (4) LP implication — what this means for expected returns.
**Use case:** Market outlook documents, LP quarterly letters, conference keynotes.

### Framework B: The Credit Underwriting Case Study
**Structure:** (1) Company background — sector, size, capital need, (2) Competitive dynamic — why the deal came to Ares, (3) Underwriting process — key risk factors identified and mitigated, (4) Outcome — return achieved, relationship extended, co-investment generated.
**Use case:** LP annual reports, marketing decks, advisor education content.

### Framework C: The Platform Convergence Story
**Structure:** (1) Single company relationship, (2) Credit need addressed (direct loan), (3) Equity need addressed (PE co-invest), (4) Real assets need addressed (facility financing), (5) LP implication — one GP relationship generates multi-asset exposure.
**Use case:** Competitive differentiation materials, institutional pitch decks.

---

## 7. Banned Content Patterns

1. Speculative return projections without historical context
2. Competitor naming without factual substantiation
3. Market timing claims ("now is the best time to invest in X")
4. Lifestyle content unrelated to the investment thesis
5. Social media content that uses slang, memes, or humor
6. Any claim about "guaranteed" income or "safe" investments
7. Testimonials from named LPs without explicit compliance approval
8. Generic ESG virtue-signaling without portfolio-level data

---

## 8. Required Content Patterns

1. Loss rate data cited when making credit quality claims
2. AUM figures accompanied by vintage-year context
3. Risk disclosures integrated into marketing materials (not just footnotes)
4. Attribution: who at Ares authored or approved the market view
5. Comparison benchmarks cited when making relative return claims
6. Fund structure transparency: fees, liquidity terms, minimum investments disclosed early

## 10. Steal Sheets — Content DNA Layer

### Steal Sheet 1: The "Loan Tape Transparency" Content Category
**What Ares owns:** The willingness to disclose portfolio-level credit data that most managers treat as proprietary.
**How to steal it:** Publish a quarterly "Credit Portfolio Dashboard" — anonymized loan-level data: sector, LTV, spread, covenant package, PIK vs. cash pay split. No other manager in the middle market does this at scale. Own the transparency category.

### Steal Sheet 2: The "Cycle Letter" Annual Content Franchise
**What Ares owns:** The credibility to write retrospective cycle letters that carry authentic authority because Ares was present for every cycle described.
**How to steal it:** Launch a named annual letter format — "The Ares Credit Cycle Letter" — authored by the CIO, published every Q1, covering one full market cycle in retrospect plus forward guidance. Build institutional anticipation for this document the way investors wait for Berkshire's annual letter.

### Steal Sheet 3: The "Advisor Confidence Series" — Wealth Channel Content Moat
**What Ares owns:** Recognition that RIA gatekeepers need content that helps them explain private credit to clients — not content that explains private credit to the RIA.
**How to steal it:** Build a named content series — "The Confidence Series" — that gives advisors a new client-ready talking point every month. Each piece answers one question a client would actually ask: "What happens to my money if the company goes bankrupt?" "How is interest paid to me?" "What is a BDC?" Own the advisor confidence category before any competitor realizes it exists.
