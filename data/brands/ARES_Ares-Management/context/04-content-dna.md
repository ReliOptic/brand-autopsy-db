## Content Pillars

Ares's content is almost entirely institutional and professional — designed for LP allocators, financial intermediaries, wealth management platforms, and the broader investment community. Consumer-facing content is limited to ARCC investor relations for retail BDC holders.

| **Strategy & Product Education** | ~25% | Explanation of direct lending mechanics, BDC structures, real estate credit, infrastructure debt. Targets allocators evaluating the asset class as much as Ares specifically. (observed on aresmanagement.com/strategies) |

| **Wealth Channel / Retail** | ~10% | ARCC investor materials, wealth management platform resources, RIA-targeted education on BDC investing. (official, arcc.com; aresmanagement.com/wealth) |

## Hook Patterns

### Hook 1: The Market Dislocation Hook
Opens with a market environment observation that implies opportunity for Ares's strategies.
> "As bank lending standards tighten in response to regional bank stress, the direct lending market is seeing increased deal flow from borrowers who previously relied on bank revolving credit facilities."
Frames market conditions as an opportunity for private credit allocation. (T4_INFERRED from Ares Insights publication patterns; observed on aresmanagement.com/insights)

### Hook 2: The Track Record Hook
Opens with a specific historical performance reference anchored in a market cycle.
> "Through the 2008–2009 financial crisis, the 2020 COVID-19 dislocation, and the 2022 rate cycle, Ares's credit strategies have navigated each cycle with below-industry-average loss rates."
Builds confidence through historical durability rather than forward-looking claims. (T4_INFERRED from institutional asset manager communications conventions)

### Hook 3: The Asset Class Education Hook
Opens with a structural explanation of how private credit generates returns.
> "Direct lending generates returns through three components: a floating interest rate spread over SOFR, an origination fee at close, and covenant protections that provide downside mitigation unavailable in public high-yield markets."
Targets allocators evaluating private credit for the first time. (T4_INFERRED from asset class education content patterns)

### Hook 4: The Scale Data Hook
Opens with an Ares-specific data point that only Ares can provide.
> "Across more than 1,700 portfolio companies in Ares's credit portfolio, here is what default rates, recovery rates, and covenant breach patterns have looked like over the past 12 months."
Scale of proprietary data is the authority signal. (T4_INFERRED from institutional LP communication patterns; portfolio company count approximate from SEC 10-K FY2023)

## Content Prohibitions

1. **Forward-looking return projections**: SEC investment adviser advertising rules prohibit specific projected return claims. Any reference to historical performance requires appropriate disclaimers and comparative context. (T4_INFERRED from SEC Marketing Rule, Rule 206(4)-1)
2. **Characterizing private credit as "safe" or "low risk"**: Private credit carries credit risk, liquidity risk, and valuation risk. Characterizations that minimize these risks are both inaccurate and legally problematic. (T4_INFERRED from regulatory and legal context)
3. **Specific portfolio company identification without consent**: Identifying portfolio companies in marketing materials may require consent and creates potential issues if the company subsequently defaults or faces financial stress. (T4_INFERRED from private credit industry practice)
4. **Performance comparisons without appropriate benchmark and fee disclosure**: Any return figure cited requires vintage year context, gross vs. net disclosure, and benchmark specification per SEC Marketing Rule requirements. (T4_INFERRED from regulatory framework)
5. **Statements about competitor fund performance**: Characterizing a competitor's fund performance negatively without citing the competitor's own public disclosures creates legal exposure. (T4_INFERRED from financial services communications standards)

## Steal Sheet — 3 Transferable Principles

**1. Publish the framework, not just the conclusion.**
Ares's market outlook content is most effective when it explains the analytical framework for reaching a view — not just stating "we are constructive on senior secured direct lending" but showing the spread analysis, the default rate history, and the covenant trend data that supports the view. Allocators who evaluate managers on analytical rigor are persuaded by the quality of the reasoning, not the confidence of the assertion. Transferable principle: in professional services content, the analytical methodology is the brand signal, not the headline conclusion.

**2. The ESG content strategy is an LP retention tool, not just a reputation play.**
Large institutional LPs — particularly European pensions and sovereign wealth funds — have ESG integration requirements that make an asset manager's ESG framework a qualifying criterion, not a differentiator. Publishing rigorous ESG integration methodology protects existing LP relationships rather than primarily attracting new ones. Transferable principle: compliance-adjacent content often functions as a retention mechanism for existing relationships rather than an acquisition tool for new ones.

**3. Asset class education content builds the market before it builds the brand.**
Ares's educational content on direct lending mechanics, BDC structures, and infrastructure debt grows the allocator audience for private credit broadly — which ultimately grows the addressable market for Ares products. This is a long-cycle brand investment. Transferable principle: in markets where the product category itself requires education, investing in category-level education is a brand strategy that generates compounding returns as category adoption grows.
