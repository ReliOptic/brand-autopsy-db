## Hook Patterns (Minimum 4)

**Hook 1 — The Market Intelligence Lead**
Open with current freight market data that only CHRW's scale can generate:
> "Spot truckload rates in the Midwest-Southeast corridor declined 8% in Q3 2023 versus Q3 2022, suggesting continued carrier surplus in that lane. Here's what that means for shipper procurement strategy." (T4_INFERRED from observed CHRW market report style on chrobinson.com)

Structure: Current data point → Market interpretation → Actionable implication. Used in weekly market reports, LinkedIn posts, and shipper education content.

**Hook 2 — The Scale Proof Statement**
Open by establishing the network credential that validates expertise:
> "Our team manages more than 19 million shipments annually across 180 countries. When we say we've seen this supply chain challenge before, we mean it." (T4_INFERRED from observed chrobinson.com brand messaging)

Structure: Quantified scale → Expertise claim → Trust signal. Used in sales materials, website hero sections, and competitive pitches.

**Hook 3 — The Disruption Alert**
Open with a supply chain risk event requiring shipper attention:
> "Hurricane season is approaching. Gulf Coast port disruptions could affect 15% of US import volume for 2–3 weeks. Here's how to build contingency routing into your supply chain now." (T4_INFERRED from CHRW advisory content style)

Structure: External risk event → Quantified impact → Actionable recommendation. Used in email newsletters, customer advisory alerts, and blog content.

**Hook 4 — The Technology Efficiency Proof**
Open with a specific Navisphere automation outcome:
> "Shippers using Navisphere automated tendering reduce carrier booking time from 4 hours to 12 minutes per load on average." (T4_INFERRED from CHRW platform marketing)

Structure: Specific process step → Before metric → After metric. Used in platform marketing, case studies, and Managed Services pitches.

**Hook 5 — The Carrier Opportunity Frame**
Open with a load opportunity framed for carrier decision-making:
> "Empty backhaul from Atlanta to Chicago? We have available loads on that lane with same-day booking." (T4_INFERRED from CHRW carrier load board communications)

Structure: Carrier pain point → Specific opportunity with data → Immediate action. Used in carrier-facing communications, load board, and app notifications.

## Content Prohibitions (Minimum 5)

1. **No guaranteed rate or capacity commitments in marketing content**: CHRW operates in a variable freight market; marketing content must not imply guaranteed pricing or capacity availability that market conditions may not support. (T4_INFERRED from logistics industry standards)
2. **No carrier disparagement**: CHRW's carrier partners are the supply side of the business; content that disparages carriers — even in shipper-facing materials — undermines the network relationships the service depends on. (T4_INFERRED)
3. **No unsubstantiated percentage cost savings claims**: "Save X%" claims require customer-specific data and cannot be published as universal marketing claims without substantiation and methodology disclosure. (T4_INFERRED)
4. **No freight rate forecasts presented as certainties**: Freight market forecasts are probabilistic; presenting rate forecasts as definitive creates credibility risk when markets move differently. Preferred language: "suggests," "indicates," "projects," with explicit uncertainty acknowledgment. (T4_INFERRED)
5. **No implication of asset ownership**: CHRW does not own trucks, ships, or aircraft. Content must not imply asset ownership the company does not hold. (T4_INFERRED from business model)
6. **No customer transaction data in marketing content without explicit permission**: Case studies require documented customer permission; data must be anonymized where customers request it. (T4_INFERRED from standard B2B data privacy practices)

## Steal Sheet — 3 Transferable Principles

**1. Make your proprietary data the most valuable content you publish.**
CHRW's market rate reports and capacity outlook documents are content that no individual shipper can produce independently — they require the transaction volume that only CHRW's scale generates. This content simultaneously serves customers and demonstrates the competitive moat. Transferable structure: identify the data your transaction volume generates that is unavailable to any individual customer, and build a regular publication around it — the content is also your strongest competitive differentiation story.

**2. Serve both sides of the marketplace with dedicated content programs.**
CHRW publishes separate content tracks for shippers and carriers. A two-sided marketplace that only invests in demand-side content neglects the supply-side relationships that make the demand-side value proposition possible. Transferable structure: if your business has supply-side and demand-side participants, audit your content investment across both and build dedicated programs for each.

**3. Disruption advisories create high-engagement moments that build long-term trust.**
When a hurricane, port disruption, or rail stoppage threatens supply chains, CHRW publishes specific, actionable advisories that demonstrate real-time market intelligence. The moments when customers are most anxious are when trust is built or lost. Brands that provide specific, calm guidance in the moment gain durable relationships. Transferable structure: build a crisis advisory content program for your top three customer risk scenarios and publish them promptly when those scenarios occur.
