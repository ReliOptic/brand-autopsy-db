## Major Litigation and Legal Proceedings (Public Record)

**Carrier Classification and Labor Claims**:
The freight brokerage industry has faced litigation in various jurisdictions regarding the classification of drivers and transportation workers. CHRW, as a broker rather than a carrier, does not directly employ drivers; carrier partners bear primary responsibility for driver employment classification. Nonetheless, changes in state or federal regulations regarding independent contractor classification could affect CHRW's carrier network structure. CHRW's 10-K identifies labor and employment regulations as a risk factor. (SEC 10-K, FY2023)

**Cargo Liability Claims**:
Freight brokers face potential cargo liability claims when shipments are damaged, lost, or delayed. CHRW's standard broker agreements define liability limitations consistent with Carmack Amendment provisions governing interstate freight. Cargo claims are a normal operational matter in the logistics industry; CHRW addresses these through insurance coverage and contractual frameworks. (SEC 10-K, FY2023; T4_INFERRED from standard logistics industry practice)

**Antitrust Considerations**:
As the largest non-asset-based freight broker in North America, CHRW's market practices are subject to general antitrust laws. No significant antitrust enforcement actions against CHRW are identified in public records as of the FY2023 10-K. (SEC 10-K, FY2023) This analysis notes CHRW's market scale as context for regulatory awareness, not as a characterization of conduct.

**Securities Litigation**:
CHRW's 10-K identifies securities litigation as a standard risk; no material active securities class actions are identified in the FY2023 disclosures. (SEC 10-K, FY2023)

## Regulatory Landscape

**Federal Motor Carrier Safety Administration (FMCSA)**:
CHRW operates as a licensed property broker under FMCSA authority, which requires registration, surety bond, and compliance with broker regulations. FMCSA regulations govern broker-carrier relationships and transparency requirements. (T4_INFERRED from FMCSA regulatory framework; SEC 10-K, FY2023 references regulatory compliance)

**California AB5 and Independent Contractor Regulation**:
California's AB5 law and similar legislative trends in other states regarding independent contractor classification create potential compliance complexity for CHRW's carrier network. While CHRW is a broker rather than a carrier, regulatory changes affecting carrier classification could impact carrier availability in affected states. CHRW's 10-K identifies regulatory compliance as a risk factor. (SEC 10-K, FY2023)

**US Customs and Border Protection**:
CHRW's global forwarding business operates as a licensed customs broker, subject to CBP regulations governing import/export compliance, documentation, and trade compliance programs. (T4_INFERRED from CBP regulatory framework)

**International Trade Regulations**:
CHRW's global forwarding operations in Europe, Asia, and South America are subject to local freight brokerage, customs, and transportation regulations in each operating jurisdiction. (SEC 10-K, FY2023)

**Surface Transportation Board (STB) and Rail**:
CHRW's intermodal business involves rail carriers subject to STB oversight; broker practices affecting intermodal services fall within this regulatory framework. (T4_INFERRED)

## Project Risk Assessment

**Risk Level: LOW-MEDIUM**

CHRW's litigation profile is typical for a large logistics company: cargo claims, employment matters, and standard commercial disputes. ## Writing Risk Guide

### SAFE — Write Freely

- Citing CHRW network statistics (85,000+ carriers, 100,000+ customers) with attribution to Annual Report
- Summarizing SEC 10-K financial figures with explicit fiscal year citation
- Describing Navisphere as CHRW's proprietary TMS platform per public product descriptions
- Describing competitive market structure using publicly available competitor revenue estimates with "estimated" notation
- Noting that Convoy ceased operations in 2023 as a published matter of public record
- Describing FMCSA broker licensing requirements as regulatory fact
- Citing freight market rate trends from CHRW's own published market reports

### HEDGE — Use Carefully with Attribution

- Statements about CHRW's net revenue margin and carrier pricing practices: describe from disclosed financial data; do not attribute pricing strategies to undisclosed internal decisions
- Competitive market share estimates: always label "(estimated)" and cite basis
- Statements about carrier network quality or reliability: describe network scale facts; do not make qualitative reliability assertions without supporting data
- Statements about technology platform capabilities versus competitors: use observable public feature descriptions; do not assert technical superiority without specific documented evidence

### DO NOT WRITE

- Any assertion that CHRW's carrier relationships constitute misclassification of employees — this is an active legal and regulatory question; describe the regulatory landscape without characterizing CHRW's compliance
- Any characterization of CHRW's market position as monopolistic or anticompetitive — these are legal determinations requiring regulatory proceedings
- Any assertion about CHRW's internal pricing algorithms or carrier rate structures beyond what is publicly disclosed
- Any comparison asserting that CHRW's service is "better" or "worse" than specific competitors without specific, metric-based public data
- Any statements about pending or threatened litigation outcomes
