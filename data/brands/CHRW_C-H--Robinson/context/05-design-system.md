## Layout Principles (Minimum 4)

**1. Data density without visual noise.**
CHRW's primary content — freight market reports, lane rate tables, shipment dashboards — is inherently data-dense. Layout systems must handle large tables, multi-variable charts, and statistical displays without creating visual chaos. Grid discipline, consistent spacing, and clear typographic hierarchy enable data density without cognitive overload. (T4_INFERRED from observed design in freight market reports and Navisphere)

**2. Scale statistics as visual anchors.**
Key network statistics — 85,000+ carriers, 100,000+ customers, $17B+ annual freight — appear as large-type callouts in homepage and sales material layouts. These numbers are the brand's primary proof points and deserve the visual weight of headline treatment. (observed on chrobinson.com)

**3. Two-audience navigation architecture.**
chrobinson.com maintains distinct navigation paths for shippers and carriers, with clear entry points at the top navigation level. This two-sided audience structure is reflected in the site architecture and prevents the confusion of combining two distinct value propositions on a single content page. (observed on chrobinson.com)

**4. Technology platform visuals as brand credibility markers.**
Navisphere dashboard screenshots and platform UI visuals appear prominently in marketing materials. Showing the actual technology interface — rather than aspirational graphics — signals product maturity and builds buyer confidence that the platform is real and deployable. (observed on chrobinson.com technology pages)

**5. Maps and geographic visualization for network representation.**
Freight networks are geographic; CHRW uses lane maps and geographic heat maps to visualize carrier coverage, lane availability, and global reach in ways that statistics alone cannot convey. (T4_INFERRED from observed use of geographic visualizations in CHRW materials)

## Icon Style

- **Style**: Filled icons with rounded corners; clean, minimal detail; 2px stroke for outline variants
- **Color**: CHRW Blue for primary actions; Orange for accent and carrier-facing icons; Gray for secondary navigation
- **Scale**: 24×24px standard; 40×40px for feature highlight tiles; 16×16px for inline table use
- **Categories**: Transportation mode icons (truck, ship, plane, train); supply chain function icons (visibility, routing, analytics); carrier/shipper role icons
- **Source**: Custom logistics icon set; transportation-specific iconography not available in generic icon libraries (estimated)

## Design Prohibitions (Minimum 6)

1. **No consumer-oriented lifestyle photography**: CHRW is a B2B enterprise brand; consumer lifestyle imagery (families, leisure activities) is inconsistent with the institutional credibility that the brand requires in enterprise sales contexts. (T4_INFERRED)
2. **No implied asset ownership in photography**: Using photography of CHRW-branded trucks or owned facilities that CHRW does not possess misrepresents the non-asset business model. Photography should depict carrier trucks without CHRW branding or show the technology and people, not implied fleet assets. (T4_INFERRED from business model)
3. **No rate or capacity guarantees in design callouts**: Large-type callout boxes stating guaranteed rates or capacity availability create legal and contractual exposure; all rate and availability statements require appropriate qualifications. (T4_INFERRED)
4. **No generic map graphics without data**: Maps used as decorative elements without actual lane or network data content add visual noise without brand substance. CHRW map visuals should always show real network data. (T4_INFERRED from design standards)
5. **No font mixing outside the brand type family**: Introducing script, slab, or decorative typefaces into CHRW materials breaks the institutional consistency the B2B brand requires. (T4_INFERRED)
6. **No competitor logos or brand marks in publicly distributed materials**: Competitive comparison content must use text descriptions or neutral category labels, not actual competitor logos, without explicit permission. (T4_INFERRED from standard competitive marketing practice)
7. **No low-resolution data visualizations**: Freight market charts, lane maps, and capacity heat maps must be produced at print resolution even for digital use; pixelated data graphics undermine the data credibility the brand depends on. (T4_INFERRED)
