## 2. Revenue Anatomy

### 2.1 Three-Year Revenue Trend

| Fiscal Year | Revenue ($B) | YoY Change | Net Income ($B) | Net Margin | Source |
|-------------|-------------|------------|-----------------|------------|--------|
| FY2023 | $16.811B | — | $2.078B | 12.4% | (SEC 10-K FY2023) |
| FY2024 | $21.074B | +25.4% | $1.610B | 7.6% | (SEC 10-K FY2024) |
| FY2025 | $17.831B | -15.4% | $2.243B | 12.6% | (SEC 10-K FY2025) |

> **Note**: EDGAR XBRL data provides three years of revenue history for Bunge Global (CIK 0001996862), consistent with the company's filing history under this CIK following corporate restructuring. Earlier years are not available in XBRL under this CIK.

Revenue volatility reflects agricultural commodity price cycles inherent to Bunge's agribusiness model. The FY2024 revenue spike to $21.1B and subsequent decline to $17.8B in FY2025 are consistent with commodity price and volume fluctuations typical of global grain and oilseed trading. (T4_INFERRED)

### 2.2 Segment Revenue

Bunge operates across agribusiness, edible oils, milling, and sugar/bioenergy segments. Detailed segment revenue is disclosed in the Segment Information notes of the 10-K. (T1_OFFICIAL — SEC 10-K FY2025)

### 2.3 Geographic Revenue

Bunge is a global agribusiness with operations in the Americas, Europe, and Asia-Pacific. Geographic revenue detail is available in the Notes to Consolidated Financial Statements. (T1_OFFICIAL — SEC 10-K FY2025)

## 7. Financial Risk Matrix

### Based on SEC 10-K Risk Factors (Item 1A)

| Risk Category | Key Content | Brand Strategy Impact |
|--------------|-------------|----------------------|
| **Commodity Price Volatility** | Adverse weather conditions, including climate change impacts, may affect agricultural commodity availability and pricing | Revenue and margin volatility are structural to Bunge's business model; brand consistency requires financial resilience |
| **Geopolitical and Trade Risk** | Operations span multiple countries subject to trade policy, tariffs, and political instability | Global supply chain disruptions can affect Bunge's ability to execute on client commitments |
| **Regulatory Compliance** | Food safety regulations, environmental laws, and financial reporting requirements vary across jurisdictions | Compliance costs may increase as regulatory environments tighten globally |
| **Currency Risk** | Multi-currency operations expose Bunge to foreign exchange fluctuations | Revenue reported in USD but earned in local currencies; exchange rate moves affect reported results |
| **Counterparty and Credit Risk** | Trading operations involve significant counterparty exposures; customer or supplier default could cause financial loss | Institutional client relationships require creditworthiness and reliability that must be maintained through market cycles |

> **Source**: SEC 10-K FY2025 — Part I, Item 1A Risk Factors

## 8. Brand-Finance Nexus

### 8.1 What Financial Data Reveals About Brand Strategy

| Financial Signal | Brand Strategy Interpretation |
|-----------------|------------------------------|
| Revenue oscillating between $16.8B and $21.1B over 3 years | Bunge's brand must communicate stability and reliability to institutional clients despite inherent commodity revenue volatility |
| Gross margin in 17–27% range | Thin commodity margins mean scale and operational efficiency are the brand's competitive foundation, not product premium |
| CapEx surge to $1.122B in FY2025 | Infrastructure investment signals (T4_INFERRED) long-term capacity commitment, supporting the brand's positioning as a reliable long-term supply chain partner |
| R&D at only 0.2% of revenue | Bunge competes on logistics, scale, and relationships rather than proprietary technology or product innovation |
| Accelerated shareholder returns in FY2025 ($983M total) | Capital return signals financial confidence to institutional investors while CapEx signals operational growth commitment |

### 8.2 Brand-Finance Virtuous Cycle

```
Global Agribusiness Scale & Infrastructure
              |
              v
  Commodity Processing & Trading Volume
              |
              v
  Thin Margins (~17-27% gross) x Scale = Operating Leverage
              |
              v
  Reliable Supply Chain Performance --> Institutional Client Trust
              |
              v
  Revenue Base ($17-21B) --> CapEx for Capacity Expansion
              |
              v  (reinforces)
  Global Agribusiness Scale & Infrastructure
```
