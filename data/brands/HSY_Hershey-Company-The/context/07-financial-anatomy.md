## 2. Revenue Anatomy

### 2.1 Five-Year Revenue Trend

| Fiscal Year | Revenue ($B) | YoY Growth | Net Income ($B) | Net Margin | Source |
|-------------|-------------|------------|-----------------|------------|--------|
| FY2021 | $7.99B | — | $1.15B | 14.4% | (SEC 10-K FY2021) |
| FY2022 | $8.15B | +2.0% | $1.28B | 15.7% | (SEC 10-K FY2022) |
| FY2023 | $8.97B | +10.1% | $1.48B | 16.5% | (SEC 10-K FY2023) |
| FY2024 | $10.42B | +16.2% | $1.64B | 15.8% | (SEC 10-K FY2024) |
| FY2025 | $11.16B | +7.1% | $1.86B | 16.7% | (SEC 10-K FY2025) |

> **5-Year CAGR**: Revenue +8.7%, Net Income +10.1%. Hershey has delivered consistent revenue and profit growth over the five-year period, with the FY2024 acceleration partly reflecting pricing actions taken in response to cocoa and sugar commodity cost increases. Net margin has improved from 14.4% to 16.7%, indicating strong brand pricing power (T4_INFERRED from XBRL data).

### 2.2 Gross Profit Trend

| Fiscal Year | Gross Profit ($B) | Gross Margin | Source |
|-------------|-------------------|-------------|--------|
| FY2021 | $3.62B | 45.4% | (SEC 10-K FY2021) |
| FY2022 | $3.70B | 45.4% | (SEC 10-K FY2022) |
| FY2023 | $4.05B | 45.1% | (SEC 10-K FY2023) |
| FY2024 | $4.50B | 43.2% | (SEC 10-K FY2024) |
| FY2025 | $5.00B | 44.8% | (SEC 10-K FY2025) |

> Gross margins have remained in the 43–45% range, reflecting the strength of Hershey's branded portfolio pricing power despite commodity cost pressures. The FY2024 dip to 43.2% indicates cost absorption; the FY2025 recovery to 44.8% suggests pricing actions were effective (T4_INFERRED).

### 2.3 Segment Structure

Hershey reports across North America Confectionery, North America Salty Snacks, and International segments. Brand portfolio includes Hershey's, Reese's, Kit Kat (U.S. license), Kisses, SkinnyPop, and others. Segment details require the full 10-K Segment Information note (T1_OFFICIAL).

## 4. Investment Structure

### 4.1 Research & Development

| Fiscal Year | R&D ($M) | % of Revenue | Source |
|-------------|---------|-------------|--------|
| FY2021 | $37.1M | 0.5% | (SEC 10-K FY2021) |
| FY2022 | $37.6M | 0.5% | (SEC 10-K FY2022) |
| FY2023 | $40.1M | 0.4% | (SEC 10-K FY2023) |
| FY2024 | $46.9M | 0.5% | (SEC 10-K FY2024) |
| FY2025 | $50.0M | 0.4% | (SEC 10-K FY2025) |

> Hershey's R&D spend (~$37–50M, <0.5% of revenue) is modest, consistent with a consumer confectionery company where brand marketing, product flavor extensions, and packaging innovation drive growth rather than fundamental R&D. The 10-K risk factors note product quality and safety as a reputational risk (T1_OFFICIAL).

### 4.2 Sales, General & Administrative (SG&A)

| Fiscal Year | SG&A ($B) | % of Revenue | Source |
|-------------|----------|-------------|--------|
| FY2021 | $1.91B | 23.9% | (SEC 10-K FY2021) |
| FY2022 | $1.89B | 23.2% | (SEC 10-K FY2022) |
| FY2023 | $2.00B | 22.3% | (SEC 10-K FY2023) |
| FY2024 | $2.24B | 21.5% | (SEC 10-K FY2024) |
| FY2025 | $2.44B | 21.8% | (SEC 10-K FY2025) |

> SG&A has grown in absolute terms but declined as a percentage of revenue, indicating operating leverage. Marketing and advertising costs are included within SG&A and not separately disclosed in XBRL data. Hershey's brand investment includes iconic advertising campaigns for Hershey's, Reese's, and Kisses brands.

### 4.3 Capital Expenditure

| FY2021 | $318.2M | 4.0% | (SEC 10-K FY2021) |

## 7. Financial Risk Matrix

### Based on SEC 10-K FY2025 Risk Factors (Item 1A)

| Risk Category | Key Content | Brand Strategy Impact |
|--------------|-------------|----------------------|
| Product Quality & Safety | 10-K cites reputational risk from "issues related to the quality and safety of our products, ingredients or packaging" | A product recall or safety incident would directly damage the Hershey brand's 130+ year heritage of consumer trust |
| Manufacturing Concentration | 10-K notes "approximately 74% of our manufacturing capacity is located in the United States" | U.S. manufacturing disruption (weather, labor action) would affect the brand's ability to meet consumer demand |
| Commodity Cost Volatility | Cocoa, sugar, dairy, and nuts are subject to price volatility; hedging programs may be insufficient | Commodity spikes compress margins and may force price increases that could reduce brand volume |
| Consumer Preferences & ESG | 10-K cites "issues or concerns...relating to human and workplace rights, and other environmental, social or governance matters" | Cocoa supply chain sustainability concerns (child labor in West Africa) are a reputational risk for the entire confectionery industry |
| Competition | Hershey competes with Mars, Mondelez, and Ferrero for shelf space and consumer preference | Competitive promotional activity or product innovation by rivals could erode market share of iconic Hershey brands |
| Tariffs & Trade | 10-K notes potential cost impacts from import tariffs on key ingredients and packaging materials | Imported cocoa and other ingredients face cost risk under trade policy changes; affects pricing decisions |

> **Source**: SEC 10-K FY2025 — Part I, Item 1A, Risk Factors

## 8. Brand-Finance Nexus

### 8.1 What Financial Data Reveals About Brand Strategy

| Financial Signal | Brand Strategy Interpretation |
|-----------------|------------------------------|
| Revenue CAGR of +8.7% over 5 years | Sustained growth confirms Hershey's brand pricing power and consumer loyalty in core confectionery categories |
| Gross margins stable at 43–45% | Premium brand positioning allows consistent cost pass-through; Hershey's iconic brand portfolio commands retail shelf premium |
| SG&A declining as % of revenue | Operating leverage demonstrates scale benefits of the brand; marketing efficiency improving as brand equity compounds |
| R&D at <0.5% of revenue | Brand-led innovation (Reese's flavor extensions, seasonal campaigns) drives growth more than R&D; brand investment is the primary competitive moat |
| CapEx surge to $771M in FY2025 | Significant manufacturing investment signals management's confidence in long-term demand; positions brand for capacity-constrained supply scenarios |
| Consistent $1B+ annual shareholder returns | Mature, predictable cash generation supports long-term brand investment without financial constraints |
