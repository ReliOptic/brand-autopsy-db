## Battle Cards

### vs MetLife

- **Their claim**: comprehensive insurance scale, employer credibility, and wider product breadth. (observed)
- **Our counter**: Aflac is easier to understand because it solves one emotionally clear problem: what fills the everyday financial gap after a covered event? (T4_INFERRED)
- **Vulnerability**: MetLife can appear more complete to enterprise buyers who prefer fewer vendor relationships. (observed)
- **Caution**: Aflac must avoid sounding too narrow or too consumer-only when the buyer is evaluating broader benefits architecture. (T4_INFERRED)

### vs Unum

- **Their claim**: stronger disability-related seriousness and employer-benefits competency. (observed)
- **Our counter**: Aflac is more memorable, more direct, and better at translating coverage value into customer language ordinary people can repeat. (T4_INFERRED)
- **Vulnerability**: Unum may feel more technically grounded in certain disability-heavy conversations. (observed)
- **Caution**: if Aflac leans too hard on advertising memory without enough benefit clarity, it risks being remembered more than understood. (T4_INFERRED)

### vs HSAs / FSAs / major medical logic

- **Their claim**: healthcare financing can be managed through existing plan structures and tax-advantaged tools. (observed)
- **Our counter**: Aflac's strength is not replacing those systems; it is naming the lived disruption those systems do not emotionally or operationally solve fast enough. (T4_INFERRED)
- **Vulnerability**: buyers may misclassify supplemental insurance as optional if the gap is poorly articulated. (observed; estimated)
- **Caution**: Aflac must stay precise that it is complementary coverage, not total financial protection. (official / T4_INFERRED)

## Differentiation Summary

> Aflac's real competitive edge is that it makes supplemental insurance easier to remember, easier to explain, and easier to emotionally justify than most of its category peers.

---

## Steal Sheet — 3 Transferable Principles

**1. Specialization can beat breadth when the pain point is emotionally clear.**  
Aflac does not need to be everything. It needs to be the first brand people think of when the uncovered financial gap becomes real.

**2. Brand memory matters more in low-engagement categories than many teams admit.**  
The duck is not decoration. It is a distribution mechanism for category understanding.

**3. The best category simplification is concrete.**  
Direct cash paid to you is a much stronger strategic sentence than a general promise of financial wellness or support.
